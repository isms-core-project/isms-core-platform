# Information Security Awareness and Training Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to ensure that all employees of the organisation and, where relevant, third-party users receive appropriate information security awareness, education, and training, and regular updates on organisational policies and procedures as relevant for their job function.

This policy supports Swiss nFADP (revDSG) and the Data Protection Ordinance (DSV) by implementing training as a technical and organisational measure appropriate to risk, including awareness of personal criminal liability under nFADP Art. 60–66. Where the organisation processes data of individuals in the EU/EEA, GDPR requirements also apply.

## Scope

All employees and third-party users.

## Principle

Management is committed to information security awareness, education, and training throughout the organisation. All personnel shall understand their responsibilities and be equipped with the knowledge to protect organisation information and comply with applicable policies, laws, and regulations.

---

## Awareness and Training Programme

### Management Commitment

Executive management shall visibly support and promote the information security awareness and training programme. This includes endorsing training requirements, allocating resources, and setting the expectation that all personnel complete required training.

### Training Audiences

| Audience | Training Level | Description |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| **All employees** | Baseline awareness | Mandatory security awareness covering core topics |
| **New starters** | Onboarding training | Baseline awareness before access to systems processing confidential or personal data |
| **Third-party users** | Awareness briefing | Security obligations relevant to their access and role |
| **IT and technical staff** | Role-based technical | Secure system administration, vulnerability awareness, incident handling |
| **Developers** | Role-based technical | Secure coding practices, application security (OWASP Top 10) |
| **Privileged access holders** | Enhanced awareness | Elevated risk awareness, privileged account management, social engineering defence |
| **Management** | Governance awareness | ISMS governance, risk management, regulatory obligations, incident escalation |

---

## Training Topics

### Mandatory Topics — All Employees

The following topics shall be covered in baseline security awareness training:

| # | Topic | Key Content |
|---|-------|-------------|
| 1 | **Information security policies** | Overview of ISMS policies; management commitment; employee responsibilities |
| 2 | **Acceptable use** | Rules for using IT systems, internet, email, cloud services, and AI tools |
| 3 | **Information classification and handling** | Classification levels (Public, Internal, Confidential); labelling; handling and destruction |
| 4 | **Access and authentication** | Password hygiene; multi-factor authentication; screen locking; credential protection |
| 5 | **Phishing and social engineering** | Recognising phishing emails, vishing, smishing, pretexting, and impersonation |
| 6 | **Incident reporting** | What constitutes a security event; how to report; reporting channels; non-retaliation |
| 7 | **Data protection and privacy** | Swiss nFADP principles; personal data handling; data subject rights; personal criminal liability (CHF 250,000) |
| 8 | **Clean desk and clear screen** | Secure storage of documents; screen locking; printer collection; whiteboard clearing |
| 9 | **Remote working security** | VPN use; home Wi-Fi security; physical security of devices; public Wi-Fi risks |
| 10 | **Removable media and data transfer** | Removable media restrictions; approved transfer methods; encryption requirements |

### Additional Topics — Role-Based

| Audience | Additional Topics |
|----------|-------------------|
| **IT administrators** | Privileged access management, log review, vulnerability management, patch management, change management |
| **Developers** | Secure coding (OWASP Top 10), code review, dependency management, secrets management |
| **Finance and HR** | Business email compromise (BEC), invoice fraud detection, sensitive personal data handling |
| **Management** | Risk management, regulatory compliance, incident escalation, audit readiness |

## Training Schedule

### New Starters

Information security awareness training shall be provided to new starters **before** they are granted access to systems that process, store, or transmit confidential or personal data. Training shall be completed within **5 business days** of the start date.

The information security policy and acceptable use policy shall be provided to new starters as part of the onboarding process. Acknowledgement of these policies shall be obtained before system access is granted.

### Annual Training

General information security awareness training shall be conducted for all employees **at least annually**. Training shall be refreshed to reflect current threats, policy changes, and lessons learned from incidents.

### Ongoing Awareness

Information security awareness shall be reinforced throughout the year using a variety of methods:

| Method | Frequency | Duration | Delivery |
|--------|-----------|----------|----------|
| **Annual awareness training** | Annually | 45–60 minutes | E-learning platform (e.g., KnowBe4, Proofpoint, Mimecast, or equivalent) or instructor-led (in-person or virtual) |
| **Phishing simulations** | Quarterly | N/A | Automated phishing simulation platform with immediate feedback |
| **Micro-learning modules** | Quarterly | 5–10 minutes | E-learning platform; pushed to employees via email or intranet |
| **Security reminders** | As needed | 2–5 minutes (reading) | Email communications, intranet updates, or team briefings on emerging threats |
| **Lessons learned** | After incidents | 10–15 minutes | Anonymised case studies from internal or industry incidents shared with relevant teams |
| **New starter onboarding** | Within 5 business days of start | 30–45 minutes | E-learning platform or instructor-led |

### Role Change Training

When an employee's role changes significantly or their access to data types changes, additional training shall be provided appropriate to the new role and its associated risks.

## Phishing Simulation Programme

The organisation shall conduct a phishing simulation programme to assess and improve employee resilience to social engineering attacks.

### Programme Principles

- Simulations shall use **realistic, work-relevant scenarios** (e.g., delivery notifications, meeting invitations, password reset requests) — not deceptive or humiliating content.
- Employees who interact with simulated phishing shall receive **immediate feedback** explaining what was suspicious and how to recognise similar attacks.
- The programme is **educational, not punitive**. Simulation results shall be used to identify training needs, not for disciplinary action.
- Employees who report simulated phishing shall be **positively recognised**.
- Simulation difficulty shall be gradually increased over time.

### Phishing Metrics and Targets

| Metric | Target | Red Threshold | Action |
|--------|--------|---------------|--------|
| **Click rate** (percentage of employees who click the simulated phishing link) | <10% | >25% | Additional targeted training for affected teams |
| **Reporting rate** (percentage of employees who report the simulated phishing) | >50% | <20% | Reinforce reporting mechanism awareness |
| **Repeat offenders** (employees who click in 2+ consecutive simulations) | <5% of workforce | >10% | Mandatory one-to-one coaching session with information security |
| **Time to report** (average time from receipt to report) | <30 minutes | >4 hours | Highlight urgency in next awareness communication |

### Reporting

A single, standardised method for reporting suspected phishing shall be provided to all employees (e.g., a "Report Phishing" button in the email client). Employees shall be trained to use this reporting mechanism.

---

## Assessment and Acknowledgement

### Knowledge Assessment

Employees shall be assessed on their understanding of information security at the completion of awareness training. Assessment may include quizzes, scenario-based questions, or practical exercises.

The minimum passing score for all assessments shall be **80%**. Employees who do not achieve the passing score shall be required to repeat the training within **10 business days**. Two consecutive failures shall be escalated to the employee's line manager for additional support.

### Policy Acknowledgement

All employees shall formally acknowledge that they have received training and understand their information security responsibilities. Acknowledgement shall be recorded and retained.

New starters shall acknowledge the information security policy and acceptable use policy during onboarding before system access is granted.

---

## Training Records

### Training Register

A register of information security training and competency shall be maintained for all employees. The register shall record:

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Employee name** | Individual who received training |
| **Training type** | Onboarding, annual awareness, role-based, phishing simulation |
| **Training date** | Date training was completed |
| **Training content** | Topics covered or course name |
| **Assessment result** | Pass/fail or score (where applicable) |
| **Acknowledgement** | Signed acknowledgement received (Yes/No) |
| **Next due date** | When refresher training is due |
| **Language** | Language in which training was delivered |
| **Delivery method** | E-learning, instructor-led, briefing, simulation |

Training records shall be retained for the duration of employment plus **2 years** after departure.

### Completion Tracking

Training completion rates shall be monitored. Non-completion shall be escalated:

- **30 days overdue**: Reminder to employee and line manager.
- **60 days overdue**: Escalation to department head.
- **90 days overdue**: Escalation to CISO. Access restrictions may be applied until training is completed.

---

## Training and Awareness Plan

A training and awareness plan shall be maintained for each calendar year. The plan shall include:

- Training and awareness campaigns scheduled for the year.
- Target audiences for each activity.
- Delivery methods (e-learning, classroom, briefing, simulation).
- Budget and resource allocation.
- Alignment with legal and regulatory requirements, business needs, and risk assessments.

The plan shall be reviewed and updated at least annually by the information security management team.

---

## Training Content Updates

Training content shall be reviewed and updated in response to:

- **Annual review**: Scheduled annual refresh to incorporate current threats and policy changes.
- **Policy changes**: Within **30 days** of significant policy updates affecting employee responsibilities.
- **Significant incidents**: Lessons learned from internal incidents or major industry incidents relevant to the organisation.
- **Regulatory changes**: Changes to nFADP, GDPR, or other applicable regulations.
- **Phishing simulation results**: If phishing metrics breach red thresholds, targeted training content shall be developed within **30 days**.
- **New technology adoption**: Introduction of new tools (e.g., AI assistants, cloud services) requiring updated acceptable use guidance.

---

## Training Effectiveness Measurement

The information security management team shall measure the effectiveness of the awareness and training programme using the following framework:

| Measure | Method | Target | Reporting |
|---------|--------|--------|-----------|
| **Training completion rate** | LMS/training platform tracking | ≥95% within deadline | Quarterly to CISO |
| **Knowledge assessment scores** | Post-training quiz | ≥80% average score; <5% failures | Quarterly |
| **Phishing click rate trend** | Simulation platform | Downward trend year-over-year | Quarterly |
| **Phishing reporting rate trend** | Simulation platform | Upward trend year-over-year | Quarterly |
| **Incident reporting volume** | Incident management system | Stable or increasing (indicates awareness, not problems) | Annually |
| **Time from incident to report** | Incident management system | Decreasing trend | Annually |
| **Employee feedback** | Post-training survey (optional) | ≥3.5/5.0 satisfaction rating | Annually |

---

## Language and Accessibility

Training materials shall be provided in the languages used within the organisation, reflecting Switzerland's multilingual context:

- **Primary language**: Training shall be available in the organisation's primary business language.
- **Additional languages**: Where the workforce includes employees whose primary language differs from the business language, key training materials (at minimum: annual awareness, onboarding, and phishing reporting instructions) shall be made available in the relevant additional languages (e.g., German, French, Italian, English).
- **Accessibility**: Training platforms shall support accessibility features (screen readers, subtitles for video content, adjustable text size) where reasonably practicable.

# Policy Compliance

## Compliance Measurement

The information security management team will verify compliance with this policy through various methods, including but not limited to, training completion rate monitoring, phishing simulation results, knowledge assessment outcomes, internal and external audits, and feedback to the policy owner.

## Exceptions

Any exception to this policy shall be approved and recorded by the Information Security Manager in advance, with documented risk acceptance, compensating controls, and a defined review date. Exceptions shall be reported to the Management Review Team.

## Non-Compliance

An employee found to have violated this policy may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

## Continual Improvement

This policy is reviewed and updated as part of the continual improvement process. Reviews shall consider changes to the threat landscape, regulatory changes (including Swiss nFADP and GDPR developments), training platform capabilities, phishing simulation results and trends, and lessons learned from incidents.
