# Management Responsibilities Policy

## Purpose

This policy establishes requirements for management responsibilities in information security, ensuring that all management personnel actively support, promote, and enforce the organisation's security posture. Management shall require all personnel to apply information security in accordance with established policies and procedures.

This policy supports Swiss nFADP (revDSG) Art. 8 by implementing organisational measures to ensure adequate data security through management leadership, resource allocation, and personnel oversight. Where the organisation processes data of individuals in the EU/EEA, GDPR Art. 32 requirements for security governance also apply.

## Scope

This policy applies to all management personnel with supervisory or decision-making authority, including:

- Executive Management (CEO, CTO, COO, CFO, Board members)
- Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
- Department Heads and Directors
- Team Leads and Line Managers
- Project Managers

All personnel under management authority are indirectly within scope, as management is accountable for ensuring their compliance with information security requirements.

## Principle

Information security is a business enabler, not an obstacle. Management leads by example, allocates adequate resources, and holds personnel accountable for security compliance. The organisation's approach to information security shall be driven from the top, with visible commitment at every level of management. Management responsibilities for information security are non-delegable; while tasks may be assigned to others, accountability remains with the responsible manager.

## Management Commitment Requirements

### Visible Leadership

All management personnel shall demonstrate active commitment to information security through:

- Communicating the importance of information security to their teams in regular meetings.
- Participating in security awareness activities alongside their personnel.
- Acknowledging and recognising security-conscious behaviour.
- Allocating time within team schedules for security-related activities.
- Including information security as a standing agenda item in departmental meetings.

### Resource Allocation

Management shall ensure adequate resources are available for information security:

- Approving budget for security initiatives within their authority.
- Ensuring adequate staffing for security responsibilities.
- Providing tools, training, and infrastructure for secure operations.
- Supporting security improvement projects with personnel and funding.

**Adequacy criteria** for resource allocation decisions shall consider:

- Risk assessment outcomes — high and critical risks receive priority funding.
- Regulatory compliance requirements — mandatory obligations take precedence.
- Industry benchmarks for organisations of similar size and sector.
- CISO recommendations with documented business case justification.

Resource requests for approved security initiatives shall be processed within 30 calendar days. Disputes over resource adequacy shall be escalated per the escalation path defined in this policy.

### Policy Enforcement

Management shall ensure their teams understand and comply with applicable security policies:

- Ensuring all personnel under their authority are aware of relevant policies and procedures.
- Holding personnel accountable for policy compliance through regular oversight.
- Escalating security concerns and policy violations appropriately.
- Not requesting, approving, or facilitating policy bypasses without a formal exception approved per the exception process.

### Commitment Verification

Management commitment shall be assessed annually using objective criteria:

| Criterion | Target | Measurement |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Attendance at mandatory security reviews | >= 80% | Attendance records from review meetings |
| Completion of management security training | 100% within 30 days of role assignment | Training completion records in [LMS] |
| Resource approval response time | <= 30 calendar days | Request tracking in [GRC Tool] |
| Acknowledgment of security reports | Quarterly minimum | Documented sign-off on quarterly reports |
| Participation in incident response | When incidents affect their domain | Incident records and post-incident review attendance |

Assessment results shall be documented and reviewed by Executive Management. **Scores below 70% trigger a mandatory remediation plan** (see Remediation for Non-Compliance).

## Security Culture Leadership

Management shall foster a positive security culture across the organisation through deliberate action and consistent example.

### Leading by Example

- Following the same security policies and procedures as all other personnel — no management exemptions.
- Using strong authentication, secure communication channels, and approved devices.
- Reporting security concerns promptly and without hesitation.
- Treating security requirements as business priorities, not administrative burdens.
- Demonstrating compliance with clean desk, screen lock, and physical security practices.

### Encouraging Reporting

Management shall create an environment where personnel feel safe reporting security issues:

- Responding constructively to all security incident and near-miss reports.
- Not penalising good-faith security reporting, even when reports result from the reporter's own actions.
- Providing timely feedback on reported issues and their resolution.
- Publicly recognising employees who identify security improvements or report incidents promptly.

### Security Recognition Programme

Management shall implement a structured recognition programme for security-conscious behaviour:

**Recognition categories**:

| Category | Criteria | Recognition |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| **Proactive reporting** | Reporting security incidents, near-misses, or vulnerabilities before impact | Team meeting acknowledgment; quarterly "Security Champion" nomination |
| **Advanced training** | Completing voluntary or advanced security certifications/courses | Training completion highlighted in team communications; certification cost reimbursement where budgeted |
| **Improvement suggestions** | Proposing security improvements that are adopted and implemented | Named credit in implementation documentation; consideration for annual award |
| **Incident response** | Significant contribution to incident containment, investigation, or post-incident review | Written commendation from CISO; highlighted in management review |
| **Sustained excellence** | Consistently demonstrating security-first behaviour over 12+ months | Annual Security Excellence Award at company event; letter of commendation in personnel file |

**Programme administration**:

- **Nomination**: Any manager may nominate personnel; self-nomination permitted for Proactive Reporting.
- **Approval**: CISO reviews nominations quarterly; Annual Security Excellence Award approved by Executive Management.
- **Coordination**: HR administers the programme, including budget for any tangible recognition (gifts, training reimbursement) within approved annual allocation.
- **Communication**: Quarterly recognition announced via internal communications (newsletter, all-hands, intranet). Individual consent obtained before public recognition.
- **Anti-gaming**: Recognition based on quality and impact, not volume of reports. Fabricated or trivial reports do not qualify.

**Annual Security Excellence Award**: Presented at year-end company event. Selection criteria: sustained commitment, measurable impact, peer endorsement. Winner selected by panel (CISO + HR + previous year's winner).

### Continuous Improvement

- Participating in post-incident reviews to identify lessons learned.
- Supporting implementation of lessons learned and corrective actions.
- Advocating for security improvements based on risk assessment outcomes.
- Reviewing and acting on security culture survey results.

### Security Culture Survey

An annual Security Culture Survey shall be conducted to measure the effectiveness of management's security leadership and the organisation's overall security awareness posture.

**Survey scope and methodology**:

- **Population**: All personnel (employees, contractors, interns) — anonymous participation.
- **Administration**: HR coordinates distribution via [Survey Tool]; CISO designs questions with HR input.
- **Timing**: Q4 annually (results available for Q1 management review).
- **Minimum response rate**: 60% for results to be considered statistically valid. If below 60%, extend survey period by 2 weeks with management encouragement.

**Survey dimensions** (minimum):

| Dimension | Example Questions |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Management commitment visibility** | "My manager demonstrates commitment to information security" |
| **Reporting culture** | "I feel safe reporting security incidents without fear of blame" |
| **Policy awareness** | "I understand the security policies relevant to my role" |
| **Training effectiveness** | "Security training I received was relevant and practical" |
| **Resource adequacy** | "I have the tools and time needed to follow security procedures" |

**Results handling**:

- Results aggregated by department/team (minimum 5 responses per group for anonymity).
- CISO presents results to Executive Management with trend analysis (year-over-year comparison).
- Each department head receives their team's results and develops an action plan for any dimension scoring below 3.5/5.0.
- Action plans tracked in ISMS Gap Register. Progress reviewed at next quarterly management review.

## Personnel Compliance Accountability

Management shall ensure all personnel under their authority comply with information security requirements throughout the employment lifecycle.

### Onboarding Oversight

Management shall verify that new personnel complete all security onboarding requirements before being granted system access.

**Security Onboarding Checklist** (completed per new hire, contractor, or intern):

| # | Item | Responsible | Deadline | Verification |
|---|------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Employment contract** with information security clauses signed | HR | Before start date | Signed contract in HR file |
| 2 | **NDA/Confidentiality agreement** signed | HR | Before start date or day 1 | Signed NDA in HR file |
| 3 | **Acceptable Use Policy** acknowledged | Line Manager | Day 1 | Signed acknowledgment form |
| 4 | **Mandatory security awareness training** completed | New hire via [LMS] | Within 5 business days | [LMS] completion record |
| 5 | **Role-specific security responsibilities** communicated and understood | Line Manager | Within 5 business days | Documented briefing (email confirmation or meeting notes) |
| 6 | **Role-specific security training** completed (if applicable) | New hire via [LMS] / trainer | Before access to Confidential/Restricted systems | [LMS] completion record or trainer sign-off |
| 7 | **Access provisioned** per approved access request | IT / IAM Team | After items 1-4 completed | Access request ticket closed with provisioning confirmation |
| 8 | **Manager sign-off** on completed onboarding | Line Manager | Within 10 business days | Checklist signed and filed with HR |

**Access gate**: System access (beyond basic email and intranet) shall not be provisioned until items 1-4 are confirmed complete. [Identity Provider] group assignment conditional on [LMS] training completion status.

**Checklist storage**: Completed checklists stored in HR personnel file and referenced in [ITSM Tool] onboarding ticket.

### Ongoing Oversight

Management shall maintain continuous oversight of personnel compliance:

**Training monitoring** (automated compliance tracking):

- **Automated reporting**: [LMS] generates monthly training compliance dashboard per department/manager showing: completed, overdue, upcoming deadlines.
- **Automated alerts**: [LMS] sends automated reminder to personnel at 7 days before deadline, on deadline day, and 7 days overdue. Manager copied on overdue alerts.
- **Manager review**: Managers review quarterly training completion reports from HR/[LMS].
- **Follow-up**: Managers follow up with overdue personnel within 5 business days of notification.
- **Escalation tiers**:
  - 7 days overdue: Automated reminder to individual + manager notification.
  - 14 days overdue: Manager contacts individual directly; documents follow-up action.
  - 30 days overdue: Manager escalates to CISO; CISO may restrict system access for non-compliant personnel until training completed.
  - 45 days overdue: CISO escalates to Department Head; formal non-compliance record created.
- **Access restriction**: Personnel overdue > 30 days on mandatory security awareness training may have access to Confidential systems suspended until training completed (enforced via [Identity Provider] group membership).
- **Documenting**: All follow-up actions documented in [LMS] or [ITSM Tool] with timestamp, action taken, and outcome.

**Policy compliance**:
- Addressing security policy violations promptly using the severity framework in the ISMS Governance Policy.
- Including security compliance as a factor in performance evaluations:

**Security compliance in performance evaluations**:

For roles with defined security responsibilities (all management roles, IT staff, security team, compliance staff):

| Evaluation Component | Weight | Measurement |
|---------------------|--------|-------------|
| **Policy compliance** | 10-15% of overall evaluation | No unresolved policy violations; compliance with applicable security requirements |
| **Training completion** | 5% | All mandatory security training completed on time (personal + team if manager) |
| **Incident response participation** | 5% (when applicable) | Active participation in incident response for incidents affecting their domain |
| **Security culture contribution** | 5% | Proactive security reporting, improvement suggestions, team security leadership |

**Integration with HR performance management**:

- Security compliance criteria included in annual performance review template (HR maintains).
- Managers assess their direct reports against security criteria during standard review cycle.
- CISO provides input to HR on security-specific performance data (training completion rates, incident participation, assessment scores) for management roles.
- Exceptional security performance eligible for recognition per Security Recognition Programme.
- Sustained security non-compliance (two consecutive quarters of policy violations without remediation) treated as performance issue per HR policy.

**Important**: Security performance criteria shall be communicated to personnel at the start of each review period. No individual shall be evaluated against criteria they were not informed of in advance.
- Conducting periodic security awareness discussions in team meetings (minimum quarterly).
- Verifying that personnel who change roles receive updated security training for their new responsibilities.

### Offboarding Oversight

Management shall ensure timely and complete security offboarding for departing personnel.

**Security Offboarding Checklist** (completed per departure — resignation, termination, contract end, role change):

| # | Item | Responsible | Deadline | Verification |
|---|------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | **HR notified** of departure and termination type | Line Manager | Immediately upon resignation/decision | HR system entry or email notification |
| 2 | **IT offboarding ticket raised** | HR (auto-triggered) or Line Manager | Same day as HR notification | [ITSM Tool] ticket created |
| 3 | **Access revocation** across all systems | IT / IAM Team | Per SLA by termination type (see A.6.4-5 policy) | Per-system revocation confirmed in offboarding ticket |
| 4 | **Physical access revoked** (badge, keys, biometrics) | Facilities / IT | Same day as logical access revocation | Badge deactivation log; key return receipt |
| 5 | **Assets returned** (laptop, phone, tokens, documents) | Line Manager + IT | Within 5 business days of last working day | Asset return form signed by IT |
| 6 | **Data return/deletion confirmed** | Departing individual + IT | Last working day | Self-certification form signed |
| 7 | **Shared account passwords rotated** | IT / IAM Team | Per A.6.4-5 shared account rotation SLA | Rotation documented in offboarding ticket |
| 8 | **NDA/confidentiality reminder** issued and acknowledged | HR | Exit interview or last working day | Signed acknowledgment |
| 9 | **Exit interview completed** (security-focused) | HR + Security (if applicable) | Per A.6.4-5 exit interview timing | Exit interview form signed |
| 10 | **Manager sign-off** on completed offboarding | Line Manager | Within 5 business days of last working day | Checklist signed and filed with HR |

**Incomplete offboarding escalation**: If any checklist item remains incomplete 5 business days after departure, IT Operations escalates to CISO. Items unresolved after 10 business days trigger formal nonconformity.

**Checklist storage**: Completed checklists stored in HR personnel file and referenced in [ITSM Tool] offboarding ticket. Retained for employment duration + 2 years.

## Management Review Participation

Management shall actively participate in ISMS management review activities as required by ISO 27001:2022 Clause 9.3.

### Required Participation Schedule

| Management Level | Review Type | Frequency |
|-----------------|-------------|-----------|
| Executive Management | ISMS management review | Quarterly |
| Department Heads | Security posture briefing | Annual (minimum) |
| Team Leads | Incident review participation | When incidents affect their domain |
| All management levels | Security culture survey | Annual |

### Participation Defined

Active participation means more than attendance. Management personnel shall:

- **Attend**: Physical or virtual presence for the full session. Absences require prior approval and documented catch-up within 10 business days.
- **Contribute**: Provide requested domain information and risk updates prior to the review, per the published agenda.
- **Decide**: Document decisions and approvals within their authority during the review.
- **Follow through**: Complete assigned actions from the review within agreed timeframes.

### Quarterly ISMS Management Review Agenda

Each quarterly management review shall follow a standardised agenda to ensure consistent coverage and efficient use of executive time:

| # | Agenda Item | Presenter | Time (indicative) | Input Required |
|---|-------------|-----------|-------------------|---------------|
| 1 | **Previous actions review** — Status of actions from last review | CISO | 10 min | Action tracker update |
| 2 | **Security posture summary** — Key metrics, compliance scores, trend analysis | CISO | 15 min | Dashboard/report pre-circulated 5 days before |
| 3 | **Risk register update** — New risks, changed risk ratings, risk treatment progress | CISO / Risk Owner | 15 min | Updated risk register |
| 4 | **Incident summary** — Incidents since last review, lessons learned, remediation status | CISO / Incident Manager | 10 min | Incident summary report |
| 5 | **Audit findings** — Internal/external audit results, nonconformities, corrective actions | CISO / Internal Audit | 10 min | Audit finding tracker |
| 6 | **Regulatory and compliance update** — New regulations, compliance status, upcoming deadlines | Compliance Officer | 10 min | Regulatory Register changes |
| 7 | **Resource and budget review** — Security budget utilisation, pending requests, resource gaps | CISO / CFO | 10 min | Budget report |
| 8 | **Department security updates** — Each department head provides brief domain update | Department Heads | 15 min | Pre-submitted written updates |
| 9 | **Decisions required** — Risk acceptances, policy approvals, investment decisions | Chair (CEO or delegate) | 10 min | Decision papers pre-circulated |
| 10 | **Actions and next review** — New actions assigned with owners and deadlines | Chair | 5 min | — |

**Pre-meeting requirements**: Agenda and supporting materials circulated 5 business days before the review. Department heads submit written updates 3 business days before. Late submissions: Item deferred to next review.

**Meeting minutes**: Documented by CISO or delegate within 3 business days. Minutes include: attendees, key decisions (with rationale), assigned actions (with owner and deadline), risk acceptances (with justification). Minutes approved by Chair and distributed to all attendees.

### Input Contributions

Management shall provide the following inputs to ISMS management reviews:

- Feedback on security control effectiveness within their domains.
- Identification of emerging risks and changes to the threat landscape.
- Proposals for security improvements based on operational experience.
- Reporting of resource constraints that affect security posture.
- Status updates on remediation actions assigned from prior reviews.

### Decision Authority

Within management reviews, management personnel shall exercise their authority to:

- Approve risk treatment decisions within delegated authority.
- Accept residual risks with documented justification and risk owner acknowledgment.
- Prioritise security investments based on business impact and risk.
- Approve or escalate corrective actions arising from audit findings.

**Decision Authority Matrix**:

| Decision Type | Authority Level | Financial Threshold | Escalation |
|--------------|----------------|--------------------|-----------|
| **Risk acceptance** (low/medium risk) | CISO | N/A (no direct spend) | If risk rating disputed, escalate to Executive Management |
| **Risk acceptance** (high/critical risk) | Executive Management | N/A | Board notification for critical risks |
| **Security tool/service procurement** | CISO (within approved budget) | Up to CHF 25,000 | Department Head > CHF 25,000; Executive Management > CHF 100,000 |
| **Security initiative funding** (new project) | Department Head + CISO | Up to CHF 50,000 | Executive Management > CHF 50,000 |
| **Policy exception approval** | CISO | N/A | Executive Management for exceptions affecting regulatory compliance |
| **Corrective action approval** (from audit/incident) | CISO | Up to CHF 25,000 | Executive Management > CHF 25,000 or cross-departmental impact |
| **Emergency security expenditure** (incident response) | CISO | Up to CHF 50,000 | CEO/CFO > CHF 50,000 (post-hoc ratification within 5 business days) |
| **Vendor security assessment waiver** | CISO + Procurement | N/A | Executive Management for strategic vendors |

**Resource dispute resolution**: Where resource requests are declined or deprioritised:

1. CISO documents the security risk impact of the resource gap.
2. Declining authority acknowledges the risk in writing (email or meeting minutes).
3. If CISO assesses risk as high or critical: Escalate to Executive Management within 5 business days.
4. Executive Management makes final determination; decision documented in management review minutes.
5. If risk accepted: Documented in risk register with compensating controls and review date.

Participation shall be tracked and reported as part of the annual management commitment assessment.

## Management Commitment Assessment

### Annual Assessment

An annual Management Commitment Assessment shall be conducted by the CISO to evaluate management's fulfilment of their information security responsibilities. The assessment shall cover:

- Commitment verification criteria (attendance, training, resource approval, report acknowledgment, incident participation).
- Security culture leadership actions taken during the assessment period.
- Personnel compliance oversight effectiveness (training completion rates, violation handling timeliness).
- Management review participation and follow-through on assigned actions.

### Scoring and Remediation

| Score Range | Rating | Action Required |
|-------------|--------|-----------------|
| 90-100% | Excellent | No action; recognised as security leadership example |
| 70-89% | Satisfactory | Minor improvement areas noted; voluntary action plan |
| 50-69% | Below expectations | **Mandatory remediation plan** within 30 days |
| Below 50% | Unsatisfactory | Immediate escalation to Executive Management; role suitability review |

**Remediation plan requirements** (for scores below 70%):

- Developed collaboratively between the manager, CISO, and next-level manager.
- Specific, measurable improvement actions with target dates.
- Progress review at 30-day and 60-day milestones.
- Re-assessment within 90 days to confirm improvement.

**Remediation Plan template** (minimum content):

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Manager name and role** | Individual subject to remediation |
| **Assessment date and score** | Date of triggering assessment and overall score |
| **Failing criteria** | Specific criteria scoring below target (e.g., "Attendance at mandatory security reviews: 60% — target 80%") |
| **Root cause** | Why the criterion was not met (e.g., scheduling conflict, lack of awareness, resource constraint, deliberate non-compliance) |
| **Remediation actions** | Specific, measurable actions to address each failing criterion (e.g., "Block calendar for all quarterly reviews; complete missed reviews within 10 days") |
| **Target dates** | Per-action completion date |
| **Support required** | Resources or assistance needed from organisation (e.g., calendar blocking, delegate coverage, training slot) |
| **30-day milestone** | Interim checkpoint: What should be demonstrated by day 30? |
| **60-day milestone** | Second checkpoint: What should be demonstrated by day 60? |
| **90-day re-assessment** | Full re-assessment by CISO; target score to clear remediation: >= 70% |
| **Plan approved by** | Manager + CISO + next-level manager (all three sign) |
| **Outcome** | Remediation successful (closed) / Extended (maximum 30 additional days) / Escalated to performance management |

Plan stored in confidential management file (HR access + CISO access). Not disclosed to broader organisation.

**Progressive consequences for sustained non-compliance**:

1. First assessment below 70%: Remediation plan as described above.
2. Second consecutive assessment below 70%: Performance management process initiated with HR involvement.
3. Refusal to participate in mandatory reviews or assessments: Escalation to Executive Management for role suitability review.

### ISMS Gap Register

All management responsibility gaps shall be recorded in the central ISMS Gap Register maintained in [GRC Tool / SharePoint / Confluence]. The Gap Register is the single source of truth for tracking management commitment findings across all assessment cycles.

**Gap Register fields** (per entry):

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Gap ID** | Unique identifier (format: MGT-YYYY-NNN) |
| **Finding description** | Clear statement of the gap or deficiency |
| **Source** | How identified: Management Commitment Assessment, audit, incident review, self-reported |
| **Severity** | Critical (score < 50%), Major (50-69%), Minor (70-89%) |
| **Responsible manager** | Named individual accountable for remediation |
| **Approving authority** | Next-level manager who approves the remediation plan |
| **Remediation plan** | Specific actions with milestones and target completion dates |
| **Status** | Open, In Progress, Pending Verification, Closed |
| **Progress notes** | Timestamped updates at each milestone |
| **Evidence of closure** | Reference to evidence confirming remediation (e.g., assessment score improvement, training completion record) |
| **Escalation date** | Auto-calculated: Open date + 60 days |

**Gap lifecycle**:

1. **Identification**: Gap entered within 5 business days of discovery (assessment, audit, or incident).
2. **Remediation planning**: Responsible manager submits remediation plan within 10 business days.
3. **Execution**: Actions completed per plan milestones.
4. **Verification**: CISO verifies remediation effectiveness (re-assessment or evidence review).
5. **Closure**: Gap closed with evidence reference. Closed gaps retained in register for trend analysis.

**Escalation timeline**: Gaps unresolved after 60 days automatically escalate to Executive Management. Gaps unresolved after 90 days trigger formal nonconformity (ISO 27001 Clause 10.2).

**Quarterly reporting**: CISO reports Gap Register summary to Executive Management — total open gaps, ageing analysis, trend (improving/deteriorating), systemic issues.

## Escalation Path

The following escalation paths shall be used for security-related matters:

**Security concerns**: Team Lead --> Department Head --> CISO --> Executive Management

**Resource requests**: Team Lead --> Department Head --> Executive Management (with CISO advisory input)

**Policy exceptions**: Requestor --> CISO --> Executive Management (for approval)

**Non-compliance issues**: Line Manager --> Department Head --> CISO --> Executive Management (with HR involvement as appropriate)

# Policy Compliance

## Compliance Measurement

The information security management team will verify compliance with this policy through various methods, including but not limited to, annual Management Commitment Assessment scoring, management review attendance tracking, training completion monitoring, resource allocation response time analysis, security culture survey results, internal and external audits, and feedback to the policy owner.

Key metrics:

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Management Commitment Assessment score (organisation-wide average) | >= 80% |
| Attendance at mandatory security reviews | >= 80% per manager |
| Management security training completion (within 30 days) | 100% |
| Resource approval response time for security initiatives | <= 30 days |
| Quarterly security report acknowledgment rate | 100% |
| Personnel training completion rate (by managed team) | >= 95% |
| Remediation plan completion (for scores < 70%) | 100% within 90 days |

## Exceptions

Exceptions to management responsibility requirements are NOT generally acceptable, as management commitment is foundational to ISMS effectiveness.

**Limited exceptions**:
- Temporary delegation of specific tasks during extended absence (documented handover to a named delegate required; accountability remains with the original role holder).
- Role transition periods (maximum 30 days overlap; incoming manager completes security briefing within first 10 days).

**Not acceptable**:
- Delegating all security responsibilities to subordinates without oversight.
- Refusing participation in management reviews or commitment assessments.
- Claiming exemption from security policies that apply to all personnel.

Any exception shall be approved by Executive Management, documented with justification and compensating measures, and time-limited (maximum 90 days, renewable once with re-approval).

## Non-Compliance

A management employee found to have violated this policy may be subject to the progressive consequences defined in the Management Commitment Assessment section, including remediation plans, performance management proceedings, and role suitability review. Sustained or wilful non-compliance may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment.

## Continual Improvement

This policy is reviewed and updated as part of the continual improvement process. Reviews shall consider changes to ISO 27001 requirements, Swiss data protection law (nFADP), management structure or organisational changes, audit findings related to management commitment, Management Commitment Assessment trends, security culture survey feedback, and industry best practice developments for security governance.
