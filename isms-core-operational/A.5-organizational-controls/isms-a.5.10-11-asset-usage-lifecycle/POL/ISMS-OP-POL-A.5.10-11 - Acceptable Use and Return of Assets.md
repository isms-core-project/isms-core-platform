# Acceptable Use and Return of Assets Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to define the rules for acceptable use of information, IT systems, and other associated assets, and to ensure the return of all organisation assets upon change or termination of employment, contract, or agreement.

This policy supports Swiss nFADP (revDSG) and the Data Protection Ordinance (DSV) by implementing technical and organisational measures appropriate to risk to protect personal data through defined acceptable use standards. Where the organisation processes data of individuals in the EU/EEA, GDPR requirements also apply.

## Scope

All employees and third-party users.

All information, IT systems, devices, applications, and services deemed in scope by the ISO 27001 scope statement.

## Principle

Use of organisation assets shall be in line with applicable legislation, organisation policies, and contractual obligations. Each user is responsible for their own actions and shall act responsibly and professionally. All organisation assets shall be returned upon termination or change of role.

---

## Individual Responsibility

Access to IT systems is controlled using user IDs, passwords, and multi-factor authentication. All user IDs and passwords are uniquely assigned to named individuals. Individuals are accountable for all actions performed under their credentials.

Individuals shall not:

- Allow anyone else to use their user ID, token, or password on any organisation IT system.
- Leave their user accounts logged in at an unattended and unlocked computer.
- Use someone else's credentials to access organisation IT systems.
- Leave their password unprotected (for example, writing it down or storing it in plain text).
- Perform any unauthorised changes to organisation IT systems or information.
- Attempt to access data that they are not authorised to use or access.
- Exceed the limits of their authorisation or specific business need to interrogate systems or data.
- Connect any non-authorised device to the organisation network or IT systems.
- Store organisation data on any non-authorised equipment or personal cloud storage.
- Give or transfer organisation data or software to any person or organisation outside the company without authorisation.

Line managers shall ensure that individuals are given clear direction on the extent and limits of their authority regarding IT systems and data.

---

## Internet and Email Usage

Use of the organisation's internet and email is intended for business use. **Limited personal use is permitted** where such use does not affect the individual's business performance, is not detrimental to the organisation in any way, is not in breach of any term and condition of employment, and does not place the individual or the organisation in breach of statutory or other legal obligations.

**Examples of acceptable limited personal use**: brief personal emails during breaks, checking personal banking during lunch, emergency family contact, brief news reading during breaks.

**Examples of unacceptable use** (not "limited"): extended personal internet browsing during work hours, conducting personal business (side jobs, online selling), streaming video/audio for entertainment during work hours, personal use that consumes significant bandwidth or storage.

All individuals are accountable for their actions on the internet and email systems.

Individuals shall not:

- Use the internet or email for the purposes of harassment or abuse.
- Use profanity, obscenities, or derogatory remarks in communications.
- Access, download, send, or receive any data (including images) which the organisation considers offensive in any way, including sexually explicit, discriminatory, defamatory, or libellous material.
- Use the internet or email to make personal gains or conduct a personal business.
- Use the internet or email to gamble or engage in cryptocurrency trading.
- Use organisation computing resources for cryptocurrency mining.
- Install or play games on organisation devices.
- Use email systems in a way that could affect their reliability or effectiveness (for example, distributing chain letters or spam).
- Place any information on the internet that relates to the organisation, alter any information about it, or express any opinion about the organisation unless specifically authorised.
- Send unprotected sensitive, internal, or confidential information externally.
- Forward organisation email to personal (non-organisation) email accounts.
- Make official commitments through the internet or email on behalf of the organisation unless authorised to do so.
- Download any copyrighted material without appropriate approval.
- In any way infringe any copyright, database rights, trademarks, or other intellectual property.
- Download, install, or distribute any software from the internet without prior approval of the IT department.
- Connect organisation devices to the internet using non-standard connections.

## Cloud and SaaS Services

Organisation data shall only be processed in **sanctioned** cloud and SaaS services that have been assessed and approved by the information security management team. The sanctioned services register is maintained in the organisation's GRC platform, document management system, or intranet and shall be accessible to all employees. Employees shall verify a service is on the sanctioned list before storing organisation data.

Individuals shall not:

- Use personal cloud storage (e.g., personal Google Drive, Dropbox, iCloud) for organisation data.
- Subscribe to or activate new cloud/SaaS services for business use without IT approval.
- Store confidential or personal data in cloud services that have not been assessed for data protection compliance.
- Share organisation data via unapproved file sharing or collaboration platforms.

When using sanctioned cloud services, individuals shall:

- Apply classification-appropriate handling controls (per the Information Classification and Handling Policy).
- Use organisation-managed accounts (not personal accounts) for business use.
- Verify that data residency and cross-border transfer requirements are met before storing personal data in cloud services (per nFADP Art. 16–17).

Unsanctioned cloud services (shadow IT) discovered in use shall be reported to the information security management team for assessment. Where an unsanctioned service is found to process personal or confidential data, it shall be treated as a security event.

## Artificial Intelligence Tools

The use of generative AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT, Copilot, Claude, Gemini, or equivalent) shall be governed by the following rules.

### Approved AI Tools

The organisation shall maintain a list of **approved AI tools** for business use. Only approved tools with appropriate data protection agreements shall be used for processing organisation information.

**Approval criteria for new AI tools**:
- Enterprise data protection agreement in place (no training on organisation data).
- Data residency in Switzerland or adequate country per nFADP.
- SOC 2 Type II or ISO 27001 certification.
- Clear terms regarding intellectual property and confidentiality of inputs/outputs.

**Request process**: Users requiring new AI tools shall submit a request to the information security management team with business justification and tool details. Assessment shall be completed within **14 business days**.

Public or free-tier AI tools without enterprise data protection agreements shall not be used for organisation data.

### Prohibited Input

The following information shall **never** be entered into any AI tool (including approved tools) unless the tool has been specifically assessed and approved for that data category:

- Personal data (names, email addresses, employee records, customer data).
- Confidential information (financial records, trade secrets, contracts, credentials).
- Source code or proprietary algorithms.
- Security configurations, architecture details, or vulnerability information.

### Output Responsibility

Users remain accountable for all AI-generated content. AI-generated outputs shall be reviewed for accuracy, bias, and appropriateness before use in business decisions, external communications, or official documents.

AI-generated content shall not be presented as human-authored work in contexts where this distinction matters (e.g., regulatory submissions, legal documents, audit evidence).

## Social Media

Individuals shall exercise professional judgement when using social media in any context that could be associated with the organisation.

Individuals shall not:

- Disclose confidential or internal organisation information on social media platforms.
- Represent the organisation or express opinions on behalf of the organisation unless specifically authorised.
- Use their organisation email address to register for personal social media accounts.
- Post content that could be construed as reflecting organisation views (racist, discriminatory, defamatory content risks reputational damage even from personal accounts).

Official organisation social media accounts shall be managed only by authorised personnel. Content published on behalf of the organisation shall follow approved communication guidelines.

**If an employee makes a post that creates reputational risk**: the employee shall immediately inform their manager and the communications team. The organisation reserves the right to request removal of the post or public clarification. Disciplinary action may result for serious violations (breach of confidentiality, defamation).

---

## Working Off-Site

Laptops and mobile devices may be taken off-site for business purposes. The following controls shall apply:

- Working away from the office shall be in line with the Remote Working Policy.
- Laptop and mobile device encryption shall be enabled.
- Laptops and mobile devices shall be protected by a password, PIN, or biometric lock.
- Equipment and media taken off-site shall not be left unattended in public places, including on public transport, and shall not be left visible in a vehicle.
- Laptops and mobile devices shall be carried as hand luggage when travelling.
- Information shall be protected against loss or compromise when working remotely (for example, at home or in public places).
- Public or unsecured Wi-Fi networks shall not be used for accessing organisation systems without VPN or equivalent encrypted connection.

---

## Removable Storage Devices

Removable storage devices such as USB drives, external hard drives, and optical media shall not be used unless authorised by the information security management team.

Only organisation-owned, managed, and encrypted removable storage devices shall be used when transferring internal or confidential data. Personal removable storage devices are prohibited for organisation data.

---

## Monitoring and Filtering

All data created and stored on organisation systems is the property of the organisation. The organisation reserves the right, under defined conditions, to monitor activity on its systems, including internet and email use, to ensure systems security and effective operation and to protect against misuse.

### Swiss Monitoring Principles

Monitoring shall comply with Swiss employment law and data protection requirements:

- **Purpose limitation**: Monitoring shall serve legitimate security purposes (detecting threats, investigating incidents, verifying compliance) — not monitoring employee behaviour (Art. 26 ArGV 3).
- **Proportionality**: The least intrusive monitoring method shall be used. Anonymous or pseudonymous analysis shall be preferred; nominal analysis shall only occur when anomalies are detected (nFADP Art. 6).
- **Transparency**: Employees shall be informed in advance that monitoring takes place, what is monitored, and why — through this policy and the information security awareness programme.
- **Private communications**: The organisation shall not access private communications on organisation systems, even where limited personal use is permitted (Art. 328b CO).
- **Keystroke logging and continuous individual surveillance** are prohibited.

IT system logging shall take place in accordance with the Logging Policy. Investigations shall be commenced where **reasonable suspicion** exists of a breach of this or any other policy.

**Reasonable suspicion includes**: automated security alerts (DLP, IDS/IPS, antivirus), reports from colleagues or managers, anomalous system behaviour (unusual login times/locations, mass data access/download), observed policy violations (unattended unlocked workstation, data on unapproved media).

Investigations shall be conducted by the information security management team or designated personnel with appropriate authorisation. **Employee rights**: Employees investigated shall be informed unless doing so would compromise the investigation or violate legal obligations.

---

## Reporting

It is the responsibility of every individual to report suspected breaches of security policy without delay to their line manager, the IT department, the information security management team, or the IT helpdesk.

All breaches of information security policies shall be investigated. Where investigations reveal misconduct, disciplinary action may follow in line with organisation disciplinary procedures.

## Return of Assets

### Upon Termination or Contract End

All employees and third-party users shall return all organisation assets in their possession upon termination of their employment, contract, or agreement.

Assets to be returned include:

| Asset Type | Examples |
|------------|---------|
| **IT equipment** | Laptops, tablets, mobile phones, monitors, peripherals |
| **Removable media** | USB drives, external hard drives, backup media |
| **Authentication devices** | Security tokens, smart cards, hardware keys |
| **Physical access items** | Building passes, office keys, access cards |
| **Physical documents** | Printed materials, notebooks, files containing organisation information |
| **Software and licences** | Organisation-licensed software installed on personal devices |

### BYOD (Bring Your Own Device)

Where an employee or third-party user uses their own personal equipment (BYOD) to access organisation data, a **signed BYOD agreement** shall be in place before access is granted. The agreement shall include consent for remote wipe of organisation data upon termination or device loss, and acknowledgement that organisation data will be managed via MDM.

Upon termination or contract end:

- All organisation data shall be transferred to the organisation and securely erased from the personal device.
- MDM profiles shall be removed and remote wipe of organisation data confirmed.
- Where selective wipe (organisation data only) is not technically feasible, full device wipe consent shall be documented in the BYOD agreement.
- The BYOD record in the asset register shall be updated.

### Process

- The return of assets shall be coordinated between HR and IT as part of the offboarding process.
- Line managers shall verify that all assets assigned to the departing individual have been returned or accounted for.
- The asset register shall be updated to reflect all returned, reassigned, or disposed assets within **5 business days** of departure.
- During notice periods, the organisation shall take reasonable measures to prevent unauthorised copying of organisation information.

### Non-Return or Loss of Assets

**If an asset is not returned**:
- HR shall follow up within **5 business days** of the scheduled return date.
- The value of unreturned assets may be deducted from final pay (per employment contract and Swiss employment law).
- Access to organisation systems from the device shall be blocked immediately.
- Legal action may be pursued for high-value or sensitive assets.

**If an asset is reported lost or stolen**:
- The employee shall report the loss immediately to IT and their line manager.
- IT shall remotely wipe the device where technically feasible (mobile device, laptop with remote management).
- A security incident report shall be created per the Incident Management Policy.
- Loss circumstances shall be investigated; disciplinary action may result if negligence is determined.

### Upon Role Change

When an employee changes role within the organisation, assets that are no longer required for the new role shall be returned and reassigned. Access rights shall be reviewed and adjusted to reflect the new role (per the Access Control Policy).

# Policy Compliance

## Compliance Measurement

The information security management team will verify compliance with this policy through various methods, including but not limited to, asset return audits, acceptable use acknowledgement tracking, cloud service usage reviews, internal and external audits, and feedback to the policy owner.

## Exceptions

Any exception to this policy shall be approved and recorded by the Information Security Manager in advance, with documented risk acceptance, compensating controls, and a defined review date. Exceptions shall be reported to the Management Review Team.

## Non-Compliance

An employee found to have violated this policy may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

## Continual Improvement

This policy is reviewed and updated as part of the continual improvement process. Reviews shall consider changes to technology usage patterns (including AI and cloud services), regulatory changes (including Swiss nFADP and GDPR developments), workplace practices, and lessons learned from incidents.
