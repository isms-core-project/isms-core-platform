## Executive Summary

This policy establishes [Organisation]'s requirements for documenting legal and regulatory obligations related to PII processing, protecting PII processing records, conducting independent reviews of PIMS effectiveness, and verifying ongoing compliance with PIMS policies and standards — in accordance with ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Controls A.3.13, A.3.14, A.3.15, and A.3.16.

**Scope**: All legal, statutory, regulatory, and contractual requirements relevant to PII processing; all records related to PII processing; all independent review and compliance monitoring activities covering the PIMS.

**Purpose**: Define organisational requirements for:

- Documenting and maintaining current legal requirements for PII processing (A.3.13)
- Protecting PII processing records against loss, falsification, and unauthorised access (A.3.14)
- Independent review of the PIMS and its implementation (A.3.15)
- Regular compliance review against PIMS policies and standards (A.3.16)

This policy establishes **WHAT** requirements apply, **WHO** holds accountability, and **WHEN** reviews and updates occur. Implementation procedures (**HOW**) are documented in PRIV-IMP-A.3.13-16-UG and PRIV-IMP-A.3.13-16-TG.

**Role Applicability**: This policy applies to the organisation acting as **both PII Controller and PII Processor**. Controls A.3.13–A.3.16 are shared controls (Table A.3) and apply regardless of processing role.

**Combined Control Rationale**: A.3.13–A.3.16 form the governance assurance quadrant of the PIMS. Legal requirements (A.3.13) establish what the organisation must do; records (A.3.14) provide the evidence it has done it; independent review (A.3.15) provides external assurance; compliance review (A.3.16) provides ongoing internal monitoring. Together they fulfil the GDPR accountability principle (Article 5(2)) and the PIMS continual improvement requirements of ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Clause 10.

# Scope and Applicability

## ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Control Statements

**Control A.3.13 — Legal, statutory, regulatory and contractual requirements**
Control A.3.13 requires [Organisation] to identify, document, and keep current all legal, statutory, regulatory, and contractual requirements relevant to the information security of its PII processing activities, along with its documented approach to meeting each requirement.

**Control A.3.14 — Protection of records**
Control A.3.14 requires [Organisation] to protect records related to PII processing against loss, destruction, falsification, unauthorised access, and unauthorised release.

**Control A.3.15 — Independent review of information security**
Control A.3.15 requires [Organisation] to have its approach to managing information security for PII processing — including people, processes, and technologies — reviewed independently at planned intervals and when significant changes occur.

**Control A.3.16 — Compliance with policies, rules and standards for information security**
Control A.3.16 requires [Organisation] to regularly review compliance with its information security policy, topic-specific policies, rules, and standards as they relate to PII processing.

## What This Policy Covers

**Legal Requirements (A.3.13)**:

- All legal, statutory, regulatory, and contractual requirements that impose obligations on [Organisation]'s information security practices as they relate to PII processing
- The Privacy Legal Requirements Register (PLRR) — the documented inventory of applicable requirements and [Organisation]'s approach to meeting each
- Maintenance and currency of the PLRR in response to regulatory change, new business activities, or new contractual arrangements

**Records Protection (A.3.14)**:

- All records created and maintained as evidence of PIMS operation and PII processing compliance
- Records include: RoPA, consent records, DPIA documentation, supervisory authority notifications, data subject rights responses, processor agreements, DPO appointment records, training records, audit reports, and breach register entries
- Protection requirements: integrity, availability, confidentiality, and resistance to falsification

**Independent Review (A.3.15)**:

- Independent review of the PIMS (its design, policies, controls, and implementation)
- Independence from the operational teams responsible for PIMS implementation (DPO independence does not substitute for audit independence)
- Review scope: people (training, awareness, roles), processes (policies, procedures, workflows), and technologies (technical controls for PII)

**Compliance Review (A.3.16)**:

- Ongoing internal compliance monitoring against PIMS policies and standards
- Regular review of whether PII processing activities conform to the PIMS policy framework
- Identification, documentation, and remediation of compliance gaps

# Policy Statements: Legal and Regulatory Requirements for PII Processing (A.3.13)

## Privacy Legal Requirements Register

[Organisation] SHALL maintain a **Privacy Legal Requirements Register (PLRR)** that documents:

- All legal, statutory, and regulatory requirements applicable to [Organisation]'s PII processing activities (as determined per PRIV-POL-00)
- The contractual privacy and security obligations arising from each material contract involving PII
- [Organisation]'s documented approach to meeting each requirement (policy, control, or procedure reference)
- Current compliance status per requirement
- Date of last review and next review date

The PLRR is maintained by the DPO and reviewed at minimum annually, and upon:

- Enactment or amendment of applicable privacy legislation
- New or material contract obligations involving PII
- New processing activities that bring new regulatory obligations into scope
- DPA guidance or enforcement decisions that materially clarify obligations

The PLRR is not public-facing. It is an internal governance document classified CONFIDENTIAL. Structure and maintenance procedures are defined in PRIV-IMP-A.3.13-16-UG.

### Currency Obligation

The PLRR SHALL be current at all times. Stale requirements (not reviewed within 12 months) SHALL be treated as a compliance gap and escalated to the DPO for review. The DPO reports PLRR currency status to Executive Management at minimum annually (e.g., in the PIMS management review per Clause 9.3).

# Policy Statements: Protection of PII Processing Records (A.3.14)

## Records Protection Requirements

[Organisation] SHALL protect all records related to PII processing against:

- **Loss or destruction**: Records shall be backed up and recoverable; retention periods observed
- **Falsification**: Records shall be stored in systems with audit trails; modifications shall be logged with identity, timestamp, and change content
- **Unauthorised access**: Records classified per PRIV-POL-A.3.5-7; access restricted to authorised personnel with documented need
- **Unauthorised release**: Records subject to confidentiality controls; disclosure only on documented authority

### Mandatory PII Processing Records

The following records SHALL be created, maintained, and protected:

| Record | Owner | Minimum Retention | Classification |
|--------|-------|------------------|---------------|
| Record of Processing Activities (RoPA) | DPO | Duration of processing + 3 years | CONFIDENTIAL |
| Consent records | Data Owner / DPO | Duration of processing + 3 years (or longer if evidence of consent needed) | RESTRICTED |
| DPIA documentation | DPO | Duration of processing + 3 years | CONFIDENTIAL |
| DPO appointment record | Executive Management | Duration of appointment + 3 years | INTERNAL |
| Supervisory authority notifications | DPO | 5 years from notification | CONFIDENTIAL |
| Data subject rights response records | DPO | 3 years from response | CONFIDENTIAL |
| Processor agreements | DPO / Legal | Duration of agreement + 3 years | CONFIDENTIAL |
| International Transfer Register (TIA records) | DPO | Duration of transfer activity + 3 years | CONFIDENTIAL |
| Privacy Breach Register | DPO | 5 years | CONFIDENTIAL |
| Training and acknowledgment records | HR / DPO | Duration of employment + 3 years | INTERNAL |
| Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA) records | DPO | Duration of reliance on LI basis + 3 years | CONFIDENTIAL |
| Automated Decision-Making (ADM) Register | DPO | Duration of ADM activity + 3 years | CONFIDENTIAL |
| Sub-processor Register | DPO | Current + 3 years | CONFIDENTIAL |
| PIMS internal audit reports | PIMS Internal Auditor | 3 years | CONFIDENTIAL |
| PIMS management review records | DPO | 3 years | CONFIDENTIAL |

### Falsification Resistance

Records with regulatory significance (RoPA, consent records, DPIA, breach register, supervisory authority notifications) SHALL be stored in systems that:

- Log all modifications with author identity and timestamp
- Prevent deletion without DPO authorisation
- Retain prior versions for the full retention period

Where paper records are maintained, they SHALL be stored securely with access log and version control equivalent.

# Policy Statements: Independent Review of PIMS (A.3.15)

## Independent Review Requirements

[Organisation] SHALL conduct independent reviews of its approach to managing information security related to PII processing — covering people, processes, and technologies — at planned intervals and when significant changes occur.

### Independence Requirement

Reviews SHALL be conducted by reviewers who:

- Are independent of the operational functions responsible for PIMS implementation
- Have no conflict of interest in the review outcome
- Have sufficient competence to assess PIMS controls and regulatory requirements

DPO oversight of PIMS operations does not satisfy this independence requirement. The DPO may participate in scope definition and review of findings, but SHALL NOT conduct the review themselves unless acting as independent auditor for a function they do not manage.

**Acceptable review approaches**:

- **Internal PIMS audit**: Conducted by the PIMS Internal Auditor role (see PRIV-POL-A.3.3-4), who must be independent of the audited activities
- **External audit**: Third-party privacy or information security auditor
- **Certification audit**: ISO/IEC 27701:2025 certification body audit (Stage 1, Stage 2, or surveillance)
- **Regulatory inspection**: Supervisory authority audit (satisfies the review requirement but is not a substitute for planned internal review)

### Review Frequency

Independent reviews SHALL occur:

- **At minimum annually** (planned interval)
- **Upon significant change** to the PIMS, including: major new processing activity, material change to organisational structure or processing infrastructure, significant regulatory change, or material privacy incident

### Review Scope

Each independent review SHALL cover, at minimum:

- Policy and documentation completeness and currency
- Role assignments and competence evidence
- Evidence of PIMS operation (records from A.3.14)
- Technical controls for PII processing (access controls, encryption, logging)
- Regulatory compliance status (against PLRR)
- Status of findings from previous reviews

### Review Outputs

Independent review outputs SHALL include:

- Review report with findings, non-conformities, observations, and recommendations
- Formal presentation of findings to Executive Management
- DPO response with remediation plan and timelines
- Tracking of remediation to closure

Review reports are maintained in the PIMS records (classified CONFIDENTIAL) for minimum 3 years.

# Policy Statements: Compliance Review (A.3.16)

## Ongoing Compliance Review Requirements

[Organisation] SHALL regularly review compliance with the PIMS policy framework, topic-specific policies, rules, and standards related to PII processing.

### Compliance Review Programme

The DPO SHALL maintain an ongoing compliance review programme that covers:

- All PRIV-POL-A.x.x control group policies (this policy series)
- The Record of Processing Activities (RoPA) — currency and accuracy
- Processor agreement compliance (is each processor performing per the agreement?)
- Technical security control compliance for PII processing environments
- Training and awareness completion rates

### Compliance Review Frequency

| Review Subject | Minimum Frequency |
|---------------|------------------|
| RoPA accuracy (new processing, changed processing, discontinued processing) | Quarterly |
| Processor agreement inventory (all active processors covered) | Annually |
| Technical controls compliance for PII systems | Annually (or upon significant system change) |
| Training and acknowledgment records | Annually |
| PIMS policy compliance (self-assessment per control group) | Annually |

### Compliance Review Outputs

Compliance reviews SHALL produce:

- Written compliance assessment per review subject
- List of identified non-conformities or gaps
- Remediation actions with owner and target date
- Escalation to DPO for material compliance gaps
- Reporting to Executive Management for systemic compliance issues

Compliance review records are maintained by the DPO for minimum 3 years.

# Roles and Responsibilities

## Accountability Matrix

| Role | Responsibilities for A.3.13–A.3.16 |
|------|-------------------------------------|
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | Maintains PLRR (A.3.13); owns PII processing records framework (A.3.14); commissions and oversees independent reviews (A.3.15); leads compliance review programme (A.3.16); reports to Executive Management |
| **PIMS Internal Auditor** | Conducts independent PIMS audits per A.3.15; operates independently from DPO and operational functions; reports findings to Executive Management |
| **CISO** | Ensures technical controls for PII are included in compliance review scope; provides technical input to independent reviews; maintains audit logs as evidence per A.3.14 |
| **Legal/Compliance** | Advises on regulatory changes for PLRR currency; reviews contractual obligations for PLRR; participates in independent review scope definition |
| **IT Security Team** | Maintains technical records (access logs, system configs) that support records protection (A.3.14) and compliance review (A.3.16) |
| **Executive Management** | Receives independent review reports; approves remediation plans; provides resources for PIMS improvement |

# Audit Considerations

Auditors verifying compliance with A.3.13, A.3.14, A.3.15, and A.3.16 should expect to find:

**For A.3.13 (Legal requirements)**:
- A Privacy Legal Requirements Register (PLRR) with current regulatory and contractual obligations
- Evidence of PLRR review in the last 12 months
- Documented approach to meeting each material requirement

**For A.3.14 (Protection of records)**:
- RoPA and other mandatory records present and current
- Evidence of access controls on PII processing records
- Audit trail / modification logs for records with regulatory significance
- Defined and observed retention periods

**For A.3.15 (Independent review)**:
- Evidence of independent PIMS review in the last 12 months
- Review report with findings and management response
- Remediation tracking evidence
- Reviewer independence documented

**For A.3.16 (Compliance review)**:
- Documented compliance review programme
- Compliance assessment records for key review subjects
- Non-conformity and remediation records
- Reporting to management
