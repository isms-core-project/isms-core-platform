## Executive Summary

This policy establishes [Organisation]'s requirements for privacy awareness education and training, confidentiality and non-disclosure agreements for PII protection, and clear desk and clear screen controls for PII processing environments — in accordance with ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Controls A.3.17, A.3.18, and A.3.19.

**Scope**: All personnel and relevant interested parties who process PII or have access to PII processing environments; all confidentiality obligations related to PII; all physical and screen-based environments where PII is processed or displayed.

**Purpose**: Define organisational requirements for:

- Privacy-specific awareness, education, and training for PII processing (A.3.17)
- Confidentiality and non-disclosure agreements for PII protection (A.3.18)
- Clear desk and clear screen rules for PII processing facilities (A.3.19)

This policy establishes **WHAT** awareness, confidentiality, and physical control requirements apply to PII, **WHO** is responsible, and **WHEN** reviews and renewals occur. Implementation procedures (**HOW**) are documented in PRIV-IMP-A.3.17-19-UG and PRIV-IMP-A.3.17-19-TG.

**Role Applicability**: This policy applies to the organisation acting as **both PII Controller and PII Processor**. Controls A.3.17, A.3.18, and A.3.19 are shared controls (Table A.3) and apply regardless of processing role.

**Combined Control Rationale**: A.3.17 (awareness and training), A.3.18 (confidentiality agreements), and A.3.19 (clear desk/screen) are the people and physical layer of PII security. Technical controls protect systems; these controls protect against the human element — untrained personnel, inappropriately unbound parties, and visible or accessible PII in physical environments. They are implemented together as an integrated people security programme for PII processing.

# Scope and Applicability

## ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Control Statements

**Control A.3.17 — Information security awareness, education and training**
> *Personnel of the organization and relevant interested parties shall receive appropriate information security awareness education and training, and regular updates of the organization's information security policy, topic-specific policies and procedures, as relevant for their job function, as they relate to PII processing.*

**Control A.3.18 — Confidentiality or non-disclosure agreements**
> *Confidentiality or non-disclosure agreements reflecting the organization's needs for the protection of PII shall be identified, documented, regularly reviewed and signed by personnel and other relevant interested parties.*

**Control A.3.19 — Clear desk and clear screen**
> *Clear desk rules for papers and removable storage media and clear screen rules for information processing facilities shall be defined and appropriately enforced.*

## What This Policy Covers

**Awareness and Training (A.3.17)**:

- Privacy-specific awareness content (distinct from general information security awareness)
- Role-based privacy training for personnel with elevated PII responsibilities
- Regular updates when PIMS policies change
- Training for relevant interested parties (contractors, processors) as applicable to their role

**Confidentiality Agreements (A.3.18)**:

- All confidentiality and non-disclosure agreements covering PII protection obligations
- Requirements for what such agreements must contain for PII purposes
- Review and renewal obligations for existing agreements

**Clear Desk and Clear Screen (A.3.19)**:

- Physical workspaces where PII documents or media may be present
- Screen controls for workstations, meeting rooms, and shared display environments where PII may be visible

## What This Policy Does NOT Cover

- General information security awareness and training (see ISMS-POL-A.6.3)
- General confidentiality and NDA requirements unrelated to PII (see ISMS-POL-A.6.6)
- General clear desk requirements unrelated to PII (see ISMS-POL-A.7.7)
- Personnel screening procedures (see ISMS-POL-A.6.1)
- PIMS role definitions and appointment procedures (see PRIV-POL-A.3.3-4)

# Policy Statements: Privacy Awareness, Education and Training (A.3.17)

## Privacy Training Requirements

[Organisation] SHALL provide appropriate information security awareness, education, and training covering PII processing to all personnel and relevant interested parties, relevant to their job function.

### Training Applicability

| Personnel Category | Minimum Training Requirement |
|-------------------|------------------------------|
| **All personnel** (any access to organisational systems) | General privacy awareness (annual) |
| **Personnel with access to PII** | Privacy awareness + role-appropriate PII handling training (on engagement + annual refresher) |
| **Personnel with elevated PII responsibility** (Data Owners, Privacy Champions, System Owners for PII systems) | Role-specific privacy training covering PIMS controls for their domain (on appointment + annual) |
| **DPO and privacy function** | Advanced training; professional development; regulatory updates (ongoing) |
| **Contractors and temporary workers** | Minimum: general privacy awareness before PII access is granted |
| **Relevant processors** | Notification of applicable PIMS policies per processor agreement; training obligation flowing through agreement |

### Privacy Awareness Content Requirements

General privacy awareness content SHALL address, at minimum:

- What PII is, including special categories of PII and their heightened sensitivity
- [Organisation]'s legal obligations under GDPR and CH FADP (overview)
- The individual's role in protecting PII (handling, classification, transfer)
- How to recognise a PII incident and how to report it
- Data subject rights and how to escalate requests to the DPO
- Consequences of PII handling failures (regulatory and disciplinary)

### PIMS Policy Updates

When a PIMS policy is substantially revised or a new policy is issued, affected personnel SHALL be:

- Notified of the change and its content relevant to their role
- Required to acknowledge the updated policy before its effective date
- Provided with summary guidance where the policy change is material

Acknowledgment records are maintained in the Personnel Privacy Training and Acknowledgment Register (see PRIV-POL-A.3.3-4 for register requirements).

### Training Records

Training completion records SHALL be maintained per individual, including:

- Training module and version completed
- Date of completion
- Outcome (pass/complete)
- Next required refresher date

Training records are retained for the duration of employment plus 3 years.

# Policy Statements: Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure Agreements for PII (A.3.18)

## Confidentiality Agreement Requirements

[Organisation] SHALL identify, document, and maintain confidentiality or non-disclosure agreements (NDAs) that reflect the organisation's needs for PII protection.

### Who Must Sign

The following persons and parties SHALL be bound by a signed confidentiality or non-disclosure obligation covering PII:

| Category | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| **Employees** | Employment contract privacy/confidentiality clause (or separate NDA where employment contract is insufficient) |
| **Contractors and consultants** | Contractor agreement privacy/confidentiality clause or standalone NDA |
| **Temporary workers** | Staffing agency agreement or standalone NDA before PII access |
| **PII processors** | Processor agreement (Article 28 GDPR) — includes confidentiality obligations |
| **Professional advisors** (legal, audit, insurance) with PII access | Engagement letter or standalone NDA |
| **DPO** | Independence per GDPR Article 38.6; DPO may not hold conflicting roles but is bound by professional secrecy under Article 38.5 |

### Minimum PII Content in Confidentiality Agreements

Confidentiality or non-disclosure agreements that cover PII SHALL address:

- The obligation to process PII only for authorised purposes and under instruction
- Prohibition on disclosure of PII to unauthorised parties
- Obligation to protect PII using at least the security measures required by [Organisation]
- Obligation to report suspected PII incidents without undue delay
- Survival of confidentiality obligations post-engagement (PII obligations survive termination)
- Consequences of breach (disciplinary, contractual, and regulatory)

### Review of Agreements

Agreements covering PII SHALL be reviewed:

- At minimum every 3 years or upon regulatory change affecting the obligations
- Upon significant change to the processing activities covered by the agreement
- When an individual's role changes materially

The DPO maintains a Confidentiality Agreement Register recording agreement status, parties, date signed, scope, and next review date. Agreements that are out of date SHALL be refreshed before continued PII access.

# Policy Statements: Clear Desk and Clear Screen for PII (A.3.19)

## Clear Desk Requirements for PII

[Organisation] SHALL define and enforce clear desk rules that protect PII in physical form at workspaces.

**Mandatory clear desk requirements**:

- PII documents SHALL NOT be left unattended on desks or in open areas; they shall be secured (in drawers, cabinets, or document safes) when a workspace is unoccupied
- Special category PII documents require locked storage when unattended — unlocked drawers do not suffice
- PII documents awaiting destruction SHALL be placed in secure cross-cut shredding receptacles immediately upon completion of use; they shall not be placed in standard waste bins
- Removable media containing PII (USB drives, printed reports, portable hard drives) SHALL be physically secured when not in active use; they shall not be left unattended on desks

**Shared workspaces**:

- In hot-desking or shared workspace environments, PII documents must be cleared from the desk before vacating, without exception
- PII documents in shared workspace areas require a clean-desk check before leaving the area for any period

## Clear Screen Requirements for PII

[Organisation] SHALL define and enforce clear screen rules for workstations and display environments where PII is processed or displayed.

**Mandatory clear screen requirements**:

- Workstations accessing PII SHALL lock automatically after a maximum idle period (configured per PRIV-IMP-A.3.17-19-TG)
- Personnel SHALL manually lock their screen before leaving a workstation unattended, even briefly
- Screens displaying PII SHALL be positioned to minimise visibility to unauthorised persons; privacy screens SHALL be used in open-plan, public-facing, or shared environments where PII is routinely displayed
- Meeting rooms and shared display screens: PII SHALL NOT be projected or displayed in meeting rooms unless all attendees are authorised to view the PII; PII SHALL be closed or screened before external participants join

**Remote working**:

- Clear desk and clear screen rules apply to remote working environments as fully as to office environments
- PII documents used in remote working contexts shall be stored securely and not left visible to household members or others not authorised to access the PII

# Roles and Responsibilities

## Accountability Matrix

| Role | Responsibilities for A.3.17–A.3.19 |
|------|-------------------------------------|
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | Defines PIMS training curriculum and content; approves NDA privacy clauses; maintains Confidentiality Agreement Register; enforces clear desk/screen requirements in PII contexts; reports training completion to Executive Management |
| **HR Manager** | Ensures employment contracts contain adequate privacy obligations; coordinates new employee privacy induction; maintains training completion records |
| **CISO** | Ensures general security awareness integrates PIMS content; configures automatic screen locking per PRIV-IMP-A.3.17-19-TG |
| **Privacy Champions** | First-line privacy awareness facilitation in their business unit; support training delivery; report clear desk/screen violations to DPO |
| **Line Management** | Ensure team members complete required privacy training; enforce clear desk/screen rules in their area |
| **Procurement / Legal** | Ensure supplier agreements include required privacy confidentiality clauses; maintain agreement records |

# Audit Considerations

Auditors verifying compliance with A.3.17, A.3.18, and A.3.19 should expect to find:

**For A.3.17 (Awareness and training)**:
- Training curriculum covering PII-specific awareness content
- Training completion records demonstrating annual refresher compliance
- Evidence that new employees/contractors complete privacy awareness before PII access
- Records of policy update notifications to affected personnel

**For A.3.18 (Confidentiality agreements)**:
- Confidentiality Agreement Register with current status
- Sample signed agreements (employment contracts, contractor NDAs, processor agreements) with PII-protective clauses
- Evidence of triennial or trigger-based review of agreements

**For A.3.19 (Clear desk/screen)**:
- Written clear desk and clear screen rules for PII environments
- Evidence of enforcement (e.g., periodic workspace inspection records, awareness communications)
- Screen lock configuration settings documented in PRIV-IMP-A.3.17-19-TG
