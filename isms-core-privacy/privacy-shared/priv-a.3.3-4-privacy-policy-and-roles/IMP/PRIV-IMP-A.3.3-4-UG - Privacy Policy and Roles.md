## Purpose of This Guide

This guide explains **how to implement** the requirements of PRIV-POL-A.3.3-4 from an operational perspective. It covers:

- How to manage the PIMS policy lifecycle from creation through to retirement
- How to define, appoint, and maintain privacy and security roles
- How to maintain the Privacy Roles Register and DPO appointment documentation
- How to conduct the annual DPO role conflict assessment

**Who this guide is for**: DPO, Privacy Champions, CISO, Legal/Compliance, any personnel involved in authoring or approving PIMS policies or being assigned privacy roles.

## Part 1 — Managing PIMS Policies (A.3.3)

### 1.1 The PIMS Policy Lifecycle

Every PIMS policy (PRIV-POL-00, PRIV-POL-01, and all 21 control group POLs) follows this lifecycle:

| Stage | Who | What |
|-------|-----|------|
| **1. Definition** | DPO (lead) | Draft policy using the PIMS policy format defined in PRIV-POL-01. Address WHAT, WHO, WITH WHAT. Circulate draft to CISO and Legal for comment. |
| **2. Review** | DPO + CISO + Legal | Review draft for completeness, consistency with PRIV-POL-00/01, and regulatory accuracy. |
| **3. Approval** | Executive Management | DPO submits final draft with cover note summarising scope and key requirements. Executive Management approves in writing. |
| **4. Publication** | DPO | Publish approved policy to the PIMS document repository with version number, approval date, and next review date. Update the PIMS Policy Register. |
| **5. Communication** | DPO + relevant managers | Communicate to all relevant personnel (see 1.2 below). |
| **6. Acknowledgment** | All relevant personnel | Obtain and record acknowledgments (see 1.3 below). |
| **7. Review** | DPO (lead) | Conduct at planned intervals (minimum annually) or on trigger events per PRIV-POL-A.3.3-4. |
| **8. Retirement** | DPO | Archive retired version with reason for retirement. Retain for minimum 3 years. Update Policy Register. |

### 1.2 Policy Communication

When a new PIMS policy is published or a material update is released:

1. DPO sends a communication to all personnel with access to PII or PII processing systems, summarising:
   - What the policy covers
   - What has changed (for updates)
   - What action is required (read and acknowledge)
   - Deadline for acknowledgment
2. Relevant PII processors receive notification as required under their processor agreements
3. Supervisory authorities and certification bodies receive policies upon request — there is no proactive distribution obligation

Communication records (date, method, recipients) are retained as evidence per the Evidence Requirements in PRIV-POL-A.3.3-4.

### 1.3 Obtaining Policy Acknowledgments

Personnel with access to PII or PII processing systems must acknowledge applicable PIMS policies:

**Process**:
1. DPO or HR (as designated) issues acknowledgment request to applicable personnel
2. Personnel confirm they have read and understood the policy — see PRIV-IMP-A.3.3-4-TG for the acknowledgment form template
3. DPO records acknowledgment in the Personnel Privacy Training and Acknowledgment Register, noting: name, role, policy title and version, acknowledgment date, method
4. Acknowledgments are reviewed annually. Personnel who have not acknowledged current policy versions are followed up before or at annual review

**Trigger**: New policy publication, material policy update, new employee joining, role change giving new PII access.

### 1.4 Conducting Policy Reviews

At minimum annually, the DPO reviews each PIMS policy for:

- Continued accuracy against current regulatory requirements (cross-reference PRIV-POL-00 for recent changes)
- Continued alignment with organisational PII processing activities
- Effectiveness of the policy in practice (input from Privacy Champions, audit findings, incident records)
- Any outstanding actions from previous reviews

Review outcome documentation:
- **No changes required**: Record review date and "no change" determination in Policy Register
- **Minor update required**: Update version (e.g., 1.0 → 1.1), record changes, re-communicate if material
- **Major revision required**: Full re-draft and approval cycle per 1.1 above

Trigger-based reviews (not scheduled) follow the same documentation requirement.

## Part 2 — Defining and Maintaining Privacy Roles (A.3.4)

### 2.1 Mandatory Role Appointments

The following roles must be formally appointed before the PIMS goes into operation:

**Data Protection Officer (DPO)**:
- Appointed in writing by Executive Management
- Appointment document must confirm: independence from roles that determine PII processing purposes, reporting line, contact details, scope
- For GDPR-mandatory DPOs: published on the organisation's website and notified to the supervisory authority (per Article 37(7))
- See PRIV-IMP-A.3.3-4-TG for the DPO Appointment Record template

**Privacy Champions** (one per business unit or processing domain with material PII activity):
- Nominated by the relevant business unit manager
- Appointed by the DPO in writing
- Minimum 4 hours privacy training completed before taking up role
- Included in the Privacy Roles Register with contact details

**Data Owners** (one per processing activity or significant data category):
- Nominated by the DPO; appointed in writing by Executive Management
- Scope of data ownership documented (which processing activity or data category)
- Included in Privacy Roles Register

**PIMS Internal Auditor**:
- Appointed per the PIMS audit programme (PRIV-POL-A.3.13-16)
- Must be independent from the activities being audited — this must be documented

### 2.2 Documenting Role Responsibilities

For each role defined under A.3.4, the following must be documented in the Privacy Roles Register (see PRIV-IMP-A.3.3-4-TG for register structure):

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Role title | Standard title per PRIV-POL-A.3.3-4 |
| Incumbent name | Full name of current appointee |
| Appointment date | Date of formal appointment |
| Scope | Processing activities or domains covered |
| Responsibilities | PII processing responsibilities specific to this role |
| Reporting line | Who the role reports to and escalates to |
| Competence requirements | Required qualifications or training |
| Review date | When appointment and responsibilities are next reviewed |

The Privacy Roles Register must be accessible to the DPO and relevant managers. It is evidence of A.3.4 compliance and is reviewed at least annually.

### 2.3 Annual DPO Role Conflict Assessment

GDPR Article 38.6 prohibits the DPO from holding positions that determine purposes and means of PII processing. Each year, the DPO (or an independent reviewer where the DPO's own independence is in question) must complete a DPO Role Conflict Assessment:

**Process**:
1. List all roles and responsibilities currently held by the DPO
2. For each: assess whether the role involves determining purposes or means of PII processing
3. Document the assessment conclusion: "No conflict identified" or "Conflict identified — remediation required"
4. If a conflict is identified: immediately escalate to Executive Management and Legal for resolution before next audit cycle
5. File completed assessment — retain per Evidence Requirements

**Frequency**: Annual minimum; also conduct when DPO takes on any new responsibility.

See PRIV-IMP-A.3.3-4-TG for the DPO Role Conflict Assessment template.

### 2.4 Role Changes and Vacancies

When a role becomes vacant or changes:

1. DPO identifies the vacancy or change immediately upon notification
2. Interim cover arrangements are documented (who is acting in the role)
3. Permanent appointment is made and documented within a timeframe appropriate to the role's criticality (DPO vacancy: notify FDPIC/DPAs where legally required and appoint replacement promptly; Privacy Champion vacancy: resolve within 30 days)
4. Privacy Roles Register is updated on the date of the change

## Evidence Checklist

Use this checklist when preparing for a PIMS audit covering A.3.3 and A.3.4:

**A.3.3 — Policies:**
- [ ] PIMS Policy Register — current, includes all 23 documents (PRIV-POL-00, PRIV-POL-01, 21 control group POLs)
- [ ] Approval records for each policy (DPO + Executive Management signature)
- [ ] Communication records confirming policies distributed to relevant personnel
- [ ] Acknowledgment register — current policy versions acknowledged by all relevant personnel
- [ ] Review records — evidence of annual review for each policy (or trigger-based review with trigger documented)

**A.3.4 — Roles:**
- [ ] Privacy Roles Register — complete, current, includes all mandatory roles
- [ ] DPO appointment record — written, states independence, includes scope
- [ ] DPO Role Conflict Assessment — current year, documented conclusion
- [ ] Privacy Champion and Data Owner appointment records
- [ ] PIMS Internal Auditor appointment with independence statement
