## Executive Summary

This policy establishes [Organisation]'s requirements for identifying and documenting the specific purposes of PII processing, determining and demonstrating the lawful basis for each processing activity, and obtaining and recording consent where consent is the applicable legal basis — in accordance with ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Controls A.1.2.2, A.1.2.3, A.1.2.4, and A.1.2.5.

**Scope**: All PII processing activities conducted by [Organisation] as PII Controller; all processing purposes documented in the Record of Processing Activities (RoPA); all consent-based processing activities.

**Purpose**: Define WHAT purpose documentation and lawful basis requirements apply, WHO holds accountability for demonstrating lawful basis, and WHEN reviews and updates are required. Implementation procedures (HOW) are documented in PRIV-IMP-A.1.2.2-5-UG and PRIV-IMP-A.1.2.2-5-TG.

**Role Applicability**: This policy applies to [Organisation] acting as **PII Controller only**. Controls A.1.2.2–A.1.2.5 are controller-specific (Table A.1) and do not apply when [Organisation] acts solely as PII Processor.

**Combined Control Rationale**: A.1.2.2–A.1.2.5 form the lawfulness foundation for PII controller operations. Purpose identification (A.1.2.2) comes first; lawful basis determination follows (A.1.2.3); where consent is the basis, the consent process must be defined (A.1.2.4) and consent must be obtained and recorded (A.1.2.5). These four controls are inseparable in practice.

# Scope and Applicability

## ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Control Statements

**Control A.1.2.2 — Identify and document purpose**
> *The organization shall identify and document the specific purposes for which the PII will be processed.*

**Control A.1.2.3 — Identify lawful basis**
> *The organization shall determine, document and be able to demonstrate compliance with the relevant lawful basis for the processing of PII for the identified purposes.*

**Control A.1.2.4 — Determine when and how consent is to be obtained**
> *The organization shall determine and document a process by which it can demonstrate if, when and how consent for the processing of PII was obtained from PII principals.*

**Control A.1.2.5 — Obtain and record consent**
> *The organization shall obtain and record consent from PII principals according to the documented processes.*

## What This Policy Covers

- Identification and documentation of all processing purposes in the RoPA
- Determination and documentation of the lawful basis for each processing purpose
- Demonstrability of lawful basis compliance to supervisory authorities and data subjects
- Consent process design and documentation
- Obtaining and recording consent in accordance with regulatory requirements

## What This Policy Does NOT Cover

- Consent mechanism technical implementation (see PRIV-IMP-A.1.2.2-5-TG)
- Transparency and privacy notice content (see PRIV-POL-A.1.3.2-4)
- Withdrawal of consent and objection mechanisms (see PRIV-POL-A.1.3.5-10)
- Processing as PII Processor (see PRIV-POL-A.2.2.2-7)

## Regulatory Framework

**Tier 1: Mandatory Compliance** (per PRIV-POL-00):

- **EU GDPR**: Article 5(1)(a) (lawfulness, fairness and transparency principle); Article 5(1)(b) (purpose limitation principle); Article 6 (six lawful bases); Article 7 (validity conditions for consent); Article 8 (child consent — under 16); Article 9 (special category lawful bases — 10 conditions); Article 30(1)(b) (RoPA to include purposes and legal basis)
- **CH FADP**: Article 6 (lawfulness — legitimate purpose, proportionality); Article 17 (consent — voluntary, informed, unambiguous expression)
- **ISO/IEC 27701:2025**: Controls A.1.2.2–A.1.2.5 (normative)

# Policy Statements: Purpose Identification (A.1.2.2)

## Purpose Documentation Requirement

[Organisation] SHALL identify and document the specific, explicit, and legitimate purposes for which each category of PII is processed. Vague or generic purposes are not acceptable.

Each processing purpose SHALL be documented in the **Record of Processing Activities (RoPA)** with sufficient specificity to:

- Enable a data subject to understand what their PII is used for
- Enable [Organisation] to determine the correct lawful basis
- Enable a supervisory authority to assess purpose limitation compliance

### Purpose Limitation

PII collected for a documented purpose SHALL NOT be processed for a different purpose unless:

- The data subject provides fresh consent for the new purpose, OR
- The new purpose is compatible with the original purpose under the compatibility assessment criteria, OR
- A new legal basis exists for the new purpose

The DPO SHALL conduct and document a compatibility assessment before any secondary use of PII. Compatibility assessments are maintained in the Purpose Register (part of the RoPA).

### Purpose Review

Documented purposes SHALL be reviewed:

- At minimum annually as part of RoPA review
- When a processing activity changes materially
- Upon DPA guidance that affects how a purpose is interpreted

# Policy Statements: Lawful Basis (A.1.2.3)

## Lawful Basis Determination Requirement

[Organisation] SHALL determine, document, and be able to demonstrate the lawful basis for each processing purpose. No PII processing shall commence without a documented lawful basis.

### Lawful Basis Options

For ordinary personal data (GDPR Article 6):

| Basis | When Applicable |
|-------|----------------|
| **Consent** (Art. 6(1)(a)) | Data subject has given clear, specific, informed, unambiguous consent |
| **Contract** (Art. 6(1)(b)) | Processing necessary for performance of contract with the data subject, or to take pre-contractual steps |
| **Legal obligation** (Art. 6(1)(c)) | Processing necessary to comply with a legal obligation under EU or Member State law |
| **Vital interests** (Art. 6(1)(d)) | Processing necessary to protect vital interests of data subject or another natural person |
| **Public task** (Art. 6(1)(e)) | Processing necessary for task in public interest or exercise of official authority |
| **Legitimate interests** (Art. 6(1)(f)) | Processing necessary for legitimate interests of controller or third party, unless overridden by data subject's interests or fundamental rights — requires Legitimate Interests Assessment (LIA) |

For special category PII, an additional condition from GDPR Article 9(2) is required alongside an Article 6 basis.

### Lawful Basis Documentation

For each processing activity, the RoPA SHALL record:

- The primary lawful basis (Article 6 basis)
- For special category PII: the Article 9(2) condition
- For legitimate interests: the LIA reference and outcome
- For legal obligation: the specific legal provision
- For consent: the consent record reference

### Demonstrability

[Organisation] SHALL be able to demonstrate lawful basis compliance at any time. The DPO SHALL maintain documented evidence of each basis determination, including the reasoning and any supporting assessments.

# Policy Statements: Consent Process Design (A.1.2.4)

## Consent Process Requirement

Where consent is the lawful basis (or an additional condition for special category processing), [Organisation] SHALL determine and document a process by which it can demonstrate if, when, and how consent was obtained.

### Consent Validity Requirements

Consent used as lawful basis for PII processing SHALL meet all of the following conditions:

- **Freely given**: No detriment for refusing or withdrawing consent; consent shall not be bundled as a condition for a service unless the processing is strictly necessary for that service
- **Specific**: Separate consent per distinct processing purpose; omnibus consent is not valid for distinct purposes
- **Informed**: Data subject has been provided with the information required by PRIV-POL-A.1.3.2-4 before giving consent
- **Unambiguous**: Positive opt-in action required; pre-ticked boxes, silence, or inactivity do not constitute valid consent
- **Withdrawable**: Data subjects shall be informed of their right to withdraw consent at any time, and withdrawal shall be as easy as giving consent

### Consent Process Documentation

The documented consent process SHALL specify:

- The moment at which consent is requested
- The information provided to the data subject before consent is requested
- The mechanism by which consent is expressed (form, checkbox, signature, etc.)
- How consent is recorded and linked to the data subject's identity
- How withdrawal of consent is handled

# Policy Statements: Obtaining and Recording Consent (A.1.2.5)

## Consent Obtaining and Recording Requirement

[Organisation] SHALL obtain and record consent from PII principals according to the documented consent processes.

### Consent Records

For each consent-based processing activity, the consent record SHALL capture:

- Identity of the PII principal (or reliable link to identity)
- Timestamp of consent (date and time)
- Version of the consent statement presented
- Purposes for which consent was given
- Channel or mechanism through which consent was obtained

Consent records constitute evidence of lawful basis. They SHALL be:

- Protected against loss and falsification (per PRIV-POL-A.3.13-16)
- Retained for the duration of the processing activity plus 3 years minimum
- Accessible to the DPO for subject rights fulfilment and supervisory authority requests

### Child Consent

Where PII of children (under 16 for GDPR; applicable age threshold per CH FADP) is processed on a consent basis:

- Parental or guardian consent is required
- Age verification measures SHALL be in place (proportionate to the risk of the service)
- The DPO SHALL approve child-oriented consent processes before deployment

### Consent Withdrawal

Upon withdrawal of consent:

- Processing based solely on that consent SHALL cease without undue delay
- PII SHALL be deleted or anonymised unless another lawful basis applies
- Third parties to whom PII was disclosed SHALL be notified per PRIV-POL-A.1.3.5-10 (A.1.3.8)

# Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | Approves RoPA entries; validates lawful basis determinations; reviews consent processes before deployment; maintains LIA records; primary contact for supervisory authority queries on lawful basis |
| **Legal/Compliance** | Advises on lawful basis options; reviews contracts for Article 6(1)(b) applicability; reviews LIAs |
| **Data Owner** | Documents processing purpose and proposed lawful basis for their domain; escalates uncertain lawful basis to DPO |
| **Development / Product Teams** | Implement consent mechanisms per approved design; ensure consent records are generated and stored correctly |
| **HR** | Manages employee PII lawful basis documentation (consent where used, legal obligation, contract) |

# Evidence Requirements

| Evidence | Description | Retention |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Record of Processing Activities (RoPA) | Processing purposes, lawful basis per activity | Current + 3 years |
| Legitimate Interests Assessments (LIAs) | Documented balancing tests for legitimate interests basis | Duration of processing + 3 years |
| Consent Records | Per-individual consent records with timestamp, version, scope | Duration of processing + 3 years |
| Consent Process Documentation | Documented consent process per activity | Duration of processing + 3 years |
| Purpose Compatibility Assessments | Assessments for secondary use of PII | Duration of secondary use + 3 years |

---

# Audit Considerations

Auditors verifying compliance with A.1.2.2–A.1.2.5 should expect to find:

- RoPA with specific, documented purposes for each processing activity
- Lawful basis documented per processing activity, with supporting evidence (LIA, legal provision, or consent records)
- Consent process documentation demonstrating validity conditions are met
- Consent records with identity link, timestamp, and scope
- Evidence that withdrawal of consent leads to cessation of processing
