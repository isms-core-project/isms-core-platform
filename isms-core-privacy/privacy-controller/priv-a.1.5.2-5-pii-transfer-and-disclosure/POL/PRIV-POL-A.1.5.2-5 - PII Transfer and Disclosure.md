## Executive Summary

This policy establishes [Organisation]'s requirements for identifying the legal basis for cross-border PII transfers, documenting the countries and organisations to which PII may be transferred, recording transfers to and from third parties, and recording PII disclosures — in accordance with ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Controls A.1.5.2, A.1.5.3, A.1.5.4, and A.1.5.5.

**Scope**: All transfers of PII to third parties in other jurisdictions; all disclosures of PII to third parties; all records of such transfers and disclosures.

**Role Applicability**: This policy applies to [Organisation] acting as **PII Controller only**. Controls A.1.5.2–A.1.5.5 are controller-specific (Table A.1) within the PII sharing, transfer and disclosure objective group.

**Combined Control Rationale**: A.1.5.2–A.1.5.5 form the controller's accountability record for cross-border PII flows and third-party disclosures. Legal basis identification (A.1.5.2) determines whether a transfer is lawful; country documentation (A.1.5.3) maps where PII can go; transfer records (A.1.5.4) create the audit trail; disclosure records (A.1.5.5) complete the accountability picture for all third-party PII sharing.

# Scope and Applicability

## ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Control Statements

**Control A.1.5.2 — Identify basis for PII transfer between jurisdictions**
Control A.1.5.2 requires [Organisation] to identify and document the legal basis applicable to each transfer of PII across jurisdictional boundaries.

**Control A.1.5.3 — Countries and international organisations to which PII can be transferred**
Control A.1.5.3 requires [Organisation] to specify and document the countries and international organisations to which PII may be transferred.

**Control A.1.5.4 — Records of transfer of PII**
Control A.1.5.4 requires [Organisation] to record transfers of PII to or from third parties, and to ensure that those third parties will cooperate with future obligations towards PII principals.

**Control A.1.5.5 — Records of PII disclosures to third parties**
Control A.1.5.5 requires [Organisation] to record all disclosures of PII to third parties, including which PII was disclosed, to whom, and when.

# Policy Statements

## A.1.5.2 — Identify Basis for PII Transfer Between Jurisdictions

[Organisation] SHALL identify and document the legal basis for each transfer of PII to a third country or international organisation.

### Transfer Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Applicability |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Adequacy decision** (EU Commission / Swiss FDPIC) | Transfers to countries with adequate protection — no additional safeguards required |
| **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)** | EU SCCs (2021) for transfers from EEA; for transfers from Switzerland, the EU SCCs with appropriate adaptations recognised by the FDPIC, or FDPIC standard data protection clauses, are used (Switzerland does not maintain a separate SCC instrument equivalent to the EU 2021 SCCs) |
| **Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)** | Intra-group transfers with DPA-approved BCRs — applicable only to corporate groups that have obtained DPA approval; this mechanism is not currently in use by [Organisation] |
| **Article 49 derogations** | Exceptional use only: explicit consent, vital interests, important public interest, legal claims, public register data — NOT for systematic transfers. Derogations require DPO pre-approval, are documented in the International Transfer Register with the specific derogation invoked, and are not used as a substitute for a primary transfer mechanism where one is available |

### Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA)

Where SCCs or other contractual mechanisms are used, the DPO SHALL conduct a Transfer Impact Assessment evaluating whether the destination country's legal framework provides essentially equivalent protection. TIA records are maintained in the International Transfer Register (part of the RoPA).

---

## A.1.5.3 — Countries and Organisations to Which PII Can Be Transferred

[Organisation] SHALL specify and document the countries and international organisations to which PII may possibly be transferred.

### Transfer Country Register

The DPO maintains a **Transfer Country Register** (as part of the RoPA) that lists:

- Country or international organisation name
- Transfer mechanism and legal basis
- Categories of PII that may be transferred there
- Relevant processing activities involving the transfer
- Adequacy status or safeguard reference (SCC version, BCR reference)

The Transfer Country Register is updated when new transfer destinations are identified, when adequacy status changes, or at minimum annually.

Transfers to countries NOT listed in the Transfer Country Register require DPO approval before commencement.

---

## A.1.5.4 — Records of Transfer of PII

[Organisation] SHALL record transfers of PII to or from third parties and ensure cooperation with those parties to support future data subject rights requests.

### Transfer Records

For each transfer of PII to or from a third party, [Organisation] SHALL record:

- Date and time of transfer
- Identity of the sending and receiving party
- Categories of PII transferred and approximate volume
- Legal basis for the transfer
- Transfer mechanism (where cross-border)
- Reference to the governing agreement (processor agreement, DPA, SCC)

Transfer records enable [Organisation] to:

- Respond to data subject access requests with information on recipients
- Notify third parties of erasure or rectification obligations (per PRIV-POL-A.1.3.5-10, A.1.3.8)
- Demonstrate accountability to supervisory authorities

### Third Party Cooperation

[Organisation] SHALL require in its agreements with third parties that they cooperate with data subject rights requests involving PII that has been transferred, to the extent necessary to fulfil [Organisation]'s obligations as controller.

---

## A.1.5.5 — Records of PII Disclosures to Third Parties

[Organisation] SHALL record all disclosures of PII to third parties, including which PII was disclosed, to whom, and when.

### Disclosure Records

A disclosure is any communication of PII to a third party, whether or not under a processor agreement. This includes:

- Routine disclosures under ongoing processor or supplier relationships
- One-time disclosures (e.g., to professional advisors, auditors, regulators)
- Mandatory disclosures under legal obligation (e.g., to law enforcement, tax authorities)

Disclosure records SHALL be maintained in the **Disclosure Register**, which is part of the broader RoPA. Records are retained for minimum 3 years.

Disclosure records support data subject access requests (data subjects have the right to know recipients of their PII).

# Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | Identifies transfer basis; maintains International Transfer Register and Transfer Country Register; conducts TIAs; approves new transfer destinations; maintains Disclosure Register |
| **Legal/Compliance** | Reviews SCCs and transfer mechanisms; advises on adequacy status changes; reviews disclosure obligations under legal obligation |
| **IT Security Team** | Implements technical transfer controls (encryption, TLS) per PRIV-POL-A.3.5-7 |
| **Procurement** | Triggers DPO review before any supplier in a new jurisdiction is engaged |
| **All Personnel** | Report intended disclosures to third parties to Data Owner and DPO before sending; do not transfer PII to new international destinations without DPO approval |

# Audit Considerations

- RoPA includes transfer destinations, mechanisms, and safeguards for all international transfers
- Transfer Country Register with adequacy or SCC basis for each destination
- TIA documentation for SCC-based transfers
- Transfer records enabling accountability and data subject rights fulfilment
- Disclosure Register covering all third-party PII disclosures
- No undocumented or unapproved transfer destinations in active use
