## Executive Summary

This policy establishes [Organisation]'s requirements for determining and fulfilling obligations to PII principals, identifying the information to be provided to PII principals, and actually providing that information in a clear, accessible, and timely manner — in accordance with ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Controls A.1.3.2, A.1.3.3, and A.1.3.4.

**Scope**: All processing activities where [Organisation] collects PII directly from data subjects; all processing activities where PII is obtained indirectly; all communications with PII principals about the processing of their PII.

**Role Applicability**: This policy applies to [Organisation] acting as **PII Controller only**. Controls A.1.3.2–A.1.3.4 are controller-specific (Table A.1).

**Combined Control Rationale**: A.1.3.2 (determine obligations), A.1.3.3 (determine what information to provide), and A.1.3.4 (actually provide that information) are the three sequential steps of the transparency obligation. They cannot be separated: obligations inform content; content determines what must be provided; provision completes the duty.

# Scope and Applicability

## ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Control Statements

**Control A.1.3.2 — Determining and fulfilling obligations to PII principals**
> *The organization shall determine and document its legal, regulatory and business obligations to PII principals related to the processing of their PII and provide the means to meet these obligations.*

**Control A.1.3.3 — Determining information for PII principals**
> *The organization shall determine and document the information to be provided to PII principals regarding the processing of their PII and the timing of such a provision.*

**Control A.1.3.4 — Providing information to PII principals**
> *The organization shall provide PII principals with clear and easily accessible information identifying the PII controller and describing the processing of their PII.*

## What This Policy Does NOT Cover

- Privacy notice templates and content writing procedures (see PRIV-IMP-A.1.3.2-4-UG)
- Website privacy notice publication and maintenance (see PRIV-IMP-A.1.3.2-4-TG)
- Data subject rights response procedures (see PRIV-POL-A.1.3.5-10)

## Regulatory Framework

**Tier 1: Mandatory Compliance** (per PRIV-POL-00):

- **EU GDPR**: Article 5(1)(a) (transparency principle); Article 12 (transparent information and communication — format, timing, free of charge); Article 13 (information to be provided where PII collected from data subject); Article 14 (information to be provided where PII not obtained from data subject); Article 21(4) (explicit mention of right to object in first communication)
- **CH FADP**: Article 19 (duty to provide information at time of collection — identity, purpose, recipients, cross-border transfers); Article 20 (indirect collection — same elements)
- **ISO/IEC 27701:2025**: Controls A.1.3.2–A.1.3.4 (normative)

# Policy Statements: Determining Obligations to PII Principals (A.1.3.2)

## Obligation Identification Requirement

[Organisation] SHALL determine and document its legal, regulatory, and business obligations to PII principals for each processing activity. These obligations include:

- Transparency obligations (what information must be provided, when)
- Rights obligations (what rights data subjects can exercise and how [Organisation] must respond)
- Notification obligations (breach notification to data subjects where applicable)
- Other jurisdiction-specific obligations as determined per PRIV-POL-00

The DPO maintains a **PII Principal Obligations Register** (part of the broader PLRR per PRIV-POL-A.3.13-16) that maps each applicable obligation to the relevant processing activities and describes how [Organisation] meets each obligation.

# Policy Statements: Determining Information for PII Principals (A.1.3.3)

## Information Content Determination

[Organisation] SHALL determine and document the information to be provided to PII principals for each processing activity, including the timing of provision.

### Mandatory Information Elements

For PII collected **directly from data subjects** (GDPR Article 13), the following SHALL be provided at or before the time of collection:

- Identity and contact details of [Organisation] as controller
- Contact details of the DPO (where applicable)
- Purposes and legal basis for the processing
- Legitimate interests pursued (where legitimate interests is the basis)
- Recipients or categories of recipients
- Third country transfers and safeguards
- Retention period (or criteria to determine it)
- Data subject rights: access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection
- Right to withdraw consent (where consent is the basis)
- Right to lodge a complaint with the supervisory authority
- Whether provision of PII is statutory or contractual, and consequences of not providing
- Existence of automated decision-making (including profiling) with meaningful information about logic, significance, and consequences

For PII obtained **indirectly** (GDPR Article 14), the same elements apply, plus the source of the PII and whether it came from publicly available sources — provided within 1 month of obtaining the PII (or at first communication with the data subject, or at time of disclosure to another recipient, whichever is earliest).

### Information Content Review

Privacy notice content SHALL be reviewed:

- At minimum annually
- When a processing activity changes
- Upon regulatory change affecting the required information elements
- When a new supervisory authority guidance materially changes expectations

# Policy Statements: Providing Information to PII Principals (A.1.3.4)

## Provision Requirements

[Organisation] SHALL provide PII principals with clear and easily accessible information identifying [Organisation] as controller and describing the processing of their PII.

### Format Requirements

Information provided to PII principals SHALL be:

- **Concise**: Not excessively long; key information surfaced prominently
- **Transparent**: Plain language; no ambiguity about purposes, bases, or rights
- **Intelligible**: Language and terminology appropriate to the audience (including layered notices for general public, simpler language for children's processing)
- **Easily accessible**: Published where data subjects will reasonably find it (privacy notice link on website, in-app privacy information, printed notice with physical collection)
- **Free of charge**

### Timing of Provision

| Collection Method | When to Provide |
|------------------|----------------|
| Online/digital direct collection | At or before the point of collection (inline or clearly linked) |
| Physical direct collection | At time of collection |
| Indirect collection | Within 1 month of obtaining PII, OR at first communication with data subject, OR at time of first disclosure to a third party — whichever is earliest |
| Employee PII | At commencement of employment or engagement |

### Layered Privacy Notice

[Organisation] SHALL maintain a full privacy notice accessible at all times (via website and on request), supplemented by shorter contextual notices at key data collection points that direct users to the full notice for complete details.

# Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | Approves privacy notice content; maintains PII Principal Obligations Register; triggers review upon regulatory or processing change |
| **Legal/Compliance** | Reviews privacy notice legal accuracy; advises on jurisdiction-specific transparency requirements |
| **Marketing / Communications** | Drafts consumer-facing privacy notice content (with DPO review); ensures notices are accessible and timely |
| **HR** | Ensures employee privacy notices are provided at onboarding; updates notices upon processing changes |
| **Development / Product** | Implements inline consent and information mechanisms in products; ensures privacy notice links are present at collection points |

# Audit Considerations

Auditors verifying compliance with A.1.3.2–A.1.3.4 should expect to find:

- Privacy notices covering all Article 13/14 mandatory elements
- Evidence that notices are provided at the point of collection (direct) or within the Article 14 timeframe (indirect)
- Layered notice structure directing data subjects to the full privacy notice
- Notice version history with dates, and evidence of review at planned intervals
- PII Principal Obligations Register mapping obligations to processing activities
