## Executive Summary

This policy establishes [Organisation]'s requirements for providing PII principals with mechanisms to modify or withdraw consent, to object to processing, to access/correct/erase their PII, to inform third parties of changes, to receive a copy of their PII, and to have requests handled — in accordance with ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Controls A.1.3.5 through A.1.3.10.

**Scope**: All data subject rights requests received from PII principals in relation to PII processed by [Organisation] as controller; all mechanisms enabling data subjects to exercise their rights.

**Role Applicability**: This policy applies to [Organisation] acting as **PII Controller only**. Controls A.1.3.5–A.1.3.10 are controller-specific (Table A.1).

**Combined Control Rationale**: A.1.3.5–A.1.3.10 operationalise the individual rights that data subjects hold. Each control addresses a distinct right or class of rights. They share a common infrastructure: a rights request mechanism (A.1.3.10), response obligations (A.1.3.7, A.1.3.9), and downstream obligations to notify third parties (A.1.3.8).

# Scope and Applicability

## ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Control Statements

**Control A.1.3.5 — Providing mechanism to modify or withdraw consent**
> *The organization shall provide a mechanism for PII principals to modify or withdraw their consent.*

**Control A.1.3.6 — Providing mechanism to object to PII processing**
> *The organization shall provide a mechanism for PII principals to object to the processing of their PII.*

**Control A.1.3.7 — Access, correction or erasure**
> *The organization shall implement policies, procedures or mechanisms to meet its obligations to PII principals to access, correct or erase their PII.*

**Control A.1.3.8 — PII controllers' obligations to inform third parties**
> *The organization shall inform third parties with whom PII has been shared of any modification, withdrawal or objections pertaining to the shared PII, and implement appropriate policies, procedures or mechanisms to do so.*

**Control A.1.3.9 — Providing copy of PII processed**
> *The organization shall be able to provide a copy of the PII that is processed, when requested by the PII principal.*

**Control A.1.3.10 — Handling requests**
> *The organization shall define and document policies and procedures for handling and responding to legitimate requests from PII principals.*

# Policy Statements

## A.1.3.5 — Consent Withdrawal Mechanism

[Organisation] SHALL provide PII principals with a mechanism to modify or withdraw their consent at any time. The withdrawal mechanism SHALL be:

- As easy to use as the mechanism by which consent was given
- Clearly communicated in the privacy notice and at the point of consent
- Effective without penalty or detriment to the data subject

Upon withdrawal of consent:

- Processing based solely on consent SHALL cease without undue delay
- Where another lawful basis independently supports continued processing, the DPO SHALL assess applicability and document the basis continuation
- PII shall be deleted or anonymised unless a lawful basis for continued retention exists
- Third parties who received the PII under the consent-based processing SHALL be notified per A.1.3.8 below

---

## A.1.3.6 — Objection Mechanism

[Organisation] SHALL provide PII principals with a mechanism to object to the processing of their PII. The mechanism SHALL be accessible and clearly communicated.

### Right to Object — Legitimate Interests Basis

Where processing is based on legitimate interests or public task, a data subject's objection SHALL result in:

- Cessation of processing unless [Organisation] can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override the data subject's interests, rights, and freedoms
- DPO assessment of compelling grounds — documented with outcome
- Immediate cessation where no compelling grounds exist

### Right to Object — Direct Marketing

Where PII is processed for direct marketing purposes, a data subject's objection SHALL result in immediate cessation of direct marketing processing. No compelling grounds assessment is required; the right is absolute.

---

## A.1.3.7 — Access, Correction, and Erasure

[Organisation] SHALL implement policies, procedures, and mechanisms to respond to requests for access, correction, and erasure.

### Right of Access (Subject Access Request)

Upon a valid access request, [Organisation] SHALL provide:

- Confirmation of whether PII concerning the data subject is being processed
- A copy of the personal data being processed
- The supplementary information required by GDPR Article 15(1): purposes, categories, recipients, transfers, retention periods, rights, right to complain, data source, automated decision-making

### Right to Rectification

Upon a valid rectification request, [Organisation] SHALL correct inaccurate PII without undue delay. Where accuracy is disputed, the data subject may request restriction of processing pending resolution.

### Right to Erasure

Upon a valid erasure request, [Organisation] SHALL erase PII where:

- PII is no longer necessary for the purpose for which it was collected
- Consent is withdrawn and no other lawful basis applies
- The data subject objects and there are no overriding legitimate grounds
- PII has been unlawfully processed
- Erasure is required to comply with a legal obligation

Erasure requests that cannot be fulfilled (due to a countervailing legal obligation or other exemption) SHALL receive a documented refusal with explanation and information about the right to complain.

### Right to Restriction of Processing

Upon a valid restriction request (where accuracy is contested, processing is unlawful but deletion not wanted, retention needed for legal claims, or objection pending), [Organisation] SHALL restrict processing to storage only until the restriction condition resolves.

---

## A.1.3.8 — Informing Third Parties

Where PII has been disclosed to third parties and a data subject subsequently withdraws consent, objects, requests rectification, or requests erasure:

[Organisation] SHALL inform each third party to whom the PII was disclosed of the relevant change or request — unless this proves impossible or involves disproportionate effort (in which case the reason shall be documented).

[Organisation] SHALL inform the data subject of which third parties have been notified upon request.

---

## A.1.3.9 — Providing Copy of PII

Upon request, [Organisation] SHALL provide the data subject with a copy of their PII in a commonly used, machine-readable format (for portability requests under Article 20 where applicable) or in a generally accessible format.

The copy SHALL be provided free of charge for the first copy; a reasonable administrative fee may be charged for subsequent copies.

---

## A.1.3.10 — Handling Requests

[Organisation] SHALL define and document policies and procedures for receiving, validating, and responding to data subject rights requests.

### Response Timeframes

All data subject rights requests SHALL be acknowledged and responded to within **one month** of receipt.

Where requests are complex or numerous, this period may be extended by a further **two months** — provided the data subject is notified within the first month of the extension and the reason for it.

### Identity Verification

Before fulfilling a rights request involving disclosure of PII, [Organisation] SHALL verify the identity of the requester with reasonable measures proportionate to the sensitivity of the PII. Identity verification SHALL not be unnecessarily burdensome.

### Manifestly Unfounded or Excessive Requests

[Organisation] may refuse requests that are manifestly unfounded or excessive (including repetitive requests). Refusals SHALL be documented with reasoning, and the data subject SHALL be informed of their right to complain to the supervisory authority.

### Data Subject Rights Register

All rights requests SHALL be logged in the **Data Subject Rights Register**, recording: request type, receipt date, identity verification date, response date, outcome, and any extensions or refusals.

# Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | Primary handler of data subject rights requests; validates identity; assesses complex requests (erasure, objection); maintains Data Subject Rights Register; reports metrics to Executive Management |
| **Legal/Compliance** | Advises on exemptions to rights; reviews refusal decisions; manages supervisory authority complaints arising from rights requests |
| **IT Security Team** | Supports technical fulfilment of access and portability requests; implements erasure and restriction at technical level |
| **Privacy Champions** | First point of contact for rights requests received in their business unit; escalate to DPO promptly |
| **All Personnel** | Forward rights requests received via any channel to the DPO immediately |

# Audit Considerations

Auditors verifying compliance with A.1.3.5–A.1.3.10 should expect to find:

- Accessible consent withdrawal mechanism, as easy to use as consent was given
- Objection mechanism available and communicated in privacy notice
- Data Subject Rights Register with all requests logged
- Evidence of responses within one-month timeframe (or documented extension)
- Identity verification procedure in place
- Third party notification records where relevant
- Technical evidence of erasure or restriction where requests were fulfilled
