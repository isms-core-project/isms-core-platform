## Executive Summary

This policy establishes [Organisation]'s requirements for Privacy Impact Assessments, contracts with PII processors, joint controller arrangements, and records of processing activities — in accordance with ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Controls A.1.2.6, A.1.2.7, A.1.2.8, and A.1.2.9.

**Scope**: All new and changed PII processing activities subject to DPIA assessment; all PII processors engaged by [Organisation]; all joint controller relationships; all records maintained in support of controller obligations.

**Role Applicability**: This policy applies to [Organisation] acting as **PII Controller only**. Controls A.1.2.6–A.1.2.9 are controller-specific (Table A.1).

**Combined Control Rationale**: A.1.2.6–A.1.2.9 are the governance infrastructure of PII controller operations. DPIA (A.1.2.6) assesses risk before processing begins; processor contracts (A.1.2.7) govern the supply chain; joint controller arrangements (A.1.2.8) clarify shared responsibilities; records (A.1.2.9) provide the evidence base for all of the above.

# Scope and Applicability

## ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Control Statements

**Control A.1.2.6 — Privacy impact assessment**
Control A.1.2.6 requires [Organisation] to assess whether a privacy impact assessment is needed whenever new PII processing is planned or existing processing is changed, and to conduct that assessment where appropriate.

**Control A.1.2.7 — Contracts with PII processors**
Control A.1.2.7 requires [Organisation] to hold a written contract with every PII processor it engages, and to ensure those contracts require the processor to implement the appropriate controls from ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Annex A, Table A.2.

**Control A.1.2.8 — Joint PII controller**
Control A.1.2.8 requires [Organisation] to agree with any joint PII controller on the respective roles, responsibilities, and security requirements for the shared processing of PII.

**Control A.1.2.9 — Records related to processing PII**
Control A.1.2.9 requires [Organisation] to identify the records necessary to demonstrate compliance with its PII controller obligations, and to maintain those records securely.

# Policy Statements: Privacy Impact Assessment (A.1.2.6)

## DPIA Requirements

[Organisation] SHALL assess the need for a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) whenever new processing of PII is planned or existing processing changes materially. The DPO is the DPIA authority.

### DPIA Screening

All new processing activities and material changes to existing activities SHALL undergo a DPIA screening by the DPO to determine whether a full DPIA is required. Screening decisions shall be documented.

A **material change** includes any change to: processing purpose, categories of PII processed, data volumes, third-party sharing arrangements, or technology used to process PII.

**Full DPIA is mandatory where processing is likely to result in high risk**, including:

- Systematic and extensive profiling of individuals with decisions producing legal or significant effects
- Large-scale processing of special category PII
- Systematic monitoring of publicly accessible areas
- Use of new technologies in combination with processing of large volumes of PII
- Any processing type on the supervisory authority's DPIA required list

### DPIA Outcomes

A DPIA that identifies residual high risk (after implementing risk-reduction measures) requires:

- Prior consultation with the competent supervisory authority (GDPR Article 36; FDPIC for CH FADP)
- Executive Management approval before processing commences
- Documented risk acceptance or processing halt decision

DPIA results are documented and maintained in the DPIA Register. DPIAs are reviewed when processing changes materially or after a significant privacy incident involving the processing activity.

# Policy Statements: Processor Contracts (A.1.2.7)

## PII Processor Contract Requirement

[Organisation] SHALL have a written contract with every PII processor it uses. No PII shall be transferred to an external processor without a current, signed processor agreement.

### Mandatory Contract Content

Processor agreements SHALL address, at minimum, the obligations required by GDPR Article 28(3):

- Processing only on documented instructions from [Organisation]
- Confidentiality obligations for personnel with PII access
- Implementation of appropriate security measures (GDPR Article 32)
- Sub-processor engagement restrictions (prior written consent; flow-down of obligations; obligation to notify [Organisation] of intended sub-processor changes so that [Organisation] may exercise its right to object)
- Assisting [Organisation] with data subject rights obligations
- Assisting with security, breach notification, DPIA, and prior consultation obligations
- Return or deletion of all PII at end of service
- Providing information necessary to demonstrate compliance; cooperating with audits

Processor agreements SHALL also reference the appropriate controls from ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Table A.2 (i.e., the processor-side controls), requiring the processor to implement controls equivalent to those in PRIV-POL-A.2.x.

### Processor Agreement Register

The DPO maintains a **Processor Agreement Register** listing all active processor agreements with status, review date, and processor contact. No PII processor engagement commences without a register entry.

# Policy Statements: Joint Controller Arrangements (A.1.2.8)

## Joint Controller Requirement

Where [Organisation] jointly determines the purposes and means of PII processing with another controller, [Organisation] SHALL determine respective roles and responsibilities with that joint controller in a transparent arrangement.

### Joint Controller Arrangement Content

Joint controller arrangements SHALL determine, at minimum:

- Respective responsibilities for compliance with GDPR obligations (Article 26(1))
- Which controller is responsible for providing the privacy notice to data subjects (Article 13/14 obligations)
- Which controller is the primary point of contact for data subject rights
- Data security responsibilities for shared processing activities
- Breach notification responsibilities

The **essence of the arrangement** (i.e., which controller data subjects should contact and for what) SHALL be made available to data subjects (directly or by reference).

Joint controller arrangements require DPO and Legal/Compliance review before execution.

# Policy Statements: Records of Processing (A.1.2.9)

## Records Requirement

[Organisation] SHALL determine and securely maintain the necessary records in support of its obligations as PII Controller for all processing of PII.

### Record of Processing Activities (RoPA)

The primary controller record is the RoPA, which SHALL contain for each processing activity:

- Name and contact details of the controller (and DPO where applicable)
- Purposes of the processing
- Description of categories of data subjects and categories of PII
- Categories of recipients (including processors and third-country transfers)
- Third country transfers: destination countries and safeguards
- Retention periods (where possible)
- Description of technical and organisational security measures (reference)

The DPO owns and maintains the RoPA. It SHALL be available to supervisory authorities on request.

### Additional Records

In addition to the RoPA, [Organisation] SHALL maintain:

- DPIA Register (all DPIA screenings and full DPIAs)
- Processor Agreement Register (all active processor contracts)
- Joint Controller Arrangement Register (all active joint controller agreements)
- Consent records (per PRIV-POL-A.1.2.2-5)
- LIA records (per PRIV-POL-A.1.2.2-5)

Records maintenance and protection requirements are governed by PRIV-POL-A.3.13-16.

# Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | DPIA authority; maintains RoPA, DPIA Register, Processor Agreement Register; reviews processor and joint controller agreements; reports to supervisory authority where DPIA shows residual high risk |
| **Legal/Compliance** | Reviews processor and joint controller agreement legal terms; advises on GDPR Article 28/26 compliance; manages supervisory authority consultation process |
| **Data Owner** | Initiates DPIA screening for new/changed processing in their domain; provides processing information for RoPA entries |
| **Procurement** | Triggers DPO review before any supplier with PII access is engaged; ensures processor agreement is in place before PII transfer |

# Audit Considerations

Auditors verifying compliance with A.1.2.6–A.1.2.9 should expect to find:

- DPIA screening records for all new processing activities
- Full DPIAs for identified high-risk processing, with risk treatment evidence
- Signed processor agreements for all active processors, addressing Article 28(3) content
- Joint controller arrangements for any joint processing, with data subject information made available
- Current and accurate RoPA covering all processing activities
