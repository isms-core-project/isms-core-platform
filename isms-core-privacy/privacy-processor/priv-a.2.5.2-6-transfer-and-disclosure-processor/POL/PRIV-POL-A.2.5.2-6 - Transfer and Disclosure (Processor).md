## Executive Summary

This policy establishes [Organisation]'s requirements when acting as PII Processor for informing customers of jurisdictional transfer basis, documenting transfer destinations, recording third-party PII disclosures, notifying customers of legally binding disclosure requests, and handling legally binding and non-binding disclosure requests — in accordance with ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Controls A.2.5.2 through A.2.5.6.

**Scope**: All cross-border transfers of customer PII; all disclosures of customer PII to third parties; all governmental or regulatory requests for disclosure of customer PII.

**Role Applicability**: This policy applies to [Organisation] acting as **PII Processor only**. Controls A.2.5.2–A.2.5.6 are processor-specific (Table A.2).

---

# Scope and Applicability

## ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Control Statements

**Control A.2.5.2 — Basis for PII transfer between jurisdictions**
Control A.2.5.2 requires [Organisation] to inform customers in a timely manner of the legal basis for cross-border transfers of their PII, and of any intended changes to that basis — giving customers the opportunity to object or terminate.

**Control A.2.5.3 — Countries and international organisations to which PII can be transferred**
Control A.2.5.3 requires [Organisation] to specify and document the countries and international organisations to which customer PII may be transferred.

**Control A.2.5.4 — Records of PII disclosures to third parties**
Control A.2.5.4 requires [Organisation] to record all disclosures of customer PII to third parties, including which PII was disclosed, to whom, and when.

**Control A.2.5.5 — Notification of PII disclosure requests**
Control A.2.5.5 requires [Organisation] to notify customers when it receives a legally binding request to disclose their PII.

**Control A.2.5.6 — Legally binding PII disclosures**
Control A.2.5.6 requires [Organisation] to reject non-legally-binding disclosure requests, to consult the relevant customer before making disclosures in response to legally binding requests, and to act on contractually authorised disclosure requests.

## Regulatory Framework

**Tier 1: Mandatory Compliance** (per PRIV-POL-00):

- **EU GDPR**: Article 28(3)(a) (processor processes only on controller instruction); Article 28(3)(b) (processor ensures personnel bound to confidentiality); Article 44 (transfers subject to safeguards); Article 48 (transfers not authorised by Union law require Article 49 derogation or judicial authorisation safeguard)
- **CH FADP**: Article 9 (processor may only transfer if controller could transfer and has authorised it; equivalent protection required)
- **ISO/IEC 27701:2025**: Controls A.2.5.2–A.2.5.6 (normative)

# Policy Statements

## A.2.5.2 — Informing Customers of Transfer Basis

[Organisation] SHALL inform each customer in a timely manner of the legal basis for any PII transfers between jurisdictions carried out on their behalf, and of any intended changes to transfer arrangements.

- The transfer basis (adequacy, SCCs, BCRs, etc.) for each transfer destination SHALL be documented and provided to customers in the processor agreement or associated data processing schedule
- Where [Organisation] intends to change a transfer basis or add a new transfer destination, customers SHALL be notified in advance with sufficient time to object or terminate the contract
- The notification period for transfer changes SHALL be as specified in the processor agreement; the organisational default is a minimum 30 days for non-urgent changes; the processor agreement may specify a different period

---

## A.2.5.3 — Transfer Destination Documentation

[Organisation] SHALL specify and document the countries and international organisations to which customer PII may possibly be transferred in the course of service delivery.

- A **Transfer Destination Register** (maintained by the DPO, by customer or by service) lists all possible transfer destinations, transfer mechanism, and applicable PII categories
- The register is made available to customers in the processor agreement or data processing schedule
- Updates to the register triggered by new sub-processors, infrastructure changes, or adequacy status changes require customer notification per A.2.5.2

---

## A.2.5.4 — Records of PII Disclosures to Third Parties

[Organisation] SHALL record all disclosures of customer PII to third parties made in the course of processing.

- A **Processor Disclosure Register** logs each disclosure: date, third party identity, PII categories disclosed, basis (customer instruction, legal obligation, contractual obligation)
- The register is maintained per customer or per service
- Records are retained for minimum 3 years and made available to customers upon request
- Disclosures to sub-processors are documented separately per PRIV-POL-A.2.5.7-9

---

## A.2.5.5 — Notification of Legally Binding Disclosure Requests

When [Organisation] receives a legally binding request for disclosure of customer PII (e.g., from a law enforcement authority, court, regulatory body, or tax authority):

- [Organisation] SHALL notify the customer without undue delay, unless prohibited from doing so by law (e.g., a non-disclosure order)
- Notification SHALL include the nature of the request, the PII categories requested, and the legal authority cited — to the extent legally permissible
- Where notification is legally prohibited: [Organisation] SHALL document the constraint with Legal/Compliance sign-off, comply with the minimum necessary disclosure, maintain a sealed record for later customer notification, seek legal advice on challenging the prohibition where possible, and notify the customer as soon as the prohibition is lifted

---

## A.2.5.6 — Legally Binding and Non-Binding Disclosure Requests

**Non-legally-binding requests**: [Organisation] SHALL reject any requests for disclosure of customer PII that are not legally binding. If uncertain whether a request is legally binding, [Organisation] SHALL seek legal advice before responding.

**Foreign government requests (GDPR Article 48)**: Where a disclosure request from a foreign (non-EU/non-EEA) authority is not based on an applicable international agreement, [Organisation] will not comply without first assessing, with Legal/Compliance guidance, whether a GDPR-compatible transfer mechanism applies. This connects foreign disclosure requests to the transfer framework in A.2.5.2 and A.2.5.3.

**Legally binding requests**: [Organisation] SHALL consult with the customer before making any disclosure pursuant to a legally binding request — except where law prohibits notification. Upon consultation, [Organisation] shall:

- Implement any reasonable legal measures to limit the scope of the disclosure
- Disclose only the minimum PII necessary to comply with the legal obligation
- Document the disclosure in the Processor Disclosure Register

**Contractually authorised disclosures**: [Organisation] may accept and act upon contractually agreed requests for PII disclosures that are explicitly authorised by the customer (e.g., reporting to specified regulators, sharing with auditors under customer instruction).

# Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | Maintains Transfer Destination Register and Processor Disclosure Register; manages legally binding disclosure request process; notifies customers of disclosure requests |
| **Legal/Compliance** | Assesses whether disclosure requests are legally binding; advises on legal basis for challenging requests; reviews SCCs and transfer mechanisms |
| **Customer Success** | Coordinates customer notification for transfer changes and disclosure requests |

---

# Evidence Requirements

| Evidence | Description | Retention |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Transfer Destination Register | Countries and mechanisms per service | Current + 3 years |
| Processor Disclosure Register | Disclosures of customer PII to third parties | 5 years |
| Transfer Change Notifications | Customer notifications of transfer basis changes | 3 years from the date of notification |
| Legally Binding Disclosure Records | Requests received, assessments, customer notifications, disclosures made | 5 years |

---

# Audit Considerations

- Transfer Destination Register available to customers in agreements
- Customer notification process for transfer changes documented and evidenced
- Disclosure Register with all third-party disclosures of customer PII
- Legally binding disclosure request handling: assessment, customer notification, minimum necessary disclosure
- Non-legally-binding requests: documented refusals
