## Executive Summary

This policy establishes [Organisation]'s requirements when acting as PII Processor for disclosing sub-processor use to customers, engaging sub-processors only per customer contract, and managing changes to sub-processors — in accordance with ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Controls A.2.5.7, A.2.5.8, and A.2.5.9.

**Scope**: All third parties engaged by [Organisation] as sub-processors to assist with PII processing on behalf of customers; all sub-processor disclosures, engagements, and change notifications.

**Role Applicability**: This policy applies to [Organisation] acting as **PII Processor only**. Controls A.2.5.7–A.2.5.9 are processor-specific (Table A.2).

**Combined Control Rationale**: A.2.5.7–A.2.5.9 govern the entire sub-processor lifecycle: disclosure before use (A.2.5.7), engagement standards (A.2.5.8), and change management (A.2.5.9). Together they ensure customers retain visibility and control over the supply chain handling their PII, consistent with GDPR Article 28(2).

---

# Scope and Applicability

## ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Control Statements

**Control A.2.5.7 — Disclosure of subcontractors used to process PII**
Control A.2.5.7 requires [Organisation] to disclose to the customer, before processing commences, whether any sub-processors will be used to process the customer's PII.

**Control A.2.5.8 — Engagement of a subcontractor to process PII**
Control A.2.5.8 requires [Organisation] to engage sub-processors only in accordance with the customer contract — meaning only with the customer's authorisation and with equivalent data protection obligations flowing down to the sub-processor.

**Control A.2.5.9 — Change of subcontractor to process PII**
Control A.2.5.9 requires [Organisation], where it operates under a general written authorisation for sub-processor use, to inform customers of any intended addition or replacement of sub-processors in advance, giving customers the opportunity to object.

## Regulatory Framework

**Tier 1: Mandatory Compliance** (per PRIV-POL-00):

- **EU GDPR**: Article 28(2) (sub-processor engagement requires prior specific or general written authorisation from the controller; processor remains fully liable for sub-processor compliance); Article 28(4) (same GDPR obligations imposed on sub-processor as in the main processor agreement)
- **CH FADP**: Article 9(3) (same data protection obligations must be imposed on sub-processor as in the principal processor agreement)
- **ISO/IEC 27701:2025**: Controls A.2.5.7–A.2.5.9 (normative)

# Policy Statements

## A.2.5.7 — Disclosure of Sub-processors

Before commencing processing on behalf of a customer, [Organisation] SHALL disclose to the customer whether any sub-processors are used to process that customer's PII.

### Sub-processor List

[Organisation] maintains a **Sub-processor Register** that lists all sub-processors engaged in PII processing on behalf of customers, including:

- Sub-processor name and contact details
- Location (country/jurisdiction)
- Nature of processing performed
- Categories of PII processed
- Applicable customers or services

The Sub-processor Register is made available to customers as part of the processor agreement or a publicly accessible list maintained by [Organisation]. Customers are notified of updates per A.2.5.9.

---

## A.2.5.8 — Engaging Sub-processors

[Organisation] SHALL only engage sub-processors to process customer PII in accordance with the customer contract — i.e., with the customer's authorisation (specific or general per GDPR Article 28(2)).

### Sub-processor Agreement Requirements

Before engaging any sub-processor with access to customer PII, [Organisation] SHALL:

1. Obtain customer authorisation (or confirm general written authorisation exists in the processor agreement)
2. Execute a sub-processor agreement imposing the same data protection obligations as those in [Organisation]'s processor agreement with the customer — including:
   - Processing only on [Organisation]'s instructions
   - Implementing appropriate security measures (GDPR Article 32 standard)
   - Confidentiality obligations
   - Sub-sub-processor restrictions: sub-processors may only engage sub-sub-processors with [Organisation]'s prior written consent, and the same obligation flow-down applies; [Organisation] includes sub-sub-processor visibility in its sub-processor due diligence and change notification processes
   - Breach notification to [Organisation] without undue delay
   - Return or deletion of PII at end of engagement
   - Cooperation with audits
3. Conduct a security due diligence assessment of the sub-processor

### Processor Liability

[Organisation] remains fully liable to customers for the performance of sub-processors' obligations. If a sub-processor fails to fulfil its data protection obligations, [Organisation] remains responsible to the customer as if it had processed the PII itself (GDPR Article 28(4)).

### Sub-processor Breach Notifications

When [Organisation] receives a breach notification from a sub-processor, it shall treat that notification as its own breach event for the purposes of the customer notification timeline. [Organisation] shall notify the affected customer(s) without undue delay in accordance with PRIV-POL-A.3.11-12. The fact that the breach originated at a sub-processor does not extend [Organisation]'s notification window.

---

## A.2.5.9 — Changes to Sub-processors

Where [Organisation]'s customer agreements include a general written authorisation for sub-processor engagement (rather than specific approval per sub-processor), [Organisation] SHALL notify customers of any intended addition or replacement of sub-processors in advance, giving customers the opportunity to object.

### Change Notification Process

1. [Organisation] identifies an intended sub-processor change (addition or replacement)
2. DPO reviews the change for compliance impact and updates the Sub-processor Register
3. Customers are notified in advance of the effective date — notification period as specified in the processor agreement; the organisational default is a minimum 30 days; the processor agreement may specify a different period
4. Customers may object to the change — [Organisation] SHALL have a process for handling objections, which may include:
   - Implementing alternative technical measures that do not require the new sub-processor
   - Allowing the customer to terminate the service without penalty
5. If no objection is received within the notification period, the change proceeds
6. All changes and notification records are maintained in the Sub-processor Change Register

# Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | Maintains Sub-processor Register; approves sub-processor engagements; manages change notifications; reviews sub-processor agreements for compliance |
| **Legal/Compliance** | Reviews sub-processor agreements for Article 28(4) compliance; advises on customer authorisation mechanisms |
| **Procurement** | Triggers DPO review before engaging any new sub-processor; maintains sub-processor due diligence records |
| **Customer Success** | Coordinates customer notification for sub-processor changes; manages objection handling |

# Audit Considerations

- Sub-processor Register current and disclosed to customers
- Sub-processor agreements in place with same obligations as main processor agreement
- Customer authorisation documented for all sub-processor engagements
- Change notification process: advance notice sent, no-objection or objection handling documented
- [Organisation] acknowledges full liability for sub-processor compliance
