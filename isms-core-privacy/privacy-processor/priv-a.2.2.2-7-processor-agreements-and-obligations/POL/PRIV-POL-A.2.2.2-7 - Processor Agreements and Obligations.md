## Executive Summary

This policy establishes [Organisation]'s requirements when acting as a PII Processor — covering customer agreement obligations, purpose restriction, marketing use prohibition, infringing instruction handling, customer compliance support, and processing records — in accordance with ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Controls A.2.2.2 through A.2.2.7.

**Scope**: All PII processing activities conducted by [Organisation] as PII Processor on behalf of customer controllers; all contracts governing such processing; all obligations to customers and their data subjects arising from processor engagements.

**Role Applicability**: This policy applies to [Organisation] acting as **PII Processor only**. Controls A.2.2.2–A.2.2.7 are processor-specific (Table A.2).

**Combined Control Rationale**: A.2.2.2–A.2.2.7 define the core obligations a processor owes to its customer (the controller). They cover the legal basis of the processor relationship (agreement content), the discipline of processing only on instruction (A.2.2.3), boundaries on using customer PII (A.2.2.4), the obligation to speak up when instructions are unlawful (A.2.2.5), helping customers demonstrate compliance (A.2.2.6), and maintaining the processor's own records (A.2.2.7).

# Scope and Applicability

## ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Control Statements

**Control A.2.2.2 — Customer agreement**
Control A.2.2.2 requires [Organisation] to ensure that its processor agreements, where relevant, address how [Organisation] will assist the customer in fulfilling the customer's own obligations — taking into account the nature of the processing and the information available to [Organisation].

**Control A.2.2.3 — Organisation's purposes**
Control A.2.2.3 requires [Organisation] to process customer PII only for the purposes set out in the customer's documented instructions — not for any purpose of [Organisation]'s own.

**Control A.2.2.4 — Marketing and advertising use**
Control A.2.2.4 prohibits [Organisation] from using customer PII for marketing or advertising unless the relevant PII principal has given prior consent, and prohibits making that consent a condition of service.

**Control A.2.2.5 — Infringing instruction**
Control A.2.2.5 requires [Organisation] to inform the customer if [Organisation] considers that a customer processing instruction would infringe applicable legal requirements.

**Control A.2.2.6 — Customer obligations**
Control A.2.2.6 requires [Organisation] to provide customers with the information they need to demonstrate their own compliance with applicable data protection obligations.

**Control A.2.2.7 — Records related to processing PII**
Control A.2.2.7 requires [Organisation] to identify and maintain the records needed to demonstrate compliance with its obligations under each customer processor agreement.

## Regulatory Framework

**Tier 1: Mandatory Compliance** (per PRIV-POL-00):

- **EU GDPR**: Article 28 (processor must process only on documented instructions; assist with controller's obligations; maintain processing records; implement security measures; Article 28(3) mandatory content); Article 29 (processor and its personnel process only on instruction)
- **CH FADP**: Article 9 (processor must provide equivalent data protection; may only process as contractually agreed)
- **ISO/IEC 27701:2025**: Controls A.2.2.2–A.2.2.7 (normative)

# Policy Statements

## A.2.2.2 — Customer Agreement Content

When [Organisation] acts as PII Processor, the processor agreement SHALL address, where relevant, [Organisation]'s role in:

- Processing only on documented instructions from the customer (explicit in the agreement per GDPR Article 28(3)(a))
- Maintaining confidentiality obligations for all personnel with access to customer PII (GDPR Article 28(3)(b))
- Implementing appropriate technical and organisational security measures (Article 32)
- Assisting the customer with data subject rights obligations (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection)
- Assisting with security obligations, breach notification, DPIA, and prior consultation
- Providing information necessary for the customer to demonstrate compliance
- Cooperating with audits or inspections by the customer or their supervisory authority
- Return or deletion of all PII upon service termination

The DPO reviews all processor agreements before execution to confirm these obligations are adequately addressed, taking into account the nature of the processing and the information available to [Organisation].

---

## A.2.2.3 — Processing Only on Customer Instructions

[Organisation] SHALL ensure that PII processed on behalf of a customer is processed only for the purposes expressed in the customer's documented instructions.

### Instruction Compliance

- PII shall not be used for [Organisation]'s own purposes (including product improvement, service enhancement, or benchmarking, or any other purpose not specified in the customer's documented instructions) unless explicitly authorised by the customer in writing
- All processing operations applied to customer PII must be traceable to a customer instruction (contract, SLA, or written instruction)
- Verbal or informal instructions (email, support ticket, or similar) must be confirmed in writing by the customer before [Organisation] acts on them; the DPO or Customer Success team is responsible for obtaining and filing that confirmation
- Where [Organisation]'s personnel seek to use customer PII for a purpose not covered by the contract, they SHALL escalate to the DPO for assessment before proceeding

---

## A.2.2.4 — Marketing and Advertising Prohibition

[Organisation] SHALL NOT use PII processed under a customer contract for marketing or advertising purposes unless prior consent from the PII principal has been established.

- Such consent SHALL be separately obtained and not bundled with the service agreement
- Providing consent for marketing SHALL NOT be a condition of receiving the service
- [Organisation] SHALL maintain records of any marketing consent obtained and shall not send marketing to individuals who have not consented

---

## A.2.2.5 — Infringing Instruction

If [Organisation] considers that a customer's processing instruction infringes applicable legal requirements (GDPR, CH FADP, or other applicable law), [Organisation] SHALL inform the customer in writing.

### Escalation Process

1. Processing personnel identify a potentially infringing instruction → escalate to DPO immediately
2. DPO assesses the instruction — obtains Legal/Compliance input where uncertain
3. DPO documents the assessment and communicates the concern to the customer in writing
4. Pending resolution: [Organisation] SHALL NOT execute the infringing instruction
5. If the customer insists on proceeding after notification, [Organisation] SHALL document its position and may exercise its contractual right to suspend or terminate the engagement

All infringing instruction notifications and customer responses SHALL be documented and retained.

---

## A.2.2.6 — Supporting Customer Compliance Demonstration

[Organisation] SHALL provide customers with the information necessary to enable them to demonstrate compliance with their obligations as PII Controller.

This includes providing:

- Security documentation (e.g., ISO 27001/27701 certificates, audit reports, penetration test summaries, SOC 2 reports) as appropriate for the nature of processing
- Sub-processor lists and associated agreements (per PRIV-POL-A.2.5.7-9)
- Evidence of security measures implemented (encryption status, access controls, backup procedures)
- Cooperation with customer audits or supervisory authority inspections
- Breach notifications enabling the customer to meet Article 33/34 obligations (per PRIV-POL-A.3.11-12)

The DPO maintains a **Customer Compliance Support Register** that tracks what has been provided to each customer and when.

---

## A.2.2.7 — Processor Records

[Organisation] SHALL maintain records in support of demonstrating compliance with its processor obligations for each customer engagement.

### Processor Record of Processing Activities

[Organisation] maintains a processor-side **Record of Processing Activities (RoPA)** per GDPR Article 30(2), which includes for each processing activity performed on behalf of customers:

- Name and contact details of the customer controller (and the controller's DPO where applicable) — required by GDPR Article 30(2)
- Name and contact details of the processor (and DPO)
- Categories of processing carried out on behalf of each customer
- Transfers to third countries (where applicable) and safeguards
- General description of technical and organisational security measures

### Additional Processor Records

In addition to the processor RoPA, [Organisation] maintains:

- Processor Agreement Register (all customer agreements with status and review date)
- Sub-processor Register (per PRIV-POL-A.2.5.7-9)
- Infringing Instruction Register (all notifications sent and customer responses)
- Customer Compliance Support Register (documentation provided per customer)

# Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | Reviews processor agreements before execution; manages infringing instruction escalation; maintains processor RoPA and supporting registers; provides compliance support to customers |
| **Legal/Compliance** | Reviews processor agreement legal terms; advises on Article 28 mandatory content; supports infringing instruction assessment |
| **Customer Success / Account Management** | First point of contact for customer compliance queries; escalates to DPO for substantive responses |
| **IT Security Team** | Provides technical security documentation for customer compliance support; implements Article 32 measures |

# Audit Considerations

- Processor agreements addressing Article 28(3) mandatory content
- Evidence of processing only on customer instructions (no own-purpose use)
- No use of customer PII for [Organisation]'s marketing without consent
- Infringing instruction notifications where applicable, with documented assessment
- Customer compliance support documentation provided on request
- Processor RoPA current and accurate
