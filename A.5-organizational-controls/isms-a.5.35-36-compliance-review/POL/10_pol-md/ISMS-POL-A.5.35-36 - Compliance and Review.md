# Executive Summary

This policy establishes [Organisation]'s requirements for independent review of information security and compliance with information security policies, rules, and standards in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Controls A.5.35 and A.5.36.

**Purpose**: Define organisational requirements for ensuring information security management is independently reviewed and that compliance with established policies is verified. This policy establishes WHAT review and compliance capabilities are required, WHO is accountable, and WHEN actions must occur. Implementation procedures (HOW to execute reviews and compliance verification) are documented separately in ISMS-IMP-A.5.35-36 Implementation Guides.

**Scope**: This policy applies to all information security management activities, controls, policies, and standards within [Organisation]'s ISMS scope, including all personnel, systems, processes, and third parties subject to [Organisation]'s information security requirements.

**Combined Control Approach**: These two controls are implemented as a unified compliance and review framework:

1. **Independent Review (A.5.35)** - Third-party or independent assessment of ISMS effectiveness
2. **Compliance Verification (A.5.36)** - Ongoing operational compliance checking by management

Despite unified implementation, each control maintains distinct requirements for Statement of Applicability (SoA) purposes.

# Scope & Applicability

## In Scope

**Independent Review (A.5.35)** covers:

- ISMS policies, procedures, and documentation
- Information security controls and their implementation
- Management's approach to information security
- Security objectives and their achievement
- Organisational security posture overall
- Changes to the security environment since last review

**Compliance Verification (A.5.36)** covers:

- Adherence to information security policy
- Adherence to topic-specific security policies
- Compliance with documented security procedures
- Compliance with security standards and rules
- Technical control implementation status
- User compliance with security requirements


## Out of Scope

The following require Executive Management approval and documented risk acceptance:

- Review of information classified as Restricted where review would require special clearance
- Third-party systems not covered by contractual security requirements
- Activities explicitly excluded from ISMS scope per Statement of Applicability

## Third-Party Applicability

Third-party service providers, contractors, and partners accessing [Organisation] systems or handling [Organisation] data MUST:

- Cooperate with [Organisation]'s independent review activities when requested
- Provide evidence of their own compliance with contractual security requirements
- Participate in compliance assessments of activities within their scope
- Remediate identified non-compliance within agreed timeframes


## Regulatory Applicability

This policy addresses mandatory compliance requirements per ISMS-POL-00 (Regulatory Applicability Framework):

**Tier 1: Mandatory Compliance**

- **ISO/IEC 27001:2022**: Controls A.5.35 (Independent Review) and A.5.36 (Compliance Verification)
- **Swiss nDSG (FADP)**: Art. 8 - Organisational measures including verification of compliance
- **EU GDPR**: Art. 32 - Verification of security measures effectiveness


**Tier 2: Conditional Applicability**

- PCI DSS v4.0, FINMA, DORA, NIS2, HIPAA - Apply where [Organisation]'s business activities trigger applicability per ISMS-POL-00

# Policy Statements

## 1. Independent Review of Information Security (A.5.35)

### 1.1 Review Requirements

**PS-1.1.1 Planned Interval Reviews**: [Organisation] SHALL conduct independent reviews of its approach to managing information security at planned intervals. Reviews SHALL occur at least annually.

**PS-1.1.2 Significant Change Reviews**: Independent reviews SHALL also be conducted when significant changes occur that impact information security, including:

- Major organisational restructuring
- Significant changes to IT infrastructure or architecture
- Introduction of new products or services with security implications
- Changes to laws, regulations, or standards affecting information security
- Following significant security incidents or breaches
- Major changes to the business model or operations

**PS-1.1.3 Review Independence**: Reviews MUST be conducted by persons independent of the area being reviewed. Independence may be achieved through:

- Internal auditors not responsible for the area under review
- External third-party auditors or consultants
- Qualified personnel from separate departments with no operational responsibility for areas reviewed

**PS-1.1.4 Reviewer Competence**: Reviewers MUST possess appropriate competence to conduct information security reviews, demonstrated through:

- Relevant qualifications (e.g., CISA, ISO 27001 Lead Auditor, CISSP)
- Demonstrated experience in information security auditing
- Knowledge of ISO 27001:2022 and relevant regulatory requirements

Reviewer qualifications SHALL be verified and documented in the Reviewer Registry before assignment to review activities.

**Verification**: Reviewer qualifications verified before engagement; documented in ISMS-IMP-A.5.35-36.1 Reviewer Registry.

### 1.2 Review Scope

**PS-1.2.1 Comprehensive Scope**: Independent reviews SHALL assess whether [Organisation]'s information security approach:

- Meets the requirements set by the organisation for information security
- Achieves the stated information security objectives
- Performs effectively as designed
- Remains adequate for the organisation's risk environment

**PS-1.2.2 Scope Documentation**: Each review's scope SHALL be documented in advance, including:

- Controls, processes, or areas to be reviewed
- Time period covered by the review
- Review methodology to be applied
- Documentation and evidence requirements
- Planned interviews and assessments

**Verification**: Review scope documented using ISMS-IMP-A.5.35-36.1 Review Scope sheet before each review commences.

### 1.3 Review Execution

**PS-1.3.1 Review Planning**: Independent reviews SHALL follow a documented plan that includes:

- Review objectives and scope
- Review criteria and audit standards
- Sampling methodology where applicable
- Timeline and milestones
- Resource requirements
- Reporting requirements

**PS-1.3.2 Evidence Collection**: Reviews SHALL be evidence-based, collecting and retaining:

- Documentation reviewed (policies, procedures, records)
- Interview notes and summaries
- Observation records
- Technical assessment results
- Sampling methodology and sample records

**PS-1.3.3 Review Tracking**: Review execution progress SHALL be tracked and monitored using the ISMS-IMP-A.5.35-36.1 Review Execution sheet, recording actual vs. planned progress.

**Verification**: Review evidence retained in ISMS Evidence Library (SharePoint/Confluence or equivalent) with reference recorded in Evidence Register.

### 1.4 Review Findings

**PS-1.4.1 Finding Documentation**: All review findings SHALL be documented in the ISMS-IMP-A.5.35-36.3 Findings Register, including:

- Finding ID and description
- Control or area affected
- Severity classification (Critical, High, Medium, Low, Observation)
- Root cause (where determinable)
- Recommended corrective action
- Evidence references

**PS-1.4.2 Finding Severity Classification**:

| Severity | Definition | Example |
|----------|------------|---------|
| Critical | Fundamental control failure with immediate risk | Missing access control entirely |
| High | Significant gap requiring prompt attention | Incomplete access reviews |
| Medium | Partial implementation requiring improvement | Inconsistent documentation |
| Low | Minor gap with limited risk | Procedure clarification needed |
| Observation | Improvement opportunity, not a gap | Process efficiency enhancement |

**PS-1.4.3 Management Reporting**: Review findings SHALL be reported to appropriate management:

- Critical findings: Immediate notification to CISO and Executive Management
- High findings: Reported within 5 business days
- Medium/Low findings: Reported with review completion
- Observations: Included in review report for consideration

**Verification**: Finding reports distributed per reporting requirements; receipt confirmed.

### 1.5 Management Action

**PS-1.5.1 Corrective Action Requirement**: When independent reviews identify inadequacies in [Organisation]'s approach to information security or its implementation, management SHALL initiate corrective actions.

**PS-1.5.2 Corrective Action Timeliness**:

| Finding Severity | Action Plan Required | Target Resolution |
|------------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Critical | Within 24 hours | 30 days maximum |
| High | Within 5 business days | 60 days maximum |
| Medium | Within 10 business days | 90 days maximum |
| Low | Within 30 business days | Next review cycle |

**PS-1.5.3 Action Tracking**: Corrective actions SHALL be tracked in ISMS-IMP-A.5.35-36.3 Remediation Actions sheet until verified closure.

**PS-1.5.4 Closure Verification**: Corrective actions SHALL not be marked as complete until:

- Action has been implemented
- Evidence of implementation exists
- Verification confirms effectiveness
- Independent verification for Critical/High findings

**Verification**: Closure documented in Verification Log; Critical/High closures verified by person independent of action owner.


## 2. Compliance with Policies, Rules and Standards (A.5.36)

### 2.1 Compliance Review Requirements

**PS-2.1.1 Regular Review Requirement**: Compliance with [Organisation]'s information security policy, topic-specific policies, rules, and standards SHALL be regularly reviewed.

**PS-2.1.2 Review Frequency**:

| Review Type | Frequency | Responsible |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| Self-assessment by system owners | Quarterly | System/Asset Owners |
| Management compliance review | Semi-annually | Department Managers |
| Comprehensive compliance assessment | Annually | Information Security Team |

**PS-2.1.3 Review Scope**: Compliance reviews SHALL verify:

- Adherence to the Information Security Policy (ISMS-POL-01)
- Compliance with all applicable topic-specific security policies
- Compliance with documented security procedures
- Compliance with technical security standards
- User compliance with acceptable use requirements

**Verification**: Review completion recorded in ISMS-IMP-A.5.35-36.2 compliance assessment sheets.

### 2.2 Manager Responsibilities

**PS-2.2.1 Manager Accountability**: Managers SHALL be accountable for ensuring the teams and departments for which they are responsible maintain compliance with security policies and rules.

**PS-2.2.2 Manager Review Activities**: Managers SHALL:

- Conduct or oversee periodic compliance reviews of their areas
- Ensure staff are aware of and trained on applicable security requirements
- Address identified non-compliance promptly
- Report compliance status through governance channels

**PS-2.2.3 Documentation Requirements**: Manager compliance reviews SHALL be documented, including:

- Areas/systems/processes reviewed
- Review date and reviewer
- Compliance status (Compliant, Partial, Non-Compliant, Not Assessed)
- Evidence of compliance or non-compliance
- Planned actions for non-compliant areas

**Verification**: Manager review records maintained using ISMS-IMP-A.5.35-36.2 Department Assessment sheet.

### 2.3 Compliance Verification Methods

**PS-2.3.1 Verification Approaches**: Compliance SHALL be verified through appropriate methods, including:

- Document review (policies signed, procedures followed)
- Technical assessment (configuration review, vulnerability scanning)
- Interview and observation
- Sampling and testing
- Automated compliance monitoring where available

**PS-2.3.2 Evidence Requirements**: Compliance claims MUST be supported by evidence, including:

- Screenshots or system reports for technical compliance
- Signed acknowledgments for policy acceptance
- Training records for awareness requirements
- Logs and records for procedural compliance

**PS-2.3.3 Evidence Storage**: Compliance evidence SHALL be stored in the ISMS Evidence Library (SharePoint/Confluence or equivalent) with references recorded in the relevant Evidence Register.

### 2.4 Non-Compliance Management

**PS-2.4.1 Non-Compliance Identification**: Instances of non-compliance SHALL be recorded in the ISMS-IMP-A.5.35-36.2 NonCompliance Register with:

- Description of non-compliance
- Policy, rule, or standard violated
- Area/department/system affected
- Severity assessment
- Owner responsible for remediation

**PS-2.4.2 Timely Corrective Action**: When non-compliance is identified, corrective action SHALL be taken in a timely manner. Non-compliance MUST be resolved by the next scheduled review at latest.

**PS-2.4.3 Non-Compliance Severity**:

| Severity | Definition | Action Required |
|----------|------------|-----------------|
| Critical | Complete disregard for security requirement | Immediate containment, 30-day resolution |
| High | Significant deviation from requirement | 60-day resolution |
| Medium | Partial compliance, gaps exist | 90-day resolution |
| Low | Minor deviation, limited risk | Next review cycle |

**PS-2.4.4 Escalation**: Non-compliance not resolved within target timeframes SHALL be escalated:

- 30 days overdue: Escalate to Department Head
- 60 days overdue: Escalate to CISO
- 90 days overdue: Escalate to Executive Management

**Verification**: Escalation triggers tracked in ISMS-IMP-A.5.35-36.4 Dashboard; overdue items highlighted automatically.


## 3. Consolidated Findings Management

### 3.1 Unified Findings Register

**PS-3.1.1 Single Register**: All findings from independent reviews (A.5.35) and compliance assessments (A.5.36) SHALL be consolidated in a single Findings Register (ISMS-IMP-A.5.35-36.3).

**PS-3.1.2 Finding Classification**: Each finding SHALL be classified by:

- Source (Independent Review, Compliance Assessment, Internal Audit, External Audit)
- Control affected
- Severity
- Status (Open, In Progress, Remediated, Verified Closed, Risk Accepted)

**PS-3.1.3 Finding Uniqueness**: Duplicate findings from multiple sources SHALL be consolidated to prevent redundant remediation tracking.

### 3.2 Root Cause Analysis

**PS-3.2.1 RCA Requirement**: Root cause analysis (RCA) SHALL be conducted for:

- All Critical severity findings
- All High severity findings
- Recurring findings (same or similar finding in two consecutive reviews)
- Findings with systemic implications

**PS-3.2.2 RCA Documentation**: Root cause analysis SHALL be documented in ISMS-IMP-A.5.35-36.3 Root Cause Analysis sheet, including:

- Finding reference
- RCA methodology used (5 Whys, Fishbone, etc.)
- Root cause identified
- Contributing factors
- Corrective and preventive actions recommended

**Verification**: RCA completion tracked; Critical/High findings require RCA before closure.

### 3.3 Remediation Management

**PS-3.3.1 Action Planning**: Each finding requiring remediation SHALL have documented corrective action(s) in ISMS-IMP-A.5.35-36.3 Remediation Actions sheet, including:

- Action description
- Owner accountable for completion
- Target completion date
- Resources required
- Dependencies or constraints

**PS-3.3.2 Progress Tracking**: Remediation progress SHALL be tracked and reported:

- Weekly for Critical findings
- Bi-weekly for High findings
- Monthly for Medium/Low findings

**PS-3.3.3 Verification Requirements**: Remediation completion SHALL be verified through:

| Finding Severity | Verification Requirement |
|------------------|-------------------------|
| Critical | Independent verification by qualified assessor |
| High | Independent verification by qualified assessor |
| Medium | Verification by Information Security team member |
| Low | Self-attestation by owner with evidence |

**Verification**: Verification completion documented in Verification Log with evidence reference.

### 3.4 Risk Acceptance

**PS-3.4.1 Risk Acceptance Option**: Findings that cannot be remediated within acceptable timeframes or where remediation cost exceeds risk may be submitted for risk acceptance.

**PS-3.4.2 Risk Acceptance Authority**:

| Finding Severity | Risk Acceptance Authority |
|------------------|---------------------------|
| Critical | CEO/Executive Management |
| High | CISO with CIO endorsement |
| Medium | CISO |
| Low | Department Head with CISO notification |

**PS-3.4.3 Risk Acceptance Documentation**: Risk acceptance requests SHALL include:

- Finding description and severity
- Business justification for acceptance
- Compensating controls in place
- Duration of acceptance (maximum 12 months; renewals require same approval authority level as initial acceptance)
- Review trigger conditions
- Risk owner acknowledgment

Renewal requests must demonstrate re-assessment of risk and continued justification for acceptance.

**Verification**: Risk acceptances recorded in Findings Register with status "Risk Accepted" and documented in risk register.


## 4. Compliance Dashboard and Reporting

### 4.1 Executive Dashboard

**PS-4.1.1 Dashboard Maintenance**: An executive compliance dashboard SHALL be maintained providing consolidated visibility into:

- Overall ISMS compliance status
- Independent review findings status
- Compliance assessment results
- Remediation progress
- Trend analysis

**PS-4.1.2 Dashboard Components**: The dashboard (ISMS-IMP-A.5.35-36.4) SHALL include:

- Executive summary with key metrics
- Review status tracking
- Compliance status by policy/control
- Findings overview with aging analysis
- KPI scorecard
- Trend analysis over time

**Verification**: Dashboard updated monthly; presented to CISO monthly and Executive Management quarterly.

### 4.2 Key Performance Indicators

**PS-4.2.1 Compliance KPIs**: The following KPIs SHALL be tracked:

| KPI | Target | Measurement |
|-----|--------|-------------|
| Independent review completion rate | 100% | Scheduled vs. completed reviews |
| Finding closure rate | 90% within target | Closed on time vs. total |
| Critical finding MTTR | < 30 days | Mean time to remediate |
| High finding MTTR | < 60 days | Mean time to remediate |
| Compliance assessment completion | 100% | Assessments completed vs. scheduled |
| Policy compliance rate | > 90% | Compliant items vs. total |

**PS-4.2.2 KPI Reporting**: KPI status SHALL be reported:

- Monthly to CISO
- Quarterly to Executive Management
- Annually in Management Review

**Verification**: KPI calculations documented in ISMS-IMP-A.5.35-36.4 KPI Scorecard sheet.

### 4.3 Trend Analysis

**PS-4.3.1 Trend Tracking**: Compliance and review metrics SHALL be tracked over time to identify:

- Improvement or degradation trends
- Recurring finding patterns
- Areas requiring focused attention
- Effectiveness of remediation efforts

**PS-4.3.2 Trend Data Retention**: Trend data SHALL be retained for minimum 3 years to support long-term analysis.

**PS-4.3.3 Trend Reporting**: Trend analysis SHALL be included in:

- Quarterly management reports
- Annual management review
- Certification audit preparation

# Exception Handling

## Exception Requests

Exceptions to this policy require documented risk acceptance:

1. **Request**: Submit exception request to CISO with business justification
2. **Assessment**: Information Security assesses risk and proposes compensating controls
3. **Approval**: Exception approved per authority levels in PS-3.4.2
4. **Duration**: Maximum 12 months, subject to renewal
5. **Review**: Exceptions reviewed at each compliance review cycle

## Timeline Exception Handling

If remediation SLAs cannot be met:

1. **Immediate**: Implement compensating controls where possible
2. **Document**: Record reason for delay and revised timeline
3. **Escalate**: Per escalation requirements in PS-2.4.4
4. **Track**: Continue tracking with "Delayed" status and updated target

# Compliance Scoring Methodology

## Independent Review Scoring (A.5.35)

Reviews score each area assessed:

| Score | Status | Definition |
|-------|--------|------------|
| 1.0 | Fully Effective | Control operating as designed, no issues |
| 0.75 | Largely Effective | Minor issues, control substantially working |
| 0.5 | Partially Effective | Significant gaps but some controls operating |
| 0.25 | Minimally Effective | Major gaps, limited control effectiveness |
| 0 | Ineffective | Control not operating or not implemented |

**Overall Score**: Average of all control scores, weighted by control significance.

## Compliance Assessment Scoring (A.5.36)

Compliance status for each requirement:

| Status | Score | Definition |
|--------|-------|------------|
| Compliant | 1.0 | Full adherence to requirement |
| Partial | 0.5 | Partial adherence, gaps exist |
| Non-Compliant | 0 | Requirement not met |
| Not Assessed | N/A | Excluded from scoring |

**Department Score**: (Total points earned / Total possible points) x 100%
