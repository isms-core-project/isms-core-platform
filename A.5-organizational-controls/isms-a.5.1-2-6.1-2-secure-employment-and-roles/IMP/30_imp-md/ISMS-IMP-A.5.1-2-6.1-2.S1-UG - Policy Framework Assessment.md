# Purpose and Scope

## Assessment Objective

This assessment verifies [Organization]'s compliance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Control A.5.1 (Policies for Information Security) as implemented through ISMS-POL-A.5.1-2-6.1-2, Section 4.

**Control A.5.1 Requirement:**
> *Information security policy and topic-specific policies should be defined, approved by management, published, communicated to and acknowledged by relevant personnel and relevant interested parties, and reviewed at planned intervals and if significant changes occur.*

**Assessment Focus:**

- Policy inventory completeness (all required policies exist)
- Policy hierarchy and classification compliance
- Policy lifecycle management (creation, approval, review, update, sunset)
- Policy governance and accountability
- Policy communication and acknowledgment
- Policy effectiveness and currency

## Relationship to Parent Policy

This assessment implements verification procedures for **ISMS-POL-A.5.1-2-6.1-2, Section 4: Policy Framework Requirements**.

**Policy Requirements Being Assessed:**

- **Section 4.1**: Policy Hierarchy (ISMS Master Policy, Domain Policies, Topic-Specific Policies, Standards, Procedures)
- **Section 4.2**: Policy Lifecycle Management (creation, approval, publication, review, update, sunset)
- **Section 4.3**: Policy Governance (ownership, accountability, approval authority)
- **Section 4.4**: Policy Classification (internal, confidential, public)
- **Section 4.5**: Policy Communication and Acknowledgment
- **Section 4.6**: Policy Repository and Access

**Evidence Collected**: Policy inventory, policy metadata, approval records, review schedules, acknowledgment logs, version history

## Assessment Frequency

**Standard Schedule:**

- **Quarterly**: All organizations (routine compliance verification)
- **Annual**: Comprehensive policy framework review
- **Triggered**: After major policy changes, organizational restructuring, audit findings, or regulatory updates

**Why Quarterly?**

- Policies evolve continuously (new policies, updates, sunsets)
- Quarterly assessment catches policy lifecycle issues early
- Aligns with management review cycle
- Maintains audit readiness

## Assessment Ownership

**Primary Owner**: CISO or Information Security Manager

**Participants**:

- Policy owners (for domain-specific policy verification)
- Compliance Officer (for regulatory policy mapping)
- Legal Counsel (for policy approval verification)
- HR Manager (for employee acknowledgment verification)
- Internal Audit (for independent verification)

**Time Investment**: 4-6 hours for initial assessment, 2-3 hours for quarterly updates

# Assessment Methodology

## Assessment Approach

This assessment uses a **systematic inventory and verification approach**:

**Step 1: Policy Discovery**

- Identify all information security policies in scope
- Catalog policies by hierarchy level (Master, Domain, Topic-Specific)
- Document policy metadata (ID, title, owner, version, status)

**Step 2: Lifecycle Verification**

- Verify each policy has completed required lifecycle stages
- Check approval records exist and are valid
- Confirm review schedules are defined and followed
- Validate version control practices

**Step 3: Governance Assessment**

- Verify ownership and accountability assignments
- Confirm approval authority appropriate for policy level
- Check policy classification matches content sensitivity
- Validate repository access controls

**Step 4: Communication Verification**

- Confirm policies are published and accessible
- Verify stakeholder acknowledgment for relevant policies
- Check training integration for high-impact policies

**Step 5: Gap Analysis**

- Identify missing policies (policy coverage gaps)
- Identify overdue reviews (policy staleness)
- Identify governance gaps (missing owners, approvers)
- Identify communication gaps (low acknowledgment rates)

## Data Sources

**Primary Sources**:

- Policy management system or repository
- Policy metadata (from policy documents or tracking system)
- Approval workflows (email, document management, approval tool)
- Review schedules (policy tracking spreadsheet, calendar)
- Training system (acknowledgment records)
- Version control system (policy history)

**Supporting Sources**:

- ISMS Master Policy (policy hierarchy definition)
- Policy templates and standards
- Organizational charts (ownership verification)
- Management review meeting minutes
- Internal audit reports

## Compliance Criteria

**Compliant Policy** meets ALL criteria:

- ✅ Policy exists and is current (not overdue for review)
- ✅ Policy has valid approval (approved by appropriate authority)
- ✅ Policy is published and accessible (in official repository)
- ✅ Policy has assigned owner and approver
- ✅ Policy has defined review schedule
- ✅ Policy classification appropriate for content
- ✅ Policy acknowledgment (where required) ≥ 90%

**Partial Compliance** occurs when:

- ⚠️ Policy exists but overdue for review (< 90 days overdue)
- ⚠️ Policy approval valid but documentation incomplete
- ⚠️ Policy acknowledgment 70-89%
- ⚠️ Minor metadata gaps (missing version date, incomplete owner info)

**Non-Compliant** when:

- ❌ Required policy does not exist
- ❌ Policy approval missing or invalid
- ❌ Policy significantly overdue for review (> 90 days)
- ❌ Policy owner not assigned
- ❌ Policy not published/accessible
- ❌ Policy acknowledgment < 70% (for mandatory acknowledgment)

## Scoring Methodology

**Overall Compliance Score** = Weighted average across assessment domains

**Domain Weights**:

- Policy Inventory Completeness: 25% (most critical - policies must exist)
- Policy Lifecycle Compliance: 25% (policies must be current and approved)
- Policy Governance: 20% (clear ownership and accountability)
- Policy Classification: 10% (appropriate access controls)
- Policy Communication: 15% (stakeholder awareness)
- Policy Repository: 5% (accessible and organized)

**Compliance Score Interpretation**:

- **90-100%**: Excellent (audit-ready, no significant gaps)
- **80-89%**: Good (minor gaps, routine remediation)
- **70-79%**: Acceptable (moderate gaps, focused remediation needed)
- **60-69%**: Needs Improvement (significant gaps, priority remediation)
- **< 60%**: Critical (major gaps, immediate action required)

# Policy Hierarchy and Inventory

## Understanding the Policy Hierarchy

**ISMS-POL-A.5.1-2-6.1-2 defines a four-tier hierarchy:**

**Tier 1: ISMS Master Policy** (1 policy)

- Highest-level information security governance document
- Establishes ISMS scope, objectives, and governance structure
- Approved by Executive Management/Board
- Example: "ISMS Master Policy", "Information Security Policy"

**Tier 2: Domain Policies** (~5-15 policies)

- Broad domain-level requirements
- Cover major ISMS domains (Access Control, Data Protection, Operations, Physical Security, HR Security)
- Approved by CISO + Executive Management
- Examples: "Access Control Policy", "Data Protection Policy", "Network Security Policy"

**Tier 3: Topic-Specific Policies** (~20-50 policies)

- Detailed requirements for specific controls or technologies
- ISO 27001 Annex A control implementations
- Approved by CISO
- Examples: "Cryptography Policy", "Backup Policy", "Incident Response Policy"

**Tier 4: Standards and Procedures** (~50-200 documents)

- Technical standards, configurations, procedures
- Not "policies" but supporting implementation documents
- Approved by functional managers (IT Manager, Security Manager)
- Examples: "Password Standard", "Backup Procedure", "Vulnerability Scanning Standard"

**Assessment Scope**: This assessment focuses on **Tiers 1-3** (policies). Tier 4 (standards/procedures) are tracked separately.

## Policy Inventory Checklist

**Minimum Required Policies** (per ISO 27001:2022):

**Tier 1 - ISMS Master Policy:**

- [ ] ISMS Master Policy (overarching governance)

**Tier 2 - Domain Policies:**

- [ ] Access Control Policy (A.5.15-18 foundation)
- [ ] Data Protection Policy (A.8.10-12 foundation)
- [ ] Network & Infrastructure Security Policy (A.8.20-24 foundation)
- [ ] Operations Security Policy (A.8.1-9 foundation)
- [ ] Physical & Environmental Security Policy (A.7.1-14 foundation)
- [ ] Human Resources Security Policy (A.6.1-8 foundation - THIS CONTROL)
- [ ] Supplier & Third-Party Management Policy (A.5.19-23 foundation)
- [ ] Incident & Business Continuity Policy (A.5.24-30 foundation)

**Tier 3 - Topic-Specific Policies** (aligned with ISO 27001:2022 Annex A):

- [ ] Organizational Context (A.5.1-14): Policies for information security, roles/responsibilities, project management
- [ ] People Controls (A.6.1-8): Screening, employment terms, awareness, discipline
- [ ] Physical Controls (A.7.1-14): Physical access, monitoring, equipment
- [ ] Technological Controls (A.8.1-34): 34 technology-focused policies

**Note**: Not all Annex A controls require separate policies. Controls may be addressed through:

- Combined policies (stacked controls like this one)
- Reference to standards/procedures
- Risk-based exclusions (documented in Statement of Applicability)

## Policy Discovery Process

**For Organizations Starting Fresh:**

**Step 1**: List all existing information security policies

- Check policy repository/portal
- Check document management system
- Check intranet/SharePoint
- Ask policy owners what policies they maintain
- Review organizational chart for policy ownership

**Step 2**: Classify each policy by hierarchy tier

- Tier 1: Only 1-2 master governance policies
- Tier 2: Domain-level policies (5-15)
- Tier 3: Topic-specific/control-specific (20-50)
- Other: Standards, procedures, guidelines (track separately)

**Step 3**: Identify policy gaps

- Compare inventory to minimum required policies
- Identify Annex A controls without policies
- Check Statement of Applicability for excluded controls
- Prioritize gap remediation (critical controls first)

**For Organizations with Existing ISMS:**

**Step 1**: Export policy inventory from ISMS tool

- Policy ID, Title, Owner, Version, Status
- Last Review Date, Next Review Date
- Approval Date, Approved By

**Step 2**: Validate inventory completeness

- Cross-check against Annex A controls
- Verify all domain policies exist
- Check for orphaned or duplicate policies

**Step 3**: Update policy metadata

- Correct any outdated information
- Assign missing owners/approvers
- Define review schedules for policies without them

## Sheet 2: Policy_Inventory

**Purpose**: Master inventory of all information security policies.

**Completion Process**:

**Step 1: Policy Discovery**

- List ALL information security policies in [Organization]
- Include Tier 1 (Master), Tier 2 (Domain), Tier 3 (Topic-Specific)
- Exclude Tier 4 (Standards/Procedures) - tracked separately

**Step 2: Metadata Collection**
For each policy, document:

**Q1: Policy_ID**

- **Question**: What is the unique policy identifier?
- **Format**: Organizational standard (e.g., ISMS-POL-A.5.1, POL-SEC-001)
- **Example**: "ISMS-POL-A.5.1-2-6.1-2", "POL-IAM-001"
- **Required**: YES
- **Tip**: Use consistent numbering scheme across all policies

**Q2: Policy_Title**

- **Question**: What is the official policy title?
- **Format**: Free text
- **Example**: "Secure Employment and Roles", "Cryptography Policy", "Access Control Policy"
- **Required**: YES
- **Tip**: Use title from policy document header

**Q3: Policy_Hierarchy_Tier**

- **Question**: Which hierarchy tier does this policy belong to?
- **Dropdown**: Tier 1 (Master) / Tier 2 (Domain) / Tier 3 (Topic-Specific)
- **Examples**:
  - Tier 1: "ISMS Master Policy"
  - Tier 2: "Data Protection Policy", "Access Control Policy"
  - Tier 3: "Cryptography Policy", "Backup Policy", "Incident Response Policy"
- **Required**: YES
- **Validation**: Tier 1 should have only 1-2 policies

**Q4: ISO_Control_Mapping**

- **Question**: Which ISO 27001:2022 Annex A controls does this policy address?
- **Format**: Free text, comma-separated
- **Examples**:
  - "A.5.1, A.5.2, A.6.1, A.6.2" (stacked control)
  - "A.8.24" (single control)
  - "A.5.15, A.5.16, A.5.18" (access control family)
- **Required**: YES (for Tier 3), OPTIONAL (for Tier 1/2)
- **Tip**: Check policy document Section 1 for control alignment

**Q5: Policy_Owner**

- **Question**: Who is accountable for this policy?
- **Format**: Free text - Name and Role
- **Examples**:
  - "Jane Smith (CISO)"
  - "HR Manager"
  - "Data Protection Officer"
- **Required**: YES
- **Note**: Policy owner is accountable for policy content, review, and updates

**Q6: Policy_Approver**

- **Question**: Who approves this policy?
- **Format**: Free text - Name and Role
- **Examples**:
  - Tier 1: "CEO" or "Executive Management"
  - Tier 2: "CISO + CIO" or "Executive Management"
  - Tier 3: "CISO"
- **Required**: YES
- **Validation**: Approver authority must match hierarchy tier

**Q7: Current_Version**

- **Question**: What is the current version number?
- **Format**: Numeric (e.g., 1.0, 2.1, 3.0)
- **Required**: YES
- **Tip**: Check policy document header

**Q8: Version_Date**

- **Question**: When was the current version effective?
- **Format**: Date (DD.MM.YYYY)
- **Required**: YES
- **Tip**: Version date = approval date for current version

**Q9: Approval_Date**

- **Question**: When was the current version approved?
- **Format**: Date (DD.MM.YYYY)
- **Required**: YES
- **Evidence**: Approval email, signature, workflow record

**Q10: Last_Review_Date**

- **Question**: When was this policy last reviewed?
- **Format**: Date (DD.MM.YYYY)
- **Required**: YES
- **Note**: Review may result in "no changes" - still counts as review

**Q11: Next_Review_Date**

- **Question**: When is the next review scheduled?
- **Format**: Date (DD.MM.YYYY)
- **Required**: YES
- **Calculation**: Typically Last_Review_Date + 12 months (annual) or + 24 months (biennial)
- **Status Trigger**: If Next_Review_Date < TODAY(), policy is OVERDUE

**Q12: Review_Frequency**

- **Question**: How often should this policy be reviewed?
- **Dropdown**: Annual / Biennial / Quarterly / Triggered-Only
- **Guidance**:
  - Annual: High-change policies (technology, regulatory)
  - Biennial: Stable policies (governance, organizational)
  - Quarterly: Policies tied to fast-changing regulations (data protection)
  - Triggered: Low-change policies reviewed only when changes occur
- **Required**: YES

**Q13: Policy_Status**

- **Question**: What is the current status?
- **Dropdown**: Active / Draft / Under-Review / Retired / Superseded
- **Definitions**:
  - Active: Published, in effect, current
  - Draft: Being developed, not yet approved
  - Under-Review: Being updated, current version still active
  - Retired: No longer in use, superseded by another policy
  - Superseded: Replaced by newer policy (specify replacement in notes)
- **Required**: YES
- **Validation**: Only "Active" policies count toward compliance

**Q14: Policy_Classification**

- **Question**: What is the document classification?
- **Dropdown**: Internal / Confidential / Public
- **Guidance**:
  - Internal: Default for most policies (employees only)
  - Confidential: Contains sensitive information (restricted distribution)
  - Public: May be shared externally (customer-facing, public web)
- **Required**: YES
- **Access Control**: Classification determines who can access policy

**Q15: Acknowledgment_Required**

- **Question**: Do users need to acknowledge this policy?
- **Dropdown**: Yes / No / Role-Specific
- **Guidance**:
  - Yes: All employees must acknowledge (Acceptable Use, Data Protection, Code of Conduct)
  - No: Reference policies, no acknowledgment needed
  - Role-Specific: Only certain roles acknowledge (e.g., developers acknowledge Secure Development policy)
- **Required**: YES
- **Impact**: If "Yes", acknowledgment rate tracked in Sheet 6

**Q16: Acknowledgment_Rate**

- **Question**: What % of required users have acknowledged?
- **Format**: Percentage (0-100%)
- **Required**: IF Acknowledgment_Required = "Yes"
- **Data Source**: Training system, LMS, acknowledgment logs
- **Benchmark**: ≥ 90% = Compliant, 70-89% = Partial, < 70% = Non-Compliant

**Q17: Repository_Location**

- **Question**: Where is this policy published?
- **Format**: Free text - URL or file path
- **Examples**:
  - "SharePoint: https://org.sharepoint.com/sites/isms/policies/"
  - "Confluence: /wiki/spaces/SECURITY/pages/policies"
  - "DMS: /Policies/Information_Security/"
  - "Intranet: https://intranet.org.com/policies"
- **Required**: YES
- **Validation**: Location must be accessible to intended audience

**Q18: Related_Documents**

- **Question**: What other policies or documents does this reference?
- **Format**: Free text, comma-separated
- **Examples**:
  - "ISMS-POL-00, ISMS-POL-A.8.24"
  - "Standards: STD-PWD-001, STD-ENC-002"
  - "Procedures: PROC-IAM-001"
- **Required**: OPTIONAL
- **Use**: Track policy dependencies

**Q19: Gap_Identified**

- **Question**: Does this policy have any compliance gaps?
- **Dropdown**: No-Gap / Minor-Gap / Significant-Gap / Critical-Gap
- **Definitions**:
  - No-Gap: Fully compliant
  - Minor-Gap: Small issues, routine remediation
  - Significant-Gap: Moderate issues, focused remediation
  - Critical-Gap: Major issues, immediate action

**Q20: Notes**

- **Question**: Any additional context?

- **Examples**:

  - Approval documented (signature, email, workflow)
  - Approval within authority scope
  - Approval not revoked or superseded
- **Evidence**: Approval email, signed document, workflow record
- **Compliant**: "Yes"

**Q5: Approval_Documentation**

- **Question**: Is approval properly documented?
- **Dropdown**: Complete / Incomplete / Missing
- **Evidence Types**:
  - Email approval from approver
  - Signed approval page in policy document
  - Workflow system approval record
  - Meeting minutes (for Board/Exec approvals)

**Q6: Publication_Status**

- **Question**: Is policy published and accessible?
- **Dropdown**: Published / Not-Published / Partially-Accessible
- **Verification**:
  - Policy available in official repository
  - Policy accessible to intended audience
  - Repository location documented
  - Policy version in repository is current
- **Evidence**: Repository screenshot, access test
- **Compliant**: "Published"

**Q7: Review_Schedule_Defined**

- **Question**: Is review schedule clearly defined?
- **Dropdown**: Yes / No
- **Verification**: Next_Review_Date populated in inventory (Sheet 2)
- **Compliant**: "Yes"

**Q8: Review_Status**

- **Question**: What is the review status?
- **Dropdown**: Current / Overdue-<30-Days / Overdue-30-90-Days / Overdue->90-Days
- **Calculation**: 
  - Current: Next_Review_Date ≥ TODAY()
  - Overdue-<30-Days: Next_Review_Date between (TODAY() - 30) and TODAY()
  - Overdue-30-90-Days: Next_Review_Date between (TODAY() - 90) and (TODAY() - 30)
  - Overdue->90-Days: Next_Review_Date < (TODAY() - 90)
- **Compliant**: "Current" or "Overdue-<30-Days"

**Q9: Last_Review_Evidence**

- **Question**: Is last review documented?
- **Dropdown**: Yes / Partial / No
- **Evidence Types**:
  - Review meeting minutes
  - Policy review checklist (completed)
  - Email confirmation of "no changes" review
  - Version history showing review date
- **Compliant**: "Yes"

**Q10: Version_Control_Practice**

- **Question**: Are version control practices followed?
- **Dropdown**: Excellent / Good / Adequate / Poor
- **Criteria**:
  - **Excellent**: Version history maintained, change log documented, previous versions archived
  - **Good**: Version numbering consistent, major changes documented
  - **Adequate**: Current version tracked, minimal history
  - **Poor**: Inconsistent versioning, no change tracking
- **Compliant**: "Excellent" or "Good"

**Q11: Sunset_Process**

- **Question**: For retired policies, was sunset process followed?
- **Dropdown**: N/A (Active) / Yes / No
- **Verification** (if policy retired):
  - Sunset decision documented
  - Stakeholders notified
  - References updated in other policies
  - Policy marked "Retired" in repository
- **Compliant**: "N/A" (for active policies) or "Yes" (for retired)

**Q12: Lifecycle_Compliance_Rating**

- **Question**: Overall lifecycle compliance for this policy?
- **Dropdown**: Compliant / Partial / Non-Compliant
- **Calculation**:
  - **Compliant**: All Q3-Q11 = compliant status, no critical issues
  - **Partial**: 1-2 minor issues (e.g., overdue <30 days, documentation incomplete)
  - **Non-Compliant**: Critical issues (no approval, overdue >90 days, not published)
- **Auto-Calculate**: Based on Q3-Q11 responses

**Q13: Gap_Description**

- **Question**: Describe any lifecycle gaps identified
- **Format**: Free text
- **Examples**:
  - "Policy overdue for review by 45 days"
  - "Approval documentation missing (policy approved verbally)"
  - "Version control inconsistent - previous versions not archived"
  - "Policy not yet published to repository"
- **Required**: IF Lifecycle_Compliance_Rating = "Partial" or "Non-Compliant"

**Q14: Evidence_Reference**

- **Question**: Where is supporting evidence documented?
- **Format**: Free text - Evidence IDs from Sheet 9
- **Example**: "EVD-001 (Approval email), EVD-015 (Repository screenshot)"
- **Required**: For all policies (link to evidence)

---

**Lifecycle Assessment Tips**:

1. **Prioritize approval verification**: Most critical lifecycle element - policies without valid approval are non-compliant

2. **Check overdue reviews**: Use TODAY() function to identify overdue policies automatically

3. **Document "no changes" reviews**: Even if policy unchanged, review MUST be documented

4. **Collect evidence proactively**: As you verify each element, immediately note evidence location

5. **Escalate critical gaps**: Policies without approval or significantly overdue (>90 days) are audit risks

# Review & Approval Process

## Assessment Completion Timeline

**Typical Timeline**:

- **Data Collection**: 3-4 hours (first assessment), 1-2 hours (quarterly update)
- **Gap Analysis**: 1 hour
- **Evidence Collection**: 1-2 hours (concurrent with data collection)
- **Self-Review**: 30 minutes
- **Level 1 Preparation**: 30 minutes
- **Level 2 Review**: 1-2 days
- **Level 3 Approval**: 2-3 days
- **Total**: 1-2 weeks from start to final approval

## Review Process

**Step 1: Self-Review** (Assessment Owner)

- Complete Quality Checklist (Section 6)
- Fix identified issues
- Verify all evidence accessible
- Set Status to "Draft"

**Step 2: Level 2 Technical Review** (Security Manager / Compliance Officer)

- Spot-check 10% of policies (metadata accuracy)
- Verify gap risk levels appropriate
- Check evidence quality and accessibility
- Provide feedback for improvements

**Step 3: Remediation** (Assessment Owner - if needed)

- Address review feedback
- Update assessment
- Re-submit if significant changes

**Step 4: Level 3 Final Approval** (CISO)

- Review overall compliance score and trends
- Approve remediation timelines
- Accept documented risks (low-priority gaps)
- Sign off assessment
- Set Next Review Date (90 days)

## After Approval

**Immediate Actions**:
1. **Distribute Assessment**: Email to stakeholders (policy owners, compliance, audit)
2. **Update Repository**: Store approved assessment in ISMS document repository
3. **Track Action Items**: Import Sheet 10 to project tracking system (Jira, ServiceNow, etc.)
4. **Schedule Follow-Ups**: Calendar reminders for action item check-ins

**Ongoing Monitoring**:

- **Weekly**: Action item progress updates (Sheet 10)
- **Monthly**: Action item escalation review
- **Quarterly**: Next assessment (90 days)
- **Annual**: Comprehensive policy framework review

## Triggers for Immediate Re-Assessment

Re-assess policy framework outside scheduled cycle if:

- Major organizational change (restructure, acquisition, significant growth)
- New regulations effective (requiring new policies)
- Significant audit findings on policy framework
- Executive management request
- Regulatory examination imminent
