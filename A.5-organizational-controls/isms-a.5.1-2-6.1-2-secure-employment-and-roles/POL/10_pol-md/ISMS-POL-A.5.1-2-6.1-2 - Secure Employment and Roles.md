## Executive Summary

This policy establishes [Organization]'s requirements for information security governance, organizational structure, personnel screening, and employment security in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Controls A.5.1, A.5.2, A.6.1, and A.6.2.

**Scope**: All personnel (employees, contractors, temporary workers, interns), all information security policies, all organizational security roles, all pre-employment screening activities, and all employment contracts.

**Purpose**: Define organizational requirements for:

- Information security policy governance (A.5.1)
- Security role definition and accountability (A.5.2)
- Personnel screening requirements (A.6.1)
- Contractual security obligations (A.6.2)

This policy establishes WHAT governance structures are required, WHO holds security responsibilities, and WHEN reviews occur. Implementation procedures (HOW) are documented in ISMS-IMP-A.5.1-2-6.1-2.S1 through S5.

**Combined Control Framework**: These four controls form the foundational "people layer" of the ISMS:
1. **Policy Foundation (A.5.1)**: Governance rules and policy hierarchy
2. **Role Structure (A.5.2)**: Organizational security accountability
3. **Pre-Employment Vetting (A.6.1)**: Personnel screening before access
4. **Contractual Binding (A.6.2)**: Legally binding security obligations

**Statement of Applicability Independence**: Although implemented as a unified framework, A.5.1, A.5.2, A.6.1, and A.6.2 are assessed independently in the Statement of Applicability with distinct requirements and evidence collection.

# Scope and Applicability

## ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Control Statements

**Control A.5.1 - Policies for Information Security**
> *Policies for information security and topic-specific policies should be defined, approved by management, published, communicated to and acknowledged by relevant personnel and relevant interested parties, and reviewed at planned intervals and if significant changes occur.*

**Control A.5.2 - Information Security Roles and Responsibilities**
> *Information security roles and responsibilities should be defined and allocated according to the organization needs.*

**Control A.6.1 - Screening**
> *Background verification checks on all candidates to become personnel should be carried out prior to joining the organization and on an ongoing basis taking into consideration applicable laws, regulations and ethics and should be proportional to the business requirements, the classification of the information to be accessed and the perceived risks.*

**Control A.6.2 - Terms and Conditions of Employment**
> *The employment contractual agreements should state the personnel's and the organization's responsibilities for information security.*

# Policy Statements: Information Security Policies (A.5.1)

## Policy Hierarchy Requirements

[Organization] SHALL maintain a hierarchical information security policy framework:

| Tier | Type | Approval Authority | Review Frequency |
|------|------|-------------------|------------------|
| 1 | Master Information Security Policy | Executive Management (CEO/Board) | Annual |
| 2 | Topic-Specific Policies (Annex A) | CISO | Annual |
| 3 | Standards and Guidelines | Security Team Lead | Quarterly |
| 4 | Procedures and Work Instructions | Process Owner | Semi-annual |

**Policy Hierarchy Principles**:

- Higher-tier policies set requirements; lower-tier documents provide implementation
- Lower-tier documents SHALL NOT contradict higher-tier policies
- All tiers SHALL be accessible to relevant personnel

## Policy Lifecycle Requirements

[Organization] SHALL manage information security policies through defined lifecycle stages:

**Creation**: Policies developed using organizational template, reviewed by stakeholders (Legal, HR, IT, affected business units).

**Approval**: Policies approved by appropriate authority per tier before publication.

**Publication**: Policies published to organizational policy repository with version control.

**Communication**: Policies communicated to all affected personnel; supplemental training provided for complex policies.

**Complex Policy Definition**: Policies introducing new technical controls, significantly changing user workflows, or addressing new regulatory requirements. Policy owner SHALL determine training need in consultation with CISO. Training formats include: webinar, workshop, e-learning module, or annotated policy guide.

**Acknowledgment**: Critical policies (Master Policy, AUP, Code of Conduct) require personnel acknowledgment annually. Acknowledgments SHALL be collected via [electronic signature in policy portal / attestation form in HR system] and tracked in the Personnel Training and Acknowledgment Register (maintained by HR). The register SHALL record: policy name, version, personnel name, acknowledgment date, and signature/attestation method. Acknowledgment compliance is reported quarterly to ISMS Committee via IMP-S5 Governance Dashboard.

**Review**: Policies reviewed at defined intervals or upon:

- Significant regulatory change (defined as: new applicable regulation per Tier 1/2 in POL-00, material amendments to existing regulation, regulatory guidance affecting control implementation, court rulings impacting compliance interpretation, or regulatory enforcement action in industry)
- Major security incident (Severity 1-2 per A.5.24-27)
- Organizational change (merger, acquisition, significant restructure)
- Failed audit finding requiring policy amendment

**Retirement**: Retired policies archived with audit trail retained.

## Policy Content Requirements

All topic-specific policies (Tier 2) SHALL include:

- Document Control (ID, version, approval, dates)
- Executive Summary (purpose, scope)
- Control Alignment (ISO controls addressed)
- Requirements Framework (WHAT/WHO/WHEN)
- Roles and Responsibilities
- Regulatory Framework reference
- Implementation guidance references

## Policy Exceptions

Policy exceptions SHALL be:

- Documented with business justification
- Risk-assessed with compensating controls identified
- Approved by CISO (medium risk) or Executive Management (high risk)
- Time-limited with defined review dates (maximum 12 months for medium risk, 6 months for high risk)
- Tracked in Policy and Control Exception Register

**Policy and Control Exception Register** (maintained by CISO in [GRC platform / shared drive with controlled access]) SHALL record:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Exception ID | Unique identifier |
| Control/Policy ID | Affected control or policy |
| Exception Description | What is being excepted |
| Requestor | Who requested the exception |
| Business Justification | Why exception is necessary |
| Risk Assessment | Likelihood/impact analysis |
| Compensating Controls | Alternative controls in place |
| Approval Authority | CISO or Executive Management |
| Approval Date | When approved |
| Expiry Date | When exception must be renewed or closed |
| Review Status | Current, Due for Review, Expired |

Exceptions are reviewed quarterly by ISMS Committee and reported in Management Review. Expired exceptions SHALL be re-assessed or closed within 30 days.

## Policy Violation Handling

Policy violations SHALL be classified by severity using the organizational Severity Rating Framework (aligned with A.5.24-27 incident management):

| Severity | Criteria | Investigation | Response |
|----------|----------|---------------|----------|
| **Severity 1 (Critical)** | Intentional misconduct, data breach, regulatory violation, significant business impact | Executive Management investigation within 24 hours | Disciplinary action up to immediate termination; legal/regulatory reporting if required; root cause analysis mandatory |
| **Severity 2 (High)** | Repeated violations, deliberate circumvention of controls, moderate business impact | CISO investigation within 3 business days | Formal disciplinary action (written warning minimum); mandatory retraining; monitoring for 90 days |
| **Severity 3 (Medium)** | Unintentional non-compliance, isolated incident, minor business impact | Manager investigation within 5 business days | Documented coaching; corrective action plan; follow-up in 30 days |
| **Severity 4 (Low)** | Procedural error, no security impact, first occurrence | Manager-led discussion | Policy clarification; no formal disciplinary action unless recurring |

All violations (Severity 1-3) SHALL be logged in the Security Incident Register (maintained by Security Team) with investigation findings, remediation actions, and closure date. Violations are analyzed quarterly by ISMS Committee for trends and policy effectiveness improvement.

# Policy Statements: Roles and Responsibilities (A.5.2)

## Organizational Security Structure Requirements

[Organization] SHALL establish a clear security organizational structure with:

- **Clear Accountability**: Every security responsibility assigned to a specific role
- **Segregation of Duties**: Separation between policy definition, execution, and oversight
- **CISO Independence**: CISO reports to executive level (CEO/COO/CTO)
- **Adequate Resources**: Security roles provided with budget, tools, personnel, and training commensurate with organizational size, complexity, and risk profile

**Resource Adequacy Assessment**: CISO SHALL assess resource adequacy annually as part of budget planning. Assessment SHALL document:

- Current Security Team headcount vs. industry benchmark (suggested: 1 security FTE per 100-200 employees)
- Security tool budget (SIEM, vulnerability management, GRC platform) vs. requirements identified in A.8.1-34 controls
- Training budget per security role (minimum CHF 2,000/year for professional development)
- Open security positions with recruitment status and expected fill date

Resource adequacy assessment SHALL be submitted to Executive Management with annual budget request. Assessment results are documented in IMP-S2 and reviewed in Management Review. Resource gaps SHALL be tracked in ISMS Gap Register with remediation plan
- **Commensurate Authority**: Security roles granted authority matching responsibility

## Key Security Roles

[Organization] SHALL define and assign the following security roles:

**Executive Management (CEO/Board)**:

- Ultimate accountability for ISMS and regulatory compliance
- Approves Master Information Security Policy and risk appetite
- Allocates resources for information security program
- Reviews ISMS performance annually

**Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)**:

- Accountable for ISMS design, implementation, and continuous improvement
- Approves topic-specific policies (Tier 2)
- Oversees Security Team, incident response, and compliance
- Reports ISMS performance to Executive Management

**Data Protection Officer (DPO)**:

- Accountable for GDPR/nDSG compliance
- Reviews policies for data protection compliance
- Independent reporting to Executive Management
- Contact point for data subjects and supervisory authorities

**Information Security Team**:

- Operational implementation of security controls
- Security monitoring, incident response, assessments
- Security awareness training delivery
- Audit support and evidence collection

**System Owners**:

- Accountable for security of assigned systems
- Implement controls, maintain compliance, conduct access reviews
- Report incidents affecting assigned systems

**Data Owners**:

- Accountable for classification and protection of assigned data
- Define and approve access requirements
- Conduct data access reviews

**Line Managers**:

- Ensure direct reports complete training and comply with policies
- Initiate joiner/mover/leaver processes
- Approve access requests (business justification)

**All Personnel**:

- Comply with policies, complete training, protect assets
- Report security incidents immediately
- Maintain confidentiality

## Role Documentation Requirements

All security-related roles SHALL be documented in Role Description Documents maintained in:

- **HR system**: Employee roles (CISO, Security Team, All Personnel)
- **GRC platform**: Control roles (System Owners, Data Owners)
- **Policy Appendix**: Executive roles (Executive Management)

**Minimum Role Description Document Elements**:

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Role title and purpose | 1-2 sentences describing the role |
| Key responsibilities | Specific, measurable (typically 5-10 items) |
| Required qualifications | Education, certifications, experience |
| Authority and decision rights | What role can approve/decide independently |
| Reporting structure | Reports to, supervises |
| Performance metrics | See Performance Metrics section below |
| Review date | Annual minimum |

**Performance Metrics by Role** (example metrics):

| Role | Performance Metrics |
|------|---------------------|
| **CISO** | ISMS audit findings closed within SLA (≥95%), policy review currency (≥95%), security awareness training completion (≥95%), Management Review completion (annual, 100%) |
| **Security Team** | Vulnerability remediation within SLA (per A.8.8), incident investigation closure time (per A.5.26 SLA), control effectiveness test completion (quarterly, 100%) |
| **System Owners** | System-specific control compliance (≥90%), access review completion (quarterly, 100%), incident reporting timeliness (immediate per A.6.8, 100%) |
| **Data Owners** | Data classification accuracy (≥95%), access rights approval timeliness (<2 business days, ≥90%), data access review completion (quarterly, 100%) |

Role descriptions SHALL be reviewed annually by role owner's manager in coordination with CISO. Updates SHALL be version-controlled and communicated to affected personnel

## Third-Party Security Responsibilities

Third-party security responsibilities SHALL be:

- Defined in service agreements and contracts
- Proportional to access granted
- Subject to periodic review (at minimum annually)
- Managed by contract owner in coordination with Security Team

**Third-Party Verification (Spot-Checking)**:

Spot-checking SHALL include annual sample of 10% of vendor-provided personnel (minimum 2 individuals per critical vendor) verifying:

- Screening completion (per contractual requirements)
- Security training completion
- Contract clause acknowledgment (NDA, AUP)

Spot-check results SHALL be documented in the Third-Party Risk Assessment Register (maintained by Security Team per A.5.19-23). Non-compliance SHALL trigger vendor escalation and remediation within 30 days. Persistent non-compliance (>60 days unresolved) triggers contract review per A.5.20

## Succession Planning

Key security roles (CISO, Security Team Lead, DPO) SHALL have documented succession plans to ensure continuity during unplanned absences (illness, departure) or planned transitions.

**Security Role Succession Plan** (maintained by CISO, reviewed by CEO annually) SHALL include for each key role:

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Primary role** | Role title and current incumbent |
| **Interim Successor 1** | Name, current role, readiness level (ready now / ready with 1-3 months training) |
| **Interim Successor 2** | Backup if Successor 1 unavailable |
| **Notification procedure** | Who notifies successors upon unplanned absence (e.g., CEO notifies CISO Successor 1 within 4 hours) |
| **Knowledge transfer checklist** | Critical systems (admin access), ongoing projects (handover notes), key contacts (vendors, auditors, regulators) |
| **Cross-training schedule** | Successors shadow primary role (minimum quarterly half-day sessions), attend critical meetings (ISMS Committee, Management Review) |
| **Review date** | Annual review by CISO and CEO |

**Succession Plan Testing**: Plans tested biennially via tabletop exercise simulating key role unplanned absence. Exercise results documented with lessons learned and plan updates.

**Emergency Succession Activation**: If primary role becomes unavailable (<24 hours notice):

1. Interim Successor 1 assumes role immediately with CEO approval
2. Formal succession (>30 days expected absence) requires Executive Management approval
3. Communication to ISMS Committee, Internal Audit, and external auditors (if during audit period)

# Policy Statements: Screening (A.6.1)

## Screening Principles

[Organization] SHALL conduct background verification checks based on:

**Proportionality**: Screening intensity proportional to:

- Sensitivity of information accessed (per data classification)
- Criticality of role (impact of compromise)
- Level of system access and privileges
- Regulatory requirements

**Legality**: All screening complies with:

- Swiss Code of Obligations (OR) Art. 328, 328b
- Swiss Federal Data Protection Act (nDSG/FADP)
- EU GDPR Art. 88 (where applicable)
- Local employment law in each jurisdiction

**Fairness**: Screening conducted transparently with:

- Candidate informed before conducting
- Candidate consent obtained where required
- Non-discriminatory, job-relevant criteria
- Opportunity to clarify adverse findings

**Confidentiality**: Screening results:

- Accessed only by HR, hiring manager, authorized personnel
- Stored securely with access controls
- Retained per legal requirements
- Disposed securely when no longer needed

## Screening Levels

[Organization] SHALL classify roles by sensitivity and apply appropriate screening:

| Level | Applicable To | Verification Required |
|-------|--------------|----------------------|
| **Level 1: Basic** | All personnel | Identity, right to work, recent employer reference, education |
| **Level 2: Standard** | Roles with Internal data/system access | Level 1 + extended employment history (5 years), professional references |
| **Level 3: Enhanced** | Privileged access, Confidential/Restricted data, Security Team, Finance | Level 2 + criminal record check, credit check (if financial role), adverse media |
| **Level 4: Security Clearance** | CISO, highest sensitivity roles | Level 3 + extended background investigation, character references |

Role-to-screening-level mapping documented in ISMS-IMP-A.5.1-2-6.1-2.S3.

## Screening Timing Requirements

**Pre-Employment**: Screening SHALL be completed BEFORE granting organizational access.

**Interim Access Exception**: Emergency access before screening complete requires:

- Manager and CISO approval
- Compensating controls (close supervision, limited access)
- Maximum 30 days duration
- Documented business justification

**Ongoing Screening**:

- Level 4 roles: Re-screening every 2-3 years
- Privileged access roles: Re-screening every 5 years or upon significant role change
- All roles: Right to work verification (permit renewals)

## Adverse Findings Handling

Adverse findings SHALL be:

- Discussed with candidate (opportunity to clarify)
- Assessed for relevance, recency, and severity
- Documented with decision rationale
- Handled consistently and non-discriminatorily

## Third-Party Personnel Screening

Third-party personnel screening SHALL be:

- Specified in contracts (minimum screening requirements)
- Attested by vendor (vendor-provided personnel)
- Spot-checked by organization (sample verification)
- Equivalent to employee screening for comparable access

# Policy Statements: Terms and Conditions of Employment (A.6.2)

## Contractual Security Obligations

[Organization] SHALL include information security obligations in all employment-related contracts:

- Employment contracts (employees)
- Contractor agreements
- Vendor agreements (for on-site/access personnel)
- Internship agreements

## Mandatory Contract Clauses

All employment contracts SHALL include:

**Confidentiality and Data Protection**:

- Obligation to maintain confidentiality of organizational information
- Compliance with data protection policies and laws
- Obligations continue after termination

**Acceptable Use Acknowledgment**:

- Agreement to comply with Acceptable Use Policy
- Acknowledgment of monitoring rights
- Understanding of prohibited activities

**Security Training Obligation**:

- Requirement to complete mandatory security awareness training
- Timeline for initial and annual refresher training

**Incident Reporting Obligation**:

- Requirement to report security incidents immediately
- Cooperation with investigations

**Access Rights and Termination**:

- Access granted at organization's discretion, revocable at any time
- Return of all organizational assets upon termination
- Post-termination access prohibition

**Disciplinary Action**:

- Security policy violations subject to disciplinary action
- Serious violations may result in immediate termination

## Additional Clauses for Sensitive Roles

**Privileged Access Roles**: Enhanced background check consent, conflict of interest declaration, ongoing monitoring acknowledgment.

**Roles with Restricted Data**: Special handling requirements, elevated breach notification, regulatory awareness.

**Executive Management**: Leadership commitment, governance accountability, resource allocation responsibility.

## Contract Execution Requirements

**Timing**: Contracts SHALL be signed BEFORE access granted:

- Employment contract before first day
- NDA before access to confidential information
- AUP acknowledged before system access

**Acknowledgment Tracking**: Acknowledgments (NDA, AUP, training) SHALL be:

- Collected with signature (physical or electronic)
- Tracked in HR system
- Re-collected annually for critical policies

## Enforcement

Contractual obligations SHALL be:

- Monitored through technical controls and HR processes
- Enforced through disciplinary framework
- Documented when violations occur
- Applied consistently across personnel

# Roles and Responsibilities (Policy Governance)

## Accountability Matrix

| Role | Accountability |
|------|---------------|
| **Executive Management** | Ultimate accountability for personnel security and policy framework |
| **CISO** | Policy framework maintenance, roles definition, screening requirements, contract security clauses |
| **DPO** | Review policies and screening for data protection compliance |
| **CHRO** | Employment contract execution, screening operations, HR process integration |
| **HR Team** | Execute screening, manage contracts, track training/acknowledgments |
| **Legal/Compliance** | Review policies for legal compliance, contract enforceability |
| **Line Managers** | Ensure direct reports comply, initiate joiner/mover/leaver processes |
| **All Personnel** | Comply with policies, complete training, maintain confidentiality |

## Governance Oversight

**Quarterly**: ISMS Committee reviews policy changes, compliance metrics, exceptions.

**Annual**: Management Review includes policy framework assessment, roles effectiveness, screening compliance, contract completeness.

## HR Process Integration

Security requirements SHALL be integrated throughout the employee lifecycle:

**Joiner Process**: Job posting security requirements, screening, contract signing, onboarding security training, policy acknowledgment.

**Mover Process**: Security impact assessment, enhanced screening if needed, access adjustment, role-specific training.

**Leaver Process**: Access deprovisioning, asset return, exit interview reminding of post-termination obligations.
