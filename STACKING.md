## Why Group Controls?

<p align="center">
<img src="https://img.shields.io/badge/Industry_Standard-20--25_Policies-9400D3?style=for-the-badge" alt="Industry Standard"/>
</p>

ISO 27001 allows grouping multiple controls into single policy/implementation documents. Industry practice shows organizations typically maintain **20-25 policy documents** covering all 93 Annex A controls.

---

## Our Approach: Domain-Based Stacking

<p>
<img src="https://img.shields.io/badge/Current-Domain_Based-00AA00?style=flat-square" alt="Domain Based"/>
<img src="https://img.shields.io/badge/42_Groups-Complete-0066CC?style=flat-square" alt="42 Groups"/>
</p>

Controls grouped by **functional similarity**:

| Stack | Controls | Domain |
|-------|----------|--------|
| A.5.1-2, 6.1-2 | 4 controls | HR Security & Roles |
| A.5.15-16-18 | 3 controls | Identity & Access |
| A.5.19-23 | 5 controls | Cloud & Suppliers |
| A.5.24-28 | 5 controls | Incident Management |
| A.8.1-7-18-19 | 4 controls | Endpoint Security |
| A.8.20-22 | 3 controls | Network Security |

## Alternative: NIST CSF Alignment

<p>
<img src="https://img.shields.io/badge/Alternative-NIST_CSF_2.0-FF6600?style=flat-square" alt="NIST CSF"/>
<img src="https://img.shields.io/badge/6_Functions-Lifecycle-0066CC?style=flat-square" alt="6 Functions"/>
</p>

NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 groups by **lifecycle phase**:

| Function | Code | ISO 27001 Controls | Purpose |
|----------|------|-------------------|---------|
| **GOVERN** | GV | A.5.1-4, A.5.31, A.5.35-36 | Strategy & governance |
| **IDENTIFY** | ID | A.5.7-13 | Asset & risk mgmt |
| **PROTECT** | PR | A.5.15-18, A.6.x, A.7.x, A.8.1-12, A.8.24-32 | Safeguards |
| **DETECT** | DE | A.8.8, A.8.15-17 | Monitoring |
| **RESPOND** | RS | A.5.24-28, A.6.4-5, A.6.8 | Incident response |
| **RECOVER** | RC | A.5.29-30, A.8.13-14 | Continuity |
