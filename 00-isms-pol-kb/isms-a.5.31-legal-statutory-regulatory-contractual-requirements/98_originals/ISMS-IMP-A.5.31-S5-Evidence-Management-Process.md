# ISMS-IMP-A.5.31-S5: Evidence Management Process
## SKELETON WITH WRITING INSTRUCTIONS

**Status**: SKELETON - Ready for content development  
**Target Length**: 10-12 pages  
**Purpose**: Step-by-step guide for systematically managing compliance evidence for audit readiness

**Related Policy**: ISMS-POL-A.5.31-S4 (Change Management & Evidence Framework)

---

## SECTION STRUCTURE:

1. **Process Overview** (1-2 pages)
   - Purpose, when used, who performs, output
   - Process flowchart: Identify → Collect → Organize → Verify → Maintain → Present
   - Timeline and key artifacts
   - Evidence lifecycle diagram

2. **Step 1 - Defining Evidence Requirements** (2-3 pages)
   - For each regulation in POL-00, determine evidence needs
   - For each control mapped to requirements, determine evidence type
   - Using evidence requirements matrix (Regulation × Control → Evidence Type)
   - Examples of evidence requirements per regulation type
   - Examples of evidence requirements per control type

3. **Step 2 - Evidence Collection** (2 pages)
   - Sources of evidence (document systems, logs, scans, reports, etc.)
   - Collection frequency (continuous, periodic, point-in-time, event-driven)
   - Collection procedures for each evidence type
   - Metadata capture (what info to record about each evidence item)

4. **Step 3 - Evidence Organization & Storage** (2-3 pages)
   - Setting up centralized evidence repository
   - Folder structure design (by regulation, by control, by type - show examples)
   - Naming conventions for files
   - Metadata tagging for searchability
   - Access controls setup
   - Audit trail configuration

5. **Step 4 - Evidence Verification & Validation** (2 pages)
   - Verification process (Control Owner verifies)
   - Validation process (Compliance/Audit validates)
   - Verification criteria: Authenticity, Completeness, Currency, Accessibility
   - Verification records and documentation
   - Re-verification schedule

6. **Step 5 - Evidence Maintenance & Refresh** (1-2 pages)
   - Refresh cycles for time-sensitive evidence
   - Alerts for expiring evidence
   - Evidence gap identification and remediation
   - Retention management
   - Secure disposal procedures

7. **Step 6 - Audit Readiness Preparation** (1-2 pages)
   - Pre-audit evidence review
   - Assembling evidence packages per regulation
   - Providing evidence index to auditors
   - Evidence presentation procedures
   - Post-audit improvements

8. **Example Evidence Management Scenarios** (1-2 pages)
   - 3-4 examples showing evidence lifecycle for different evidence types
   - Generic examples

9. **Templates & Tools** (1 page)
   - Evidence Register template (Workbook 5)
   - Evidence verification checklist
   - Audit evidence package template

10. **Quality Checks & Common Pitfalls** (1 page)

11. **Related Documents & References** (0.5 page)

**Total: 10-12 pages**

---

## KEY WRITING NOTES FOR IMP-S5:

- **This is about AUDIT READINESS** - emphasize throughout
- Evidence is what proves compliance - critical importance
- Practical guidance on evidence repository setup
- Show different evidence types and their specific requirements
- Verification is key - can't just collect and forget
- Examples should cover various evidence types (policy, log, report, certificate, etc.)
- Make it scalable and sustainable (not heroic manual effort)
