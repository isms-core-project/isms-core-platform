# ISMS-IMP-A.5.31-S3: Control Mapping Process
## SKELETON WITH WRITING INSTRUCTIONS

**Status**: SKELETON - Ready for content development  
**Target Length**: 10-12 pages  
**Purpose**: Step-by-step guide for mapping requirements to ISO 27001 controls and identifying gaps

**Related Policy**: ISMS-POL-A.5.31-S3 (Requirements Mapping Framework)

---

## SECTION STRUCTURE:

1. **Process Overview** (1-2 pages)
   - Purpose, when used, who performs, output
   - Process flowchart: Requirements → Analyze → Map → Identify Gaps → Document
   - Timeline and key artifacts

2. **Step 1 - Requirements Analysis** (1-2 pages)
   - Understanding what requirement mandates
   - Breaking down complex requirements
   - Identifying technical vs. organizational vs. operational nature

3. **Step 2 - ISO 27001 Control Identification** (2-3 pages)
   - Systematic review of Annex A controls (all 93)
   - Identifying Primary, Secondary, Supporting controls
   - Using control descriptions to match requirements
   - Quick reference: Common requirement types → typical controls

4. **Step 3 - Control Mapping Matrix Population** (2 pages)
   - How to use the mapping matrix (Workbook 4)
   - Marking cells: P, S, Su, (blank)
   - Handling one-to-many and many-to-one mappings
   - Version control

5. **Step 4 - Gap Analysis** (2-3 pages)
   - Identifying complete gaps (no P/S/Su for requirement)
   - Identifying partial gaps
   - Identifying implementation gaps
   - Gap prioritization (High/Med/Low based on risk and tier)
   - Gap remediation approaches: new control, enhance existing, compensating, risk accept

6. **Step 5 - Beyond Annex A Controls** (1-2 pages)
   - When no Annex A control fits
   - Creating organization-specific controls (CTRL-ORG-###)
   - Documenting new controls
   - Integrating with ISMS

7. **Step 6 - Evidence Mapping** (1 page)
   - For each requirement-control mapping, identify evidence type needed
   - Link to Evidence Register (Workbook 5)

8. **Step 7 - Review & Approval** (1 page)
   - Mapping completeness review
   - Gap validation
   - Approval workflow

9. **Example Control Mappings** (2-3 pages)
   - 4-5 worked examples showing different mapping scenarios
   - Generic examples only

10. **Templates & Tools** (1 page)
    - Reference to Workbook 4 (Control Mapping Matrix)
    - Quick reference guides

11. **Quality Checks & Common Pitfalls** (1 page)

12. **Related Documents & References** (0.5 page)

**Total: 10-12 pages**

---

## KEY WRITING NOTES FOR IMP-S3:

- **This is the most complex IMP section** - mapping is an art and science
- Provide clear decision criteria for P vs. S vs. Su
- Examples are critical - show actual mapping decisions
- Emphasize traceability (requirement → control → evidence)
- Reference all 93 Annex A controls somewhere (maybe as appendix quick reference)
- Make gap analysis systematic and risk-based
- Templates must be practical and usable
