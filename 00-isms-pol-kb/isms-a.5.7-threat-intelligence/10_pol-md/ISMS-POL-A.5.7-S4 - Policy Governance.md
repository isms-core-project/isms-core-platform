## 4.1 Policy Review and Maintenance

### 4.1.1 Review Cycle

This threat intelligence policy framework SHALL be reviewed according to the following schedule:

**Annual Review (Mandatory)**:
- Comprehensive review of all policy sections (S1-S5)
- Assessment of policy effectiveness and relevance
- Alignment check with current threat landscape and organizational changes
- Compliance verification with updated regulations and standards
- Stakeholder feedback incorporation
- **Scheduled for: Q4 (October-November) of each calendar year**
- **Evidence review**: Verify all audit evidence requirements met (per ISMS-POL-A.5.7 Section 4.4)
- **KPI targets review**: Assess appropriateness of targets, adjust if needed with justification

**Quarterly Light Review**:
- **Metrics-only review** conducted during quarterly CISO briefings
- KPI performance against targets (per ISMS-POL-A.5.7 Section 4.6)
- Trend analysis and gap identification
- No policy text changes unless urgent
- Immediate issues escalated to full review if needed

**Triggered Reviews**:

The policy SHALL be reviewed immediately upon:
- **Major regulatory changes**: New or updated regulations affecting threat intelligence (NIS2, DORA, data protection laws, etc.)
- **Significant organizational changes**: Mergers, acquisitions, major business model shifts, geographic expansion
- **Major CVSS version updates**: e.g., CVSS 5.0 release requiring framework assessment
- **Threat landscape shifts**: Emergence of fundamentally new threat categories or attack paradigms
- **Major security incidents**: Incidents revealing threat intelligence gaps or policy deficiencies
- **Technology changes**: Adoption of new threat intelligence platforms or major security architecture changes
- **Audit findings**: External or internal audit recommendations requiring policy updates

**Review Responsibilities**:
- **Policy Owner (CISO)**: Overall review accountability, final approval
- **Threat Intelligence Coordinator**: Technical review, gap identification, recommendation development, **audit evidence validation**
- **Legal/Compliance**: Regulatory compliance review
- **Risk Management (CRO)**: Risk alignment review, **Clause 6.1 integration effectiveness**
- **IT Management**: Operational feasibility review
- **Stakeholder Representatives**: User feedback and effectiveness assessment

### 4.1.2 Review Process

Policy review SHALL follow this structured process:

**Step 1: Review Initiation**
- Review scheduled or triggered event identified
- Review team assembled (core: CISO, TI Coordinator, Legal, Risk)
- Review scope and objectives defined
- Review timeline established (typically 30-60 days for annual review)

**Step 2: Current State Assessment**
- **Policy effectiveness metrics analyzed** against targets (per ISMS-POL-A.5.7 Section 4.6)
- Stakeholder feedback collected and summarized
- Compliance gap analysis conducted
- **Audit evidence completeness review** (per ISMS-POL-A.5.7 Section 4.4)
- Industry best practice benchmarking performed
- Threat landscape evolution assessed

**Step 3: Gap Analysis**
- Policy deficiencies identified
- Regulatory compliance gaps documented
- **Evidence generation gaps** identified
- Operational challenges catalogued
- Stakeholder pain points compiled
- Improvement opportunities prioritized

**Step 4: Revision Development**
- Policy updates drafted by Threat Intelligence Coordinator
- Legal/Compliance review conducted
- Risk impact assessment performed
- Stakeholder consultation completed
- Revision rationale documented

**Step 5: Approval and Communication**
- Draft revisions reviewed by Policy Owner (CISO)
- Formal approval obtained from required stakeholders
- Version control updated
- Changes communicated to affected parties
- Training updates scheduled where necessary

**Step 6: Implementation and Monitoring**
- Updated policy published to document management system
- Implementation tracked
- Effectiveness of changes monitored
- Lessons learned captured

### 4.1.3 Version Control and Documentation

All policy versions SHALL be managed according to the following standards:

**Versioning Scheme**:
- **Major version (X.0)**: Structural changes, scope modifications, new regulatory requirements, major evidence requirement changes
- **Minor version (X.Y)**: Content clarifications, additions, corrections without scope change
- **Draft versions**: Labeled as "Draft" until formal approval

**Document Retention**:
- Current approved version: Indefinite (active)
- Previous versions: Archived with full change history for minimum 7 years
- Draft versions: Retained during development, archived after approval

**Change Documentation**:
- All changes documented in Document Control table
- Change rationale maintained in policy development records
- Stakeholder approvals retained
- Communication records archived

## 4.2 Change Management

### 4.2.1 Change Request Process

Proposed changes to the threat intelligence policy SHALL follow a formal change request process:

**Change Request Initiation**:
- Any stakeholder may propose policy changes
- Change requests submitted to Policy Owner (CISO) via formal change request form
- Change request SHALL include:
  - Description of proposed change
  - Justification and business need
  - Impact assessment (operational, resource, compliance, evidence generation)
  - Affected stakeholders
  - Proposed implementation timeline
  - Risk analysis if change not implemented

**Change Evaluation**:
- Policy Owner (CISO) or designee evaluates change request
- Consultation with relevant stakeholders (Legal, Risk, TI Coordinator, etc.)
- Impact and feasibility assessment conducted
- Priority assigned based on urgency, impact, and resource requirements

**Change Decision**:
- **Approved**: Change proceeds to revision development
- **Deferred**: Change scheduled for future consideration (e.g., next annual review)
- **Rejected**: Change not pursued; rationale documented and communicated to requestor

**Change Implementation**:
- Approved changes incorporated through review process (Section 4.1.2)
- Emergency changes may follow expedited approval with post-implementation review

### 4.2.2 Emergency Change Process

Critical policy changes required to address immediate threats or regulatory requirements MAY follow an expedited process:

**Emergency Criteria**:
- Immediate regulatory compliance requirement with legal penalties
- Critical threat intelligence gap enabling active exploitation
- Major security incident requiring immediate policy adjustment
- Executive directive based on imminent organizational risk

**Expedited Process**:
- Emergency change request to CISO with justification
- Abbreviated stakeholder review (24-48 hours)
- CISO approval authority for emergency changes
- Implementation authorization
- **Post-implementation full review required within 30 days**

**Documentation**:
- Emergency changes fully documented
- Rationale for expedited process recorded
- Post-implementation review validates change appropriateness
- Permanent policy update or rollback determined

## 4.3 Compliance Monitoring and Enforcement

### 4.3.1 Compliance Monitoring

The organization SHALL monitor compliance with threat intelligence policy requirements through:

**Ongoing Monitoring**:
- Threat intelligence program metrics reviewed monthly
- Quality assurance checks on intelligence products
- **Source reliability assessments (quarterly)** per ISMS-POL-A.5.7 Section 4.4.3
- Integration effectiveness with security operations
- Timeliness of intelligence delivery measured
- **Evidence generation tracking** per ISMS-POL-A.5.7 Section 4.4

**Periodic Assessments**:
- **Quarterly**: Compliance self-assessments by Threat Intelligence Coordinator
  - KPI review against targets
  - Evidence completeness check (Sections 4.4.1-4.4.6)
  - Assessment workbook generation (ISMS-IMP-A.5.7.1 through A.5.7.4)
  - Sanity check script validation
- **Semi-annual**: CISO review of program compliance and effectiveness
- **Annual**: Comprehensive compliance audit

**Audit Activities**:
- Internal audits by ISMS audit function or independent team
- External audits by certification body (ISO 27001) or third-party assessors
- Regulatory audits where applicable (NIS2, DORA, etc.)
- **Audit preparation** per ISMS-POL-A.5.7 Section 4.5 (30 days before audit)

**Compliance Metrics (Evidence-Based per ISMS-POL-A.5.7 Section 4.6)**:
- **Clause 6.1 risk assessment integration**: ≥3 updates per quarter
- **Prevented incidents (validated)**: ≥3 per quarter with technical evidence
- **Source accuracy rate**: ≥85% (validated quarterly)
- **P1/P2 incidents using TI**: ≥70%
- **Intelligence-driven decisions**: ≥5 per quarter with documented outcomes
- **Critical assets with TI coverage**: 100%
- **Critical TI alerts disseminated**: Within ≤4 hours
- **Stakeholder satisfaction**: ≥4.0/5.0
- **Business continuity**: 100% of critical roles have documented backup personnel

### 4.3.2 Non-Compliance Identification and Remediation

Non-compliance with threat intelligence policy SHALL be addressed through structured remediation:

**Identification**:
- Non-compliance identified through monitoring, audits, or incident reviews
- **Evidence gaps identified** through quarterly validation
- Severity assessed: Critical, High, Medium, Low
- Root cause analysis conducted

**Remediation**:
- Corrective action plan developed by responsible party
- Remediation timeline established based on severity:
  - Critical: Immediate action (24-48 hours)
  - High: 1-2 weeks
  - Medium: 1 month
  - Low: Next review cycle
- Resources allocated for remediation
- Progress monitored by Policy Owner or designee

**Verification**:
- Remediation effectiveness validated
- Re-audit of affected area conducted
- **Evidence regeneration** if audit evidence was affected
- Closure approved by Policy Owner

**Lessons Learned**:
- Root causes documented
- Process improvements identified
- Policy clarifications considered
- Training needs assessed

### 4.3.3 Policy Violations and Enforcement

Violations of threat intelligence policy are subject to enforcement actions:

**Violation Categories**:
- **Unauthorized disclosure**: Sharing threat intelligence without authorization
- **Misuse**: Using threat intelligence for non-security purposes
- **Negligence**: Failure to follow intelligence handling procedures
- **Circumvention**: Intentional bypassing of policy controls
- **Unlawful collection**: Collection methods violating laws or regulations
- **Evidence falsification**: Fabricating or manipulating audit evidence

**Enforcement Actions** (aligned with organizational disciplinary policies):
- **Minor violations**: Verbal or written warning, mandatory retraining
- **Moderate violations**: Written reprimand, temporary access restriction, performance improvement plan
- **Severe violations**: Suspension, termination of access, employment termination
- **Criminal violations**: Referral to law enforcement where applicable

**Due Process**:
- Violations investigated before enforcement actions
- Accused party given opportunity to respond
- Proportionate response to violation severity
- Consistent enforcement across organization
- Appeals process available
- Documentation maintained

## 4.4 Performance Measurement and Reporting

### 4.4.1 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

The threat intelligence program SHALL be measured using **evidence-based KPIs** aligned with ISMS-POL-A.5.7 Section 4.6:

**Evidence-Based KPIs (ISO 27001 Audit-Ready)**:

| KPI Category | Metric | Target | Evidence Location | Audit Relevance |
|--------------|--------|--------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Clause 6.1 Integration** | Risk assessments updated with TI per quarter | ≥3 | ISMS-IMP-A.5.7.3 Sheet 13 | Demonstrates risk assessment integration |
| **Effectiveness** | Prevented incidents per quarter (with validation) | ≥3 | ISMS-IMP-A.5.7.3 Sheet 7 | Demonstrates security posture improvement |
| **Quality** | Source accuracy rate (validated) | ≥85% | ISMS-IMP-A.5.7.1 Sheet 14 | Demonstrates intelligence quality |
| **Integration** | P1/P2 incidents using TI per quarter | ≥70% | ISMS-IMP-A.5.7.3 Sheet 14 | Demonstrates A.5.24-5.28 integration |
| **Impact** | Intelligence-driven decisions per quarter | ≥5 | ISMS-IMP-A.5.7.3 Sheet 15 | Demonstrates business value |
| **Coverage** | Critical assets with TI coverage | 100% | ISMS-IMP-A.5.7.3 Sheet 4 | Demonstrates scope adequacy |
| **Timeliness** | Critical TI alerts disseminated within | ≤4 hours | ISMS-IMP-A.5.7.3 Sheet 3 | Demonstrates operational effectiveness |
| **Stakeholder Engagement** | Stakeholder satisfaction score | ≥4.0/5.0 | ISMS-IMP-A.5.7.3 Sheet 8 | Demonstrates Clause 9.1 monitoring |
| **Production Volume** | TI reports produced per quarter | ≥30 | ISMS-IMP-A.5.7.2 Sheet 5 | Demonstrates operational capacity |
| **Actionability** | % of TI reports resulting in decisions | ≥30% | ISMS-IMP-A.5.7.3 Sheet 15 | Demonstrates intelligence relevance |

**Additional Operational KPIs**:

**Intelligence Quality KPIs**:
- **False Positive Rate**: Percentage of IOCs generating false alerts (Target: ≤15%)
- **Confidence Level Distribution**: Percentage of intelligence at high/medium/low confidence

**Timeliness KPIs**:
- **Intelligence Production Time**: Average time from collection to analysis completion (Target: ≤24 hours for operational, ≤7 days for tactical)
- **Dissemination Time**: Average time from production to stakeholder delivery (Target: ≤2 hours for critical)

**Coverage KPIs**:
- **Threat Actor Coverage**: Number of relevant threat actors profiled and tracked
- **Attack Vector Coverage**: Percentage of organizational attack surface covered by intelligence
- **Source Diversity**: Number of active internal and external intelligence sources

**Operational KPIs**:
- **IOC Deployment Rate**: Percentage of validated IOCs deployed to security tools (Target: ≥95% within 24 hours)
- **Intelligence Requests Fulfilled**: Percentage of stakeholder intelligence requests met (Target: ≥90%)
- **Training Completion**: Percentage of intelligence personnel meeting competency requirements (Target: 100%)

**KPI Target Review**:
- Targets reviewed annually during Q4 review
- Adjustments require documented justification
- Historical performance considered when adjusting targets

## 4.5 Audit Preparation and Evidence Management

### 4.5.1 Audit Preparation Timeline

Per ISMS-POL-A.5.7 Section 4.5, the Threat Intelligence Team SHALL prepare for ISO 27001 certification audits:

**T-30 Days Before Audit**:
- Generate all quarterly assessment workbooks (ISMS-IMP-A.5.7.1 through A.5.7.4)
- Run sanity check scripts on all workbooks (excel_sanity_check_a57_1 through a57_4)
- Prepare audit evidence package per ISMS-POL-A.5.7 Section 4.5:
  - ≥3 examples: Threat intelligence → Risk assessment updates (Clause 6.1)
  - ≥3 examples: Prevented incidents with validation evidence
  - 1 quarter: Source accuracy validation report
  - ≥3 examples: Incidents where TI was used (with handler feedback)
  - 1 quarter: Intelligence-driven decisions with outcomes
  - Business continuity plan + test results

**T-21 Days**:
- Prepare policy documentation package:
  - All POL sections (S1-S5) approved and current
  - Version control history
  - Exception log (if any)
- Prepare process evidence:
  - 3 sample TI reports (strategic, tactical, operational)
  - Dissemination logs
  - Stakeholder feedback surveys
  - Training records

**T-14 Days**:
- **CISO review of audit readiness**
- Gap remediation if needed
- Prepare auditor briefing document:
  - One-page summary: "Control A.5.7 - How We Do Threat Intelligence"
  - Metrics dashboard
  - Integration map

**T-7 Days**:
- Auditor briefing document finalized
- Evidence uploaded to audit portal
- Threat Intelligence Team briefed on audit process

**T-0 (Audit Day)**:
- Evidence available in audit portal
- Threat Intelligence Team available for questions
- Workbooks ready for auditor review

**Initial Training** (upon assuming threat intelligence role):
- Threat intelligence policy and procedures
- Threat intelligence fundamentals and methodologies
- Organizational threat profile and risk context
- Threat intelligence tools and platforms
- Legal and ethical considerations
- Information handling and classification
- **Audit evidence generation requirements** (per ISMS-POL-A.5.7 Section 4.4)

**Ongoing Training** (annual minimum):
- Emerging threats and attack techniques
- Threat intelligence tool updates and new features
- Regulatory changes affecting threat intelligence
- Policy and procedure updates
- Advanced analytical techniques
- Integration with security operations
- **Evidence documentation best practices**

**Specialized Training** (role-specific):
- Malware analysis for Threat Intelligence Analysts

- Intelligence writing and briefing skills
- Framework training (MITRE ATT&CK, Diamond Model, etc.)

**Awareness Training** (for broader organization):

**Audit Preparation Training**:
- **Conducted 30 days before certification audit**
- Evidence generation requirements
- Workbook completion procedures

**Business Continuity Training**:
- **Cross-training** per schedule in ISMS-POL-A.5.7-S3 Section 3.10.1

Training SHALL be delivered through:

Training completion SHALL be tracked through:
- Learning management system records

Threat intelligence personnel SHALL demonstrate competency through:
- Successful completion of required training

## 4.7 Exception Management

### 4.7.1 Exception Request Process

Exceptions to threat intelligence policy requirements MAY be granted under specific circumstances:

**Valid Exception Reasons**:
- Technical limitations preventing policy compliance
- Resource constraints requiring temporary deviation
- Business requirements necessitating alternative approach
- Regulatory conflicts requiring jurisdictional adaptation
- Risk acceptance decision by executive leadership

**Exception Request Requirements**:
- Formal exception request submitted to Policy Owner (CISO)
- Business justification and impact analysis
- Risk assessment of exception
- **Impact on audit evidence** (if exception affects evidence generation)
- Compensating controls (if applicable)
- Exception duration (temporary vs. permanent)
- Responsible party for exception oversight

**Exception Approval Authority**:
- **Minor exceptions** (<30 days, low risk): Threat Intelligence Coordinator
- **Moderate exceptions** (30-90 days, medium risk): CISO
- **Major exceptions** (>90 days or high risk): CISO with Risk Committee review
- **Permanent exceptions**: CISO with Executive Leadership and Risk Committee approval

**Exception Documentation**:
- All exceptions documented in exception register
- Approval chain maintained
- Risk acceptance documented
- Compensating controls verified
- Review dates established
- **Included in audit evidence package**

### 4.7.2 Exception Monitoring and Review

Approved exceptions SHALL be actively managed:

**Monitoring**:
- Exception register reviewed monthly by Threat Intelligence Coordinator
- Risk of exceptions assessed regularly
- Compensating controls validated
- Expiration dates tracked

**Review**:
- Temporary exceptions reviewed 30 days before expiration
- Renewal, remediation, or acceptance decision made
- Permanent exceptions reviewed annually
- **Exceptions included in audit scope**

**Revocation**:
- Exceptions revoked if risk changes unacceptably
- Exceptions expired if not renewed
- Compliance restoration plan required upon revocation

## 4.8 Integration with ISMS

### 4.8.1 ISMS Framework Integration

Threat intelligence policy integrates with the broader Information Security Management System (ISMS) as follows:

**Clause 6.1 - Actions to Address Risks and Opportunities (MANDATORY)**:
- **Threat intelligence informs risk assessments** with current threat data
- Likelihood and impact estimates updated based on threat landscape
- New risks identified from emerging threats
- Risk treatment options informed by adversary capabilities
- **Target**: ≥3 risk assessment updates per quarter driven by TI
- **Evidence tracked in**: ISMS-IMP-A.5.7.3 Sheet 13
- **Risk Management (CRO) accountability**: Review and approve TI-driven risk updates

**Clause 9.2 - Internal Audit**:
- Threat intelligence program included in ISMS audit scope
- Compliance with Control 5.7 requirements verified
- Effectiveness of threat intelligence controls assessed
- Non-conformities and improvement opportunities identified
- **Audit evidence validated** per ISMS-POL-A.5.7 Section 4.4

**Clause 10 - Improvement**:
- Threat intelligence metrics contribute to ISMS performance evaluation
- Non-conformities addressed through corrective actions
- Continual improvement initiatives for threat intelligence program
- **Gap analysis** when KPI targets not met

### 4.8.2 Integration with Related Controls

Threat intelligence policy supports implementation of related ISO 27001 controls:

**Control A.5.24-5.28 - Incident Management (MANDATORY)**:
- Threat intelligence feeds incident detection and triage
- Intelligence provides context for incident investigation
- Incident data contributes to threat intelligence collection
- Post-incident reviews validate intelligence effectiveness
- **Target**: ≥70% of P1/P2 incidents use threat intelligence
- **Evidence tracked in**: ISMS-IMP-A.5.7.3 Sheet 14

**Control A.8.16 - Monitoring Activities (MANDATORY)**:
- Threat intelligence provides detection signatures for monitoring
- Monitoring telemetry feeds threat intelligence collection
- IOCs deployed to monitoring tools
- Monitoring alerts enriched with threat context

**Control A.8.8 - Management of Technical Vulnerabilities (OPTIONAL - when implemented)**:
- Threat intelligence prioritizes vulnerability remediation
- Active exploitation data triggers emergency patching
- Vulnerability trends inform threat intelligence collection
- **VulnerabilityThreatLink (VTL) schema** enables automated integration
- **Note**: This integration is OPTIONAL and only applies when organization implements Control A.8.8

**Control 5.7 - Threat Intelligence** (this control):
- Primary policy framework for threat intelligence
- Detailed requirements for collection, analysis, and dissemination

**Control A.5.19-5.22 - Supplier Management**:
- Threat intelligence includes supply chain threats
- Supplier vulnerabilities tracked through intelligence
- Threat intelligence shared with suppliers where appropriate

**Control A.5.23 - Cloud Security**:
- Cloud-specific threat intelligence collected
- Cloud provider intelligence integrated
- Cloud service threats monitored and analyzed

### 4.8.3 Cross-Control Coordination

Coordination between threat intelligence (A.5.7) and related controls SHALL be maintained through:
- **Regular coordination meetings** between control owners (MANDATORY: CISO, TI Coordinator, CRO)
- Shared data schemas and integration points (VTL schema when A.8.8 implemented)
- Unified dashboards and reporting
- Integrated assessment and audit processes
- Consolidated training and awareness
- Joint continuous improvement initiatives

## 4.9 Records and Documentation

### 4.9.1 Required Records

The following records SHALL be maintained to demonstrate compliance with threat intelligence policy:

**Program Records**:
- Threat intelligence policy and procedures (all versions)
- Intelligence requirements and priorities
- Source evaluation and selection documentation
- Technology platform configurations and change history

**Operational Records**:
- Threat intelligence reports (strategic, tactical, operational)
- Intelligence dissemination logs
- IOC deployment records
- Stakeholder feedback and satisfaction surveys

**Governance Records**:
- Policy review documentation
- Change request records and approvals
- Exception register and approval documentation
- Audit reports and findings
- Non-conformity and corrective action records

**Training Records**:
- Training completion records
- Competency assessments
- Certification tracking
- Awareness campaign materials and attendance

**Performance Records**:
- KPI measurements and trend analysis
- Monthly, quarterly, and annual reports
- Continuous improvement initiatives and outcomes

**Audit Evidence Records (per ISMS-POL-A.5.7 Section 4.4)**:
- Clause 6.1 risk assessment updates (cross-references, approvals)
- Prevented incidents with validation evidence
- Source accuracy validation reports
- Incident-TI integration records
- Intelligence-driven decisions with outcomes
- Business continuity plans and test results

### 4.9.2 Retention Requirements

Records SHALL be retained according to the following schedule:

**Policy and Governance Documents**:
- Current policy: Indefinite (active)
- Superseded policy versions: Minimum 7 years after supersession
- Audit reports: Minimum 7 years
- Exception approvals: Duration of exception + 3 years

**Operational Records**:
- Threat intelligence reports: Minimum 3 years
- IOC deployment logs: Minimum 2 years
- Intelligence dissemination records: Minimum 2 years
- Source performance data: Minimum 3 years

**Training Records**:
- Training completion: Duration of employment + 3 years
- Competency assessments: Minimum 3 years

**Incident-Related Records**:
- Intelligence supporting incidents: Aligned with incident retention (typically 7 years)

**Audit Evidence Records**:
- Assessment workbooks: Minimum 3 years (retain 3 most recent quarters)
- Evidence packages: Duration of certification + 3 years

**Legal and Regulatory Requirements**:
- Where specific retention requirements exist, longer of policy requirement or legal/regulatory requirement applies

### 4.9.3 Record Security and Access

Threat intelligence records SHALL be protected according to their classification:
- Access restricted to authorized personnel based on need-to-know
- Encryption for confidential and restricted intelligence
- Secure storage in approved document management systems
- Audit logging of access to sensitive intelligence
- Secure destruction at end of retention period
