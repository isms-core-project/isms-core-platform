## Executive Summary

This structure plan defines the implementation approach for **ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Controls A.5.15, A.5.16, and A.5.18** as a unified **Identity & Access Management (IAM) Foundation Framework**.

**Combined Control Rationale**:
These three controls form the foundational layer of identity and access governance:
- **A.5.15 - Access Control**: Strategic policy framework (WHO gets access to WHAT, and WHY)
- **A.5.16 - Identity Management**: Identity lifecycle management (HOW identities are created, maintained, removed)
- **A.5.18 - Access Rights**: Rights assignment and review (WHAT specific access each user/group gets)

Implementing these controls separately would result in:
- ❌ Disconnected access governance and identity processes
- ❌ Redundant user inventory and access review activities  
- ❌ Fragmented IAM policy framework
- ❌ Inefficient evidence collection
- ❌ Confusion between "policy" vs "lifecycle" vs "rights"

**Combined implementation provides**:
- ✅ Unified IAM governance framework
- ✅ Single user inventory serving all three controls
- ✅ Integrated access review process
- ✅ Consolidated evidence collection
- ✅ ~3x efficiency improvement vs. separate implementations

**Reference Models**:
- **Structure**: A.8.20-22 Network Security (combined controls pattern)
- **Quality**: A.8.23 Web Filtering (comprehensive assessment approach)

**Integration Context**:
This IAM foundation feeds into **ISMS-A.8.2-3-5-Authentication-Privileged-Access**:
- A.5.15/16/18 = **Governance layer** (who gets access, why, lifecycle)
- A.8.2-3-5 = **Technical security layer** (how access is authenticated, restricted, privileged)

## 1. Control Alignment & Scope

### 1.1 ISO 27001:2022 Control Texts

**A.5.15 - Access Control (Zugangssteuerung)**

> *Regeln zur Steuerung des physischen und logischen Zugriffs auf Informationen und andere damit verbundene Werte müssen auf der Grundlage von Geschäfts- und Informationssicherheitsanforderungen aufgestellt und umgesetzt werden.*

**English Translation**:
> *Rules to control physical and logical access to information and other associated assets shall be established and implemented based on business and information security requirements.*

**Focus**: Strategic access control policy, business justification for access, segregation of duties, access control principles, exception management.

---

**A.5.16 - Identity Management (Identitätsmanagement)**

> *Der gesamte Lebenszyklus von Identitäten sollte verwaltet werden.*

**English Translation**:
> *The full life cycle of identities should be managed.*

**Focus**: Joiner/mover/leaver processes, account provisioning/deprovisioning, orphaned account detection, contractor/vendor identity management, identity repository management.

---

**A.5.18 - Access Rights (Zugangsrechte)**

> *Zugangsrechte zu Informationen und anderen damit verbundenen Werten müssen in Übereinstimmung mit der themenspezifischen Richtlinie und den Regeln der Organisation für die Zugangssteuerung bereitgestellt, überprüft, geändert und entfernt werden.*

**English Translation**:
> *Access rights to information and other associated assets shall be provisioned, reviewed, modified and removed in accordance with the organization's topic-specific policy and rules for access control.*

**Focus**: Access rights assignment, role-based access control (RBAC), group management, access reviews/recertification, privilege creep detection, emergency access procedures.

2. **Lifecycle Process (A.5.16)** implements identity management

5. **Technical Implementation** (A.8.2-3-5) builds on this governance foundation

### 1.3 Applicability & Scope

**Framework applies to**:
- **All user types**: Employees, contractors, vendors, partners, customers, service accounts
- **All identity systems**: Active Directory, Azure AD, Okta, Google Workspace, LDAP, custom systems
- **All access types**: Application access, system access, data access, physical access (door badges)
- **All environments**: On-premises, cloud (SaaS, IaaS, PaaS), hybrid
- **All organizational sizes**: Small (10 users) to enterprise (10,000+ users)

**Framework is completely generic**:
- Technology-agnostic (works with any identity platform)
- Organization-agnostic (works for any industry, size, maturity)
- Deployment-agnostic (on-prem, cloud, hybrid)

**Out of Scope**:
- Physical access control systems (covered in A.7.x Physical Security)
- Detailed authentication mechanisms (covered in A.8.5 Secure Authentication)
- Privileged access management (PAM) details (covered in A.8.2 Privileged Access Rights)
- User awareness training (covered in A.6.3 Information Security Awareness)

#### Section 2: Access Control Policy Requirements (A.5.15)
**ISMS-POL-A.5.15-16-18-S2_Access_Control_Policy_A515.md**

**Content**:
1. **Control Objective & Scope**
   - A.5.15 official text (German + English)
   - Why access control policy matters
   - Scope of A.5.15 requirements

2. **Access Control Principles**
   - REQ-A515-001: **Least Privilege** - users have minimum access needed
   - REQ-A515-002: **Need-to-Know** - access limited by business justification
   - REQ-A515-003: **Segregation of Duties (SoD)** - conflicting roles separated
   - REQ-A515-004: **Default Deny** - no access unless explicitly granted
   - REQ-A515-005: **Defense in Depth** - multiple layers of access control

3. **Access Classification Requirements**
   - REQ-A515-010: **User Type Classification**
     - Employee (internal staff)
     - Contractor (time-bound, sponsored)
     - Vendor (third-party service providers)
     - Partner (business partners with system access)
     - Customer (external users for customer-facing systems)
     - Service account (non-human accounts)
     - Emergency/break-glass account (exceptional access)
   
   - REQ-A515-011: **System Criticality Classification**
     - Critical (business-critical, requires multi-person approval)
     - High (important but not critical, requires manager approval)
     - Medium (standard business systems, requires manager approval)
     - Low (non-critical systems, self-service with justification)
   
   - REQ-A515-012: **Data Sensitivity Classification**
     - Restricted (highly sensitive - PII, financial, IP)
     - Confidential (internal only)
     - Internal (all employees)
     - Public (no restrictions)

4. **Business Justification Framework**
   - REQ-A515-020: Access requests require business justification
   - REQ-A515-021: Justification documented in access request ticket
   - REQ-A515-022: Approval chain based on criticality/sensitivity
   - REQ-A515-023: Periodic re-validation of business justification

5. **Access Control Roles & Responsibilities**
   - REQ-A515-030: **Access Requestor** - submits request with justification
   - REQ-A515-031: **Business Owner/Manager** - approves access based on business need
   - REQ-A515-032: **System Owner** - approves access to their system
   - REQ-A515-033: **Security Team** - validates compliance with policy
   - REQ-A515-034: **IT Operations** - provisions access after approvals
   - REQ-A515-035: **Access Reviewer** - periodically reviews access rights
   - REQ-A515-036: **Internal Audit** - verifies access control effectiveness

6. **Segregation of Duties (SoD) Requirements**
   - REQ-A515-040: SoD matrix defines conflicting roles
     - Example: Request + Approve (same person can't do both)
     - Example: Developer + Production Admin (separation of dev/prod)
     - Example: Finance + Payroll (fraud prevention)
   - REQ-A515-041: SoD violations detected automatically
   - REQ-A515-042: SoD exceptions require senior management approval
   - REQ-A515-043: Compensating controls for SoD exceptions
   - REQ-A515-044: SoD monitoring and reporting

7. **Access Control Exception Management**
   - REQ-A515-050: Exceptions to access policy require approval
   - REQ-A515-051: Exception approval workflow defined
   - REQ-A515-052: Exception justification documented
   - REQ-A515-053: Exceptions reviewed periodically
   - REQ-A515-054: Exception removal after no longer needed

8. **Remote Access Policy Considerations**
   - REQ-A515-060: Remote access subject to access control policy
   - REQ-A515-061: VPN access requires business justification
   - REQ-A515-062: Cloud access (SaaS, IaaS) follows same policies

9. **Third-Party Access Governance**
   - REQ-A515-070: Vendor/partner access requires sponsorship
   - REQ-A515-071: Third-party access time-bound (contract period)
   - REQ-A515-072: Third-party access reviewed quarterly
   - REQ-A515-073: Immediate revocation when contract ends

10. **Integration with HR Processes**
    - REQ-A515-080: Access control integrated with onboarding
    - REQ-A515-081: Access control integrated with offboarding
    - REQ-A515-082: Access control integrated with role changes

11. **Audit & Evidence Requirements**
    - Evidence: Access control policy document
    - Evidence: Access request approval records
    - Evidence: SoD matrix and violation reports
    - Evidence: Exception approval records
    - Evidence: Third-party access register

#### Section 3: Identity Management Requirements (A.5.16)
**ISMS-POL-A.5.15-16-18-S3_Identity_Management_A516.md**

**Content**:
1. **Control Objective & Scope**
   - A.5.16 official text (German + English)
   - Why identity lifecycle management matters
   - Scope of A.5.16 requirements

2. **Identity Lifecycle Framework**
   - REQ-A516-001: **Joiner Process** - new user onboarding
   - REQ-A516-002: **Mover Process** - role change, transfer
   - REQ-A516-003: **Leaver Process** - termination, contract end
   - REQ-A516-004: Lifecycle stages defined and documented

3. **User Account Provisioning Requirements**
   - REQ-A516-010: Provisioning triggered by HR system (authoritative source)
   - REQ-A516-011: **Provisioning Timeliness**:
     - Employee: Access ready by start date
     - Contractor: Access ready by contract start
     - Vendor: Access within 2 business days of approval
   - REQ-A516-012: Account creation workflow documented
   - REQ-A516-013: Initial access assignment based on role
   - REQ-A516-014: Account activation procedures
   - REQ-A516-015: Welcome email with access instructions

4. **User Account Deprovisioning Requirements**
   - REQ-A516-020: Deprovisioning triggered by HR system termination
   - REQ-A516-021: **Deprovisioning Timeliness**:
     - Termination (involuntary): Immediate (within 1 hour)
     - Resignation (voluntary): Last working day
     - Contractor contract end: Contract end date
     - Leave of absence: Account disabled for duration
   - REQ-A516-022: Account disablement vs. deletion:
     - Immediate: Account disabled (login prevented)
     - After 90 days: Account deleted (if no retention requirement)
   - REQ-A516-023: Access removal verification (audit log)
   - REQ-A516-024: Data handover procedures (mailbox, files)
   - REQ-A516-025: Manager notification of deprovisioning

5. **Account Type Requirements**
   - REQ-A516-030: **Employee Accounts**
     - Personal accounts (one account per person)
     - Linked to HR system
     - Standard lifecycle (joiner/mover/leaver)
   
   - REQ-A516-031: **Contractor/Vendor Accounts**
     - Sponsored by internal employee
     - Time-bound (contract start/end date)
     - Automatic expiration if not extended
     - External identifier in username (e.g., EXT-)
   
   - REQ-A516-032: **Service Accounts**
     - Used by applications/systems (non-human)
     - Owner assigned (person responsible)
     - No expiration date (until service decommissioned)
     - Privileged service accounts (special handling per A.8.2)
     - Password rotation requirements
   
   - REQ-A516-033: **Shared Accounts**
     - Discouraged (violates accountability)
     - If required: Business justification + approval
     - Documented: Who uses account, why shared
     - Enhanced monitoring (all actions logged)
   
   - REQ-A516-034: **Emergency/Break-Glass Accounts**
     - Used only in emergencies (system recovery)
     - Stored securely (password vault)
     - Use triggers incident investigation
     - After-the-fact justification required

6. **Identity Repository Requirements**
   - REQ-A516-040: **Authoritative Source** - HR system is source of truth
   - REQ-A516-041: **Identity Synchronization**:
     - Identity data synced from HR → Identity systems
     - Frequency: Real-time or daily (depending on system)
     - Sync failures detected and escalated
   - REQ-A516-042: **Identity Data Accuracy**:
     - User attributes accurate (name, email, department, manager)
     - Updates propagated to all systems
     - Data quality monitored
   - REQ-A516-043: **Multiple Identity Systems**:
     - Active Directory (on-prem)
     - Azure AD (cloud)
     - Okta (cloud identity platform)
     - Custom LDAP directories
     - Consistency maintained across systems

7. **Orphaned Account Detection & Remediation**
   - REQ-A516-050: **Orphaned Account Definition**:
     - Account exists but user no longer in HR system
     - Account exists but no valid business owner
     - Inactive account (no login in 90+ days)
   - REQ-A516-051: **Detection Frequency**: Monthly automated scan
   - REQ-A516-052: **Detection Methodology**:
     - Cross-reference identity systems with HR data
     - Flag accounts not in HR system
     - Flag inactive accounts
   - REQ-A516-053: **Remediation Procedures**:
     - Identify owner (if still needed)
     - Document justification (if still needed)
     - Disable account (if orphaned)
     - Delete account after 30 days disabled
   - REQ-A516-054: **Reporting**: Monthly orphaned account report

8. **Contractor/Vendor Identity Management**
   - REQ-A516-060: **Sponsorship Requirements**
     - Internal sponsor assigned (employee responsible)
     - Sponsor justifies access need
     - Sponsor notified before contract end
   - REQ-A516-061: **Time-Bound Access**
     - Contract start/end date documented
     - Automatic expiration on contract end
     - Access extension requires re-approval
   - REQ-A516-062: **Vendor Lifecycle**:
     - Onboarding: Background check (if required), access approval
     - Active: Quarterly access review
     - Offboarding: Immediate access removal on contract end

9. **Identity for Non-Human Entities**
   - REQ-A516-070: Service accounts managed like user accounts
   - REQ-A516-071: IoT devices (if applicable) have identities
   - REQ-A516-072: API keys/tokens managed as identities
   - REQ-A516-073: Ownership and lifecycle defined

10. **Audit & Evidence Requirements**
    - Evidence: Complete user inventory (all account types)
    - Evidence: Provisioning timeliness reports
    - Evidence: Deprovisioning timeliness reports
    - Evidence: Orphaned account detection reports
    - Evidence: Contractor/vendor access register

#### Section 4: Access Rights Management Requirements (A.5.18)
**ISMS-POL-A.5.15-16-18-S4_Access_Rights_Management_A518.md**

**Content**:
1. **Control Objective & Scope**
   - A.5.18 official text (German + English)
   - Why access rights management matters
   - Scope of A.5.18 requirements

2. **Access Rights Assignment Requirements**
   - REQ-A518-001: Access assigned based on role/job function
   - REQ-A518-002: **Access Request Workflow**:
     - Requestor submits access request (ticket system)
     - Business justification documented
     - Manager approves request
     - System owner approves (for sensitive systems)
     - IT provisions access
     - Requestor notified
   - REQ-A518-003: **Provisioning SLA**:
     - Standard access: Within 1 business day
     - Sensitive access: Within 3 business days (approval workflow)
     - Emergency access: Same day (with after-the-fact justification)
   - REQ-A518-004: Documentation: Who requested, who approved, when, why

3. **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) Framework**
   - REQ-A518-010: **Role Definition**:
     - Role catalog maintained (list of standard roles)
     - Role description (job function, responsibilities)
     - Role-to-access mapping (role → systems/data/access level)
     - Role owner assigned (person responsible for role definition)
   - REQ-A518-011: **Role Assignment**:
     - Users assigned to roles (not direct access)
     - Role assignment based on job function
     - Role assignment approved by manager
   - REQ-A518-012: **Role Hierarchy** (if applicable):
     - Parent roles, child roles
     - Inheritance of permissions
   - REQ-A518-013: **Role Review & Maintenance**:
     - Roles reviewed annually
     - Access mappings updated as systems change
     - Obsolete roles removed

4. **Group Membership Management**
   - REQ-A518-020: **Group Purpose Documentation**:
     - Every group has documented purpose
     - Group description (what access it grants)
   - REQ-A518-021: **Group Ownership**:
     - Every group has assigned owner
     - Owner responsible for membership approval
     - Owner responsible for periodic review
   - REQ-A518-022: **Group Membership Approval**:
     - Membership requests approved by group owner
     - Approval documented (who approved, when)
   - REQ-A518-023: **Nested Groups** (if applicable):
     - Group-in-group relationships documented
     - Nested group permissions understood

5. **Access Review/Recertification Requirements**
   - REQ-A518-030: **Review Frequency**:
     - Critical systems: Quarterly
     - High-sensitivity systems: Semi-annual
     - Standard systems: Annual
     - Low-criticality systems: Biennial
   - REQ-A518-031: **Review Scope**:
     - All users reviewed (employees, contractors, service accounts)
     - All systems reviewed
     - All groups reviewed
   - REQ-A518-032: **Reviewer Accountability**:
     - Managers review their direct reports' access
     - System owners review access to their systems
     - Security team reviews privileged access
   - REQ-A518-033: **Review Execution**:
     - Review data prepared (who has access, to what)
     - Reviewer confirms access is appropriate OR removes access
     - Review completion tracked (% complete)
     - Unresponsive reviewers escalated
   - REQ-A518-034: **Review Documentation**:
     - Review period (Q1 2026, etc.)
     - Review completion date
     - Access confirmed count
     - Access removed count
     - Reviewer sign-off
   - REQ-A518-035: **Review Evidence Retention**: 3 years

6. **Access Removal Requirements**
   - REQ-A518-040: **Removal Timelines**:
     - Termination: Immediate (within 1 hour)
     - Role change: Within 1 business day
     - Access no longer needed: Within 3 business days
   - REQ-A518-041: **Removal Verification**:
     - Removal logged in audit trail
     - Post-removal verification (account disabled test)
   - REQ-A518-042: **Temporary Access Removal**:
     - Leave of absence: Access disabled
     - Return from leave: Access re-enabled (after verification)

7. **Privilege Creep Detection Requirements**
   - REQ-A518-050: **Privilege Creep Definition**:
     - User accumulates excess rights over time
     - User retains access from previous roles
     - User has access no longer needed for current role
   - REQ-A518-051: **Detection Methodology**:
     - Compare current access vs. required access (for current role)
     - Identify discrepancies (access not aligned with role)
     - Flag users with excessive access
   - REQ-A518-052: **Remediation**:
     - Manager confirms access is needed OR approves removal
     - Excess access removed
     - Documentation updated

8. **Emergency Access Procedures**
   - REQ-A518-060: **Break-Glass Account Management**:
     - Emergency accounts identified
     - Stored securely (password vault)
     - Access restricted (CISO, IT Director only)
   - REQ-A518-061: **Emergency Access Approval**:
     - Used only in emergencies (system outage, security incident)
     - After-the-fact justification required (within 24 hours)
     - CISO notification required
   - REQ-A518-062: **Emergency Access Logging**:
     - All actions logged
