## SECTION 2: Regulatory Applicability Identification Process

### WRITING INSTRUCTIONS:
Define the SYSTEMATIC process for identifying potentially applicable regulations.

### KEY CONTENT:

#### 2.1 Trigger Events
When does [Organization] need to identify new regulations?

**Write about:**
- **Periodic Review**: Scheduled scan (e.g., quarterly or annually)
- **Expansion Triggers**:
  - Entering new geographic market
  - Offering new services/products
  - Acquiring new customers in regulated industries
  - Mergers/acquisitions
- **Contractual Triggers**:
  - New customer contracts with compliance requirements
  - New supplier agreements with pass-through obligations
- **Certification Triggers**:
  - Pursuing new certifications (ISO, SOC2, etc.)
  - Certification body requirements
- **Internal Triggers**:
  - Strategic initiative with compliance implications
  - Risk assessment identifies new regulatory exposure
- **External Triggers**:
  - New laws/regulations enacted
  - Industry guidance published
  - Regulatory authority notifications

#### 2.2 Sources for Regulatory Intelligence
Where to look for applicable regulations?

**Write about:**
- **Legal Databases**:
  - Commercial services (LexisNexis, Westlaw, etc.)
  - Government legal databases
  - Regulatory authority websites
- **Industry Associations**:
  - Sector-specific associations
  - Cross-border industry groups
  - Standards bodies (ISO, NIST, etc.)
- **Legal Counsel**:
  - In-house legal team
  - External legal advisors (by jurisdiction)
  - Specialized regulatory counsel
- **Peer Networks**:
  - Industry forums and working groups
  - Professional associations
  - Compliance communities
- **Professional Services**:
  - Audit firms (Big 4, etc.)
  - Compliance consultants
  - Regulatory monitoring services
- **Customer/Supplier Channels**:
  - Customer compliance requirements
  - Supplier contractual obligations
  - RFP/RFI compliance questions

#### 2.3 Initial Screening Criteria
How to filter the universe of regulations down to candidates?

**Write about:**
- **Relevance Check**: Does regulation relate to information security, data protection, or IT services?
- **Jurisdictional Check**: Does [Organization] have presence/operations in jurisdiction?
- **Operational Check**: Does regulation apply to [Organization]'s activities?
- **Threshold Check**: Does [Organization] meet applicability thresholds (size, revenue, data volume)?
- **Preliminary Assessment**: Quick yes/no/maybe before deep analysis

**Output**: List of candidate regulations for full applicability assessment

## SECTION 3: Applicability Criteria Framework

### WRITING INSTRUCTIONS:
Define the THREE-DIMENSIONAL assessment framework for determining applicability.

This is the CORE of the applicability methodology - be thorough and systematic.

### KEY CONTENT:

#### 3.1 Geographic Scope Assessment
Does the regulation apply based on WHERE [Organization] operates?

**Write about:**
- **[Organization] Operations in Jurisdiction**:
  - Physical presence (offices, facilities)
  - Legal entities registered
  - Employees located in jurisdiction
  - Direct service delivery in jurisdiction
  
- **[Organization] Customers/Data Subjects in Jurisdiction**:
  - Customers physically located in jurisdiction
  - Data subjects whose data is protected by jurisdiction's laws
  - Targeting customers in jurisdiction (website, marketing)
  
- **[Organization] Data Processing in Jurisdiction**:
  - Data centers or servers located in jurisdiction
  - Data processed by service providers in jurisdiction
  - Data transmitted through jurisdiction
  
- **Extraterritorial Application**:
  - Some regulations apply based on data subject location (e.g., GDPR)
  - Even without physical presence in jurisdiction
  
**Assessment Questions**:
- Does [Organization] have operations in [Jurisdiction]?
- Does [Organization] serve customers in [Jurisdiction]?
- Does [Organization] process data of individuals in [Jurisdiction]?
- Does the regulation claim extraterritorial reach?

#### 3.2 Operational Scope Assessment
Does the regulation apply based on WHAT [Organization] does?

**Write about:**
- **Type of Services Provided**:
  - IT services, cloud services, consulting, software development, etc.
  - Some regulations apply only to specific service types
  
- **Industry Sectors Served**:
  - Financial services, healthcare, critical infrastructure, etc.
  - Sector-specific regulations apply when serving those sectors
  
- **Type of Data Processed**:
  - Personal data, health data, financial data, classified data
  - Data-specific regulations apply based on data types
  
- **Type of Operations**:
  - E-commerce, telecommunications, payment processing, etc.
  - Operation-specific regulations
  
- **Organizational Characteristics**:
  - Size (number of employees, revenue)
  - Organizational type (public/private, for-profit/non-profit)
  - Thresholds that trigger applicability

**Assessment Questions**:
- Does [Organization] provide services that trigger this regulation?
- Does [Organization] serve industries covered by this regulation?
- Does [Organization] process data types covered by this regulation?
- Does [Organization] meet size/threshold criteria?

#### 3.3 Contractual Scope Assessment
Does the regulation apply based on CONTRACTUAL OBLIGATIONS?

**Write about:**
- **Customer Contractual Requirements**:
  - Customers may require compliance as contract term
  - Especially government or enterprise customers
  - Compliance as competitive differentiator
  
- **Supplier Obligations Pass-Through**:
  - [Organization]'s suppliers may have obligations that pass through
  - Subcontractor compliance requirements
  - Supply chain compliance mandates
  
- **Certification/Accreditation Requirements**:
  - Certifications [Organization] has or pursues (ISO 27001, SOC 2, etc.)
  - Certification bodies may require compliance with specific regulations
  - Industry accreditations with compliance components
  
- **Voluntary Commitments**:
  - [Organization] may voluntarily commit to frameworks
  - Competitive positioning
  - Industry best practice adoption

**Assessment Questions**:
- Do customer contracts require compliance with this regulation?
- Do supplier agreements create pass-through obligations?
- Is compliance required for certifications [Organization] holds/pursues?
- Has [Organization] made voluntary commitments?

## SECTION 4: Categorization Framework (Three-Tier)

### WRITING INSTRUCTIONS:
Define the THREE-TIER categorization aligned with ISMS-POL-00.

This determines how regulations are classified in the regulatory register.

### KEY CONTENT:

#### 4.1 Tier 1: Mandatory Compliance
Regulations with LEGAL OBLIGATION or contractual enforceability.

**Write about:**
- **Definition**: 
  - Regulatory requirements that are LEGALLY BINDING on [Organization]
  - Non-compliance results in legal penalties, fines, sanctions
  - Or contractually binding with enforceability
  
- **Criteria for Tier 1**:
  - Legal obligation in jurisdictions where [Organization] operates
  - Applies based on geographic, operational scope assessment
  - Contractual commitment with enforcement mechanisms
  - Certification/accreditation requirements with audit
  - High consequence of non-compliance
  
- **Examples (Generic)**:
  - Data protection laws in jurisdictions of operation
  - Sector-specific regulations where [Organization] operates
  - Contractual requirements from major customers
  - Certification requirements (ISO 27001, SOC 2)
  
- **Treatment**:
  - Full compliance required
  - Highest priority for resources
  - Regular compliance audits
  - Evidence maintained continuously

#### 4.2 Tier 2: Conditional Applicability
Regulations that MAY BECOME applicable or are PARTIALLY applicable.

**Write about:**
- **Definition**:
  - Potential future applicability
  - Currently not binding but may become so
  - Sector-specific regulations [Organization] may enter
  - Voluntary frameworks adopted for advantage
  
- **Criteria for Tier 2**:
  - Regulations in jurisdictions [Organization] is considering entering
  - Regulations for industries [Organization] may serve
  - Emerging regulations not yet in force
  - Voluntary frameworks [Organization] follows
  - Competitive differentiation frameworks
  
- **Examples (Generic)**:
  - Regulations in potential expansion markets
  - Industry-specific regulations for sectors [Organization] may enter
  - Draft/proposed regulations under consideration
  - Industry best practices adopted voluntarily
  
- **Treatment**:
  - Monitoring and readiness
  - Gap analysis to understand compliance effort
  - Strategic decision on adoption
  - Partial implementation possible

#### 4.3 Tier 3: Informational Reference
Frameworks used for GUIDANCE, BENCHMARKING, or MATURITY assessment.

**Write about:**
- **Definition**:
  - No legal or contractual obligation
  - Used as reference for best practices
  - Benchmarking and maturity assessment
  - Industry standards and frameworks
  
- **Criteria for Tier 3**:
  - Industry best practices (NIST, CIS Controls, etc.)
  - Frameworks used for maturity assessment
  - Guidelines for control design
  - No compliance obligation
  
- **Examples (Generic)**:
  - NIST Cybersecurity Framework
  - CIS Controls
  - OWASP guidelines
  - Industry-specific best practices
  
- **Treatment**:
  - Reference for control design
  - Benchmarking purposes
  - No formal compliance reporting
  - Used to enhance security posture

#### 4.4 Tier Assignment Decision Logic

**Write about:**
Create a decision tree or flowchart logic:
```
Is there a legal obligation? 
  → YES → Tier 1
  → NO → Continue

Is there a contractual obligation with enforcement?
  → YES → Tier 1
  → NO → Continue

Is there potential future applicability?
  → YES → Tier 2
  → NO → Continue

Is it used for guidance/benchmarking?
  → YES → Tier 3
  → NO → Not Applicable (don't add to ISMS-POL-00)
```

## SECTION 5: Documentation Requirements

### WRITING INSTRUCTIONS:
Define what documentation is required when performing applicability assessments.

### KEY CONTENT:

#### 5.1 Applicability Assessment Documentation
For each regulation assessed:

**Write about:**
- **Minimum Required Documentation**:
  - Regulation identification (name, jurisdiction, authority, effective date)
  - Applicability criteria evaluation (geographic, operational, contractual)
  - Assessment outcome (Applicable/Not Applicable/Tier assignment)
  - Rationale for determination (WHY applicable or not)
  - Evidence supporting determination
  - Assessor name and date
  - Reviewer/approver name and date
  
- **Documentation Template**:
  - Standardized assessment form
  - Ensures consistency across assessments
  - Captures all required information
  - Supports audit trail
  
- **Evidence to Attach**:
  - Legal opinion (if obtained)
  - Regulatory text or citation
  - Jurisdictional analysis
  - Contractual requirements (if applicable)
  - Decision records

#### 5.2 Approval Workflow
Who must approve applicability determinations?

**Write about:**
- **Assessment Level** (initial determination):
  - Performed by Compliance Officer or Legal
  
- **Review Level** (validation):
  - ISMS Manager reviews for ISMS implications
  - Legal counsel reviews for legal accuracy
  
- **Approval Level** (final decision):
  - Tier 1 determinations: Executive approval (high impact)
  - Tier 2/3 determinations: ISMS Manager approval
  - Disputed determinations: Executive escalation
  
- **Approval Records**:
  - Electronic signatures or documented approvals
  - Date and role of approvers
  - Any conditions or caveats

#### 5.3 Adding to ISMS-POL-00
Once assessment is approved:

**Write about:**
- **Update Process**:
  - Add regulation to appropriate tier in ISMS-POL-00
  - Include all metadata (jurisdiction, authority, effective date, etc.)
  - Reference the applicability assessment documentation
  - Version control for POL-00
  
- **Communication**:
  - Notify stakeholders of new applicable regulation
  - Brief affected control owners
  - Update regulatory compliance dashboard

## SECTION 6: Review Frequency & Triggers

### WRITING INSTRUCTIONS:
Define when applicability assessments are performed and reviewed.

### KEY CONTENT:

#### 6.1 Periodic Review Schedule
Regular reassessment of applicability:

**Write about:**
- **Annual Review** (minimum):
  - Review ALL regulations in ISMS-POL-00
  - Confirm applicability still valid
  - Check for changes in circumstances
  - Update tier if needed
  
- **Tier-Specific Review**:
  - Tier 1: Annual review (mandatory)
  - Tier 2: Annual or biennial review
  - Tier 3: Biennial review or as needed
  
- **Documentation**:
  - Review date recorded
  - Outcome documented (no change, tier change, removed)
  - Approver signature

#### 6.2 Event-Driven Review Triggers
Specific events that trigger reassessment:

**Write about:**
- **Organizational Changes**:
  - Entering new jurisdiction
  - New service offerings
  - Mergers/acquisitions
  - Significant size change
  
- **Regulatory Changes**:
  - Regulation amended
  - New regulations enacted
  - Regulations repealed or superseded
  - Thresholds change
  
- **Contractual Changes**:
  - New customer contracts with requirements
  - Contract renewals with changed terms
  - New certifications pursued
  
- **Risk Events**:
  - Compliance gap identified
  - Regulatory inquiry or investigation
  - Industry enforcement action (peer)

#### 6.3 Escalation for Applicability Disputes
When there is disagreement on applicability:

**Write about:**
- **Internal Disputes**:
  - Between Compliance and Business units
  - Between Legal and ISMS Manager
  - Between Control Owners
  
- **Escalation Path**:
  - Initial: Compliance Officer + ISMS Manager discussion
  - If unresolved: Legal counsel consultation
  - If still unresolved: Executive management decision
  
- **External Consultation**:
  - When to engage external legal counsel
  - When to seek regulatory authority guidance
  
- **Decision Record**:
  - Document the dispute
  - Document the resolution
  - Document the rationale
