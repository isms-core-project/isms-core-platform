## D.2 What is Web Filtering?

**Web Filtering** = Technology that monitors and controls access to websites based on security policies.

**Purpose**:
- 🛡️ Protect you and the organization from web-based threats (malware, phishing)
- 📋 Enforce acceptable use of internet resources
- 🔍 Monitor web access for security incidents
- ⚖️ Comply with legal and regulatory requirements

**What it does**:
- Blocks access to known malicious websites
- Blocks or monitors access to specific website categories (varies by organization policy)
- Logs web access for security monitoring
- Alerts security team to threats

**What it does NOT do**:
- Monitor content of your communications (emails, chats)
- Read files you upload/download (unless scanning for malware)
- Block all personal internet use (depends on organization policy)
- Replace your judgment (you are still responsible for safe web practices)

## D.3 What Gets Blocked? (Threat Protection - ALWAYS)

These are **ALWAYS** blocked for your protection (S2.1):

| Threat Type | What It Is | Why Blocked |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| 🦠 **Malware** | Sites distributing viruses, trojans, ransomware | Prevents system infection |
| 🎣 **Phishing** | Fake login pages stealing credentials | Protects your accounts |
| 🤖 **C2 (Command & Control)** | Infrastructure used by malware to communicate | Prevents data theft |
| 💥 **Exploits** | Sites exploiting software vulnerabilities | Prevents system compromise |
| 💰 **Ransomware** | Sites distributing or supporting ransomware | Prevents encryption attacks |

**If these are blocked**: This is the system protecting you. Do NOT attempt to bypass.

## D.4 Category Filtering - Know Your Organization's Approach

Organizations take different approaches to category filtering (S2.2):

### Approach A: Restrictive Blocking

- **What**: Specific categories blocked (e.g., social media, gambling, adult content)
- **Why**: Risk reduction, compliance requirements
- **Your responsibility**: Respect blocks, use exception process if business need

### Approach B: Trust-Based Monitoring

- **What**: Most categories allowed, activity monitored
- **Why**: Employee autonomy, business enablement
- **Your responsibility**: Use internet responsibly, follow Acceptable Use Policy

### Approach C: Hybrid (Risk-Based)

- **What**: High-risk categories blocked, medium-risk monitored, low-risk allowed
- **Why**: Balanced approach
- **Your responsibility**: Understand which categories are blocked in your environment

**Check with your IT/Security team to understand your organization's approach.**

## D.5 Common Scenarios - What to Do

### Scenario 1: Legitimate Site is Blocked (False Positive)

**What happened**: You tried to access a work-related site and it was blocked.

**What to do**:
- ✅ Report to IT Help Desk or Security Team
- ✅ Provide: Site URL, what you were trying to do, business justification
- ✅ Security team will review (typically within 24-48 hours)
- ✅ If legitimate, site will be unblocked

**Do NOT**: Use proxy or VPN to bypass (policy violation)

---

### Scenario 2: Need Access to Blocked Category (Business Need)

**What happened**: You need access to a blocked category for work (e.g., social media for marketing).

**What to do**:
- ✅ Submit exception request (Annex B - Exception Request Form)
- ✅ Provide clear business justification
- ✅ Wait for approval (3-5 business days standard, 1 day urgent)
- ✅ Use responsibly if approved

**Do NOT**: Bypass filtering while waiting for approval

---

### Scenario 3: Malware/Phishing Site Blocked

**What happened**: You got a "blocked - malware" or "blocked - phishing" message.

**What to do**:
- ✅ **STOP** - Do not try to access the site
- ✅ Close browser tab/window
- ✅ If you clicked a link in an email: Report email to Security Team (possible phishing campaign)
- ✅ If you entered credentials before block: Contact Security Team immediately for password reset

*This is the system protecting you. Be grateful, not frustrated.* 😊

---

### Scenario 4: Site Seems Suspicious But Not Blocked

**What happened**: You accessed a site that seems sketchy (asks for unusual info, looks unprofessional, unexpected popup).

**What to do**:
- ✅ **CLOSE** the site immediately
- ✅ Do NOT enter any information (credentials, credit cards, personal data)
- ✅ Report to Security Team (helps improve filtering)
- ✅ Run antivirus scan on your computer (if you clicked around on the site)

*Remember: Filtering isn't perfect. Trust your instincts.*

---

### Scenario 5: Multiple Sites Blocked (Not Working)

**What happened**: Many sites are blocked or internet seems completely restricted.

**What to do**:
- ✅ Check if other colleagues have same issue (system-wide problem?)
- ✅ Report to IT Help Desk
- ✅ Be patient - may be system maintenance or technical issue
- ✅ Do NOT try alternative internet access (mobile hotspot, personal VPN) for work tasks

*If system failure: IT/Security working to restore service. Use offline work in the meantime.*

## D.6 Your Responsibilities

As a user, you are responsible for:

### ✅ Following Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

- Use internet resources for authorized business purposes
- Respect organizational policies and filtering controls
- Exercise good judgment online

### ✅ Security Awareness

- Recognize phishing attempts (suspicious emails, unexpected links)
- Avoid risky websites (piracy, illegal content, unverified downloads)
- Protect your credentials (never enter passwords on suspicious sites)

### ✅ Reporting

- Report false positives (legitimate sites blocked)
- Report security concerns (suspicious sites, phishing emails)
- Report policy violations (if you see colleagues bypassing controls)

### ✅ Compliance

- Do NOT attempt to bypass web filtering (proxies, VPNs, Tor)
- Do NOT share credentials to bypass user-based policies
- Use exception process for legitimate business needs

### ❌ Violations May Result In:

- Verbal or written warning
- Restriction of internet access
- Suspension or termination (for serious or repeated violations)

## D.7 Decision Tree: "Should I Access This Site?"
```
START: You want to access a website
    ↓
Is it BLOCKED by web filtering?
    ├─ YES → Go to Section D.8 (Site Blocked - What Now?)
    └─ NO → Continue ↓
         ↓
Is it work-related?
    ├─ YES → Is the site trustworthy?
    │        ├─ YES → Access OK ✅
    │        └─ NO/UNSURE → Don't access, report to Security Team ⚠️
    └─ NO (Personal) → Does your organization allow personal use?
             ├─ YES (Trust-based or hybrid) → Use responsibly ✅
             └─ NO (Restrictive) → Don't access ❌
```

**When in doubt, ask IT/Security. Better safe than sorry.**

## D.8 Decision Tree: "Site is Blocked - What Now?"
```
START: Site is blocked by web filtering
    ↓
Why is it blocked?
    ├─ MALWARE/PHISHING/C2 → DO NOT ACCESS. Close browser. Report if clicked from email. ⛔
    │
    ├─ CATEGORY POLICY → Do you have business need?
    │   ├─ YES → Submit exception request (Annex B) 📝
    │   └─ NO → Respect the block, find alternative ❌
    │
    └─ FALSE POSITIVE (legitimate site blocked) → Report to IT Help Desk 🛠️
```

**NEVER bypass filtering using proxies, VPNs, or other tools. Policy violation.**

## D.9 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1: Is my web browsing monitored?**  
A: Yes, for security purposes. Web access is logged (sites visited, time, user). This is for threat detection and incident response, not micromanagement. You should have no expectation of privacy on organizational networks.

**Q2: Can I use social media / streaming / shopping sites?**  
A: Depends on your organization's policy (see D.4). Check with IT/Security or your manager.

**Q3: Can I access personal email (Gmail, Yahoo, etc.)?**  
A: Usually yes (if not blocked), but use organizational email for work. Personal email may be monitored if accessed on company network.

**Q4: What if I need to access a blocked site for research or competitive analysis?**  
A: Submit exception request with business justification. Research and competitive intelligence are valid use cases.

**Q5: Can I use my personal phone's hotspot to bypass filtering?**  
A: Technically yes, but it's a policy violation if using for work tasks that bypass filtering. Also, you miss security protections (no malware blocking on personal hotspot).

**Q6: I got blocked for a site I didn't try to access. Why?**  
A: Could be malware on your computer trying to "phone home" (C2), or background process (app, browser plugin). Contact Security Team immediately for investigation.

**Q7: How long does it take to unblock a false positive?**  
A: Usually 24-48 hours for review. Urgent business needs can be expedited (contact IT Help Desk, explain urgency).

**Q8: Can I get an exception for my whole department?**  
A: Yes, department managers can request group exceptions. Requires business justification and approval.

**Q9: What happens if I try to bypass filtering?**  
A: Policy violation. First offense: warning and education. Repeated: disciplinary action (written warning, suspension, termination depending on severity).

**Q10: Is web filtering perfect? Will it catch every threat?**  
A: No. Web filtering is very effective but not 100%. You are still the last line of defense. Use common sense and report suspicious sites.

## D.12 Quick Tips for Safe Web Browsing

✅ **Think before you click** - Verify links before clicking (hover to see real URL)  
✅ **Look for HTTPS** - Secure sites show padlock icon and "https://"  
✅ **Be suspicious of urgent requests** - "Verify your account NOW!" = likely phishing  
✅ **Verify sender before clicking links** - Email from CEO asking for password reset? Call to confirm.  
✅ **Don't trust popups** - "Your computer is infected! Click here!" = scam  
✅ **Keep software updated** - Enable automatic updates for browsers and plugins  
✅ **Use strong passwords** - Don't reuse work passwords on personal sites  
✅ **Report suspicious activity** - When in doubt, ask Security Team

**Remember: Web filtering helps protect you, but YOU are the most important security control.** 🧠🛡️
