## 3. Sheet 2: Completeness Check Specifications

### 3.1 Purpose

Verify that the inventory captures all assets within scope—nothing is missing.

### 3.2 Verification Methods

**Method 1: Discovery Reconciliation**

*Purpose:* Compare inventory to automated discovery results

*Procedure:*
1. Run automated discovery tools (network scan, endpoint scan, cloud API)
2. Export discovery results
3. Match discovered assets to inventory records
4. Calculate: `Reconciliation % = (Matched Assets / Discovered Assets) × 100%`
5. Investigate unmatched assets (new discoveries or inventory gaps)

*Data Captured in Sheet:*
- Discovery tool used
- Date of scan
- Total assets discovered
- Assets matched to inventory
- Assets not in inventory (new discoveries)
- Reconciliation percentage
- Target: ≥95%

**Method 2: Management Attestation**

*Purpose:* Business leaders certify their areas are complete

*Procedure:*
1. Generate asset list per department/business unit
2. Send to department heads for review
3. Manager reviews and certifies completeness OR identifies missing items
4. Collect signed attestations
5. Calculate: `Attestation % = (Departments Attested / Total Departments) × 100%`

*Data Captured in Sheet:*
- Department name
- Manager name
- Assets assigned to department
- Attestation status (✅ Complete / ⏳ Pending / ❌ Issues Found)
- Attestation date
- Missing items identified
- Target: 100% attestation

**Method 3: Sample Deep-Dive**

*Purpose:* Intensive discovery in sample locations to estimate completeness

*Procedure:*
1. Select 3-5 sample locations/departments (stratified sampling)
2. Conduct exhaustive discovery (automated + manual)
3. Compare findings to inventory
4. Calculate: `Deep-Dive % = (Inventoried Assets / Found Assets) × 100%`
5. Extrapolate to overall completeness estimate

*Data Captured in Sheet:*
- Sample location
- Discovery methods used
- Assets found
- Assets already in inventory
- Missing assets
- Completeness percentage per sample
- Overall estimate

**Method 4: User Survey**

*Purpose:* Ask users if they know of assets not in inventory

*Procedure:*
1. Survey sample of users (e.g., 20% of organization)
2. Ask: "Do you use any systems/information not in the inventory?"
3. Investigate reported items
4. Calculate: `Survey Score = % of responses that identified no gaps`

*Data Captured in Sheet:*
- Survey respondents
- Date completed
- Items identified
- Investigation results
- Score

### 3.3 Completeness Score Formula

**Overall Completeness Score:**

```
Completeness Score = 
    (0.40 × Discovery_Reconciliation_%) +
    (0.30 × Management_Attestation_%) +
    (0.20 × Sample_DeepDive_%) +
    (0.10 × User_Survey_Score)
```

**Weights Rationale:**
- Discovery Reconciliation (40%): Most objective, automated
- Management Attestation (30%): Business accountability
- Sample Deep-Dive (20%): High confidence but limited scope
- User Survey (10%): Supplementary signal

**Target:** ≥95% overall completeness

**Interpretation:**
- **95-100%**: Excellent (✅ Green)
- **85-94%**: Acceptable (🟡 Yellow) - Minor gaps
- **<85%**: Needs Improvement (🔴 Red) - Significant gaps

## 4. Sheet 3: Accuracy Verification Specifications

### 4.1 Purpose

Verify that the inventory data is correct—attributes match reality.

### 4.2 Verification Methods

**Method 1: Attribute Sampling**

*Purpose:* Statistically sample and verify asset attributes

*Procedure:*
1. Select random sample (e.g., 5% of assets, minimum 30 items)
2. For each sampled asset, verify key attributes:
   - **Technical attributes**: IP address, hostname, location (automated check)
   - **Ownership**: Contact owner to confirm (email/interview)
   - **Classification**: Review with owner (is it correct?)
   - **Status**: Verify asset is actually active/archived/etc.
3. Document verification results (correct, incorrect, unable to verify)
4. Calculate: `Accuracy % = (Correct Records / Sampled Records) × 100%`

*Sample Size Guidance:*
- Small inventory (<100 assets): Sample 20% (minimum 20)
- Medium inventory (100-500): Sample 10% (minimum 30)
- Large inventory (>500): Sample 5% (minimum 50)

*Data Captured in Sheet:*
- Sample size
- Sampling method (random, stratified)
- Asset IDs sampled
- Attributes verified per asset
- Verification results (✅ Correct / ❌ Incorrect / ❓ Unable to Verify)
- Accuracy percentage
- Target: ≥95%

**Method 2: Owner Attestation**

*Purpose:* Asset owners certify their assets are accurate

*Procedure:*
1. Send each owner their complete asset list
2. Owner reviews for accuracy
3. Owner confirms OR corrects inaccuracies
4. Calculate: `Owner_Attestation % = (Owners Attested / Total Owners) × 100%`

*Data Captured in Sheet:*
- Owner name
- Assets assigned
- Attestation status
- Corrections provided
- Attestation date

**Method 3: System Integration Validation**

*Purpose:* Cross-check inventory against authoritative sources

*Procedure:*
1. Compare inventory to:
   - **CMDB**: Technical attributes (IP, hostname, config)
   - **Active Directory**: System/user accounts
   - **HR System**: Personnel records
   - **Procurement**: Purchase records
2. Identify discrepancies
3. Calculate: `Integration_Consistency % = (Matching Records / Checked Records) × 100%`

*Data Captured in Sheet:*
- System checked
- Records compared
- Matches found
- Discrepancies found
- Consistency percentage

### 4.3 Accuracy Score Formula

**Overall Accuracy Score:**

```
Accuracy Score = 
    (0.50 × Attribute_Sampling_%) +
    (0.30 × Owner_Attestation_%) +
    (0.20 × System_Integration_%)
```

**Target:** ≥95% overall accuracy

## 5. Sheet 4: Owner Assignment Status Specifications

### 5.1 Purpose

Verify that all assets have assigned, acknowledged owners who understand their responsibilities.

### 5.2 Metrics

**Metric 1: Owner Assignment Coverage**

```
Assignment_Coverage % = (Assets_With_Owners / Total_Assets) × 100%
```

**Target:** 100% (mandatory requirement)

**Data Captured:**
- Total assets
- Assets with assigned owners
- Assets without owners (list them!)
- Coverage percentage

**Metric 2: Owner Acknowledgment Rate**

```
Acknowledgment_Rate % = (Owners_Acknowledged / Total_Owners) × 100%
```

**Target:** 100%

**Owner Acknowledgment Requirements:**
- Owner has received notification of assignment
- Owner has reviewed assigned assets
- Owner understands owner responsibilities (per ISMS-POL-A.5.9-S2 Section 2.3)
- Owner has signed acknowledgment form (ISMS-POL-A.5.9-S5.B)

**Data Captured:**
- Owner name
- Assets assigned (count)
- Acknowledgment status (✅ / ⏳ / ❌)
- Acknowledgment date
- Form location/reference

**Metric 3: Owner Capability Assessment**

*Purpose:* Ensure owners have appropriate knowledge and authority

*Assessment Questions (per owner):*
1. Does owner understand asset classification? (✅ Yes / ❌ No)
2. Does owner have authority to make access decisions? (✅ Yes / ❌ No)
3. Has owner received ISMS training? (✅ Yes / ❌ No)
4. Does owner have sufficient time to fulfill responsibilities? (✅ Yes / ❌ No)

```
Capability_Score % = (Owners_Meeting_Criteria / Total_Owners) × 100%
```

**Target:** ≥95%

### 5.3 Owner Assignment Score Formula

**Overall Owner Assignment Score:**

```
Owner_Score = 
    (0.40 × Assignment_Coverage_%) +
    (0.40 × Acknowledgment_Rate_%) +
    (0.20 × Capability_Score_%)
```

**Target:** ≥95% overall

**Note:** Assignment Coverage MUST be 100% for compliance. If <100%, overall score is automatically non-compliant regardless of other metrics.

## 6. Sheet 5: Review Schedule Tracking Specifications

### 6.1 Purpose

Verify that asset reviews are happening on schedule based on criticality/classification.

### 6.2 Review Period Requirements

**From ISMS-POL-A.5.9-S2:**

| Classification/Criticality | Review Period | Max Days |
|----------------------------|---------------|----------|
| Strictly Confidential / Critical | Quarterly | 90 days |
| Confidential / High | Semi-annually | 180 days |
| Internal / Medium | Annually | 365 days |
| Public / Low | Every 2 years | 730 days |

### 6.3 Metrics

**Metric: Review Currency**

```
Review_Currency % = (Assets_Reviewed_OnTime / Total_Active_Assets) × 100%
```

**"Reviewed On-Time" Definition:**
- Last_Reviewed_Date exists
- Next_Review_Date = Last_Reviewed_Date + Review_Period
- Current_Date ≤ Next_Review_Date

**Target:** ≥90%

### 6.4 Data Captured

**Summary Statistics:**
- Total active assets
- Assets with review dates
- Assets reviewed on-time
- Assets overdue <30 days
- Assets overdue >30 days
- Review currency percentage

**Overdue Reviews List:**
- Asset ID
- Asset Name
- Classification/Criticality
- Owner
- Last Reviewed Date
- Next Review Date (due)
- Days Overdue
- Escalation Status

**Review by Classification/Criticality:**
- Breakdown of review currency by classification level
- Identify which categories need attention

## 7. Sheet 6: Gap Analysis Specifications

### 7.1 Purpose

Document all gaps identified during assessment and prioritize remediation.

### 7.2 Gap Types

**Completeness Gaps:**
- Assets discovered but not inventoried
- Departments not attested
- Known systems missing from inventory

**Accuracy Gaps:**
- Incorrect asset attributes
- Outdated information
- Misclassifications

**Owner Gaps:**
- Assets without owners
- Owners not acknowledged
- Owners lacking capability

**Process Gaps:**
- Missing workflows
- Inadequate integration
- Insufficient documentation

### 7.3 Data Captured Per Gap

**Gap Identification:**
- Gap ID (GAP-001, GAP-002, etc.)
- Gap Type (Completeness, Accuracy, Owner, Process)
- Description (what is the gap?)
- Source (how was it discovered?)
- Date Identified

**Impact Assessment:**
- Affected Assets (count or list)
- Security Impact (High, Medium, Low)
- Compliance Impact (Critical, Significant, Minor)
- Business Impact (description)

**Root Cause Analysis:**
- Why did this gap occur?
- Process failure? Tool limitation? Human error?

**Priority:**
- 🔴 Critical: Immediate action required (fix within 1 week)
- 🟡 High: Significant issue (fix within 1 month)
- 🟢 Medium: Notable issue (fix within 3 months)
- ⚪ Low: Minor issue (fix within 6 months)

**Priority Criteria:**
```
Priority = f(Security_Impact, Compliance_Impact, Business_Impact)

Critical: High Security + Critical Compliance
High: High Security OR Significant Compliance
Medium: Medium Security OR Minor Compliance
Low: Low Security + No Compliance impact
```

### 7.4 Sheet Layout

**Columns:**
1. Gap ID
2. Gap Type
3. Description
4. Affected Assets (count)
5. Security Impact
6. Compliance Impact
7. Business Impact
8. Root Cause
9. Priority
10. Status (🔴 Open / 🟡 In Progress / ✅ Closed)
11. Assigned To
12. Target Date
13. Notes

## 8. Sheet 7: Remediation Tracking Specifications

### 8.1 Purpose

Track remediation actions for identified gaps from Gap ID to verified closure.

### 8.2 Remediation Workflow

```
Gap Identified (Sheet 6: Gap Analysis)
         │
         ▼
Remediation Plan Created (Sheet 7: Remediation Tracking)
         │
         ├─ What: Remediation action
         ├─ Who: Responsible party
         ├─ When: Due date
         └─ How: Verification method
         │
         ▼
Action Taken (Status: In Progress → Complete)
         │
         ▼
Verification (Evidence collected)
         │
         ▼
Gap Closed (Update Sheet 6 status)
```

### 8.3 Data Captured Per Remediation

**Linkage:**
- Gap ID (reference to Sheet 6)
- Remediation ID (REM-001, REM-002, etc.)

**Plan:**
- Remediation Action (what will be done?)
- Responsible Party (who will do it?)
- Due Date (when must it be complete?)
- Resources Required (budget, tools, people)

**Execution:**
- Status (📋 Planned / ⏳ In Progress / ✅ Complete / ❌ Blocked)
- Start Date (when did work begin?)
- Completion Date (when was it finished?)
- Blockers/Issues (what's preventing completion?)

**Verification:**
- Verification Method (how will we prove it's fixed?)
- Verification Date (when was it verified?)
- Verified By (who confirmed?)
- Evidence Location (link to proof)

**Closure:**
- Gap Status (update Sheet 6)
- Lessons Learned (what did we learn?)
- Preventive Actions (how to avoid recurrence?)

**Columns:**

## 12. Assessment Cycle Timing

**Initial Assessment:** 6-8 weeks after inventory completion

**Ongoing Assessments:**
- **Quarterly**: Review currency check, gap remediation progress
- **Semi-Annually**: Completeness spot-checks, owner attestation
- **Annually**: Full comprehensive assessment (all methods)

**Trigger Events for Unscheduled Assessment:**
- Significant organizational changes (merger, restructuring)
- Major technology changes (cloud migration, system refresh)
- Security incidents affecting multiple assets
- Audit findings
- Executive request

## 13. Quality Standards

### 13.1 Assessment Quality Criteria

**Objective Evidence:**
- All claims supported by documented evidence
- Sampling methodology defensible (random, stratified)
- Verification procedures documented and repeatable
- Results reproducible by third party (auditor)

**Professional Standards:**
- Assessment conducted by qualified personnel
- Independence maintained (assessors ≠ asset owners)
- Conflicts of interest disclosed and managed
- Professional skepticism applied

**Documentation Quality:**
- Complete (all sections filled)
- Accurate (findings match evidence)
- Clear (auditor can understand without explanation)
- Traceable (evidence links work)

### 13.2 Auditor Expectations

**What Auditors Will Check:**
1. Did [Organization] conduct completeness verification?
   - Evidence: Discovery reconciliation reports, attestation forms
2. Did [Organization] verify accuracy?
   - Evidence: Sample verification records, owner attestations
3. Are all assets assigned owners?
   - Evidence: Owner register, acknowledgment forms
4. Are reviews happening on schedule?
   - Evidence: Review tracking data, overdue reports
5. Are gaps being remediated?
   - Evidence: Remediation tracking, closure verification

**Audit Trail Requirements:**
- Who conducted assessment (person, date)
- What methods used (documented procedures)
- What found (results, evidence)
- What action taken (remediation plans, completion)

## 14. Summary

**Workbook 3: Quality & Compliance** is the verification engine of the A.5.9 assessment framework. It provides systematic, evidence-based verification that:

1. ✅ **Inventory is complete** (we know what we have)
2. ✅ **Inventory is accurate** (the data is correct)
3. ✅ **Owners are assigned** (accountability is clear)
4. ✅ **Reviews are current** (information is up-to-date)
5. ✅ **Gaps are managed** (issues are tracked to closure)

**Key Success Factors:**
- Use multiple verification methods (triangulation)
- Document everything (evidence-based)
- Be objective (don't fool yourself)
- Track to closure (remediate gaps)
- Maintain over time (continuous compliance)
