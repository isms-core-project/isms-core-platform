# Purpose and Scope

## Assessment Objective

This assessment verifies [Organisation]'s compliance with ISMS-POL-01 (ISMS Governance and Decision-Making Framework) as aligned with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 5.3.

**Clause 5.3 Requirement:**
> *Top management shall ensure that the responsibilities and authorities for roles relevant to information security are assigned and communicated within the organisation.*

**Assessment Focus:**

- Authority boundaries for ISMS decision-making (technical, regulatory, risk)
- Regulatory applicability monitoring and SoA justification
- Exception handling process integrity and volume control
- Change management for compliance criteria
- Governance review effectiveness and continual improvement

## Relationship to Parent Policy

This assessment implements verification procedures for **ISMS-POL-01 (ISMS Governance and Decision-Making Framework)**, covering its five governance domains:

- **Domain 1**: Authority Boundaries — who is authorised to make which ISMS decisions
- **Domain 2**: Applicability Decisions — how regulatory scope is determined and maintained
- **Domain 3**: Exception Handling — how non-compliance is managed within defined process
- **Domain 4**: Change Management — how compliance criteria changes are identified, assessed, and implemented
- **Domain 5**: Governance Review — how ISMS governance effectiveness is reviewed and improved

**Evidence Collected**: Decision logs, approval signatures, meeting minutes, SoA documentation, exception register entries, change log entries, assessment reports

## Assessment Frequency

**Standard Schedule:**

- **Annual**: Full governance framework assessment (aligns with ISO 27001 surveillance cycle)
- **Triggered**: After significant organisational change, audit findings on governance, or change of CISO/key governance role

**Why Annual?**

- Governance frameworks are relatively stable — quarterly assessment is not required
- Annual frequency aligns with management review (ISO 27001:2022 Clause 9.3)
- Sufficient to demonstrate operational governance effectiveness to auditors

## Assessment Ownership

**Primary Owner**: CISO or Internal Audit Lead

**Participants**:

- Legal/Compliance Officer (for Domains 1 and 2)
- Executive Management representative (for Domains 1 and 3)
- Information Security Manager (for Domains 3, 4, and 5)

**Time Investment**: 3-4 hours for annual assessment, 1-2 hours for triggered re-assessment

# Assessment Methodology

## Assessment Approach

This assessment uses a **document and evidence verification approach**:

**Step 1: Evidence Gathering**

- Identify and collect evidence for each domain before starting the workbook
- Gather approvals, meeting minutes, logs, registers, and reports
- Organise evidence in the Evidence Register (Sheet 7) before completing domain sheets

**Step 2: Domain-by-Domain Verification**

- Work through each domain sheet systematically (Sheets 2–6)
- For each requirement, verify the evidence, then set the Compliance Status
- Document the Evidence Reference and Verified Date for every row

**Step 3: Gap Identification**

- Any requirement marked Partial or Non-Compliant requires a Notes entry
- Notes must describe: what is missing, why it is deficient, and the recommended remediation
- Use the Notes column to capture findings that will feed into corrective action

**Step 4: Summary Review**

- Complete the Summary Dashboard (auto-calculated from domain sheets)
- Review overall compliance picture before escalating to Approval Sign-Off

## Compliance Status Definitions

| Status | Meaning | Evidence Required |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| **Compliant** | Requirement fully met — evidence is present and complete | Documented evidence referenced |
| **Partial** | Requirement partially met — evidence exists but has gaps | Evidence with gap description in Notes |
| **Non-Compliant** | Requirement not met — evidence absent or process not followed | Notes must describe the gap |
| **N/A** | Requirement not applicable to this organisation | Notes must explain why N/A |

**Auditor Expectation**: Every row must have an Evidence_Reference unless the status is N/A. A Compliance_Status of "Compliant" with no evidence reference is not accepted.

## Sheet 2: Domain 1 – Authority Boundaries

**Purpose**: Verify that ISMS decisions are authorised at the correct level per ISMS-POL-01.

**Domain Summary**: Domain 1 tests whether the three categories of ISMS decision (technical controls, regulatory applicability, and risk acceptance) are approved by the right people, and whether those people meet the competence criteria defined in the policy.

**Requirements in this domain**: GOV-01 to GOV-04

---

### GOV-01: Technical control implementations approved by CISO

**What you are verifying**: Policy approval signatures on technical control implementations confirm CISO authority over technical controls.

**How to verify**:

1. Obtain the set of POL documents most recently approved or updated
2. Check each policy's approval record — specifically the CISO signature
3. Confirm the CISO was the approving authority (not delegated to a lower role without authorisation)
4. If sub-policies or IMP documents required CISO approval, verify those too

**Evidence to collect**:

- Signed approval page from each relevant POL document
- Email approvals where CISO approved via email
- If a delegation exists, the delegation authorisation document

**Compliance_Status guidance**:

- **Compliant**: CISO approval signatures present on all technical control POLs
- **Partial**: Some POLs have signatures, others are draft or unsigned
- **Non-Compliant**: POLs approved by someone other than CISO without documented delegation, or no approvals at all

**Common pitfalls**:

- Policies "approved" via verbal instruction with no documentation → Non-Compliant
- Old approvals from a previous CISO who has left → requires re-approval or delegation confirmation
- Approval email buried in inbox — centralise these in the Evidence Register before assessment day

---

### GOV-02: Regulatory applicability determinations approved by Legal/Compliance

**What you are verifying**: POL-00 Section 8 contains Legal/Compliance approval signatures on regulatory applicability decisions.

**How to verify**:

1. Open ISMS-POL-00 Section 8 (Approvals section)
2. Confirm Legal/Compliance Officer has signed
3. For any in-year regulatory changes that triggered applicability updates, verify Legal/Compliance was consulted and approved

**Evidence to collect**:

- POL-00 approval record (Section 8)
- Any supplementary approval emails for triggered regulatory determinations
- Minutes of meetings where regulatory applicability was discussed with Legal

**Compliance_Status guidance**:

- **Compliant**: Legal/Compliance approval present in POL-00 Section 8
- **Partial**: Approval exists but pre-dates a significant regulatory change that has not been re-approved
- **Non-Compliant**: No Legal/Compliance approval on POL-00

---

### GOV-03: Risk acceptance decisions approved by Executive Management

**What you are verifying**: The Risk Acceptance Register shows Executive Management signatures on all risk acceptance decisions — not just CISO.

**How to verify**:

1. Open the Risk Acceptance Register
2. For each entry, confirm the approving authority field shows Executive Management (or equivalent — Board, CEO, COO depending on organisational structure)
3. Confirm no risk acceptances were approved by CISO alone (CISO can approve compensating controls, not risk acceptances)

**Evidence to collect**:

- Risk Acceptance Register extract (current year)
- Meeting minutes where risk acceptances were approved
- Signed risk acceptance forms

**Compliance_Status guidance**:

- **Compliant**: All risk acceptances have Executive Management approval signatures
- **Partial**: Most have Executive approval; some older entries signed only by CISO
- **Non-Compliant**: No Executive Management approval on risk acceptances

**Note**: Risk acceptances approved by CISO alone are a finding per ISMS-POL-01, which explicitly distinguishes CISO authority (Alternative Controls) from Executive authority (Risk Acceptance).

---

### GOV-04: Competence requirements verified for all decision-makers

**What you are verifying**: Section 2.3 of ISMS-POL-01 defines competence criteria for CISO, Legal, DPO, and Executive Management. Verify these criteria have been checked for the current incumbents.

**How to verify**:

1. Review ISMS-POL-01 Section 2.3 for the defined criteria
2. For each role (CISO, Legal, DPO, Executive Management), locate evidence of competence verification:
   - Qualifications, certifications, or equivalent experience
   - Role-specific training records
   - Annual performance review confirming ISMS governance competence
3. Confirm verification is current (within the last 12 months, or since the last role change)

**Evidence to collect**:

- CV/résumé extracts showing qualifications, or HR confirmation letter
- Training completion certificates (e.g., CISM, ISO 27001 Lead Auditor, legal CPD records)
- Performance review records confirming ISMS governance responsibilities

**Compliance_Status guidance**:

- **Compliant**: Competence verified for all four role categories per Section 2.3 criteria
- **Partial**: Competence verified for some roles, others not documented
- **Non-Compliant**: No competence verification process in place

---

**Domain 1 Tips**:

1. **Do this domain first**: Authority boundary verification is the foundation — if approvals are missing, every other domain will have cascade issues
2. **Centralise approvals**: Maintain a single "Approvals" folder in your evidence repository — avoid hunting for email approvals at assessment time
3. **Check role changes**: If the CISO or Legal officer changed in the past year, ensure new incumbent approvals exist on key documents
4. **Delegation is acceptable if documented**: If CISO delegated Tier 3 approvals to Security Manager, the delegation must be in writing
5. **Risk acceptance ≠ alternative control**: These are explicitly different in POL-01 — do not conflate them when assessing approval authority

## Sheet 3: Domain 2 – Applicability Decisions

**Purpose**: Verify that regulatory applicability is monitored continuously and that the Statement of Applicability is complete and current.

**Domain Summary**: Domain 2 tests the operational health of the regulatory monitoring function. It checks quarterly monitoring cadence, triggered assessments for business changes, SoA completeness for all 93 ISO 27001 controls, and the Challenge Protocol if it was invoked.

**Requirements in this domain**: GOV-05 to GOV-08

---

### GOV-05: POL-00 quarterly monitoring completed

**What you are verifying**: Four complete quarters of regulatory monitoring logs exist, each with Legal/Compliance and CISO signatures.

**How to verify**:

1. Open the quarterly monitoring log (maintained per POL-00 Section 5)
2. Confirm four entries exist for the assessment year (Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4)
3. Verify each entry has:
   - Date of review
   - Regulatory sources checked
   - Finding summary (including "no changes" if applicable)
   - Legal/Compliance signature
   - CISO signature

**Evidence to collect**:

- Quarterly monitoring log (all four quarters)
- If delivered as meeting minutes, the signed minutes from each quarterly review

**Compliance_Status guidance**:

- **Compliant**: All four quarters documented with dual signatures
- **Partial**: Monitoring done but some quarters missing signatures, or one quarter missing entirely
- **Non-Compliant**: Fewer than three quarters completed, or monitoring process not in place

**Tip**: "No regulatory changes this quarter" is a valid and acceptable finding — as long as it is documented. Absence of documentation is the problem, not absence of changes.

---

### GOV-06: Triggered assessments documented

**What you are verifying**: When business changes occurred (new product, new jurisdiction, acquisition, significant IT change), a triggered regulatory assessment was documented per POL-00 Section 5.

**How to verify**:

1. Review the list of significant business changes in the assessment year (consult with IT, Legal, and Business Development)
2. For each change that should have triggered an assessment, confirm documentation exists
3. If no changes occurred that met the trigger criteria, document that explicitly

**Business changes that trigger assessment** (from POL-00 Section 5):

- New products or services
- New jurisdictions or markets
- Mergers, acquisitions, or significant changes in customer types
- Major IT infrastructure changes or cloud migrations
- Changes to data processing activities

**Evidence to collect**:

- Triggered assessment records (date, change, regulatory scope checked, outcome)
- If no triggers occurred: a brief written confirmation with CISO sign-off

**Compliance_Status guidance**:

- **Compliant**: All triggering changes have documented assessments, or documented confirmation no triggers occurred
- **Partial**: Some triggering changes have documentation, others do not
- **Non-Compliant**: Known business changes occurred without regulatory assessment, or no process in place

---

### GOV-07: SoA justifications complete for all 93 controls

**What you are verifying**: The Statement of Applicability documents each of the 93 ISO 27001:2022 Annex A controls with implementation status and justification.

**How to verify**:

1. Open the Statement of Applicability
2. Count the controls documented — there must be all 93 Annex A controls from ISO 27001:2022
3. For each control, verify:
   - Implementation status is recorded (Implemented / Partially Implemented / Not Implemented / Not Applicable)
   - Justification for the status is present (not blank)
   - For "Not Applicable" controls, the exclusion justification must be specific

**Evidence to collect**:

- Current Statement of Applicability (full document)
- If maintained in a spreadsheet or GRC tool, an export showing all 93 controls

**Compliance_Status guidance**:

- **Compliant**: All 93 controls documented with status and justification
- **Partial**: 80–92 controls documented, or some justifications are blank or generic
- **Non-Compliant**: Fewer than 80 controls documented, or no justifications present

**Common mistakes**:

- "Not Applicable — not relevant" is not a justification. Explain WHY it is not relevant to this specific organisation.
- SoA maintained for ISO 27001:2013 (114 controls) rather than ISO 27001:2022 (93 controls) — verify the correct standard version is used.

---

### GOV-08: Challenge Protocol followed if invoked

**What you are verifying**: If the Challenge Protocol (ISMS-POL-01 Section 3.3) was invoked during the assessment year, it was executed correctly and documented.

**How to verify**:

1. Ask Legal/Compliance, CISO, and Information Security Manager if the Challenge Protocol was invoked
2. If invoked: verify the Section 3.3 execution steps are documented (challenger, challenged party, escalation path, resolution)
3. If NOT invoked: mark N/A with a note confirming no invocations occurred

**Evidence to collect**:

- Challenge Protocol record (if invoked): challenger identity, basis of challenge, response, escalation if any, and resolution
- Written confirmation of no invocations (if N/A)

**Compliance_Status guidance**:

- **Compliant**: Protocol followed and documented (or N/A with confirmation)
- **Partial**: Challenge occurred but documentation incomplete
- **Non-Compliant**: Challenge occurred but process was not followed

---

**Domain 2 Tips**:

1. **The SoA is your biggest evidence item**: Allow extra time to compile all 93 control justifications — do not underestimate the effort
2. **"No changes" is valid evidence**: For GOV-05 and GOV-06, documented confirmation of no activity is evidence of a functioning monitoring process
3. **Synchronise with Legal quarterly**: The easiest way to ensure GOV-05 is met is to schedule a standing quarterly meeting with Legal/Compliance — the meeting minutes serve as evidence
4. **SoA exclusion justifications are auditor scrutiny targets**: Generic justifications will be challenged. "Not applicable — we are an SME" is insufficient. Specify why the control does not apply to your specific activities and risks.
5. **Check the ISO version**: ISO 27001:2022 Annex A has 93 controls in 4 themes. ISO 27001:2013 had 114 controls in 14 clauses. If your SoA still references 2013 controls, it needs updating.

## Sheet 4: Domain 3 – Exception Handling

**Purpose**: Verify that non-compliance with ISMS requirements is managed through the defined 5-step exception process and that exception volumes remain within policy targets.

**Domain Summary**: Domain 3 tests the operational integrity of the exception management function. It checks whether the 5-step process is followed, residual risks are assessed, approval authority is correct, and whether the organisation is within acceptable exception volume thresholds.

**Requirements in this domain**: GOV-09 to GOV-12

---

### GOV-09: Exceptions follow 5-step process

**What you are verifying**: Exception Register entries demonstrate all five steps were completed for each exception: (1) Identification, (2) Risk Assessment, (3) Control Selection, (4) Approval, (5) Tracking.

**How to verify**:

1. Open the Exception Register
2. Select a sample of current exceptions (at minimum: all Critical and High exceptions, plus 20% of Medium exceptions)
3. For each sampled exception, verify all 5 steps are documented

**The 5 steps** (per ISMS-POL-01):

| Step | What to verify |
|------|---------------|
| 1. Identification | Exception clearly describes the non-compliance and affected control |
| 2. Risk Assessment | Likelihood, impact, and residual risk scores are documented |
| 3. Control Selection | Either Alternative Control or Risk Acceptance path is chosen and documented |
| 4. Approval | Correct authority signed off (see GOV-11) |
| 5. Tracking | Expiry date, status, and owner are maintained |

**Evidence to collect**:

- Exception Register (full extract)
- Supporting risk assessments for sampled exceptions
- Approval records for sampled exceptions

**Compliance_Status guidance**:

- **Compliant**: All sampled exceptions have all 5 steps documented
- **Partial**: Steps present but some are incomplete (e.g., risk assessment missing likelihood/impact detail)
- **Non-Compliant**: Exceptions tracked without following the 5-step process, or no Exception Register in place

---

### GOV-10: Residual risk assessed for all exceptions

**What you are verifying**: Every exception has a completed Step 2 risk assessment including likelihood, impact, and residual risk scores.

**How to verify**:

1. Review the Exception Register for the residual risk column
2. Confirm each exception has:
   - Likelihood score (numeric or categorical)
   - Impact score (numeric or categorical)
   - Residual risk calculation
3. Confirm residual risk is referenced in the approval decision

**Evidence to collect**:

- Exception Register showing likelihood/impact/residual risk columns populated
- Risk assessment methodology reference (confirming the scoring scale used is aligned with the organisation's risk management framework)

**Compliance_Status guidance**:

- **Compliant**: All exceptions have complete residual risk assessments
- **Partial**: Most exceptions assessed; a few missing residual risk calculation
- **Non-Compliant**: Residual risk column absent or consistently blank

---

### GOV-11: Risk acceptances approved by Executive Management

**What you are verifying**: ISMS-POL-01 distinguishes two approval paths:
- **Alternative Control** exceptions → approved by CISO
- **Risk Acceptance** exceptions → approved by Executive Management

Verify the approval path and authority are correctly applied.

**How to verify**:

1. From the Exception Register, separate exceptions by path (Alternative Control vs Risk Acceptance)
2. For Risk Acceptance exceptions: confirm Executive Management signature
3. For Alternative Control exceptions: confirm CISO signature
4. Identify any cross-over errors (Risk Acceptance signed only by CISO)

**Evidence to collect**:

- Exception Register showing exception type (Alternative Control / Risk Acceptance)
- Approval records showing approver identity and role
- If Executive Management approved via committee (e.g., Risk Committee), the signed meeting minutes

**Compliance_Status guidance**:

- **Compliant**: Approval authority correctly applied for all exceptions (CISO for AC, Exec for RA)
- **Partial**: Most correct; some older entries have wrong authority
- **Non-Compliant**: Risk acceptances routinely approved by CISO alone

---

### GOV-12: Exception volume within targets

**What you are verifying**: Total exception volume is within the policy-defined thresholds.

**Policy targets** (from ISMS-POL-01):

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Total exceptions as % of total controls | < 5% |
| Risk acceptances as % of total exceptions | < 3% |
| Deferred exceptions as % of total exceptions | < 2% |
| Exceptions overdue > 90 days | 0 |

**How to verify**:

1. From the Exception Register, calculate:
   - Total exceptions / total ISMS controls (93) × 100
   - Risk acceptances / total exceptions × 100
   - Deferred status count / total exceptions × 100
   - Count of exceptions where status is overdue and age > 90 days
2. Compare each metric against the target

**Evidence to collect**:

- Exception Register (current snapshot with date)
- Calculated metrics (document in the Notes column)

**Compliance_Status guidance**:

- **Compliant**: All four metrics within targets
- **Partial**: 1–2 metrics slightly above target, with documented awareness
- **Non-Compliant**: Any metric significantly above target, or overdue exceptions > 90 days exist

**Tip**: Track these metrics monthly — do not wait for the annual assessment to discover volume has exceeded targets. The CISO dashboard should include these metrics for ongoing visibility.

---

**Domain 3 Tips**:

1. **Sample systematically, not randomly**: Always include all Critical exceptions in your sample — do not let sampling miss the highest-risk items
2. **Date the Exception Register snapshot**: Assessment conclusions depend on the state at a point in time — record the date of your extract
3. **"Accepted-Risk" is not the same as "No Action Required"**: Even accepted risks need residual risk documentation and Executive approval — acceptance does not remove the documentation requirement
4. **Watch for evergreen exceptions**: Exceptions that have been renewing without genuine review are a common audit finding. Check whether "renewal" entries include an updated risk assessment or simply extend the expiry date.
5. **Zero overdue > 90 days is absolute**: Even one exception overdue by more than 90 days is a Non-Compliant finding for GOV-12

## Sheet 5: Domain 4 – Change Management

**Purpose**: Verify that changes to compliance criteria are identified, assessed, and implemented through the defined 6-step change management process.

**Domain Summary**: Domain 4 tests whether the ISMS change management function (for compliance criteria changes, not operational IT changes) is operating correctly. It focuses on the ISMS Change Log, the 6-step process adherence, reassessment timelines, and audit verification.

**Requirements in this domain**: GOV-13 to GOV-16

---

### GOV-13: Compliance criteria changes documented in ISMS Change Log

**What you are verifying**: Every change to ISMS compliance criteria (regulatory changes, new standards, policy revisions, scope changes) has a Change Log entry with: trigger, affected controls, rationale, and approval.

**What constitutes a "compliance criteria change"**:

- A new regulation comes into force that requires new or updated controls
- ISO 27001 or another applicable standard is revised
- A policy is materially updated (not just a minor correction)
- The ISMS scope is expanded or contracted
- A risk treatment decision changes which controls are in scope

**How to verify**:

1. Open the ISMS Change Log
2. Review all entries for the assessment year
3. Cross-reference with known changes (consult with Legal for regulatory changes, IT for scope changes, CISO for standard revisions)
4. Confirm each entry has: Date, Trigger, Affected Controls, Rationale, Approver

**Evidence to collect**:

- ISMS Change Log (assessment year extract)
- For each entry: the triggering event documentation (e.g., regulatory gazette, standard revision notice)

**Compliance_Status guidance**:

- **Compliant**: Change Log maintained with complete entries for all known changes
- **Partial**: Log exists but some known changes are not recorded, or entries lack one or more required fields
- **Non-Compliant**: No ISMS Change Log maintained

---

### GOV-14: Changes follow 6-step process

**What you are verifying**: Changes in the ISMS Change Log demonstrate all 6 steps were followed: (1) Identify, (2) Assess Impact, (3) Propose, (4) Approve, (5) Implement, (6) Verify.

**How to verify**:

1. Select a sample of Change Log entries (at minimum: all changes with High/Critical impact, plus 30% of Medium changes)
2. For each sampled change, verify all 6 steps:

| Step | What to verify |
|------|---------------|
| 1. Identify | Change is logged with date, trigger, and affected controls |
| 2. Assess Impact | Impact assessment documented (which controls need updating, compliance risk) |
| 3. Propose | Proposed changes to policy/controls documented |
| 4. Approve | Approved by appropriate authority (CISO for control changes, Legal for regulatory scope) |
| 5. Implement | Implementation actions completed and dated |
| 6. Verify | Verification action documented (internal audit, self-assessment sign-off) |

**Evidence to collect**:

- Change Log entries with all 6 steps
- Supporting documents for each step (impact assessments, approval emails, implementation records, verification reports)

**Compliance_Status guidance**:

- **Compliant**: All sampled changes have all 6 steps documented
- **Partial**: Steps present but some verification steps missing or implementation incomplete
- **Non-Compliant**: 6-step process not followed; changes implemented without documentation

---

### GOV-15: Reassessments completed within 90 days

**What you are verifying**: The Gap Register shows that reassessments triggered by changes are completed within 90 days, with a completion rate > 95% and zero overdue items.

**Policy targets**:

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Reassessment completion rate | > 95% |
| Overdue items (> 90 days) | 0 |

**How to verify**:

1. Open the Gap Register
2. Filter for reassessment items triggered by Change Log entries
3. Calculate completion rate (completed items / total items × 100)
4. Count items where status ≠ Completed and age > 90 days

**Evidence to collect**:

- Gap Register extract (current snapshot with date)
- Calculated completion rate and overdue count

**Compliance_Status guidance**:

- **Compliant**: Completion rate ≥ 95% and zero overdue items
- **Partial**: Completion rate 85–94%, or 1–2 overdue items with documented justification
- **Non-Compliant**: Completion rate < 85% or multiple overdue items without justification

---

### GOV-16: Changes verified by internal audit

**What you are verifying**: The internal audit programme includes verification of controls changed during the year, with signed audit reports confirming the changed controls were reviewed.

**How to verify**:

1. Review the internal audit schedule/plan for the assessment year
2. Confirm changed controls (from Change Log) were included in the audit scope
3. Review internal audit reports for findings on changed controls
4. Confirm audit reports are signed and issued

**Evidence to collect**:

- Internal audit plan showing scope of controls audited
- Internal audit reports covering changed controls (signed)

**Compliance_Status guidance**:

- **Compliant**: Internal audit reports exist covering all high-impact changed controls
- **Partial**: Some changed controls audited; others missed due to scheduling
- **Non-Compliant**: Changed controls not included in audit scope, or no internal audit conducted

---

**Domain 4 Tips**:

1. **Change Log vs IT change management**: The ISMS Change Log is about *compliance criteria* changes, not operational IT change tickets. Do not conflate the two — they are separate processes
2. **Keep the Change Log concise**: Not every policy edit needs a Change Log entry — only material changes to compliance criteria. A typo fix is not a compliance criteria change.
3. **Verification (Step 6) is often the weakest link**: Organisations implement changes (Step 5) but do not complete formal verification. Make Step 6 a calendar event at the 90-day mark.
4. **Connect Gap Register to Change Log**: Every Change Log entry that triggers a reassessment should create a corresponding Gap Register item. If the two registers are not linked, gaps will be missed.
5. **Audit scope planning happens early**: If you want Step 6 (internal audit verification) to be met, the internal audit plan must include changed controls in scope. Brief your internal auditor before they finalise scope.

## Sheet 6: Domain 5 – Governance Review

**Purpose**: Verify that the annual governance review is conducted, covers all required topics, and produces documented improvement actions.

**Domain Summary**: Domain 5 tests the governance review function — the annual check that the ISMS governance framework itself is working effectively. It goes beyond individual control compliance to assess whether the overall governance machinery (authority boundaries, applicability framework, exception handling, change management) is operating as intended.

**Requirements in this domain**: GOV-17 to GOV-20

---

### GOV-17: Annual governance review completed

**What you are verifying**: A formal governance review meeting was held with Executive Management attendance, and signed meeting minutes exist.

**How to verify**:

1. Locate the most recent governance review meeting minutes
2. Confirm the meeting occurred within the last 12 months
3. Check the attendee list for Executive Management representation
4. Verify the minutes are signed by CISO and at least one Executive Management member

**Evidence to collect**:

- Governance review meeting minutes (dated and signed)
- Attendee list confirming Executive Management attendance

**Compliance_Status guidance**:

- **Compliant**: Annual review held within 12 months, Executive Management attended, minutes signed
- **Partial**: Review held but Executive Management absent, or minutes not signed
- **Non-Compliant**: No annual review conducted, or review held without Executive Management attendance for two consecutive years

**Note**: This review is distinct from the ISO 27001:2022 Clause 9.3 Management Review. The governance review focuses specifically on the ISMS governance framework (ISMS-POL-01), whereas the management review is broader. They may be combined if the agenda covers both.

---

### GOV-18: Review covers all 6 required topics

**What you are verifying**: The governance review agenda and minutes demonstrate all six mandatory topics were discussed.

**Required topics** (per ISMS-POL-01 Section 6.1):

| # | Topic | What to look for in minutes |
|---|-------|---------------------------|
| 1 | Authority boundaries | Discussion of any authority boundary issues, proposed changes |
| 2 | Applicability framework | Regulatory monitoring effectiveness, SoA currency |
| 3 | Exception handling | Exception volumes, trends, overdue items |
| 4 | Change management | Change Log review, reassessment completion rates |
| 5 | Auditor feedback | Internal and external audit findings on governance |
| 6 | Governance efficiency | Process improvements, tool effectiveness, resource adequacy |

**How to verify**:

1. Read the meeting minutes
2. Tick each of the 6 topics as evidenced (explicit mention plus substantive discussion, not just listed on agenda)
3. If a topic is listed on the agenda but minutes show no substantive discussion, mark as Partial

**Evidence to collect**:

- Meeting minutes with sufficient detail to show all 6 topics were discussed
- If topics were covered in pre-read documents, retain those documents alongside the minutes

**Compliance_Status guidance**:

- **Compliant**: All 6 topics explicitly discussed and minuted
- **Partial**: 4–5 topics covered; 1–2 topics listed but not substantively discussed
- **Non-Compliant**: Fewer than 4 topics covered, or meeting minutes too sparse to verify coverage

---

### GOV-19: Continual improvement actions documented

**What you are verifying**: The governance review produced improvement actions with assigned owners, due dates, and status tracking.

**How to verify**:

1. From the meeting minutes (GOV-17), identify all improvement actions agreed
2. Confirm each action has:
   - Description of the improvement
   - Assigned owner (name and role)
   - Target completion date
   - Status (open/closed)
3. For actions from the previous year's review, confirm they were tracked and closed

**Evidence to collect**:

- Improvement action log or action tracker (can be embedded in meeting minutes or a separate register)
- Evidence of closure for completed actions

**Compliance_Status guidance**:

- **Compliant**: All improvement actions documented with owners, dates, and status
- **Partial**: Actions documented but some missing owners or dates; prior year actions not tracked
- **Non-Compliant**: No improvement actions documented, or prior year actions abandoned without outcome

---

### GOV-20: Lessons learned register maintained

**What you are verifying**: The Lessons Learned Register (ISMS-POL-01 Section 6.2) is maintained with required fields: Date, Event, Lesson, Action, Status, Verified.

**How to verify**:

1. Open the Lessons Learned Register
2. Review entries from the current assessment year
3. Confirm each entry has all six required fields populated
4. Confirm at least one "Verified" entry exists (demonstrating a lesson was applied and verified to have improved the process)

**Evidence to collect**:

- Lessons Learned Register (current state)
- For "Verified" entries: evidence that the lesson was applied (updated procedure, training record, etc.)

**Compliance_Status guidance**:

- **Compliant**: Register maintained with complete entries; at least one verified closed-loop entry
- **Partial**: Register exists but some entries incomplete, or no verified lessons this year
- **Non-Compliant**: No Lessons Learned Register, or register empty for the current year

---

**Domain 5 Tips**:

1. **Governance review and management review are complementary, not duplicates**: If you combine them, ensure the agenda explicitly covers all 6 governance topics — auditors will check
2. **Minutes quality matters more than length**: One paragraph of substantive discussion per topic is sufficient — what auditors want to see is evidence the topics were genuinely discussed, not just listed
3. **Improvement actions are your improvement evidence**: ISO 27001 Clause 10.1 requires documented nonconformities and corrective actions. Governance review improvement actions feed this evidence pool.
4. **Lessons learned is often the weakest evidence item**: Organisations collect lessons but do not close the loop (Verified = closed). Make "verify and close" part of the governance review agenda — review last year's lessons and confirm which have been verified.
5. **Executive attendance is non-negotiable for audit**: If the annual review was conducted with only CISO and security team, that is a finding. "Executive Management" means at least one Board member, C-suite executive, or formally delegated representative.

# Common Do's and Don'ts

## Do's

| Do | Why |
|----|-----|
| Collect evidence before starting domain sheets | Prevents placeholder entries that get forgotten |
| Document "no activity" as explicit confirmation | Absence of triggers must be confirmed, not assumed |
| Link every Evidence_Reference to a specific EVD-xxx entry | Makes evidence traceable for auditors |
| Record the date of your Exception Register and Gap Register extracts | Assessment conclusions are point-in-time |
| Combine governance review with ISO 27001 management review if practical | Reduces meeting overhead while satisfying both requirements |
| Track domain-level trends across annual assessments | Demonstrates continual improvement |

## Don'ts

| Don't | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Don't mark a requirement Compliant without evidence | Unsubstantiated compliance claims are a finding |
| Don't use generic SoA justifications ("not relevant") | Auditors will reject these — specificity is required |
| Don't let CISO approve risk acceptances (only Alternative Controls) | This is explicitly split in ISMS-POL-01 |
| Don't leave overdue exceptions (> 90 days) unaddressed | Zero tolerance per GOV-12 — this is a definitive Non-Compliant |
| Don't combine triggered assessments into the quarterly cycle retroactively | Triggered assessments must be documented at the time of the business change |
| Don't skip the Lessons Learned closure check | Unclosed lessons are a persistent audit finding |
