## Executive Summary

This policy establishes [Organisation]'s requirements for working in secure areas, clear desk and clear screen practices, and secure disposal or re-use of equipment.

**Scope**: This policy applies to all personnel working in secure areas, all workspaces where information is processed, and all equipment being disposed of or re-used.

**Purpose**: Define requirements for secure area procedures, information protection during work activities, and secure equipment disposal. This policy establishes WHAT security requirements apply and WHO is responsible. Implementation procedures (HOW) are documented in ISMS-IMP-A.7.6-7-14.

**Regulatory Alignment**: This policy addresses mandatory compliance requirements per ISMS-POL-00 (Regulatory Applicability Framework), including Swiss nDSG, EU GDPR, and ISO/IEC 27001:2022. Conditional sector-specific requirements (FINMA, PCI DSS v4.0.1, DORA) apply where [Organisation]'s business activities trigger applicability.

**Combined Control Approach**: Controls A.7.6 (Working in Secure Areas), A.7.7 (Clear Desk and Clear Screen), and A.7.14 (Secure Disposal or Re-Use of Equipment) are implemented together because they address complementary aspects of physical information protection during work activities and equipment lifecycle.

**ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Controls A.7.6, A.7.7, and A.7.14**

**ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A.7.6 - Working in Secure Areas**

> *Security measures for working in secure areas should be designed and implemented.*

**ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A.7.7 - Clear Desk and Clear Screen**

> *Clear desk rules for papers and removable storage media and clear screen rules for information processing facilities should be defined and appropriately enforced.*

**ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A.7.14 - Secure Disposal or Re-Use of Equipment**

> *Items of equipment containing storage media should be verified to ensure that any sensitive data and licensed software has been removed or securely overwritten prior to disposal or re-use.*

**Control Objectives**:

- Protect information and assets in secure areas through appropriate working procedures
- Reduce risk of unauthorised access to information during and outside working hours
- Prevent information leakage from equipment being disposed of or re-used

**Control Type**: Preventive
**Control Category**: Physical

**This Policy Addresses**:

- Secure area working procedures and conduct requirements
- Clear desk and clear screen requirements by classification
- Equipment disposal methods and verification
- Re-use procedures and documentation requirements

## What This Policy Does

This policy:

- **Defines** conduct requirements for personnel working in secure areas
- **Establishes** clear desk and clear screen rules by information classification
- **Specifies** disposal methods appropriate to data classification
- **References** applicable regulatory requirements per ISMS-POL-00

## What This Policy Does NOT Do

This policy does NOT:

- **Define physical access control to secure areas** (see ISMS-POL-A.7.1-3)
- **Specify storage media management lifecycle** (see ISMS-POL-A.7.10)
- **Detail information deletion procedures** (see ISMS-POL-A.8.10)
- **Provide disposal vendor selection criteria** (see ISMS-IMP-A.7.6-7-14)

**Rationale**: Separating policy requirements from implementation guidance enables:

- Policy stability despite operational or technology changes
- Flexibility for different disposal vendors and methods
- Clear distinction between governance (policy) and execution (implementation)

# Policy Statements

## Working in Secure Areas (A.7.6)

### Access and Conduct

Personnel working in secure areas SHALL follow these requirements:

**Access Controls**:

- Access to secure areas SHALL be granted only to authorised personnel
- Access SHALL be based on job role and need-to-know principle
- Visitors in secure areas SHALL be escorted at all times
- Access rights SHALL be reviewed quarterly and revoked when no longer required

**Conduct in Secure Areas**:

- Personnel SHALL only be aware of activities within secure areas on a need-to-know basis
- Personnel SHALL NOT work alone in secure areas outside normal business hours unless an approved exception exists in ISMS-REG-EXCEPTIONS and compensating controls are in place (e.g., check-in procedure, CCTV coverage, or buddy system)
- Empty secure areas SHALL be locked and periodically checked
- Photography, video, audio, or other recording SHALL be prohibited unless specifically authorised

**Information Protection**:

- Sensitive information SHALL not be discussed where it may be overheard
- Whiteboards and flipcharts SHALL be erased after meetings
- Sensitive documents SHALL be removed from printers immediately
- Clean desk requirements SHALL be enforced in all secure areas

### Third-Party Access

**Contractor and Visitor Requirements**:

- Third parties SHALL be escorted in secure areas
- Third-party access SHALL be logged and time-limited
- Third parties SHALL sign confidentiality agreements before access
- Third-party equipment SHALL NOT be brought into secure areas unless authorised by the Secure Area Owner or Security Team; where authorised, equipment containing storage media, wireless capability, or audio/video recording capability SHALL be inspected and recorded in the visitor log

## Clear Desk and Clear Screen (A.7.7)

### Clear Desk Requirements

**During Work Hours**:

- Sensitive documents SHALL be stored securely when not in immediate use
- Documents awaiting printing SHALL be collected immediately
- Only documents actively being worked on SHALL be on desks

**End of Day / Extended Absence**:

- All sensitive documents SHALL be locked in drawers or cabinets
- Removable storage media (USB drives, external drives) SHALL be secured
- Access cards and keys SHALL not be left on desks
- Notebooks and sticky notes with sensitive information SHALL be secured

**Classification-Specific Requirements**:

| Classification | Clear Desk Requirement |
|----------------|----------------------|
| **CONFIDENTIAL** | Locked storage mandatory when unattended |
| **INTERNAL** | Locked storage at end of day |
| **PUBLIC** | Best practice but not mandatory |

### Clear Screen Requirements

**Screen Lock**:

- All corporate workstations SHALL automatically lock after maximum 5 minutes of inactivity; this baseline applies regardless of data classification and SHALL be enforced via endpoint management policy
- Users SHALL manually lock screens (Win+L / Ctrl+Cmd+Q) when leaving workstation
- Password-protected screensavers SHALL be enabled

**Information Display**:

- Sensitive information SHALL not be displayed where it can be viewed by unauthorised persons
- Privacy screens SHALL be used in open-plan offices and public areas
- Projector and screen sharing sessions SHALL be ended immediately after use
- Virtual meeting backgrounds SHALL be used where sensitive information may be visible

**End of Day**:

- All applications SHALL be closed
- Workstations SHALL be logged off or shut down per IT policy
- Sensitive documents SHALL be closed before leaving workstation

### Enforcement

**Verification**:

- Random clear desk audits SHALL be conducted monthly
- Non-compliance SHALL be reported to line managers
- Repeated non-compliance SHALL be escalated to HR

**Evidence Repository**:

- Audit checklists stored in GRC Platform - Compliance Module
- Non-compliance records maintained for 12 months

## Secure Disposal or Re-Use of Equipment (A.7.14)

### Pre-Disposal Requirements

**Assessment**:

- All equipment SHALL be assessed for sensitive data and licensed software before disposal
- Equipment containing storage media SHALL be identified and handled per this policy
- Asset management records SHALL be updated to reflect disposal

**Data Classification Review**:

- Maximum data classification ever stored on equipment SHALL be determined
- Disposal method SHALL match classification level

### Disposal Methods

**Physical Destruction** (Required for CONFIDENTIAL data):

- Storage media SHALL be physically destroyed (shredding, degaussing, disintegration)
- Destruction SHALL be performed by approved service providers
- Certificate of destruction SHALL be obtained and retained
- Destruction SHALL be witnessed or verified

**Secure Overwriting** (Acceptable for INTERNAL data):

- Data SHALL be overwritten using approved secure deletion tools defined in ISMS-IMP-A.7.6-7-14.3
- Magnetic media SHALL use approved overwrite methods; SSD/flash media SHALL use cryptographic erasure
- Verification of successful erasure SHALL be performed; IT Operations SHALL retain a wipe/erase report for each asset including: asset tag/serial, method/tool used, date/time, operator, and completion status
- For CONFIDENTIAL assets, a second-person review of wipe verification SHALL be recorded

**Standard Deletion** (Acceptable only for PUBLIC data):

- Standard deletion tools may be used
- Format and reinstall acceptable

### Disposal Methods by Equipment Type

| Equipment Type | CONFIDENTIAL | INTERNAL | PUBLIC |
|----------------|--------------|----------|--------|
| Hard Drives (HDD) | Physical destruction | 3-pass overwrite or destruction | Format |
| Solid State Drives (SSD) | Physical destruction | Cryptographic erase or destruction | Secure erase |
| Mobile Devices | Physical destruction | Factory reset + verification | Factory reset |
| USB/Removable Media | Physical destruction | Secure overwrite or destruction | Format |
| Printers/Copiers | HDD removal + destruction | HDD removal + secure wipe | Clear memory |
| Network Equipment | N/A (config wipe) | Config wipe + verification | Config reset |

### Re-Use Requirements

**Internal Re-Use**:

- All data SHALL be securely erased before reassignment
- Licensed software SHALL be transferred or removed per licensing terms
- Asset records SHALL be updated with new assignee
- Equipment SHALL be inspected and refurbished if necessary

**External Re-Use (Donation/Sale)**:

- CONFIDENTIAL equipment SHALL NOT be re-used externally
- INTERNAL equipment SHALL have storage media destroyed or securely wiped
- All organisation identifiers SHALL be removed
- Factory defaults SHALL be restored

### Chain of Custody

**Internal Holding**:

- Media/equipment awaiting wipe/destruction SHALL be stored in a designated secure holding area with access limited to authorised disposal personnel

**Off-Site Destruction**:

- When media/equipment leaves [Organisation] premises for destruction by an approved vendor, chain-of-custody SHALL be documented including: handover date/time, asset identifiers (serial numbers), releasing party identity, receiving party identity, and vendor transport reference
- Certificates of destruction SHALL match recorded serial numbers; discrepancies SHALL be escalated immediately and logged as security events

### Documentation

**Disposal Records** SHALL include:

- Equipment asset tag and serial number
- Data classification level
- Disposal method used
- Date of disposal
- Person authorising disposal
- Certificate of destruction (where applicable)
- Verification evidence

**Retention**: Disposal records SHALL be retained for 7 years.

# Governance & Compliance

## Assessment Framework

| Assessment | Frequency | Owner | Evidence |
|------------|-----------|-------|----------|
| Clear desk audits | Monthly | Facilities Manager | Audit checklists |
| Secure area compliance review | Quarterly | Security Team | Access logs, incident reports |
| Equipment disposal audit | Semi-annual | IT Operations | Disposal records, certificates |
| Screen lock compliance | Quarterly | IT Operations | Endpoint management reports |

**Governance Metrics**:

- Clear desk audit pass rate (target: >95%)
- Screen lock compliance (target: 100%)
- Disposal with certificate (target: 100% for CONFIDENTIAL)
- Secure wipe verification (target: 100%)
- Secure area incidents (target: 0)

## Policy Review

- **Frequency**: Annual minimum
- **Triggers**: Security incidents, audit findings, technology changes, regulatory updates
- **Reviewers**: CISO, Facilities Manager, IT Operations
- **Approval**: Executive Management

## Exception Management

**Permitted Exceptions**:

- Extended screen timeout for specific operational requirements (with enhanced monitoring)
- Alternative disposal methods for legacy equipment (with documented risk acceptance)
- Temporary relaxation of clear desk for project war rooms (with enhanced access controls)

**Exception Process**:

1. Document business justification
2. Risk assessment of exception impact
3. CISO approval with compensating controls
4. Time-limited approval (maximum 6 months)
5. Documentation in exception register

**Not Permissible**:

- Exceptions eliminating disposal verification for CONFIDENTIAL data
- Permanent bypass of clear screen requirements
- Disposal without documented authorisation

All exceptions SHALL be recorded in the Exception Register (ISMS-REG-EXCEPTIONS).

## Corrective Action Linkage

Nonconformities related to this policy (e.g., clear desk failures, improper disposal, secure area violations, missing destruction certificates) SHALL be recorded and managed through the ISMS corrective action process (Clause 10.2) with root cause analysis and tracked remediation.

# Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Secure Area** | A physically protected location with access controls where sensitive information is processed or stored |
| **Clear Desk** | Practice of removing all sensitive materials from desks when not in use |
| **Clear Screen** | Practice of locking or logging off computer screens when unattended |
| **Secure Disposal** | Methods of disposing of equipment that prevent data recovery |
| **Cryptographic Erasure** | Destroying encryption keys to render encrypted data unrecoverable |
| **Degaussing** | Using magnetic fields to erase data from magnetic storage media |
