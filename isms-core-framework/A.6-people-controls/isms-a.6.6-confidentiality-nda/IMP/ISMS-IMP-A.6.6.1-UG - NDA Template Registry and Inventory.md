## Assessment Overview

### Purpose

This workbook maintains the authoritative registry of Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) and confidentiality agreement templates used across the organisation. It serves as the single source of truth for:

- **Template Inventory**: Complete catalogue of all approved NDA templates
- **Version Control**: Historical tracking of template changes and approvals
- **Applicability Guidance**: Clear guidance on which template to use for each stakeholder category
- **Clause Library**: Standardised clauses ensuring consistency and legal enforceability
- **Legal Review Tracking**: Evidence of regular legal review and currency

The NDA Template Registry is foundational to the organisation's confidentiality management programme. Without a controlled, centrally-managed template inventory, the organisation risks using outdated agreements, inconsistent terms, and unenforceable clauses.

### Scope

This assessment covers the following components:

**In Scope:**
- All NDA and confidentiality agreement templates (internal and external use)
- Mutual and one-way non-disclosure agreements
- Employment confidentiality clauses and addenda
- Contractor and consultant confidentiality agreements
- Vendor and supplier confidentiality requirements
- Customer and partner confidentiality provisions
- Board member and investor confidentiality agreements
- Visitor access confidentiality acknowledgements
- Template version history and change documentation
- Legal review records and approval evidence
- Applicability matrices and decision guidance
- Standard clause libraries and approved wording

**Out of Scope:**
- Execution of individual agreements (covered in ISMS-IMP-A.6.6.2)
- NDA compliance tracking and monitoring (covered in ISMS-IMP-A.6.6.3)
- Compliance dashboards and metrics (covered in ISMS-IMP-A.6.6.4)
- Specific agreement instances (only templates, not executed agreements)

### Business Value

A well-maintained NDA template registry delivers:

| Value Area | Benefit |
|------------|---------|
| **Legal Protection** | Enforceable agreements protect confidential information |
| **Consistency** | Standardised terms across all stakeholder relationships |
| **Efficiency** | Pre-approved templates accelerate agreement execution |
| **Audit Readiness** | Clear evidence trail for ISO 27001 auditors |
| **Risk Reduction** | Eliminates use of outdated or unapproved templates |
| **Compliance** | Demonstrates systematic confidentiality management |

## Control Requirements

### ISO 27001:2022 Control A.6.6

> *"Confidentiality or non-disclosure agreements reflecting the organisation's needs for the protection of information should be identified, documented, regularly reviewed and signed by personnel and other relevant interested parties."*

**Control Type:** Preventive
**Security Properties:** Confidentiality
**Cybersecurity Concepts:** Protect
**Operational Capabilities:** Legal and Compliance

### Implementation Requirements

Control A.6.6 requires organisations to establish a systematic approach to confidentiality agreements. The key requirements are:

**1. Identification**
- Identify all parties requiring confidentiality agreements
- Determine which types of agreements are needed for each party category
- Identify information types requiring protection through NDAs

**2. Documentation**
- Document all NDA templates with version control
- Maintain clear records of template ownership and approval
- Create applicability guidance for template selection

**3. Regular Review**
- Conduct periodic legal review of all templates
- Update templates when business requirements change
- Review templates when regulations or laws change

**4. Signature Requirements**
- Ensure agreements are signed before access to confidential information
- Maintain evidence of signed agreements
- Track agreement validity and renewal requirements

### What Auditors Look For

ISO 27001 auditors examining Control A.6.6 will verify:

| Audit Focus | Evidence Required |
|-------------|-------------------|
| **Template Existence** | Current, approved NDA templates for all stakeholder categories |
| **Version Control** | Evidence that templates are version-controlled with change history |
| **Legal Review** | Dated evidence of legal review within the past 12 months |
| **Applicability Guidance** | Clear documentation of which template applies in which situation |
| **Currency** | Templates reflect current regulations (GDPR, nDSG, etc.) |
| **Completeness** | Coverage of all relevant stakeholder categories |
| **Professional Drafting** | Evidence that templates were legally reviewed/drafted |

## Stakeholder Categories

### Understanding Stakeholder Categories

A comprehensive NDA programme must cover all parties who may access confidential information. The table below provides guidance on stakeholder categorisation:

#### Internal Stakeholders

| Category | Description | Typical NDA Type | Timing |
|----------|-------------|------------------|--------|
| **Permanent Employees** | Full-time and part-time employees | Employment confidentiality clause | At hiring, before access |
| **Temporary Employees** | Fixed-term and seasonal workers | Employment confidentiality clause | At hiring, before access |
| **Executive Leadership** | C-suite and senior management | Executive confidentiality agreement | At appointment |
| **Board Members** | Directors and advisors | Board confidentiality agreement | At appointment |
| **Interns and Trainees** | Students and apprentices | Intern confidentiality agreement | Before start date |

#### External Stakeholders

| Category | Description | Typical NDA Type | Timing |
|----------|-------------|------------------|--------|
| **Contractors** | Individual service providers | Contractor NDA | Before contract start |
| **Consultants** | Professional advisors | Consultant NDA | Before engagement |
| **Vendors/Suppliers** | Product and service providers | Vendor NDA | Before contract |
| **Partners** | Strategic and business partners | Mutual NDA | Before discussions |
| **Customers** | Clients with access to systems | Customer confidentiality agreement | Before access |
| **Potential Investors** | Due diligence participants | Investor NDA | Before disclosure |
| **Job Candidates** | Interviewees for sensitive roles | Candidate NDA | Before interviews |
| **Visitors** | On-site visitors to secure areas | Visitor confidentiality acknowledgement | At arrival |
| **Subcontractors** | Third parties of contractors | Subcontractor flow-down NDA | Before access |
| **Auditors** | External audit firms | Professional confidentiality | Engagement letter |

#### Special Categories

| Category | Description | Typical NDA Type | Considerations |
|----------|-------------|------------------|----------------|
| **Researchers** | Academic or commercial researchers | Research NDA | IP protection, publication rights |
| **Media** | Journalists, PR contacts | Media NDA | Embargo provisions |
| **Regulators** | Government inspectors | Regulatory provision | May not require NDA |
| **Legal Counsel** | External lawyers | Professional duty | Attorney privilege applies |
| **M&A Parties** | Acquisition targets/buyers | M&A NDA | Enhanced provisions |

### Stakeholder Category Decision Tree

```
Is the party accessing confidential information?
├── NO → No NDA required
└── YES → Continue...
    │
    └── Is the party an employee?
        ├── YES → Employment confidentiality clause
        │         (permanent, temporary, intern)
        └── NO → Continue...
            │
            └── Is this a vendor/supplier relationship?
                ├── YES → Vendor NDA
                └── NO → Continue...
                    │
                    └── Is this a partnership discussion?
                        ├── YES → Mutual NDA
                        └── NO → Continue...
                            │
                            └── Is this a contractor/consultant?
                                ├── YES → Contractor NDA
                                └── NO → Assess specific category
```

- **Rules:** Maximum 100 characters, use title case
- **Guidance:** Name should clearly indicate stakeholder category and purpose

#### Column C: Template_Type

- **Format:** Select from dropdown validation list
- **Options:**
  - Standard NDA
  - Mutual NDA
  - One-Way NDA (Disclosing)
  - One-Way NDA (Receiving)
  - Employment Confidentiality
  - Contractor Agreement
  - Vendor Agreement
  - Customer Agreement
  - Partner Agreement
  - Investor NDA
  - Visitor Acknowledgement
- **Guidance:** Select the type that best describes the template structure

#### Column D: Stakeholder_Category

- **Format:** Select from dropdown validation list
- **Options:** (Multiple selections allowed, comma-separated)
  - Employees
  - Contractors
  - Consultants
  - Vendors
  - Suppliers
  - Partners
  - Customers
  - Board Members
  - Investors
  - Visitors
  - Interns
  - Candidates
- **Guidance:** Select all stakeholder categories for which this template applies

#### Column E: Current_Version

- **Format:** X.Y (where X is major version, Y is minor)
- **Example:** 2.1
- **Rules:** Increment Y for minor changes, X for significant revisions
- **Guidance:** Version 1.0 is the initial approved version

#### Column F: Effective_Date

- **Format:** DD.MM.YYYY (Swiss date format)
- **Example:** 15.01.2026
- **Rules:** Date when current version became effective
- **Guidance:** Must be on or after the legal approval date

#### Column G: Legal_Review_Date

- **Format:** DD.MM.YYYY (Swiss date format)
- **Example:** 10.01.2026
- **Rules:** Date of most recent legal review
- **Guidance:** Should be within 12 months; flag if older

- **Format:** Full name and title

- **Format:** Free text
- **Example:** Updated for GDPR requirements in v2.0

- **Guidance:** Include any special considerations or restrictions

## NDA Template Types

### Standard NDA Types

#### One-Way NDA (Disclosing Party)

**Use when:** Your organisation is disclosing information to another party who will not disclose information back.

**Key characteristics:**
- Unilateral protection
- Disclosing party defines confidential information
- Receiving party has all obligations
- Common for: Vendor evaluations, consultant briefings

**Required clauses:**
- Definition of Confidential Information
- Non-disclosure obligations
- Permitted use restrictions
- Return/destruction of information
- Term and survival period

#### One-Way NDA (Receiving Party)

**Use when:** Your organisation is receiving confidential information from another party.

**Key characteristics:**
- Protects the disclosing party's information
- Your organisation has the obligations
- Common for: Receiving RFP responses, customer data

**Required clauses:**
- Definition of Confidential Information (from discloser)
- Your obligations as receiver
- Permitted use limitations
- Information handling requirements

#### Mutual NDA (Bilateral)

**Use when:** Both parties will exchange confidential information.

**Key characteristics:**
- Symmetrical obligations
- Both parties are disclosers and receivers
- Common for: Partnership discussions, joint ventures, M&A

**Required clauses:**
- Mutual definitions
- Reciprocal obligations
- Balanced remedies
- Equal term provisions

#### Employment Confidentiality Agreement

**Use when:** Employees (permanent, temporary, interns) access confidential information.

**Key characteristics:**
- Often part of employment contract
- Covers employee's entire tenure
- Survives termination of employment
- May include IP assignment clauses

**Required clauses:**
- Definition of company confidential information
- Non-disclosure during and after employment
- Return of materials at termination
- Non-compete provisions (where legal)
- IP assignment (if applicable)

### Specialised NDA Types

#### Vendor/Supplier NDA

**Key considerations:**
- Subcontractor flow-down requirements
- Right to audit clause
- Data processing provisions (GDPR)
- Insurance requirements
- Breach notification obligations

#### Customer NDA

**Key considerations:**
- May protect customer's information
- System access provisions
- Data protection compliance
- Service level commitments
- Limitation of liability

#### Investor/Due Diligence NDA

**Key considerations:**
- Standstill provisions
- Non-solicitation of employees
- Exclusivity clauses
- Enhanced remedies
- Specific carve-outs for deal process

## Standard Clause Library

### Essential Clauses

Every NDA template should include these foundational clauses:

#### 1. Definition of Confidential Information

**Purpose:** Clearly defines what information is protected.

**Best practice elements:**
- Broad definition covering all forms (written, oral, electronic)
- Reference to information classification markings
- Examples of confidential information types
- Catch-all provisions
- Exclusions (public information, independently developed, etc.)

**Sample language:**
> "Confidential Information means all non-public information disclosed by the Disclosing Party, whether in writing, orally, or by inspection, including but not limited to: business plans, financial data, customer lists, technical specifications, trade secrets, and any other information marked or identified as confidential at the time of disclosure or that should reasonably be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure."

#### 2. Obligations of Receiving Party

**Purpose:** Defines what the receiving party must do (and not do).

**Best practice elements:**
- Standard of care (at least same as own confidential information)
- Prohibition on disclosure to third parties
- Limited disclosure to those with need-to-know
- Requirement to inform recipients of obligations
- Protection against inadvertent disclosure

#### 3. Permitted Use

**Purpose:** Defines how confidential information may be used.

**Best practice elements:**
- Specific permitted purposes
- Prohibition on use for other purposes
- No competitive use
- No reverse engineering

#### 4. Exclusions from Confidential Information

**Purpose:** Defines what is NOT confidential information.

**Standard exclusions:**
- Information already public (not through breach)
- Information already known to receiver
- Information independently developed
- Information received from third party without restriction
- Information required to be disclosed by law

#### 5. Term and Duration

**Purpose:** Defines how long obligations last.

**Considerations:**
- Agreement term (when can new information be shared)
- Survival period (how long must information be protected)
- Perpetual obligations for trade secrets
- Regulatory minimum periods (GDPR: as long as processing continues)

#### 6. Return or Destruction

**Purpose:** Addresses information handling at termination.

**Best practice elements:**
- Requirement to return or destroy
- Certification of destruction
- Exceptions for legal holds
- Backup and archive handling

#### 7. Remedies

**Purpose:** Addresses breach consequences.

**Key elements:**
- Acknowledgement of irreparable harm
- Right to injunctive relief
- Monetary damages
- Indemnification provisions

#### 8. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

**Purpose:** Establishes legal framework.

**Considerations:**
- Choice of law
- Exclusive jurisdiction
- Arbitration vs. litigation
- Service of process

### Regulatory Compliance Clauses

#### GDPR Compliance Clause

Required when personal data may be shared:

**Key elements:**
- Data controller/processor identification
- Legal basis for processing
- Data subject rights acknowledgement
- International transfer restrictions
- Sub-processor requirements
- Data breach notification (72 hours)

#### Swiss nDSG/FADP Compliance Clause

For Swiss organisations:

**Key elements:**
- FDPIC registration compliance
- Cross-border transfer restrictions
- Adequate protection requirements
- Data subject notification

## Common Pitfalls

Avoid these common mistakes when completing the NDA Template Registry assessment:

### Template Management Pitfalls

❌ **MISTAKE**: Using outdated templates without legal review
✅ **CORRECT**: Verify template currency through Legal_Review_Date field; flag any template with review date older than 12 months; establish automatic review reminders

❌ **MISTAKE**: No version control on templates
✅ **CORRECT**: Implement mandatory version numbering (X.Y format); document all changes in Template_Versions sheet; never modify templates without creating new version

❌ **MISTAKE**: Templates stored in multiple locations
✅ **CORRECT**: Designate single authoritative location per template; document in Storage_Location field; implement access controls on authoritative location; delete or mark as "reference only" any copies

❌ **MISTAKE**: No ownership assigned to templates
✅ **CORRECT**: Assign Template_Owner to every template; owner is accountable for currency, reviews, and updates; document in Template_Registry

❌ **MISTAKE**: Using generic templates from the internet
✅ **CORRECT**: Have all templates reviewed and approved by qualified legal counsel; document Legal_Reviewer and Legal_Review_Date for each template

❌ **MISTAKE**: Assuming employment contracts include confidentiality
✅ **CORRECT**: Verify employment contracts contain explicit confidentiality clauses; if not, implement separate confidentiality agreement; document as separate template

### Applicability Pitfalls

❌ **MISTAKE**: Unclear guidance on which template to use
✅ **CORRECT**: Create comprehensive Applicability_Matrix covering all stakeholder categories; provide decision tree or flowchart; train relevant staff on selection

❌ **MISTAKE**: Not covering all stakeholder categories
✅ **CORRECT**: Systematically review all parties with potential access to confidential information; include visitors, candidates, interns; document in Applicability_Matrix

❌ **MISTAKE**: Same template for all situations
✅ **CORRECT**: Develop appropriate templates for different stakeholder categories; consider mutual vs. one-way, employment vs. commercial; balance protection with practicality

❌ **MISTAKE**: Not specifying when NDA must be signed
✅ **CORRECT**: Document timing requirements in Applicability_Matrix; NDA must be signed BEFORE access to confidential information; no exceptions without documented approval

### Legal and Compliance Pitfalls

❌ **MISTAKE**: Missing regulatory clauses (GDPR, nDSG)
✅ **CORRECT**: Ensure templates include data protection provisions where personal data may be shared; include processor obligations under GDPR Article 28; document compliance in Clause_Library

❌ **MISTAKE**: No post-termination confidentiality period
✅ **CORRECT**: Every template must specify how long confidentiality obligations survive after relationship ends; typically 2-5 years or indefinite for trade secrets; document in templates and Applicability_Matrix

❌ **MISTAKE**: Templates not covering subcontractors
✅ **CORRECT**: Vendor and contractor NDAs must include flow-down provisions; require disclosure of subcontractors; mandate equivalent confidentiality obligations

❌ **MISTAKE**: No breach reporting requirements
✅ **CORRECT**: Include clause requiring receiving party to notify of actual or suspected breach; align with incident response procedures; specify notification timeline (e.g., 24-48 hours)

### Documentation Pitfalls

❌ **MISTAKE**: Incomplete Evidence_Register
✅ **CORRECT**: Document every piece of evidence with unique Evidence_ID, description, location, and collection date; evidence must be retrievable for audits

❌ **MISTAKE**: Not documenting change rationale
✅ **CORRECT**: In Template_Versions, document both Change_Description (what changed) and Change_Reason (why it changed); enables future reviewers to understand context

❌ **MISTAKE**: Missing legal approval evidence
✅ **CORRECT**: Every template version must have documented legal approval; store approval evidence (email, memo, signature) in Evidence_Register; link to specific version

❌ **MISTAKE**: Clause library not maintained
✅ **CORRECT**: Regularly review and update Clause_Library; ensure standard text reflects current legal requirements; document last review date for each clause

## Regulatory Compliance

### Data Protection Requirements

NDA templates must address data protection requirements when personal data may be shared:

#### EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

**Applicable when:** Personal data of EU residents may be shared

**NDA requirements:**
| Requirement | Template Provision |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Legal basis | Identify legal basis for processing |
| Purpose limitation | Specify permitted purposes |
| Data minimisation | Limit to necessary data |
| Controller/Processor | Define roles and responsibilities |
| Sub-processors | Require prior approval for sub-processors |
| International transfers | Address adequacy decisions or SCCs |
| Data breach notification | 72-hour notification requirement |
| Data subject rights | Enable exercise of rights |
| Security measures | Require appropriate technical measures |

#### Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (nDSG/FADP)

**Applicable when:** Personal data of Swiss residents may be shared

**NDA requirements:**
| Requirement | Template Provision |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Proportionality | Data processing proportionate to purpose |
| Cross-border transfers | Adequate protection or consent |
| Data security | Appropriate technical and organisational measures |
| Notification | Inform data subjects of processing |
| Right to information | Enable data subject access |

### Industry-Specific Requirements

#### Financial Services

- Enhanced confidentiality for financial data
- Regulatory reporting exceptions
- Audit trail requirements
- FINMA compliance provisions

#### Healthcare

- Patient data confidentiality
- HIPAA provisions (for US data)
- Research data protections
- Ethics committee requirements

#### Technology/IP Intensive

- Source code protection
- Patent application confidentiality
- Trade secret preservation
- Non-compete provisions
