## Purpose and Scope

### Assessment Objective

This assessment verifies [Organization]'s compliance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Control A.6.1 (Screening) as implemented through ISMS-POL-A.5.1-2-6.1-2, Section 6.

**Control A.6.1 Requirement:**
> *Background verification checks on all candidates to become personnel should be carried out prior to joining the organisation and on an ongoing basis taking into consideration applicable laws, regulations and ethics and should be proportional to the business requirements, the classification of the information to be accessed and the perceived risks.*

**Three pillars of this control:**
- **Before entry**: Pre-employment screening must occur before the individual begins work
- **Continuously**: Screening is not a one-time event — re-screening must occur at defined intervals or upon triggering events
- **Proportionate**: The depth and scope of screening must match the sensitivity of the role, the classification of information accessible, and the risk the role presents

**Assessment Focus:**
- Screening process documentation and completeness
- Screening level definitions matched to role sensitivity (from .S2 Role Inventory)
- Pre-employment screening compliance per individual
- Continuous / periodic re-screening program
- Legal compliance of screening activities (Swiss FADP, GDPR)
- Screening provider governance (where third-party screening services are used)
- Contractor and third-party workforce screening

### Relationship to Parent Policy

This assessment implements verification procedures for **ISMS-POL-A.5.1-2-6.1-2, Section 6: Screening & Vetting Requirements**.

**Policy Requirements Being Assessed:**
- **Section 6.1**: Screening Principles (proportionality, legality, ethical conduct)
- **Section 6.2**: Screening Level Framework (role-based screening tiers)
- **Section 6.3**: Pre-Employment Screening Process (identity verification, reference checks, criminal record checks, CV verification)
- **Section 6.4**: Continuous Screening Requirements (periodic re-screening, event-triggered re-screening)
- **Section 6.5**: Screening Provider Management (third-party screening service governance)
- **Section 6.6**: Legal and Ethical Compliance (FADP, GDPR, consent, data retention, candidate rights)

**Evidence Collected**: Screening process documentation, screening completion records, consent forms, screening provider contracts, legal review documentation, re-screening schedules, individual screening files

### Assessment Frequency

**Standard Schedule:**
- **Quarterly**: All organizations (routine screening compliance verification)
- **Annual**: Comprehensive screening program review including legal compliance re-assessment
- **Triggered**: After organizational restructuring, new regulatory requirements, screening failures, or audit findings

**Why Quarterly?**
- Workforce changes continuously (new hires, role changes, departures)
- Re-screening deadlines must be tracked and enforced
- Legal landscape evolves (FADP enforcement actions provide new guidance)
- Screening provider performance must be monitored regularly
- Supports continuous compliance posture required by ISO 27001

### Assessment Ownership

**Primary Owner**: CISO and CHRO (joint ownership — security defines requirements, HR executes)

**Participants:**
- CISO (screening requirement definition, risk assessment)
- CHRO / HR Manager (screening execution, candidate handling)
- DPO (legal compliance verification — FADP consent, data retention)
- Legal / Compliance Officer (legal framework review)

- Screening Provider Account Manager (if third-party screening used)
- Internal Audit (independent verification)

**Time Investment:** 4-6 hours for initial assessment, 2-3 hours for quarterly updates

## Assessment Methodology

### Assessment Approach

This assessment uses a **process-then-person verification approach** — first verify the screening process is properly defined, then verify each individual has been screened per the process.

**Step 1: Process Assessment**
- Verify screening process is documented
- Confirm screening procedures exist for each screening type (identity, references, criminal, financial, CV)
- Validate process ownership (who is responsible for each screening step)
- Check process integration with recruitment workflow

**Step 2: Screening Level Calibration**
- Verify screening levels are defined for each role tier (from .S2 Role Inventory)
- Confirm screening level assignments are proportionate to role risk
- Cross-reference role sensitivity with information classification access
- Validate screening level matrix is current

**Step 3: Individual Screening Compliance**
- Verify each employee/contractor has been screened at the required level
- Confirm screening was completed BEFORE the individual started work (or within an approved grace period)
- Check screening documentation is complete and retained
- Identify individuals with screening gaps

**Step 4: Continuous Screening Verification**
- Verify re-screening schedule is defined and tracked
- Confirm periodic re-screening has been executed per schedule
- Check event-triggered re-screening has been performed where required
- Identify overdue re-screenings

**Step 5: Legal Compliance Review**
- Verify consent has been obtained where required (Swiss FADP Art. 6(7) — explicit consent for sensitive personal data including criminal records)
- Confirm DPIA has been conducted if screening involves high-risk processing (FADP Art. 22)
- Verify candidate information rights have been communicated (FADP Art. 25)
- Check screening data retention complies with FADP purpose limitation (Art. 6(3))
- Validate screening provider contracts include data protection clauses

**Step 6: Gap Analysis**
- Consolidate all gaps from Steps 1-5
- Assess risk level per gap
- Define remediation actions

### Compliance Criteria

**Compliant Individual Screening** meets ALL criteria:
- ✅ Screening level assigned based on role tier and information access
- ✅ All required screening checks completed (per assigned level)
- ✅ Screening completed before or at start of employment (within approved grace period)
- ✅ Screening documentation retained and accessible
- ✅ Required consent obtained (where legally mandated)
- ✅ Re-screening on schedule (not overdue)

**Partial Compliance** when:
- ⚠️ Screening completed but one check is missing or incomplete
- ⚠️ Screening completed after start date but within grace period (max 30 days, documented justification)
- ⚠️ Re-screening overdue by < 90 days
- ⚠️ Consent obtained but documentation incomplete

**Non-Compliant** when:
- ❌ No screening performed for individual in a security-relevant role
- ❌ Screening level too low for role sensitivity (e.g., Tier 1 role screened at Basic level)
- ❌ Required consent not obtained for criminal record check
- ❌ Re-screening overdue by > 90 days
- ❌ Screening documentation missing or destroyed prematurely

### Scoring Methodology

**Overall Compliance Score** = Weighted average across assessment domains

**Domain Weights:**
- Screening Process Documentation: 20% (process must be defined)
- Screening Level Calibration: 15% (levels must match role risk)
- Individual Screening Compliance: 30% (each person must be screened — largest weight)
- Continuous Screening Program: 20% (re-screening must be enforced)
- Legal Compliance: 15% (FADP/GDPR obligations must be met)

**Compliance Score Interpretation:**
- **90–100%**: Excellent (audit-ready, no significant gaps)
- **80–89%**: Good (minor gaps, routine remediation)
- **70–79%**: Acceptable (moderate gaps, focused remediation needed)
- **60–69%**: Needs Improvement (significant gaps, priority remediation)
- **< 60%**: Critical (major gaps, immediate action required)

## Screening Level Framework

### Proportionality Principle

ISO 27001:2022 Control A.6.1 explicitly requires screening to be **proportionate**. This means:
- Screening depth increases with role sensitivity
- Screening scope matches information classification accessible
- Screening checks are justified by the actual risk the role presents
- Over-screening (collecting data beyond what the role requires) violates Swiss FADP proportionality requirements (Art. 6(2))

**Swiss FADP Constraint**: Under Art. 6(7), **explicit consent** is required before processing sensitive personal data. Criminal record information is explicitly classified as sensitive personal data under Art. 4(c)(5). Therefore, criminal record checks require documented explicit consent from the candidate — consent cannot be implied by signing an employment contract.

### Screening Level Definitions

**Four screening levels, mapped to role tiers from .S2:**

**Level 1 — Basic Screening** (Tier 4: General Workforce)
Required checks:
- Identity verification (passport or equivalent government ID)
- CV / résumé verification (education and employment history spot-check)

Scope: All new employees regardless of role. Minimum baseline.

**Level 2 — Standard Screening** (Tier 3: Security Operational Roles)
Required checks:
- Everything in Level 1, PLUS:
- Professional reference check (minimum 2 references from previous employers)
- Education verification (degree/certification confirmation with issuing institution)

Scope: Roles with access to Internal or Confidential classified information. IT roles, system administrators, operational security roles.

**Level 3 — Enhanced Screening** (Tier 2: Security Management Roles)
Required checks:
- Everything in Level 2, PLUS:
- Criminal record check (requires explicit consent under Swiss FADP Art. 6(7))
- Credit / financial history check (only where role involves financial authority — requires explicit consent and documented business justification)

Scope: Roles with access to Confidential or Restricted classified information. Management roles with approval authority, financial roles, compliance roles.

**Level 4 — Comprehensive Screening** (Tier 1: Security Leadership)
Required checks:
- Everything in Level 3, PLUS:
- Extended reference check (minimum 3 references, including at least one direct supervisor)
- Verification of all qualifications and certifications (not spot-check — complete verification)
- Social media / public record review (limited to publicly available professional information)
- Conflicts of interest assessment (documented review for financial interests, prior relationships with competitors)

Scope: CISO, DPO, Security Committee members, C-suite roles with security oversight. Roles with unrestricted access to Restricted information or critical security infrastructure.

### Contractor and Third-Party Screening

Contractors and third-party workers must be screened at a level matching their **access requirements**, not their employment type. A contractor with admin access to critical systems requires the same screening as an employee in an equivalent role.

**Screening responsibility allocation:**
- **Contractors employed via agency**: Organization verifies screening was performed by agency at required level (obtain written confirmation and screening report)
- **Direct contractors**: Organization performs screening directly, same process as employees
- **Vendor personnel with site/system access**: Vendor must confirm screening at required level; organization retains right to audit vendor screening records

### Screening Exemptions

In exceptional cases, screening may be delayed or modified. All exemptions require:
- Written justification documenting the business necessity
- Risk assessment documenting the residual risk of proceeding without full screening
- Compensating controls (e.g., supervised access only, restricted permissions until screening complete)
- CISO and DPO approval
- Maximum grace period: 30 days for Levels 1–2; screening must be completed before any unsupervised access for Levels 3–4
- Documented in the screening registry

### Sheet 7: Legal_Compliance_Review

**Purpose**: Verify that [Organization]'s screening activities comply with Swiss FADP and EU GDPR. This is a critical sheet — screening involves processing sensitive personal data (criminal records under FADP Art. 4(c)(5)), which carries specific legal obligations.

**Structure**: Single-column question-answer format (A = Question, B = Answer). 30 questions organized into 5 legal compliance domains.

**Domain A: Consent Management (Q1–Q7)**

**Q1: Consent_Framework_Exists**
- Question: Does [Organization] have a documented consent framework for screening?
- Dropdown: Yes / No
- Legal Basis: FADP Art. 6(7) — explicit consent required for processing sensitive personal data
- Compliant: "Yes"

**Q2: Consent_Form_Exists**
- Question: Does a screening consent form exist for candidates?
- Dropdown: Yes / No
- Compliant: "Yes"

**Q3: Consent_Form_Location**
- Question: Where is the consent form stored/accessible?
- Format: Free text (URL or file path)
- Required: IF Q2 = "Yes"

**Q4: Consent_Covers_Criminal_Check**
- Question: Does the consent form explicitly cover criminal record check processing?
- Dropdown: Yes / No / N/A
- Legal Basis: Criminal record data = sensitive personal data (FADP Art. 4(c)(5)). Explicit consent required (Art. 6(7)(a)).
- Compliant: "Yes" or "N/A" (if no criminal checks performed)

**Q5: Consent_Covers_Financial_Check**
- Question: Does the consent form explicitly cover financial/credit check processing (where applicable)?
- Dropdown: Yes / No / N/A
- Compliant: "Yes" or "N/A"

**Q6: Consent_Voluntary**
- Question: Is consent genuinely voluntary (not a condition of employment offer)?
- Dropdown: Yes / Unclear / No
- Legal Basis: FADP Art. 6(6) — consent must be given voluntarily
- Critical Note: Employment offer CANNOT be conditional on consent to criminal check. Screening consent must be separate from employment offer acceptance. If candidate declines consent, screening must proceed without criminal check, and risk must be assessed.
- Compliant: "Yes"

**Q7: Consent_Withdrawal_Process**
- Question: Can candidates withdraw consent, and is the withdrawal process documented?
- Dropdown: Yes / No
- Compliant: "Yes"

**Domain B: Candidate Information Rights (Q8–Q13)**

**Q8: Candidate_Informed_About_Processing**
- Question: Are candidates informed about what personal data is collected and why (before screening begins)?
- Dropdown: Yes / No
- Legal Basis: FADP Art. 19 (duty to inform), Art. 25 (right to information)
- Compliant: "Yes"

**Q9: Information_Notice_Exists**
- Question: Does a screening privacy notice / information statement exist?
- Dropdown: Yes / No
- Compliant: "Yes"

**Q10: Notice_Covers_Required_Elements**
- Question: Does the notice cover all required elements?
- Dropdown: Yes / Partial / No
- Required Elements (per FADP Art. 19/25):
  - Identity of the controller ([Organization])
  - Purpose of processing (security screening)
  - Types of data collected (identity, references, criminal records as applicable)
  - Legal basis for processing (legitimate interest / consent)
  - Retention period
  - Candidate's rights (access, rectification, deletion where applicable)
  - Whether data is shared with third parties (screening providers)
- Compliant: "Yes"

**Q11: Candidate_Right_to_Access**
- Question: Can candidates access their screening data upon request?
- Dropdown: Yes / No
- Legal Basis: FADP Art. 25 — right to information
- Compliant: "Yes"

**Q12: Candidate_Right_to_Rectification**
- Question: Can candidates request correction of inaccurate screening data?
- Dropdown: Yes / No
- Compliant: "Yes"

**Q13: Adverse_Finding_Communication**
- Question: Are candidates informed when adverse screening findings affect their candidacy, and given opportunity to explain?
- Dropdown: Yes / No
- Best Practice: Before rejecting a candidate based on screening findings, the candidate should be informed of the finding and given opportunity to provide context. This reduces legal risk and ensures fairness.
- Compliant: "Yes"

**Domain C: Data Protection Impact Assessment (Q14–Q17)**

**Q14: DPIA_Conducted**
- Question: Has a Data Protection Impact Assessment been conducted for the screening process?
- Dropdown: Yes / No / Not-Required
- Legal Basis: FADP Art. 22 — DPIA required if processing likely to result in high risk. Processing criminal record data (sensitive personal data) at scale qualifies.
- Compliant: "Yes" (strongly recommended even if not strictly required)

**Q15: DPIA_Location**
- Question: Where is the DPIA documented?
- Format: Free text
- Required: IF Q14 = "Yes"

**Q16: DPIA_Current**
- Question: Is the DPIA current (reviewed within 12 months or after significant process changes)?
- Dropdown: Yes / No / N/A
- Required: IF Q14 = "Yes"
- Compliant: "Yes" or "N/A"

**Q17: DPO_Consulted**
- Question: Was the DPO consulted on the screening process design?
- Dropdown: Yes / No
- Legal Basis: FADP Art. 23 — consultation obligation for high-risk processing
- Compliant: "Yes"

**Domain D: Data Retention and Deletion (Q18–Q23)**

**Q18: Retention_Policy_Defined**
- Question: Is a retention policy defined for screening data?
- Dropdown: Yes / No
- Legal Basis: FADP Art. 6(4) — data destroyed or anonymized when no longer required
- Compliant: "Yes"

**Q19: Retention_Period_Appropriate**
- Question: Is the retention period appropriate (employment duration + legally required period)?
- Dropdown: Yes / No / Unknown
- Guidance: Screening data should be retained for the duration of employment plus any statutory period (typically 3 years post-termination for employment records in Switzerland)
- Compliant: "Yes"

**Q20: Deletion_Process_Exists**
- Question: Is there a defined process for deleting screening data when retention period expires?
- Dropdown: Yes / No
- Compliant: "Yes"

**Q21: Deletion_Tracked**
- Question: Is screening data deletion tracked and documented?
- Dropdown: Yes / No
- Compliant: "Yes"

**Q22: Criminal_Check_Data_Handling**
- Question: Is criminal record check data handled with enhanced security controls (given sensitive data classification)?
- Dropdown: Yes / No / N/A
- Enhanced Controls Include: Access restricted to minimum necessary personnel, stored separately from general HR records, encryption at rest, audit logging of all access
- Compliant: "Yes" or "N/A"

**Q23: Cross_Border_Transfer_Compliance**
- Question: If screening providers are located outside Switzerland/EU, are cross-border transfer requirements met?
- Dropdown: Yes / No / N/A
- Legal Basis: FADP Chapter 5 (disclosure abroad); GDPR Chapter V
- Compliant: "Yes" or "N/A"

**Domain E: Screening Provider Compliance (Q24–Q28)**

**Q24: Provider_DPA_Complete**
- Question: Is the Data Processing Agreement with screening provider complete and compliant?
- Dropdown: Yes / No / N/A
- Legal Basis: FADP Art. 9; GDPR Art. 28
- Required Elements: Processing scope, data subject rights obligations, security measures, sub-processor restrictions, breach notification, return/deletion on termination
- Compliant: "Yes" or "N/A"

**Q25: Provider_Security_Assessed**
- Question: Has the screening provider's security posture been assessed?
- Dropdown: Yes / No / N/A
- Assessment Methods: SOC 2 report review, security questionnaire, audit
- Compliant: "Yes" or "N/A"

**Q26: Provider_Breach_Notification_Clause**
- Question: Does the provider contract include a breach notification clause (72-hour notification)?
- Dropdown: Yes / No / N/A
- Legal Basis: FADP Art. 33 (breach notification to FDPIC); GDPR Art. 33 (72-hour notification to supervisory authority)
- Compliant: "Yes" or "N/A"

**Q27: Sub_Processor_Controls**
- Question: Are sub-processor restrictions documented in the provider contract?
- Dropdown: Yes / No / N/A
- Compliant: "Yes" or "N/A"

**Q28: Provider_Jurisdiction**
- Question: In which jurisdiction is the screening provider located?
- Format: Free text (Country)
- Required: IF Sheet 2 Q19 = "Yes"

**Overall Legal Compliance Rating (Q29–Q30):**

**Q29: Legal_Compliance_Rating**
