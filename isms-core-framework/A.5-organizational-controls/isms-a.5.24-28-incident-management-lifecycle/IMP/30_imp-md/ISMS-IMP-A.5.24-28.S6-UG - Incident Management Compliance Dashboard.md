# Assessment Overview

## Purpose

This dashboard consolidates incident management compliance metrics from all five assessment domains (S1-S5) into executive-ready visualisations, providing comprehensive visibility into the organisation's incident management lifecycle maturity.

**What This Dashboard Covers:**

- Executive summary of overall incident management compliance
- Control-by-control compliance scoring (A.5.24 through A.5.28)
- Gap analysis consolidation across all assessment domains
- Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) for incident management
- Trend analysis and historical performance tracking
- Evidence register linking to source assessments
- Approval and sign-off workflow

**What This Dashboard Does NOT Cover:**

- Detailed assessment questions (captured in S1-S5 workbooks)
- Individual incident case management (use incident ticketing system)
- Real-time security monitoring (use SIEM/SOC dashboard)
- Threat intelligence feeds (see A.5.7 Threat Intelligence)

**Dashboard Output:**

- Consolidated Excel workbook with executive summary
- Control compliance heat map
- Gap prioritisation matrix
- KPI scorecards with traffic light indicators
- Evidence traceability across all assessment domains
- Management approval workflow

## Why This Matters

**ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Incident Management Controls:**

> **A.5.24:** *"The organisation should plan and prepare for managing information security incidents by defining, establishing and communicating information security incident management processes, roles and responsibilities."*

> **A.5.25:** *"The organisation should assess information security events and decide if they are to be categorised as information security incidents."*

> **A.5.26:** *"Information security incidents should be responded to in accordance with the documented procedures."*

> **A.5.27:** *"Knowledge gained from information security incidents should be used to strengthen and improve the information security controls."*

> **A.5.28:** *"The organisation should establish and apply procedures for the identification, collection, acquisition and preservation of evidence related to information security incidents."*

**Business Impact of Fragmented Compliance Visibility:**

- **Audit Risk:** Stage 2 auditors expect consolidated evidence of incident management maturity
- **Management Blind Spots:** Executives lack visibility into operational incident response capability
- **Resource Misallocation:** Without unified metrics, improvement efforts lack prioritisation
- **Regulatory Gaps:** Compliance with GDPR, Swiss nDSG, PCI DSS v4.0.1 requires demonstrable incident management capability
- **Insurance Requirements:** Cyber insurance policies increasingly require evidence of mature incident response

**This Dashboard Addresses:**

- What is our overall incident management compliance posture?
- Which controls have the largest compliance gaps?
- Are we meeting our incident response KPIs?
- What evidence supports our compliance claims?
- How has our compliance posture changed over time?

## Who Should Complete This Dashboard

**Primary Responsibility:** CISO, CSIRT Manager, or Information Security Manager

**Required Knowledge:**

- Understanding of all five incident management assessment domains (S1-S5)
- Access to completed assessment workbooks from each domain
- Familiarity with organisational KPI reporting requirements
- Understanding of regulatory compliance requirements

**Support Roles:**

- **CSIRT Manager:** Domain-specific assessment data, incident statistics
- **SOC Manager:** Detection and response metrics, tool capabilities
- **Compliance Officer:** Regulatory alignment, audit evidence requirements
- **IT Operations:** Integration metrics, system availability data
- **Legal/Privacy:** Breach notification compliance, regulatory reporting

**Collaboration Required:**

- Requires input from owners of S1-S5 assessments
- Coordination with compliance team for regulatory mapping
- Review sessions with executive leadership for KPI validation

# Assessment Workflow

## High-Level Process

**Step-by-Step Dashboard Completion Flow:**

```
1. Data Import (1-2 hours)
   ├── Open S1-S5 assessment workbooks
   ├── Extract compliance scores from each dashboard sheet
   ├── Import gap inventories from each Gap Analysis sheet
   └── Reconcile any data discrepancies

2. Compliance Scoring (30 minutes)
   ├── Calculate weighted compliance scores by control
   ├── Determine overall compliance percentage
   ├── Apply traffic light status indicators
   └── Validate calculations against source data

3. Gap Consolidation (1 hour)
   ├── Combine gaps from all assessment domains
   ├── Remove duplicates and normalize entries
   ├── Prioritize by severity and impact
   └── Assign remediation ownership and timelines

4. KPI Dashboard (30 minutes)
   ├── Populate incident metrics from ticketing system
   ├── Calculate KPIs (MTTD, MTTR, MTTC, etc.)
   ├── Compare against targets
   └── Update trend data

5. Evidence Linkage (30 minutes)
   ├── Map evidence references to source workbooks

   ├── Validate all formulas and calculations
   ├── Cross-check data against source workbooks

   ├── Generate executive summary
   ├── CSIRT Manager review and approval
   ├── CISO review and approval
   └── Document filing and distribution
```

# Section-by-Section Guidance

## Sheet 2: Executive Summary

**Purpose:** Provide executive leadership with a concise overview of incident management compliance status

**Key Elements:**

---

### **Overall Compliance Score**

- **Calculation:** Weighted average of five control compliance scores
- **Weighting:**
  - A.5.24 (Framework): 25% - Foundation of incident management
  - A.5.25 (Detection): 20% - Critical for early identification
  - A.5.26 (Response): 25% - Core operational capability
  - A.5.27 (Learning): 15% - Continuous improvement driver
  - A.5.28 (Evidence): 15% - Forensic and legal requirements
- **Traffic Light Status:**
  - ≥90%: GREEN - Compliant
  - 75-89%: AMBER - Partially Compliant
  - <75%: RED - Non-Compliant

### **Control Heat Map**

- **Visual representation** of compliance by control
- **Row:** Control number (A.5.24 through A.5.28)
- **Columns:** Assessment domains within each control
- **Cell colour:** GREEN/AMBER/RED based on compliance level

### **Top 5 Gaps**

- **Prioritised list** of most critical gaps requiring attention
- **Selection criteria:**
  - Severity (Critical > High > Medium > Low)
  - Regulatory impact (GDPR, PCI DSS v4.0.1, etc.)
  - Audit finding likelihood
  - Remediation effort (quick wins first)
- **Information captured:** Gap ID, Description, Severity, Owner, Due Date

### **KPI Scorecard Summary**

- **Key metrics** with target comparison
- **Visual indicators:** ▲ (improving), ▬ (stable), ▼ (declining)
- **Metrics included:**
  - MTTD (target: <24 hours for critical)
  - MTTR (target: <4 hours for critical)
  - Incident closure rate (target: >95%)
  - Post-incident review completion (target: 100%)

### **Recommendations**

- **Top 3 recommendations** for improvement
- **Format:** Action, Owner, Timeline, Expected Benefit

## Sheet 4: Gap Analysis

**Purpose:** Consolidated view of all gaps across incident management domains

**Column Structure:**

| Column | Description | Format |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Gap_ID | Unique identifier | GAP-A524-001 format |
| Source | Source assessment (S1-S5) | Dropdown |
| Control | Related ISO control | A.5.24-A.5.28 |
| Domain | Assessment domain | Text |
| Gap_Description | Description of the gap | Free text |
| Severity | Critical/High/Medium/Low | Dropdown |
| Impact | Business/regulatory impact | Free text |
| Root_Cause | Underlying cause | Free text |
| Remediation_Action | Required remediation | Free text |
| Owner | Responsible person | Free text |
| Status | Open/In Progress/Closed | Dropdown |
| Due_Date | Target completion date | Date |
| Days_Open | Age of gap | Calculated |
| Evidence | Evidence of remediation | Reference |
| Notes | Additional comments | Free text |

**Gap Severity Definitions:**

- **Critical:** Significant compliance risk, audit finding likely, regulatory violation potential
- **High:** Material gap affecting incident response capability, requires immediate attention
- **Medium:** Moderate gap, improvements recommended within 90 days
- **Low:** Minor observation, improvement opportunity, no immediate risk

**Gap Status Workflow:**

1. **Open:** Gap identified, not yet addressed
2. **In Progress:** Remediation underway, owner assigned
3. **Closed:** Remediation complete, evidence documented

**Gap Aging Thresholds:**

- Critical: >30 days = OVERDUE (RED)
- High: >60 days = OVERDUE (RED)
- Medium: >90 days = OVERDUE (AMBER)
- Low: >180 days = OVERDUE (AMBER)

# Common Pitfalls

## Data Consolidation Errors

❌ **MISTAKE:** Copying data manually from S1-S5 workbooks instead of using cell references or Power Query.
✅ **CORRECT:** Use Excel cell references, Power Query, or automated import to ensure data accuracy and traceability.

❌ **MISTAKE:** Consolidating gaps without removing duplicates that appear in multiple assessments.
✅ **CORRECT:** De-duplicate gaps during consolidation, preserving the most complete description and linking to all relevant sources.

❌ **MISTAKE:** Using outdated S1-S5 assessment data without verifying currency.
✅ **CORRECT:** Verify all source assessments are current (completed within last 12 months) before consolidation.

❌ **MISTAKE:** Importing scores without understanding the weighting methodology from source workbooks.
✅ **CORRECT:** Document the calculation methodology for each imported score and validate against source data.

## Compliance Scoring Errors

❌ **MISTAKE:** Applying equal weighting to all five controls regardless of organisational risk profile.
✅ **CORRECT:** Adjust control weightings based on organisational priorities and risk appetite, documenting the rationale.

❌ **MISTAKE:** Using only pass/fail scoring instead of graduated maturity levels.
✅ **CORRECT:** Use graduated compliance scoring (0-100%) to enable trend tracking and identify partial compliance.

❌ **MISTAKE:** Calculating compliance percentages without accounting for "Not Applicable" controls.
✅ **CORRECT:** Exclude N/A controls from compliance calculations, adjusting the denominator accordingly.

❌ **MISTAKE:** Reporting compliance scores without confidence indicators or data quality notes.
✅ **CORRECT:** Include data quality indicators and note any assumptions or limitations with reported scores.

## Gap Analysis Errors

❌ **MISTAKE:** Prioritising gaps solely by severity without considering remediation effort or quick wins.
✅ **CORRECT:** Use a multi-factor prioritisation matrix including severity, effort, regulatory impact, and audit risk.

❌ **MISTAKE:** Assigning gaps to generic roles (e.g., "IT") instead of specific accountable individuals.
✅ **CORRECT:** Assign each gap to a named individual with clear accountability and authority to remediate.

❌ **MISTAKE:** Setting unrealistic remediation due dates without stakeholder agreement.
✅ **CORRECT:** Agree due dates with gap owners, considering resource constraints and dependencies.

❌ **MISTAKE:** Closing gaps without evidence of remediation completion.
✅ **CORRECT:** Require documented evidence before marking any gap as "Closed."

## KPI Calculation Errors

❌ **MISTAKE:** Calculating MTTD/MTTR using clock time instead of business hours or incident severity adjustments.
✅ **CORRECT:** Define calculation methodology clearly (clock hours vs. business hours) and apply consistently.

❌ **MISTAKE:** Including test incidents or exercises in operational KPI calculations.
✅ **CORRECT:** Exclude test data from production KPI calculations, but track separately for exercise effectiveness.

❌ **MISTAKE:** Reporting KPIs without trend comparison or target variance analysis.
✅ **CORRECT:** Always present KPIs with target comparison and trend indicators (improving/stable/declining).

❌ **MISTAKE:** Using sample data instead of complete incident datasets.
✅ **CORRECT:** Calculate KPIs from complete incident datasets for the reporting period.

## Evidence Management Errors

❌ **MISTAKE:** Referencing evidence by vague descriptions (e.g., "incident policy") without specific file paths.
✅ **CORRECT:** Use specific file paths or document IDs for all evidence references.

❌ **MISTAKE:** Including evidence older than 12 months without verification of continued validity.
✅ **CORRECT:** Flag evidence older than 12 months for re-verification or update.

❌ **MISTAKE:** Storing sensitive evidence (incident details, forensic data) in unsecured locations.
✅ **CORRECT:** Apply appropriate access controls to evidence storage locations based on data sensitivity.

❌ **MISTAKE:** Not maintaining version control for updated evidence.
✅ **CORRECT:** Use version numbering or date stamps for all evidence and maintain change history.

# Review & Approval

## Review Process

**Step 1: Self-Review (Dashboard Owner)**

- Validate all calculations and data imports
- Verify evidence linkages
- Check completeness against checklist

**Step 2: Technical Review (CSIRT Manager)**

- Validate KPI calculations against incident data
- Confirm gap prioritisation is appropriate
- Review recommendations for technical feasibility

**Step 3: Compliance Review (Compliance Officer)**

- Verify regulatory alignment of gap priorities
- Confirm evidence supports compliance claims
- Review for audit readiness

**Step 4: Executive Review (CISO)**

- Review executive summary
- Approve recommendations
- Authorise distribution

## Approval Criteria

**Approval requires:**

1. All mandatory fields populated
2. Calculations validated
3. Evidence references verified
4. Gap owners confirmed
5. KPI targets validated
6. Recommendations agreed

**Conditional approval may be granted with:**

- Minor formatting corrections required
- Clarifications needed on specific items
- Additional evidence collection in progress

**Rejection criteria:**

- Material errors in compliance scores
- Missing evidence for critical controls
- Significant gaps without remediation plans
- KPI data incomplete or inaccurate
