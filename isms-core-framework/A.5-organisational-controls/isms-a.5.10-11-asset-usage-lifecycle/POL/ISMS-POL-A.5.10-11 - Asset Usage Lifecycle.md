## Executive Summary

This policy establishes [Organisation]'s requirements for the acceptable use of information assets throughout their lifecycle and the return of assets upon termination or change of employment, in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Controls A.5.10 and A.5.11.

**Scope**: This policy applies to all personnel (employees, contractors, consultants, temporary staff), all organisational assets (physical, digital, information), and all phases of employment/engagement lifecycle.

**Purpose**: Define organisational requirements for asset usage governance and return procedures. This policy establishes WHAT acceptable use and return requirements apply and WHO is accountable. Implementation procedures (HOW) are documented separately in ISMS-IMP-A.5.10-11 (UG/TG variants).

**Regulatory Alignment**: This policy addresses mandatory compliance requirements per ISMS-POL-00 (Regulatory Applicability Framework), including Swiss nDSG, EU GDPR, and ISO/IEC 27001:2022. Conditional sector-specific requirements (PCI DSS v4.0.1, FINMA, DORA, NIS2) apply where [Organisation]'s business activities trigger applicability.

# Control Alignment & Scope

## ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Controls A.5.10 & A.5.11

**ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A.5.10 - Acceptable Use of Information and Other Associated Assets**

> *Rules for the acceptable use of information and other associated assets should be identified, documented and implemented.*

**ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A.5.11 - Return of Assets**

> *Personnel and other interested parties as appropriate should return all the organisation's assets in their possession upon change or termination of their employment, contract or agreement.*

**Control Objectives**:
- A.5.10: Ensure all personnel understand and comply with acceptable use requirements for organisational assets
- A.5.11: Ensure complete and verified return of organisational assets when employment or engagements end

**This Policy Addresses**:

- Acceptable use requirements for information and associated assets
- Permitted and prohibited activities for asset usage
- Asset handling requirements by classification level
- Return procedures for employment termination or role change
- Verification and sign-off requirements for asset return
- Integration with [Organisation]'s asset inventory (per ISMS-POL-A.5.9)

## What This Policy Does

This policy:

- **Defines** acceptable use requirements for all organisational asset categories
- **Establishes** prohibited activities and their consequences
- **Specifies** asset return requirements and timelines
- **Defines** accountability for asset usage governance and return verification
- **References** applicable regulatory requirements per ISMS-POL-00

## What This Policy Does NOT Do

This policy does NOT:

- **Specify detailed usage rules per asset type** (see ISMS-IMP-A.5.10-11.S2 Usage Rules Inventory)
- **Define technical enforcement mechanisms** (see ISMS-IMP-A.5.10-11 Implementation Guides)
- **Provide offboarding checklists** (see ISMS-IMP-A.5.10-11.S3 Asset Return Assessment)
- **Select monitoring or enforcement technologies** (technology selection based on risk assessment)

**Rationale**: Separating policy requirements from implementation guidance enables:

- Policy stability despite evolving asset types and technologies
- Flexibility for operational procedures without policy revision
- Clear distinction between governance (policy) and execution (implementation)

## Scope

**This policy applies to**:

- All employees, contractors, consultants, and temporary staff
- All organisational assets (hardware, software, information, digital accounts)
- All engagement types (employment, contracts, agreements)
- All phases (onboarding, active engagement, role change, termination)
- All third-party individuals with access to organisational assets

**Out of Scope**:

- Asset inventory and classification (covered under ISMS-POL-A.5.9)
- Physical security of assets (covered under ISMS-POL-A.7.x)
- Data retention and disposal (covered under ISMS-POL-A.8.10)
- Information classification (covered under ISMS-POL-A.5.12-13)

# Part A: Acceptable Use Requirements (A.5.10)

## Acceptable Use Policy Framework

[Organisation] maintains an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) that defines permitted and prohibited activities for all organisational assets.

**Policy Communication Requirements**:

| Requirement | Implementation | Verification |
|------------|----------------|--------------|
| AUP available to all personnel | Intranet policy portal, onboarding materials | Access logs, acknowledgment records |
| Acknowledgment required | Annual sign-off via LMS or HR system | Signed acknowledgment register |
| Updates communicated | Email notification, training update | Distribution records, training completion |
| New hire acknowledgment | During onboarding, before access granted | HR onboarding checklist |

**Verification Mechanism**: AUP acknowledgment rates tracked in ISMS-IMP-A.5.10-11.S1 (Acceptable Use Policy Assessment). Target: 100% acknowledgment within 30 days of hire/annual refresh date.

*Exception: Personnel on approved leave >30 days at time of policy refresh are flagged as "deferred" in acknowledgment register; acknowledgment required within 14 days of return to work. Deferred acknowledgments excluded from compliance rate calculation during absence period.*

Non-compliance >30 days (excluding deferred): escalated to manager; >60 days escalated to HR.

## Permitted Use Requirements

[Organisation] defines permitted activities for organisational assets.

**General Permitted Use**:

- Business activities aligned with job responsibilities
- Approved software and applications from authorised sources
- Authorised access to systems and data based on role requirements
- Limited personal use where explicitly permitted and during non-work time
- Communication via approved channels for business purposes

**Conditions for Permitted Use**:

| Asset Category | Permitted Use | Conditions |
|---------------|---------------|------------|
| **Email/Messaging** | Business communication | Professional content; no sharing of confidential data externally |
| **Internet Access** | Business research, approved sites | Subject to web filtering policy; no circumvention of controls |
| **Software** | Approved applications only | Only from authorised sources; proper licensing |
| **Mobile Devices** | Business and limited personal | Compliance with mobile device policy; MDM enrolled |
| **Cloud Services** | Approved services only | Data classification requirements respected |
| **Physical Assets** | Business purposes | Proper care and security; no modifications |

**Implementation Reference**: Detailed usage rules per asset category documented in ISMS-IMP-A.5.10-11.S2 (Usage Rules Inventory).

## Prohibited Use Requirements

[Organisation] explicitly prohibits activities that could compromise security, compliance, or reputation.

**Absolute Prohibitions** (No exceptions permitted):

| Prohibited Activity | Risk Category | Consequence |
|---------------------|---------------|-------------|
| Sharing credentials with others | Security breach | Immediate disciplinary action |
| Disabling security controls | Compliance violation | Immediate disciplinary action |
| Installing unauthorised software | Malware risk | Disciplinary action; device quarantine |
| Accessing illegal content | Legal/reputational | Immediate termination; potential legal action |
| Data exfiltration attempts | Data breach | Immediate termination; potential legal action |
| Circumventing access controls | Security breach | Disciplinary action |

**Restricted Activities** (Exception may be granted with documented business justification):

| Activity | Default Status | Exception Authority |
|----------|----------------|---------------------|
| Personal device connection to network | Prohibited | IT Security Manager with CISO approval |
| External file sharing | Restricted | Data Owner + IT Security |
| Use of personal cloud storage for business data | Prohibited | CISO |
| Remote access from personal devices | Restricted | IT Security Manager |

**Exception Process**: Exceptions to prohibited activities require:
1. Written business justification
2. Risk assessment completion
3. Compensating controls identification
4. Approval per exception authority table above
5. Documentation in exception register (maximum 12-month validity)
6. Evidence stored in ISMS Evidence Library (SharePoint/Confluence or equivalent)

**Exception Register Specifications**:

- Owner: IT Security Manager
- Structure: Exception ID, Request Date, Requestor, Asset/Activity, Business Justification, Risk Assessment Summary, Compensating Controls, Approval Authority, Approval Date, Expiry Date, Renewal/Closure Status
- Storage: ISMS Evidence Library (SharePoint/Confluence or equivalent)
- Retention: 7 years post-expiry (aligns with ISO 27001 records retention guidance)
- Review: Quarterly review by CISO; expired exceptions archived; renewals require re-justification

## Monitoring and Enforcement

[Organisation] monitors asset usage to detect policy violations and security threats.

**Monitoring Disclosure**:

All personnel are informed that:
- System and network activity may be monitored
- Email and messaging content may be reviewed for security purposes
- Web browsing activity is logged and analysed
- Physical access to facilities is recorded
- No expectation of privacy when using organisational assets

**Enforcement Framework**:

| Violation Severity | Response | Authority |
|--------------------|----------|-----------|
| **Critical** (data breach, illegal activity) | Immediate suspension; investigation; potential termination | CISO + HR Director |
| **High** (security control bypass, repeated violations) | Formal warning; access restriction; remedial training | IT Security Manager + Line Manager |
| **Medium** (policy violation without breach) | Written warning; manager notification; monitoring | Line Manager |
| **Low** (minor infraction, first occurrence) | Verbal warning; reminder of policy | Line Manager |

**Verification**: Violations tracked in incident register; trends reported quarterly to CISO; patterns informing policy updates documented in the Summary Dashboard.

# Part B: Return of Assets Requirements (A.5.11)

## Asset Return Framework

[Organisation] requires complete return of all organisational assets upon:

- Employment termination (resignation, retirement, dismissal)
- Contract completion (contractors, consultants)
- Role change (if assets no longer required)
- Leave of absence (where appropriate)

**Return Triggers and Timelines**:

| Trigger Event | Notification | Return Deadline | Verification |
|---------------|--------------|-----------------|--------------|
| Resignation | HR notification | Last working day | IT + Manager sign-off |
| Termination | Immediate | Same day | IT + HR sign-off |
| Contract end | 5 days prior | Contract end date | IT + Contract Manager |
| Role change | 5 days prior to change | Role change date | IT + Old/New Manager |
| Extended leave | Leave commencement | Day before leave | IT + Manager |

**Verification Mechanism**: Completed return forms signed by receiving party; recorded in HR system and asset inventory; evidence stored in ISMS Evidence Library.

## Assets Subject to Return

**Physical Assets** (All must be physically returned):

| Asset Type | Return To | Verification Method |
|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| Laptop/Desktop | IT Asset Management | Asset tag verification; condition check |
| Mobile devices | IT Asset Management | IMEI verification; MDM removal confirmed |
| Security tokens/smart cards | IT Security | Serial number verification |
| Physical keys/access badges | Facilities/Security | Badge/key register update |
| Physical documents | Document Owner | Return acknowledgment |
| Company credit cards | Finance | Card cancellation confirmation |

**Digital Assets** (Access to be revoked; data transferred as appropriate):

| Asset Type | Action Required | Responsible Party |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Network credentials | Disable within 24 hours of last day | IT Operations |
| Email account | Disable; auto-reply if needed; archive | IT Operations |
| Application accounts | Disable or transfer | Application Owners |
| VPN certificates | Revoke | IT Security |
| Cloud service access | Remove user | Cloud Service Administrators |
| Shared drive access | Remove; transfer data ownership | IT Operations |

**Timeline Exception Process**: If standard 24-hour access revocation SLA cannot be met due to operational requirements:

1. Immediate compensating control implemented (choose based on risk):
   - Account disabled but not deleted (prevents login while preserving data)
   - Access restricted to read-only for knowledge transfer period
   - Session monitoring activated with daily review
   - MFA requirement added (prevents unauthorised access even if password known)
2. Business justification documented in exception register
3. IT Security Manager approval required (CISO approval if >72 hours)
4. Full revocation within 72 hours maximum (no exceptions beyond this)
5. Exception reviewed in quarterly access control audit

**Verification Mechanism**: Access revocation verified via AD query, system logs, or login attempt verification; documented in ISMS-IMP-A.5.10-11.S3 (Access Revocation sheet).

## BYOD and Personal Device Data

Where personnel used personal devices for work (BYOD):

**Return Requirements** (verification rigor based on data classification):

| Requirement | Verification | Responsible Party |
|-------------|--------------|-------------------|
| Company data removed (MDM-enrolled devices) | MDM wipe confirmation | IT Security |
| Company data removed (non-MDM devices, Public/Internal data) | Self-attestation + IT confirmation of account removal | End User + IT Security |
| Company data removed (non-MDM devices, Confidential/Restricted data) | Self-attestation + exit interview data handling review | End User + HR + IT Security |
| Email profile removed | Self-attestation with verification | End User + IT |
| Application data removed | Self-attestation | End User |
| Cloud sync disabled | Service account removed | IT Operations |

**Attestation Form**: Required attestation confirming all company data removed from personal devices; signed within 5 business days of termination; non-compliance escalated to HR.

## Knowledge Transfer

**Requirements Before Departure**:

| Requirement | Timeline | Minimum Deliverable | Responsible Party |
|-------------|----------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Document key processes | Final 2 weeks | Written process documentation or updated knowledge base | Departing employee |
| Transfer project knowledge | Final 2 weeks | Project status summary, handover meeting with successor | Manager + Departing employee |
| Hand over active work | Final week | Task list with status, access credentials for shared accounts, contact list for external parties | Manager |
| Handover sign-off | Last day | Manager confirmation form: deliverables received, knowledge transfer adequate, outstanding items documented | Manager |

**Notice Period Controls**: During notice period, [Organisation]:
- Monitors data access for unusual patterns
- May restrict access to sensitive systems
- Ensures IP and confidential information are not copied
- Documents any concerns in HR file

## Return Process Completion

**Sign-Off Requirements**:

| Area | Verifier | Sign-Off Required |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| Physical assets returned | IT Asset Management | Yes - Asset return form |
| Access revoked | IT Operations | Yes - Access revocation checklist |
| Data wiped/transferred | IT Security | Yes - Data handling confirmation |
| BYOD cleared | IT Security | Yes - Attestation form |
| Knowledge transferred | Line Manager | Yes - Handover completion |
| Final HR exit | HR | Yes - Exit interview/checklist |

**Incomplete Return Process**:

If return is not completed:
1. Outstanding items escalated to HR within 48 hours
2. Manager notified; recovery efforts documented
3. If asset not returned within 14 days: Finance notified for payroll deduction (where legally permitted)
4. If data exposure risk: Security incident initiated
5. Legal action considered for high-value or critical assets

**Evidence Repository**: All return documentation, sign-off forms, and attestations stored in ISMS Evidence Library with retention per records management policy.

**Review Cadence**:

| Activity | Frequency | Responsible | Evidence |
|----------|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Policy review | Annual | CISO | Policy version history |
| AUP acknowledgment audit | Quarterly | HR | Acknowledgment reports |
| Asset return compliance review | Quarterly | IT Security | Return completion statistics |

- AUP acknowledgment rate calculated by HR; reported monthly
- Asset return completion rate calculated by IT; reported quarterly
- Trends tracked in the Summary Dashboard
- Gaps >5% below target escalated to Executive Management

**Policy Change Management**:

Out-of-cycle policy updates are triggered by:

- Regulatory requirement changes (Tier 1 mandatory; Tier 2 if applicable)
- Significant security incidents involving asset misuse or incomplete returns
- Technology changes that materially affect asset types or usage patterns
- Internal audit findings requiring policy clarification
- Management directive

Change process:

1. Change request submitted to CISO with business justification
2. Impact assessment completed (regulatory, operational, training)
3. Draft revisions reviewed by stakeholders (IT Security, HR, Legal)
4. Approval per standard approval chain (CISO → HR Director → Legal → Executive Management)
5. Communication plan executed (affected personnel notified; training updated if needed)
6. Version history updated; superseded version archived

# Definitions

**Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)**: Document defining permitted and prohibited activities when using organisational information assets and systems.

**Asset Return**: The process of recovering all organisational assets from personnel upon termination or change of employment, contract, or agreement.

**BYOD (Bring Your Own Device)**: Policy permitting personnel to use personal devices for work purposes, subject to specific security requirements.

**Offboarding**: The formal process of managing an individual's departure from the organisation, including asset return, access revocation, and knowledge transfer.

**Knowledge Transfer**: The process of documenting and transferring job-specific knowledge, processes, and information from departing personnel to their successors or the organisation.

**Access Revocation**: The removal of all system access rights, credentials, and permissions from personnel whose access is no longer authorised.

**Compensating Control**: An alternative security measure implemented when standard controls cannot be met, providing equivalent or acceptable risk reduction.

**Exception**: A formal, documented deviation from policy requirements, approved by designated authority with business justification and risk acceptance.
