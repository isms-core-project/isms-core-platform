### Assessment Overview

#### 1.1 Purpose

This workbook assesses management commitment to information security through a structured 100-point scoring rubric. It evaluates how effectively managers at all levels demonstrate visible commitment to security through their actions, resource allocation, and leadership behaviours.

The assessment serves multiple purposes:
- **Accountability**: Creates objective measurement of management security engagement
- **Improvement**: Identifies gaps requiring management development
- **Evidence**: Provides audit-ready documentation of management commitment
- **Trending**: Enables year-over-year comparison of leadership effectiveness
- **Recognition**: Identifies exemplary managers for recognition and best practice sharing

#### 1.2 Scope

The assessment covers all personnel with management responsibilities:

| Management Level | Definition | Typical Titles |
|------------------|------------|----------------|
| **Executive** | C-suite and direct reports to CEO | CEO, CFO, CTO, CISO, COO, VP |
| **Director** | Heads of major departments or functions | Director, Senior Director, Head of |
| **Manager** | Responsible for teams and deliverables | Manager, Senior Manager |
| **Team Lead** | First-line supervisors with direct reports | Team Lead, Supervisor, Lead |

**Inclusions:**
- All permanent employees with management responsibilities
- Long-term contractors (>12 months) in management roles
- Interim managers filling permanent positions
- Matrix managers with dotted-line reports (assess for direct reports only)

**Exclusions:**
- Project managers without direct reports
- Technical leads without formal management authority
- Managers on extended leave (>6 months) - assess upon return
- Contractors in temporary management positions (<12 months)

#### 1.3 Control Requirement

Per ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Control A.5.4:

> *"Management should require all personnel to apply information security in accordance with the established information security policy, topic-specific policies and procedures of the organisation."*

This control requires that management:
1. Actively requires personnel to follow security policies
2. Demonstrates commitment through visible actions
3. Provides resources for security implementation
4. Holds personnel accountable for security compliance
5. Leads by example in security-conscious behaviour

#### 1.4 Why This Matters

**Audit Perspective:**
Stage 2 auditors specifically look for evidence that management commitment is not just documented but actively demonstrated. This assessment provides:
- Quantifiable metrics showing management engagement levels
- Evidence trail linking management actions to security outcomes
- Gap identification with remediation tracking
- Year-over-year trending to demonstrate continuous improvement

**Business Perspective:**
Research consistently shows that security culture flows from the top. Organisations where management actively demonstrates security commitment experience:
- 60% fewer security incidents caused by human error
- Higher policy compliance rates across all personnel
- Faster incident response times
- Better resource allocation for security initiatives

**Risk Perspective:**
Management disengagement creates significant risks:
- Shadow IT proliferates when managers don't enforce approved tools
- Security exceptions become permanent when managers don't review them
- Training completion drops when managers don't prioritise it
- Incident reporting decreases when managers don't respond appropriately

#### 1.5 Assessment Frequency

| Management Level | Assessment Frequency | Reviewer | Timing |
|------------------|---------------------|----------|--------|
| Executive | Annually | CISO / Board | Q4 |

#### Step 2: Complete Commitment Assessment

**Purpose:** Score each manager against 11 defined criteria using evidence-based evaluation.

**Scoring Scale (0-5):**

| Score | Label | Evidence Standard |
|:-----:|-------|-------------------|
| 0 | Not demonstrated | No evidence exists; behaviour not observed |
| 1 | Rarely demonstrated | 1-2 isolated instances in assessment period |
| 2 | Sometimes demonstrated | Inconsistent; evident <50% of opportunities |
| 3 | Often demonstrated | Generally consistent; evident 50-75% of opportunities |
| 4 | Usually demonstrated | Consistent with minor gaps; evident 75-90% |
| 5 | Always demonstrated | Exemplary; evident >90% with no significant gaps |

**Scoring Categories and Criteria:**

**Category 1: Attendance (30 points total)**

| Criterion | Weight | What to Look For |
|-----------|:------:|------------------|
| Attends scheduled ISMS Committee meetings | 10 | Meeting attendance records; acceptable: ≥80% attendance |
| Participates in security governance forums | 10 | Active participation (speaking, voting) not just presence |
| Available for security escalations and decisions | 10 | Response time to escalations; availability during incidents |

**Category 2: Training (20 points total)**

| Criterion | Weight | What to Look For |
|-----------|:------:|------------------|
| Completes required security awareness training on time | 10 | LMS records; on-time = before deadline, not after |
| Ensures direct reports complete mandatory training | 10 | Team completion rate; manager follow-up actions for overdue |

**Category 3: Resource Support (20 points total)**

| Criterion | Weight | What to Look For |
|-----------|:------:|------------------|
| Approves budget for security initiatives | 10 | Budget requests approved vs. rejected; justification for rejections |
| Provides adequate staffing for security activities | 10 | Security responsibilities in job descriptions; time allocation |

**Category 4: Operational Engagement (20 points total)**

| Criterion | Weight | What to Look For |
|-----------|:------:|------------------|
| Reviews and acts on security reports/metrics | 10 | Evidence of report review; actions taken based on findings |
| Responds to security incidents within SLA | 10 | Incident response records; time to acknowledge and act |

**Category 5: Culture/Enforcement (10 points total)**

| Criterion | Weight | What to Look For |
|-----------|:------:|------------------|
| Demonstrates security-conscious behaviour | 5 | No policy bypasses; leads by example; clean desk; etc. |
| Holds personnel accountable for policy compliance | 5 | Documented conversations; performance impact; escalations |

**Worked Examples:**

**Example 1: High-Performing Manager (Score: 92/100)**
```
Manager: Sarah Chen, Director of Product Development
Assessment Period: 2026

Attendance (28/30):
- ISMS Committee: 11/12 meetings attended (Score: 5, Weight: 10) = 10
- Governance forums: Active participant, leads quarterly reviews (Score: 5) = 10
- Escalation availability: Responded within 2 hours average (Score: 4) = 8

Training (20/20):
- Personal training: Completed 2 weeks early (Score: 5) = 10
- Team training: 100% completion, personally followed up (Score: 5) = 10

Resource Support (18/20):
- Budget approval: Approved all security requests with justification (Score: 5) = 10
- Staffing: Security champion role created but unfilled (Score: 4) = 8

Operational Engagement (18/20):
- Report review: Weekly security metrics review documented (Score: 5) = 10
- Incident response: 1 incident with 6-hour response (SLA: 4 hours) (Score: 4) = 8

Culture/Enforcement (8/10):
- Personal behaviour: Clean desk, no exceptions requested (Score: 4) = 4
- Accountability: Documented coaching for 2 policy violations (Score: 4) = 4

TOTAL: 92/100 = Exemplary
```

**Example 2: Manager Requiring Improvement (Score: 58/100)**
```
Manager: Tom Wilson, IT Operations Manager
Assessment Period: 2026

Attendance (18/30):
- ISMS Committee: 7/12 meetings attended (Score: 3) = 6
- Governance forums: Attends but rarely contributes (Score: 2) = 4
- Escalation availability: Delayed responses, often unavailable (Score: 2) = 4

Training (12/20):
- Personal training: Completed 3 weeks late (Score: 2) = 4
- Team training: 75% completion, no follow-up on overdue (Score: 2) = 4

Resource Support (12/20):
- Budget approval: Rejected 2 security requests without documentation (Score: 3) = 6
- Staffing: No security responsibilities in team JDs (Score: 3) = 6

Operational Engagement (10/20):
- Report review: No evidence of review or action (Score: 2) = 4
- Incident response: Multiple incidents with >24 hour response (Score: 3) = 6

- Personal behaviour: 2 exception requests; one for permanent bypass (Score: 2) = 2
```

#### Step 3: Review Summary Scores

**Purpose:** Aggregate scores and identify managers requiring action.

The Summary Scores sheet auto-calculates:
- **Total Weight**: 100 (fixed)
- **Achieved Score**: Sum of weighted scores from Commitment Assessment
- **Percentage**: (Achieved / 100) × 100
- **Status**: Based on threshold formula

**Status Thresholds and Required Actions:**

| Percentage | Status | Action Required | Timeline |
|:----------:|--------|-----------------|----------|
| 90-100% | Exemplary | Recognition; share best practices | Acknowledge within 30 days |
| 70-89% | Adequate | Maintain current level; minor improvements | No mandatory action |
| 50-69% | Improvement Needed | Development plan required | Plan within 30 days |
| <50% | Non-Compliant | Immediate intervention; escalate to HR | Escalate within 5 days |

### After Completing the Data Sheets

Once Steps 1–3 are complete:

- **Evidence Register** — Open this sheet and record all supporting evidence used during the assessment: LMS completion reports, attendance logs, budget approval records, incident response timestamps, and any other documentation that supports your scores. The Evidence Register is the audit trail that validates your scoring decisions. Reference evidence here using the Evidence_ID format (e.g., EVD-A.5.4.1-001) and link to files stored in the SharePoint evidence folder.
- **Summary Dashboard** — Review the dashboard for an executive overview of commitment levels across the management population. It auto-aggregates scores from the Commitment Assessment and Summary Scores sheets. Verify the metrics reflect your data before advancing to the sign-off workflow.

### Scoring Calibration

#### 6.1 Calibration Sessions

To ensure consistent scoring across assessors:

1. **Pre-Assessment Calibration** (Mandatory for first-time assessors)
   - Review scoring criteria definitions
   - Score 2-3 sample cases together
   - Discuss and resolve scoring differences
   - Document calibration decisions

2. **Mid-Assessment Check-In** (Recommended for large assessments)
   - Review edge cases encountered
   - Validate consistency across departments
   - Adjust approach if systematic drift identified

3. **Post-Assessment Review** (Mandatory)
   - Review score distribution for anomalies
   - Validate extreme scores (0s and 5s)
   - Document lessons learned for next cycle

#### 6.2 Common Calibration Issues

| Issue | Resolution |
|-------|------------|
| Scoring inflation (mostly 4s and 5s) | Re-anchor against evidence requirements |
| Department variation | Cross-department calibration session |
| Assessor bias | Rotate assessors; peer review scores |
| Missing evidence | Score lower; evidence required for high scores |

### Common Pitfalls

❌ **MISTAKE**: Scoring based on intentions rather than demonstrated actions
✅ **CORRECT**: Score only observable, documented behaviours with evidence

❌ **MISTAKE**: Using different scoring standards for different managers
✅ **CORRECT**: Apply consistent criteria across all assessments; calibrate assessors

❌ **MISTAKE**: Completing assessments without consulting evidence sources
✅ **CORRECT**: Review LMS, attendance records, and budget data BEFORE scoring

❌ **MISTAKE**: Skipping managers who are "too busy" or senior
✅ **CORRECT**: All managers must be assessed regardless of seniority; no exceptions

❌ **MISTAKE**: Not documenting evidence for high scores
✅ **CORRECT**: Document evidence for ALL scores; exemplary scores need strongest evidence

❌ **MISTAKE**: Assessing managers immediately after negative interactions
✅ **CORRECT**: Conduct assessments objectively using documented evidence over full period

❌ **MISTAKE**: Failing to discuss results with the assessed manager
✅ **CORRECT**: Share results and develop improvement plans collaboratively within 30 days

❌ **MISTAKE**: Not tracking year-over-year trends
✅ **CORRECT**: Compare with prior assessments to identify patterns and improvements

❌ **MISTAKE**: Accepting manager self-assessment without verification
✅ **CORRECT**: All scores require independent verification from system records

❌ **MISTAKE**: Scoring based on team performance rather than manager actions
✅ **CORRECT**: Assess the manager's personal actions; team outcomes are indicators, not scores
