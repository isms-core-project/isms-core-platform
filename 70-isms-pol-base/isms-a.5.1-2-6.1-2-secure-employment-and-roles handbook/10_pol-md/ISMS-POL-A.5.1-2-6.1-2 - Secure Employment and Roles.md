## Executive Summary

This policy establishes [Organization]'s requirements for information security governance, organizational structure, personnel screening, and employment security in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Controls A.5.1, A.5.2, A.6.1, and A.6.2.

**Scope**: This policy applies to all personnel (employees, contractors, temporary workers, interns), all information security policies, all organizational security roles, all pre-employment screening activities, and all employment contracts and terms of employment.

**Purpose**: Define organizational requirements for information security policy governance, role definition, personnel vetting, and contractual security obligations. This policy establishes WHAT governance structures are required, WHO holds security responsibilities, WHICH screening procedures apply, and WHAT contractual obligations bind personnel to security requirements. Implementation procedures (HOW) are documented separately in ISMS-IMP-A.5.1-2-6.1-2.S1 through .S5.

**Combined Control Framework**: These four controls are implemented as a unified framework because they form the foundational "people layer" of the ISMS:

1. **Policy Foundation (A.5.1)**: Establishes governance rules and policy hierarchy
2. **Role Structure (A.5.2)**: Defines organizational security accountability (WHO does WHAT)
3. **Pre-Employment Vetting (A.6.1)**: Screens personnel BEFORE granting access
4. **Contractual Binding (A.6.2)**: Legally binds personnel to security obligations

**Process Flow**:
```
A.5.1 Policy Framework
      ↓
A.5.2 Roles & Responsibilities → Define security organization
      ↓
A.6.1 Screening & Vetting → Vet candidates before employment
      ↓
A.6.2 Employment Terms → Bind employees to security obligations
      ↓
CONTINUOUS CYCLE: Policy updates, role changes, ongoing compliance
```

**Implementation Synergy**: Combined approach eliminates redundant assessments, enables shared evidence collection (HR records, signed contracts, training completion), provides unified governance structure (same approval authorities), and integrates seamlessly with HR joiner/mover/leaver processes.

**Statement of Applicability Independence**: Although implemented as a unified framework, A.5.1, A.5.2, A.6.1, and A.6.2 are assessed independently in the Statement of Applicability. Each control retains distinct requirements, evidence collection, and compliance scoring for audit purposes.

**Regulatory Alignment**: This policy addresses mandatory compliance requirements per ISMS-POL-00 (Regulatory Applicability Framework), including Swiss nDSG (employment data protection), Swiss Code of Obligations (employment law), EU GDPR (where applicable), and ISO/IEC 27001:2022.

**Why This Matters**: People are both the greatest security asset and the greatest security risk. Industry research shows that 82% of data breaches involve a human element (Verizon DBIR 2024), insider threats account for 34% of all breaches, and the average cost of an insider incident is $648,000. This framework addresses these risks through systematic governance, clear accountability, rigorous vetting, and contractual obligations.

## 1. Control Alignment & Scope

### 1.1 ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Control Statements

**ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A.5.1 - Policies for Information Security**

> *Policies for information security and topic-specific policies should be defined, approved by management, published, communicated to and acknowledged by relevant personnel and relevant interested parties, and reviewed at planned intervals and if significant changes occur.*

**Control Objective**: Establish and maintain an appropriate set of information security policies that provide direction and support for information security in accordance with business requirements and relevant laws and regulations.

**ISO/IEC 27002:2022 Guidance Summary**:
- Information security policy defines organization's approach to managing information security
- Topic-specific policies address specific security topics (e.g., access control, cryptography, physical security)
- Policies define roles and responsibilities for information security
- Policies approved by appropriate management level
- Policies communicated to all relevant personnel and interested parties
- Policies reviewed at defined intervals or when significant changes occur
- Policy document management ensures current versions are available

**ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A.5.2 - Information Security Roles and Responsibilities**

> *Information security roles and responsibilities should be defined and allocated according to the organization needs.*

**Control Objective**: Ensure that information security responsibilities are clearly defined, assigned, understood, and accepted throughout the organization.

**ISO/IEC 27002:2022 Guidance Summary**:
- Overall responsibility for information security assigned to specific role (typically CISO or equivalent)
- Specific information security responsibilities assigned to relevant roles throughout organization
- Contact points for information security matters established
- Roles and responsibilities documented and communicated
- Responsibilities for external parties defined in agreements
- Management provides resources and support for assigned responsibilities

**ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A.6.1 - Screening**

> *Background verification checks on all candidates to become personnel should be carried out prior to joining the organization and on an ongoing basis taking into consideration applicable laws, regulations and ethics and should be proportional to the business requirements, the classification of the information to be accessed and the perceived risks.*

**Control Objective**: Ensure that candidates for employment are suitable for the roles they are being considered for and that the organization understands any risks associated with granting them access to organizational assets.

**ISO/IEC 27002:2022 Guidance Summary**:
- Background verification checks proportional to business requirements, data sensitivity, and risk
- Checks include identity verification, employment history, educational qualifications
- Checks comply with applicable laws, regulations, and ethical requirements
- Additional checks for positions involving access to sensitive information or systems
- Checks conducted before granting access to assets
- Ongoing verification for existing personnel where appropriate
- Third parties subject to equivalent screening requirements

**ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A.6.2 - Terms and Conditions of Employment**

> *The employment contractual agreements should state the personnel's and the organization's responsibilities for information security.*

**Control Objective**: Ensure that personnel understand and fulfill their information security responsibilities, and that the organization's rights regarding information security are protected.

**ISO/IEC 27002:2022 Guidance Summary**:
- Employment contracts include information security responsibilities
- Confidentiality or non-disclosure agreements signed
- Responsibilities continue after employment termination where appropriate
- Consequences of breaching information security requirements stated
- Intellectual property rights and protection of organizational information defined
- Personnel acknowledge their responsibilities before being granted access

### 1.2 Combined Control Framework Rationale

[Organization] implements these four controls as a unified framework because they represent sequential layers of the organizational security foundation:

**Layer 1 - Governance Foundation (A.5.1)**: Before assigning responsibilities or hiring personnel, the organization must establish the policy framework that defines what information security means and how it will be managed. Policies provide the rulebook that everyone must follow.

**Layer 2 - Organizational Structure (A.5.2)**: Once policies exist, the organization must define WHO is responsible for implementing, enforcing, and monitoring those policies. Without clear roles, policies remain theoretical documents.

**Layer 3 - Personnel Vetting (A.6.1)**: Before bringing new personnel into the security organization, the organization must verify they are trustworthy and suitable for their intended roles. Screening ensures the "right people" join the team.

**Layer 4 - Contractual Binding (A.6.2)**: After vetting personnel, the organization must legally bind them to the security obligations defined in policies and assigned through roles. Contracts make security responsibilities enforceable.

**Implementation Synergy**: 
- Shared HR integration: Joiner process combines A.6.1 screening + A.6.2 contract signing
- Shared evidence: HR records, signed contracts, policy acknowledgment, training completion
- Unified governance: Same CISO approves policies (A.5.1), defines roles (A.5.2), sets screening requirements (A.6.1), specifies contract terms (A.6.2)
- Single compliance framework: One assessment covering all four controls reduces duplication

**Why Separate Implementation Would Fail**:
- Policy governance disconnected from role accountability → unclear WHO enforces policies
- Role definitions without screening → unsuitable personnel assigned security responsibilities
- Screening without contractual obligations → vetted personnel not legally bound to security rules
- Fragmented evidence collection → redundant HR processes, inconsistent documentation

**Statement of Applicability Independence**: Despite unified implementation, each control maintains distinct requirements for SoA purposes:
- **A.5.1**: Policy framework completeness, approval process, review cycle, communication
- **A.5.2**: Role definition clarity, responsibility assignment, organizational structure
- **A.6.1**: Screening procedures, verification methods, legal compliance
- **A.6.2**: Contract clauses, confidentiality agreements, acknowledgment processes

### 1.3 What This Policy Does

This policy:
- **Defines** information security policy governance framework (hierarchy, lifecycle, approval)
- **Establishes** organizational security structure (key roles, responsibilities, accountability)
- **Specifies** pre-employment screening requirements (vetting levels, verification procedures)
- **Mandates** employment security contractual obligations (confidentiality, NDA, AUP)
- **Assigns** accountability for policy governance, role definition, screening, and employment security
- **References** applicable regulatory requirements per ISMS-POL-00
- **Integrates** with HR joiner/mover/leaver processes for operational efficiency

### 1.4 What This Policy Does NOT Do

This policy does NOT:
- **Specify detailed screening procedures** (see ISMS-IMP-A.5.1-2-6.1-2.S3 Screening Assessment)
- **Define specific policy content** (topic-specific policies define their own requirements)
- **Provide employment contract templates** (see Annex D for example clauses)
- **List all security roles in the organization** (see ISMS-IMP-A.5.1-2-6.1-2.S2 for role catalog)
- **Define background check vendors or methods** (vendor selection based on risk assessment)
- **Replace HR policies or employment law obligations** (complements existing HR framework)
- **Specify individual job descriptions** (security responsibilities integrated into existing job descriptions)

**Rationale**: Separating policy requirements from implementation guidance enables:
- Policy stability despite evolving HR practices and screening technologies
- Technical agility for HR process updates without policy revision
- Clear distinction between governance (policy) and execution (implementation)
- Focused audit scope (auditors audit policy compliance, not specific HR procedures)
- Organization-agnostic approach applicable to any ISMS implementation

## 2. Policy Framework Requirements (Control A.5.1)

### 2.1 Information Security Policy Hierarchy

[Organization] SHALL maintain a hierarchical information security policy framework consisting of:

**Tier 1: Master Information Security Policy**
- **Purpose**: Overarching statement of [Organization]'s approach to information security
- **Scope**: Entire organization, all assets, all personnel, all locations
- **Content**: Security objectives, management commitment, policy framework structure, roles and responsibilities, compliance obligations
- **Approval Authority**: Executive Management (CEO/Board)
- **Review Frequency**: Annual or upon significant organizational change
- **Audience**: All personnel, interested parties, auditors, regulators

**Tier 2: Topic-Specific Information Security Policies**
- **Purpose**: Define requirements for specific security domains aligned with ISO 27001 Annex A controls
- **Examples**: Access Control (A.5.15-16-18), Cryptography (A.8.24), Data Protection (A.8.10-11-12), Network Security (A.8.20-21-22)
- **Scope**: Specific security domain within organization
- **Content**: Control requirements, roles and responsibilities, compliance criteria, exceptions, references to implementation guidance
- **Approval Authority**: CISO (with Executive Management oversight)
- **Review Frequency**: Annual or upon significant regulatory/technical change
- **Audience**: All personnel (general awareness), specific roles (detailed compliance)

**Tier 3: Standards and Guidelines**
- **Purpose**: Technical standards and implementation guidance supporting policies
- **Examples**: Password complexity standards, encryption algorithm standards, patching timelines
- **Scope**: Specific technical domain or system type
- **Content**: Technical specifications, configuration baselines, implementation guidance
- **Approval Authority**: Security Team Lead / CISO
- **Review Frequency**: Quarterly or upon technology change
- **Audience**: Technical personnel, system administrators, developers

**Tier 4: Procedures and Work Instructions**
- **Purpose**: Step-by-step instructions for executing security controls
- **Examples**: User provisioning procedures (IMP documents), incident response runbooks, backup procedures
- **Scope**: Specific process or task
- **Content**: Detailed steps, responsibilities, tools, inputs/outputs
- **Approval Authority**: Process Owner / Manager
- **Review Frequency**: Semi-annual or upon process change
- **Audience**: Personnel performing specific tasks

**Policy Hierarchy Principles**:
- Higher-tier policies set requirements; lower-tier documents provide implementation details
- Lower-tier documents MUST align with higher-tier policies (no contradictions)
- Policy stability: Tier 1-2 change infrequently; Tier 3-4 evolve with technology
- Audit focus: Tier 1-2 subject to formal audit; Tier 3-4 operational verification
- Accessibility: All tiers accessible to relevant personnel through policy portal/repository

### 2.2 Policy Lifecycle Management

[Organization] SHALL manage information security policies through defined lifecycle stages:

**Stage 1: Policy Creation and Development**
- **Trigger**: New regulatory requirement, new security domain, significant risk identified, ISO 27001 control implementation
- **Process**: 
  1. Policy need identified by CISO, Compliance, or Risk Management
  2. Policy owner assigned (typically CISO for Tier 1-2 policies)
  3. Policy drafted using organizational policy template
  4. Draft reviewed by stakeholders (Legal, HR, IT, affected business units)
  5. Draft incorporates stakeholder feedback
- **Deliverable**: Draft policy ready for approval
- **Timeline**: 4-6 weeks for Tier 2 policies (topic-specific)

**Stage 2: Policy Review and Approval**
- **Process**:
  1. Draft submitted to approval authority (per policy tier)
  2. Legal review confirms regulatory compliance
  3. DPO review confirms data protection compliance (if applicable)
  4. Approval authority reviews and approves/requests changes
  5. Final approval documented (signature, approval record, version control)
- **Approval Authorities**:
  - Tier 1 (Master Policy): Executive Management (CEO/Board)
  - Tier 2 (Topic-Specific): CISO (with Executive Management notification)
  - Tier 3 (Standards): Security Team Lead
  - Tier 4 (Procedures): Process Owner
- **Deliverable**: Approved policy with documented approval record
- **Timeline**: 2-4 weeks for approval process

**Stage 3: Policy Publication and Communication**
- **Publication**:
  - Policy published to organizational policy repository (intranet, SharePoint, policy management system)
  - Version control: Document ID, version number, approval date, next review date
  - Accessibility: Available to all relevant personnel (access controls if needed for sensitive policies)
- **Communication**:
  - Policy announcement distributed to all affected personnel
  - Training provided for policies requiring specific knowledge (e.g., data classification)
  - Policy acknowledgment collected where required (e.g., AUP, code of conduct)
- **Deliverable**: Published policy accessible to organization, personnel notified
- **Timeline**: 1-2 weeks for publication and communication

**Stage 4: Policy Acknowledgment (where required)**
- **Acknowledgment Required For**:
  - Tier 1 Master Policy: All personnel annually
  - Acceptable Use Policy: All personnel with system access
  - Confidentiality/NDA: All personnel with access to confidential data
  - Code of Conduct: All personnel
  - Topic-Specific Policies: Personnel in specific roles (e.g., developers acknowledge Secure Development policy)
- **Acknowledgment Methods**:
  - Electronic acknowledgment via HR system, LMS, or policy portal
  - Signed acknowledgment form (physical or digital signature)
  - Training completion with attestation
- **Tracking**: Acknowledgment records maintained in HR system or policy management system
- **Enforcement**: Access to systems/data conditional on policy acknowledgment for critical policies

**Stage 5: Policy Review and Maintenance**
- **Review Frequency**:
  - Tier 1 (Master): Annual
  - Tier 2 (Topic-Specific): Annual or upon regulatory change
  - Tier 3 (Standards): Quarterly or upon technology change
  - Tier 4 (Procedures): Semi-annual or upon process change
- **Triggered Reviews**:
  - Significant regulatory change (new law, updated regulation)
  - Major security incident exposing policy gap
  - Organizational change (merger, acquisition, new business line)
  - Failed audit finding related to policy
  - Technology change rendering policy obsolete
- **Review Process**:
  1. Policy owner initiates review (scheduled or triggered)
  2. Review assesses: regulatory alignment, effectiveness, clarity, stakeholder feedback
  3. Updates drafted if needed (follows creation process for significant changes)
  4. Minor updates (typos, clarifications) approved by policy owner
  5. Major updates re-enter full approval process
- **Review Documentation**: Review record documenting review date, reviewer, findings, actions taken

**Stage 6: Policy Retirement and Archival**
- **Retirement Triggers**:
  - Policy superseded by updated version
  - Policy scope no longer relevant (technology retired, regulation rescinded)
  - Consolidation of multiple policies into single policy
- **Retirement Process**:
  1. Policy marked as "Retired" or "Superseded" in policy repository
  2. Replacement policy (if applicable) identified and cross-referenced
  3. Archived version retained for audit trail (minimum retention: audit cycle + 1 year)
  4. Notification sent to personnel if policy retirement impacts their responsibilities
- **Archive Access**: Retired policies accessible to auditors and compliance team for historical reference

**Policy Lifecycle Governance**:
- Policy Owner: Accountable for policy accuracy, currency, effectiveness
- CISO: Oversight of all Tier 1-2 policies, approval authority for Tier 2
- Executive Management: Approval authority for Tier 1, strategic oversight
- Legal/Compliance: Review for regulatory compliance, legal risk
- DPO: Review for data protection compliance (GDPR, nDSG)

### 2.3 Policy Classification and Accessibility

[Organization] SHALL classify information security policies to determine access controls and distribution:

**Public Policies**:
- **Definition**: Policies disclosable to external parties (customers, partners, public)
- **Examples**: Privacy policy, information security statement, whistleblower policy
- **Access Control**: Publicly accessible (website, customer portal)
- **Distribution**: No restrictions

**Internal Policies**:
- **Definition**: Policies for organizational personnel use, not for external disclosure
- **Examples**: Most Tier 2 topic-specific policies, standards, procedures
- **Access Control**: Accessible to all personnel via intranet/policy portal
- **Distribution**: Internal only, employees and authorized contractors

**Confidential Policies**:
- **Definition**: Policies containing sensitive information requiring restricted access
- **Examples**: Incident response procedures with technical details, detailed security architectures, vendor-specific configurations
- **Access Control**: Restricted to specific roles (Security Team, IT Operations, authorized personnel)
- **Distribution**: Need-to-know basis, role-based access controls

**Classification Criteria**:
- Does policy contain sensitive technical details that could aid an attacker? → Confidential
- Does policy contain proprietary business information? → Confidential
- Does policy provide general security awareness guidance? → Internal
- Is policy required for regulatory transparency or customer assurance? → Public

**Accessibility Requirements**:
- All personnel MUST have access to policies relevant to their roles and responsibilities
- Policy repository MUST be accessible during business hours (99.5% availability target)
- Policy search functionality MUST enable personnel to find relevant policies quickly
- Policies MUST be available in organizational working languages (e.g., English, German for Swiss organization)

### 2.4 Policy Content Requirements

[Organization]'s information security policies (Tier 2 topic-specific policies) SHALL include the following elements:

**Mandatory Policy Elements**:

1. **Document Control Section**:
   - Document ID (ISMS-POL-A.X.XX format)
   - Version number and version history
   - Approval authority and approval date
   - Review cycle and next review date
   - Classification (Public/Internal/Confidential)
   - Related documents and dependencies

2. **Executive Summary**:
   - Policy purpose and objectives
   - Scope and applicability
   - Key requirements summary (1-2 paragraphs)
   - Regulatory alignment statement

3. **Control Alignment & Scope**:
   - ISO 27001:2022 control(s) addressed
   - Control objective(s)
   - What policy does and does NOT do (clear boundaries)
   - Scope definition (what/who is covered)

4. **Requirements Framework**:
   - WHAT requirements apply (technical, procedural, organizational)
   - WHO is responsible (roles and accountabilities)
   - WHEN requirements apply (frequency, triggers, timelines)
   - Mandatory vs. recommended requirements clearly distinguished
   - Exception process defined

5. **Roles and Responsibilities**:
   - RACI matrix or narrative assignment of responsibilities
   - Escalation paths for policy violations or questions
   - Integration with other ISMS processes

6. **Regulatory Framework** (or reference to ISMS-POL-00):
   - Mandatory compliance requirements (Tier 1)
   - Conditional applicability requirements (Tier 2)
   - Informational references (Tier 3)

7. **References to Implementation Guidance**:
   - ISMS-IMP-A.X.XX documents for HOW requirements are implemented
   - Assessment procedures
   - Technical standards and guidelines
   - External references (ISO 27002, NIST, etc.)

**Optional Policy Elements** (include where relevant):

8. **Annexes**:
   - Templates (example forms, checklists)
   - Reference materials (technology comparisons, vendor evaluation criteria)
   - Glossary (if policy introduces specialized terminology)

9. **Evidence Section** (for Score 1-4 policy controls):
   - Stage 1 Evidence: Documentation existence requirements
   - Stage 2 Evidence: Operational effectiveness requirements
   - Makes audit expectations explicit

**Prohibited Policy Content**:
- Detailed technical procedures (belongs in IMP documents or Tier 3 standards)
- Vendor-specific product names or configurations (use generic terms, reference technical standards)
- Excessive implementation detail (bloatware - keep policies lean and principle-based)
- Regulatory interpretation requiring legal expertise (reference Legal/Compliance for interpretation)

**Policy Change Management**:
- Minor changes (typos, clarifications, formatting): Policy owner approval sufficient
- Significant changes (new requirements, scope expansion, regulatory updates): Full approval process required
- Emergency changes (urgent regulatory requirement, critical security gap): Expedited approval with post-facto full approval

[Organization] SHALL communicate information security policies and provide training to ensure understanding and compliance:

**Communication Methods**:

**Initial Publication**:
- Policy announcement via email to all affected personnel
- Intranet news post highlighting new policy
- Team meeting briefing for policies affecting specific teams
- Dedicated training session for complex policies (e.g., Data Classification, Secure Development)

**Ongoing Awareness**:
- Policy portal with search functionality accessible to all personnel
- Quarterly security newsletter highlighting policy updates
- Annual security awareness training includes policy overview
- Onboarding training for new hires includes mandatory policies (Master Policy, AUP, Code of Conduct)

**Targeted Communication**:
- Role-specific policies communicated to personnel in those roles (e.g., Secure Development policy to developers)
- Policy changes affecting specific systems/processes: direct communication to system owners and operators
- Exception approvals: communicate approved exceptions to affected personnel and managers

**Training Requirements**:

**General Personnel**:
- Annual security awareness training includes: Master Policy overview, AUP, data classification, incident reporting, acceptable use
- Training completion tracked in HR/LMS system
- Training completion required before system access granted (new hires)

**Role-Specific Personnel**:
- Administrators: Training on policies affecting system administration (Access Control, Logging, Change Management)
- Developers: Training on Secure Development, Cryptography, Data Protection policies
- Managers: Training on personnel security, access approval, policy enforcement responsibilities
- Security Team: Comprehensive training on all ISMS policies

**Training Effectiveness**:
- Training includes knowledge check (quiz, assessment) to verify understanding
- Training completion rate target: ≥95% annually
- Training records maintained for audit evidence

**Policy Acknowledgment**:
- Acknowledgment collected for critical policies (Master Policy, AUP, Code of Conduct, Confidentiality/NDA)
- Acknowledgment statement: "I have read, understood, and agree to comply with [Policy Name]"
- Acknowledgment records linked to personnel records in HR system
- Annual re-acknowledgment for critical policies

### 2.7 Policy Exceptions and Non-Compliance

[Organization] SHALL implement a defined process for policy exceptions and manage policy non-compliance:

**Exception Management**:

**When Exceptions Are Acceptable**:
- Temporary business need conflicts with policy requirement (with compensating controls)
- Technical limitation prevents full compliance (with risk acceptance)
- Legacy system retirement planned (interim exception until decommission)
- Regulatory conflict between jurisdictions (documented legal guidance required)

**Exception Request Process**:
1. Requestor submits exception request to policy owner
2. Exception request documents:
   - Policy requirement being excepted
   - Business justification for exception
   - Risk assessment (what risk is created by exception)
   - Proposed compensating controls (how risk will be mitigated)
   - Exception duration (temporary with expiration date, or permanent with ongoing review)
3. Policy owner reviews exception request
4. Risk assessment performed by Security Team
5. Approval decision:
   - Low risk exceptions: Policy owner approval
   - Medium risk exceptions: CISO approval
   - High risk exceptions: CISO approval + Executive Management notification
6. Exception approval documented (exception register)
7. Compensating controls implemented and verified
8. Exception reviewed at defined intervals (quarterly for temporary, annually for permanent)

**Exception Tracking**:
- Exception register maintained by CISO office
- Each exception entry includes: exception ID, policy reference, requestor, business justification, risk rating, compensating controls, approval authority, approval date, expiration date, review status
- Quarterly exception review: verify compensating controls still effective, exception still necessary
- Annual exception report to Executive Management

**Policy Violation Handling**:

**Violation Detection**:
- Automated policy compliance monitoring (where technical controls exist)
- Audit findings (internal or external audits)
- Incident reports (policy violation discovered during incident response)
- Self-reporting by personnel or managers
- Whistleblower reports

**Violation Response Process**:
1. Violation identified and documented
2. Initial assessment: severity (minor/major), intentional vs. unintentional, impact to organization
3. Investigation: determine root cause, scope of violation, personnel involved
4. Remediation: correct the violation, implement controls to prevent recurrence
5. Disciplinary action (if appropriate): per HR policy and employment law
   - Minor unintentional violations: Coaching, training, warning
   - Repeated violations: Written warning, performance improvement plan
   - Major violations: Suspension, termination (for cause if serious breach)
   - Malicious violations: Immediate termination, legal action if appropriate
6. Lessons learned: update policy if violation indicates policy gap or unclear requirement

**Violation Documentation**:
- Violation log maintained by CISO office and HR
- Violation record includes: incident ID, policy violated, personnel involved, violation date, severity, root cause, remediation actions, disciplinary action taken
- Aggregate violation metrics reported quarterly (types of violations, trends, effectiveness of controls)
- Individual violation records kept confidential (HR privacy requirements)

**Continuous Improvement**:
- Pattern analysis: Repeated violations of same policy indicate training gap or unclear policy
- Policy effectiveness review: Policies with high violation rates reviewed for clarity and practicality
- Cultural assessment: Violation trends inform security culture initiatives

### 3.2 Key Security Roles and Responsibilities

[Organization] SHALL define and assign the following key information security roles:

#### 3.2.1 Executive Management (CEO / Board)

**Accountability**: Ultimate accountability for information security, ISMS effectiveness, and regulatory compliance.

**Responsibilities**:
- Approve Master Information Security Policy
- Approve ISMS scope, objectives, and risk appetite
- Allocate adequate resources for information security program
- Ensure ISMS integrated into organizational processes
- Review ISMS performance (annual management review)
- Approve high-risk exceptions and risk acceptance decisions
- Demonstrate leadership commitment to information security
- Ensure information security culture throughout organization

**Authority**:
- Final decision authority on strategic security investments
- Authority to enforce security policies organization-wide
- Authority to terminate personnel for serious security violations

**Interfaces**:
- CISO (receives security reports, approves strategic initiatives)
- DPO (receives privacy compliance reports)
- Internal Audit (receives audit findings)
- External Auditors (certification audits, regulatory examinations)
- Board of Directors (for organizations with boards)

#### 3.2.2 Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)

**Accountability**: Accountable for designing, implementing, maintaining, and continuously improving the ISMS.

**Responsibilities**:

**ISMS Governance**:
- Design and maintain ISMS framework aligned with ISO 27001:2022
- Develop information security strategy aligned with business objectives
- Define information security policies (Tier 2 topic-specific policies)
- Establish and maintain information security risk management process
- Oversee Statement of Applicability (SoA) and control implementation
- Coordinate internal audits and external certification audits

**Security Operations Oversight**:
- Oversee Security Team activities (incident response, monitoring, assessments)
- Review security incidents and approve incident response actions
- Approve security architecture and design decisions
- Oversee vulnerability management and remediation prioritization
- Approve security tool selection and deployment

**Stakeholder Management**:
- Report ISMS performance to Executive Management (quarterly/annual)
- Coordinate with DPO on privacy-security integration
- Liaise with Legal/Compliance on regulatory requirements
- Engage with business units to understand security needs
- Represent organization to external stakeholders (auditors, regulators, customers)

**Resource Management**:
- Define security budget requirements
- Manage Security Team personnel (hiring, performance management, training)
- Allocate security resources across initiatives
- Ensure security team has adequate tools and training

**Authority**:
- Approve information security policies (Tier 2)
- Approve security exceptions (up to medium risk)
- Authorize security investigations
- Escalate high-risk issues to Executive Management
- Request resources for security initiatives
- Enforce security policies (including access suspension for violations)

**Qualifications**:
- Professional security certification (CISSP, CISM, or equivalent)
- Minimum 5 years information security management experience
- Knowledge of ISO 27001, GDPR, and relevant regulatory frameworks
- Understanding of organizational business context

**Interfaces**:
- Reports to: CEO, COO, or CTO
- Manages: Security Team
- Coordinates with: CIO/CTO (IT strategy), CHRO (HR integration), CFO (budget), DPO (privacy), Legal (compliance)

#### 3.2.3 Data Protection Officer (DPO)

**Accountability**: Accountable for organizational compliance with data protection regulations (GDPR, nDSG/FADP).

**Responsibilities**:

**Privacy Compliance**:
- Monitor compliance with GDPR, nDSG, and other data protection regulations
- Advise organization on data protection obligations
- Conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs)
- Maintain records of processing activities (ROPA)
- Serve as contact point for data subjects and supervisory authorities

**Privacy-Security Integration**:
- Coordinate with CISO on security controls protecting personal data
- Review information security policies for data protection compliance
- Advise on privacy-by-design and privacy-by-default requirements
- Participate in incident response for data breaches

**Awareness and Training**:
- Provide data protection training to personnel
- Raise awareness of data protection obligations
- Advise on privacy implications of new projects/systems

**Authority**:
- Independence in performing DPO duties (regulatory requirement)
- Direct access to Executive Management
- Authority to escalate data protection violations
- Veto authority on processing activities with high privacy risk (pending management review)

**Qualifications**:
- Expert knowledge of data protection law (GDPR, nDSG)
- Understanding of information security practices
- Ability to fulfill tasks independently

**Interfaces**:
- Reports to: Executive Management (independently)
- Coordinates with: CISO (security-privacy integration), Legal (regulatory interpretation), HR (employee data), IT (system design)
- External: Supervisory authorities (FDPIC in Switzerland, DPAs in EU)

**Note**: For small organizations, DPO role may be external (contracted service) or combined with Compliance Officer role (if no conflicts of interest).

#### 3.2.4 Information Security Team

**Accountability**: Operational implementation and maintenance of information security controls.

**Team Structure** (scales with organizational size):
- **Security Manager/Lead**: Team leadership, project coordination, CISO support
- **Security Architects**: Security design, architecture review, technology selection
- **Security Engineers**: Control implementation, tool deployment, integration
- **Security Analysts**: Monitoring, incident response, threat intelligence, investigations
- **Compliance Specialists**: Regulatory mapping, audit support, assessment coordination
- **Security Awareness Coordinators**: Training development, awareness campaigns, cultural initiatives

**Responsibilities**:

**Security Operations**:
- Monitor security events and alerts (SIEM, IDS/IPS, EDR)
- Respond to security incidents per incident response plan
- Conduct security assessments (vulnerability scans, penetration tests, control assessments)
- Perform threat intelligence analysis and sharing
- Manage security tools (SIEM, vulnerability scanners, EDR, firewalls, etc.)

**Control Implementation**:
- Deploy and configure security controls per policies and standards
- Maintain security baselines and hardening guides
- Implement security architecture designs
- Integrate security into systems and applications
- Automate security controls where feasible

**Risk Management**:
- Conduct risk assessments for new systems/projects
- Track vulnerabilities and coordinate remediation
- Maintain risk register
- Report risk metrics to CISO

**Compliance and Audit**:
- Support internal and external audits
- Conduct control effectiveness assessments (ISMS-IMP-A.X.XX assessments)
- Maintain evidence registers and audit trails
- Prepare compliance reports

**Awareness and Training**:
- Develop security awareness training content
- Deliver training sessions
- Coordinate phishing simulation campaigns
- Measure training effectiveness

**Authority**:
- Implement security controls within approved policies
- Investigate security incidents
- Request information from system owners for investigations
- Recommend security improvements to CISO
- Escalate security risks to CISO

**Qualifications** (role-dependent):
- Security certifications (CISSP, CEH, GCIH, Security+, etc.)
- Technical skills relevant to role (networking, systems, applications, cloud)
- Understanding of security frameworks (ISO 27001, NIST, etc.)

#### 3.2.5 System Owners

**Definition**: Individual accountable for a specific system, application, or infrastructure component.

**Accountability**: Accountable for security of assigned system throughout its lifecycle.

**Responsibilities**:

**System Security**:
- Implement security controls required by policies for assigned system
- Maintain system in compliance with security baselines
- Ensure timely patching and vulnerability remediation
- Conduct system access reviews (quarterly or as required)
- Document system security configuration

**Risk Management**:
- Identify and assess risks related to assigned system
- Propose risk treatment measures to CISO
- Accept residual risks within delegated authority
- Report security incidents affecting assigned system

**Change Management**:
- Ensure security considerations in system changes
- Request security review for significant changes
- Maintain change documentation

**Compliance**:
- Complete system-specific security assessments (ISMS-IMP assessments)

#### 3.2.6 Data Owners

**Compliance**:
- Ensure data processing complies with regulatory requirements

- Recommend disciplinary action for security violations

## 4. Screening Requirements (Control A.6.1)

### 4.1 Screening Principles and Legal Framework

[Organization] SHALL conduct background verification checks on candidates proportional to business requirements, information sensitivity, and perceived risks, in compliance with applicable laws and regulations.

**Screening Principles**:

**Proportionality**: Screening intensity proportional to:
- Sensitivity of information accessed (per data classification)
- Criticality of role (impact of role compromise on organization)
- Level of system access and privileges
- Regulatory requirements (financial sector, government contracts may mandate specific checks)

**Legality**: All screening activities comply with:
- **Swiss Code of Obligations (OR) Art. 328**: Employer duty of care and data protection in employment
- **Swiss Federal Data Protection Act (nDSG/FADP)**: Processing of personal data in employment context
- **EU GDPR Art. 88**: Processing in employment context (where applicable to EU personnel)
- Local employment law in each jurisdiction where [Organization] operates

**Fairness**: Screening conducted in fair, transparent, and non-discriminatory manner:
- Candidates informed of screening activities before conducting
- Candidate consent obtained where required by law
- Screening criteria job-relevant and non-discriminatory
- Adverse screening findings discussed with candidate (opportunity to clarify or dispute)

**Confidentiality**: Screening results handled confidentially:
- Accessed only by HR, hiring manager, and authorized personnel (need-to-know)
- Stored securely in HR system with access controls
- Retained per legal requirements, securely disposed when no longer needed
- Not shared externally without candidate consent (except where legally required)

**Legal Compliance Framework**:

**Swiss Law**:
- **OR Art. 328**: Employer may collect personal data only if related to employee's aptitude for employment relationship or necessary for execution of employment contract
- **OR Art. 328b**: Employer may not process data on personality using fully automated processes
- **nDSG Art. 6**: Data processing must be lawful, proportionate, and for specified purpose
- **nDSG Art. 25**: Data security measures must be appropriate to risk

**Key Legal Constraints**:
- Screening checks must be job-relevant (cannot conduct checks "just because")
- Criminal record checks: Only for roles where relevant (e.g., financial crimes check for finance roles, not for general office roles)
- Credit checks: Only where financial responsibility is part of role
- Social media screening: Limited to publicly available professional information (no "friending" candidates to access private data)
- Health information: Only where health impacts ability to perform essential job functions (disability law compliance required)

**Candidate Rights**:
- Right to be informed of screening activities
- Right to access screening results (upon request)
- Right to correct inaccurate data
- Right to withdraw application (no discrimination for withdrawal after disclosure of screening)

### 4.2 Screening Levels and Role Classification

[Organization] SHALL classify roles by sensitivity and apply appropriate screening level:

**Screening Level 1: Basic Verification** (All Personnel)

**Applicable To**:
- All employees, contractors, interns, temporary workers
- Minimum baseline for any personnel with organizational access

**Verification Activities**:
- **Identity Verification**: Government-issued ID (passport, national ID card, driver's license)
- **Right to Work**: Work permit verification (if applicable)
- **Employment History**: Verify current/most recent employer (reference check)
- **Educational Qualifications**: Verify highest degree/certification claimed (if relevant to role)

**Verification Methods**:
- ID documents inspected and copied (with candidate consent)
- Reference check via phone or email to previous employer HR or manager
- Educational verification via institution or credential verification service
- Online verification for professional certifications

**Screening Level 2: Standard Screening** (Standard Roles)

**Applicable To**:
- Roles with access to Internal data (per data classification)
- Roles with system access (applications, infrastructure)
- Roles with customer interaction
- Most organizational roles fall into this category

**Verification Activities**:
- All Level 1 checks PLUS:
- **Extended Employment History**: Verify last 2-3 employers (5 years history)
- **Professional References**: 2-3 professional references contacted
- **Employment Gaps**: Explanation of gaps >6 months in employment history
- **Professional Licensing**: Verify professional licenses if required for role (legal, accounting, etc.)

**Verification Methods**:
- Reference check questionnaire to references
- Documented explanation of employment gaps from candidate
- Professional license verification via licensing authority or online registry

**Screening Level 3: Enhanced Screening** (Sensitive Roles)

**Applicable To**:
- Roles with access to Confidential or Restricted data
- Roles with privileged system access (administrators, DBAs)
- Information Security Team roles
- Finance roles with financial transaction authority
- Executive Management
- Roles with regulatory requirements (financial services, etc.)

**Verification Activities**:
- All Level 1-2 checks PLUS:
- **Criminal Record Check**: Per applicable law and role relevance
  - Financial roles: Financial crimes check
  - Privileged access roles: Fraud, theft, computer crimes check
  - Scope: Jurisdiction-dependent (Swiss, EU, home country of candidate)
- **Credit Check** (if financial responsibility): For roles with financial authority >CHF 10,000 or access to financial systems
- **Extended Reference Checks**: Minimum 3 professional references, more in-depth questioning
- **Adverse Media Screening**: Public records search for adverse media mentions
- **Social Media Screening** (professional only): Review of professional social media profiles (LinkedIn)

**Legal Compliance Notes**:
- **Criminal Record Check**: Requires candidate consent, scope limited to role-relevant offenses, not all criminal history
- **Credit Check**: Only for roles with financial responsibility, not all "financial sector" roles
- **Adverse Media**: Limited to publicly available information, not private data

**Screening Level 4: Security Clearance** (Critical Roles)

**Applicable To**:
- CISO
- Roles with access to most sensitive organizational secrets (trade secrets, M&A plans, executive communications)
- Roles subject to government security clearance requirements (if applicable to organization)
- Roles with authority to bypass security controls

**Verification Activities**:
- All Level 1-3 checks PLUS:
- **Extended Background Investigation**: 7-10 years employment and residence history
- **Character References**: Personal character references (non-professional)
- **Financial Investigation**: Comprehensive financial background (if role involves high value assets or sensitive financial data)
- **Foreign Ties Assessment** (if applicable): Assessment of foreign contacts/interests if relevant to organizational security
- **Ongoing Monitoring** (if applicable): Periodic re-screening (every 2-3 years)

**Note**: Level 4 screening is rare in commercial organizations. Primarily applicable to defense contractors, government agencies, financial institutions with specific regulatory requirements. Most organizations will not need Level 4.

**Role Classification Matrix** (Example):

| Role Category | Data Access | System Access | Screening Level |
|---------------|-------------|---------------|-----------------|
| General Office Worker | Internal | Standard applications | Level 2 |
| Customer Service | Internal, limited Confidential | CRM, ticketing system | Level 2 |
| Software Developer | Internal, some Confidential (code) | Development systems, code repositories | Level 3 |
| System Administrator | Internal, Confidential, Restricted | Privileged access (servers, networks, cloud) | Level 3 |
| Database Administrator | Confidential, Restricted (database data) | Database admin access | Level 3 |
| Security Team | Confidential, Restricted (security tools, logs) | Privileged security access | Level 3 |
| Finance Team | Confidential (financial data) | Financial systems | Level 3 |
| Executive Management | Confidential, Restricted (strategic info) | Executive systems | Level 3 |
| CISO | All data classifications | All systems (security oversight) | Level 4 |

**Implementation**: Complete role classification and screening level mapping documented in ISMS-IMP-A.5.1-2-6.1-2.S3 (Screening and Vetting Assessment).

### 4.3 Screening Procedures and Timing

[Organization] SHALL conduct screening according to defined procedures and timelines:

**Screening Timeline**:

**Pre-Employment Screening**: Completed BEFORE granting organizational access

**Process Flow**:
1. **Job Offer Conditional on Screening**: Offer contingent on successful background check
2. **Candidate Consent**: Candidate signs consent form authorizing screening
3. **Screening Initiated**: HR or third-party screening provider initiates checks
4. **Screening Completion**: Results reviewed by HR
5. **Adverse Findings**: Discussed with candidate (opportunity to clarify or dispute)
6. **Hiring Decision**: Final hiring decision made considering screening results
7. **Access Provisioning**: Access granted only after successful screening completion

**Timeline**: 1-4 weeks depending on screening level and jurisdiction:
- Level 1-2: 1-2 weeks typical
- Level 3: 2-4 weeks (criminal record checks take time)
- Level 4: 4-8 weeks (extensive investigation)

**Screening Before Access**: No organizational access (systems, facilities, confidential data) granted until screening completed successfully.

**Interim Access** (Emergency Exception Only):
- Situation: Critical role needs immediate filling, screening not complete
- Conditions: Manager approval, CISO approval, compensating controls (close supervision, limited access, no sensitive data access)
- Duration: Maximum 30 days, extended only with Executive Management approval
- Documentation: Exception documented with business justification

**Ongoing Screening** (for specific roles):

**Periodic Re-Screening**:
- **Level 4 Roles**: Re-screening every 2-3 years (ongoing employment)
- **Privileged Access Roles**: Re-screening every 5 years or upon significant role change
- **All Roles**: Verification of continued right to work (work permit renewals)

**Trigger-Based Screening**:
- Role change to higher sensitivity: Enhanced screening if moving from Level 2→Level 3
- Security incident involving personnel: Investigation may include screening review
- Legal requirement: Regulatory change mandating new screening requirements

**Continuous Monitoring** (where appropriate and legal):
- **Adverse Media Alerts**: Automated alerts for adverse media mentions (for Level 3-4 roles)
- **Criminal Conviction Reporting**: Some jurisdictions allow ongoing criminal record monitoring
- **Financial Monitoring**: Credit monitoring for roles with financial authority (with consent)

### 4.4 Verification Methods and Evidence

[Organization] SHALL use reliable verification methods and maintain evidence of screening:

**Verification Methods**:

**Identity Verification**:
- **Method**: Inspect original government-issued ID, photocopy with candidate consent
- **Evidence**: Photocopy of ID (passport, national ID, driver's license) in HR file
- **Validation**: Check ID authenticity (security features, expiration date, match to candidate)

**Right to Work**:
- **Method**: Inspect work permit, visa, or citizenship documentation
- **Evidence**: Copy of work permit or equivalent in HR file
- **Validation**: Verify work permit validity and expiration date, confirm employer eligibility

**Employment History**:
- **Method**: Contact previous employer HR or manager
- **Evidence**: Reference check form completed by referee, or email/letter from referee
- **Validation**: Confirm employment dates, job title, reason for leaving, eligibility for rehire
- **Alternative**: Employment verification via third-party service (e.g., The Work Number)

**Educational Qualifications**:
- **Method**: Request transcript or diploma from candidate, verify with institution
- **Evidence**: Copy of diploma/transcript, verification letter from institution
- **Validation**: Confirm degree awarded, graduation date, major (if relevant)
- **Alternative**: Use credential verification service (National Student Clearinghouse, etc.)

**Professional References**:
- **Method**: Contact references provided by candidate (phone or email)
- **Evidence**: Reference check questionnaire completed
- **Questions**: Confirm working relationship, assess candidate's skills/performance, would referee rehire candidate?
- **Validation**: Confirm referee legitimacy (verify phone number/email via company directory if possible)

**Criminal Record Check**:
- **Method**: Request via government authority or third-party screening provider
- **Evidence**: Criminal record report (sanitized to remove non-relevant information)
- **Validation**: Verify authenticity of report, review for role-relevant offenses
- **Legal Note**: Requires candidate consent, scope limited to role-relevant offenses

**Credit Check**:
- **Method**: Request via credit bureau or third-party screening provider
- **Evidence**: Credit report (sanitized to show only summary, not full financial details)
- **Validation**: Review for financial distress indicators (bankruptcies, judgments, excessive debt)
- **Legal Note**: Requires candidate consent, only for roles with financial responsibility

**Professional License Verification**:
- **Method**: Verify via licensing authority (bar association, medical board, CPA board, etc.)
- **Evidence**: Screenshot or verification letter from licensing authority
- **Validation**: Confirm license active, not suspended or revoked

**Evidence Retention**:
- **Duration**: Screening evidence retained per local employment law requirements
  - Switzerland: Typically employment period + 1 year
  - EU: GDPR Art. 17 (right to erasure) applies after legal basis for processing ends
  - United States: EEOC requires 1 year retention of employment records
- **Storage**: HR system with access controls (HR personnel, hiring managers, authorized auditors only)
- **Disposal**: Secure disposal (shredding or secure electronic deletion) when retention period expires

**Screening Provider Management**:
- Third-party screening providers (if used) SHALL:
  - Have documented data processing agreements (GDPR DPA if processing EU data)
  - Comply with local employment screening laws
  - Provide timely and accurate reports
  - Maintain appropriate data security controls
  - Be vetted per ISMS-REF-A.5.23 (third-party assessment)

### 4.5 Handling Adverse Screening Findings

[Organization] SHALL implement a fair and consistent process for handling adverse screening findings:

**Adverse Finding Categories**:

**Category 1: Discrepancies**
- **Definition**: Candidate-provided information does not match verification
- **Examples**: Incorrect employment dates, degree not earned, false job title
- **Action**: Discuss with candidate (opportunity to clarify or correct)
- **Outcome**: Minor discrepancies (off by few months) may be acceptable; significant falsification (degree never earned) typically disqualifying

**Category 2: Undisclosed Information**
- **Definition**: Relevant information not disclosed by candidate
- **Examples**: Employment gap not explained, termination for cause not disclosed, undisclosed criminal conviction
- **Action**: Discuss with candidate (why not disclosed, nature of information)
- **Outcome**: Case-by-case assessment; failure to disclose may be as concerning as underlying issue

**Category 3: Criminal Record**
- **Definition**: Criminal conviction disclosed or discovered
- **Action**: 
  1. Verify conviction is role-relevant (financial crime for finance role, not parking tickets)
  2. Assess nature, timing, and severity of offense
  3. Consider rehabilitation (how long ago, evidence of reform)
  4. Legal advice if unclear (discrimination risk)
- **Outcome**: Role-relevant recent convictions typically disqualifying; old minor offenses may be acceptable

**Category 4: Poor References**
- **Definition**: References provide negative feedback
- **Action**: Discuss with candidate (context, candidate's perspective)
- **Outcome**: Pattern of poor references concerning; single negative reference may not be disqualifying

**Category 5: Financial Issues**
- **Definition**: Credit check reveals financial distress (bankruptcy, judgments, severe delinquency)
- **Action**: Discuss with candidate (circumstances, ongoing or resolved)
- **Outcome**: For roles with financial authority, significant unresolved financial distress may be disqualifying (risk of fraud/theft)

**Decision Process**:

1. **HR Review**: HR reviews screening results, identifies adverse findings
2. **Candidate Discussion**: Candidate provided opportunity to discuss findings (fairness, accuracy)
3. **Hiring Manager Input**: Hiring manager provides perspective on candidate's qualifications vs. findings
4. **Legal/Compliance Review** (if needed): Legal reviews for compliance with employment law, discrimination risk
5. **Decision**: Hiring decision made by HR and hiring manager (or HR alone for clear disqualifiers)
6. **Documentation**: Decision rationale documented (especially for rejections)
7. **Notification**: Candidate notified of decision

**Decision Criteria**:
- **Relevance**: Is finding relevant to job responsibilities and risks?
- **Recency**: How long ago did issue occur? Evidence of rehabilitation?
- **Severity**: How serious is the issue?
- **Pattern**: Isolated incident or pattern of behavior?
- **Impact**: Could issue impact organizational security or operations?

**Non-Discrimination**:
- Screening decisions SHALL NOT be based on protected characteristics (race, gender, age, religion, national origin, disability, etc.)
- Decisions SHALL be job-related and consistent with business necessity
- Legal review if decision could be perceived as discriminatory

**Adverse Action Process** (for rejections based on screening):

1. **Pre-Adverse Action Notice**: Candidate provided copy of screening report and opportunity to dispute
2. **Waiting Period**: Reasonable time (5-10 business days) for candidate to respond
3. **Final Decision**: After considering candidate response, final decision made
4. **Adverse Action Notice**: If rejected, candidate notified in writing
   - Statement that rejection based on screening report
   - Name and contact of screening provider (if used)
   - Candidate's rights (right to dispute report with provider)

**Legal Compliance**: Adverse action process complies with Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) if US personnel, equivalent laws in other jurisdictions.

### 4.6 Exceptions and Special Cases

[Organization] SHALL manage screening exceptions and special cases:

**Exception Scenarios**:

**Scenario 1: Senior Executive Hire**
- **Issue**: Executive candidate unwilling to provide references from current employer (confidentiality of job search)
- **Resolution**: References from previous employers acceptable; references provided after hire (with current employer consent)

**Scenario 2: Urgent Hire**
- **Issue**: Critical role needs immediate filling, screening not complete in time
- **Resolution**: Interim access with compensating controls (see Section 4.3), full screening completed within 30 days

**Scenario 3: International Candidate**
- **Issue**: Criminal record check not available or takes months in candidate's home country
- **Resolution**: Use of international screening services, sworn statement from candidate, risk acceptance if checks not feasible

**Scenario 4: Contractor from Vendor**
- **Issue**: Vendor providing contractor, vendor conducted screening
- **Resolution**: Vendor provides screening attestation (vendor-conducted checks meet organizational requirements), organization spot-checks sample, contract holds vendor liable

**Scenario 5: Volunteer or Unpaid Intern**
- **Issue**: Organization uses volunteers/unpaid interns, extensive screening may not be feasible
- **Resolution**: Scaled-down screening appropriate to access level (if only Public data access, Level 1 screening may suffice)

**Exception Approval**:
- Screening exceptions approved by: CHRO (HR exceptions), CISO (security risk exceptions)
- Exception documented with: business justification, risk assessment, compensating controls, exception duration
- Exception reviewed periodically (every 6 months or at hire date if pre-employment exception)

**Third-Party Personnel**:

Screening requirements for third-party personnel (contractors, consultants, vendor employees) depend on engagement model:

**Individual Contractor** (directly engaged by organization):
- Subject to same screening as employees (per screening level based on role)
- Organization conducts screening directly

**Vendor-Provided Personnel** (contractor provided by vendor firm):
- Vendor attests that screening conducted meeting organizational requirements
- Contract specifies minimum screening requirements
- Organization spot-checks sample (request screening evidence for 10-20% of vendor personnel)
- Vendor contractually liable for screening failures

**Temporary Workers** (via staffing agency):
- Staffing agency conducts screening per agreement
- Organization specifies minimum screening level in contract
- Short-term engagements (<90 days) with limited access may use agency screening only
- Long-term engagements or sensitive access require organization-level verification

**Implementation**: Exception procedures and third-party screening management documented in ISMS-IMP-A.5.1-2-6.1-2.S3.

## 5. Employment Contract Requirements (Control A.6.2)

### 5.1 Contractual Security Obligations Framework

[Organization] SHALL include information security responsibilities and obligations in employment contracts and agreements:

**Contract Types Requiring Security Clauses**:
- **Employment Contracts**: Full-time, part-time, temporary employees
- **Contractor Agreements**: Independent contractors, consultants
- **Vendor Agreements**: Vendors providing personnel on-site or remote access
- **Internship Agreements**: Interns, apprentices, trainees
- **Volunteer Agreements**: If organization uses volunteers with system/data access
- **Board Member Agreements**: Non-executive directors with system/data access

**Legal Framework**:
- **Swiss Code of Obligations (OR) Art. 321a**: Employee duty of loyalty and care
- **Swiss Federal Data Protection Act (nDSG)**: Data protection obligations in employment
- **EU GDPR Art. 88**: Processing in employment context (where applicable)
- **Local employment law**: Compliance with employment law in each jurisdiction

**Integration with HR**:
- Security clauses integrated into organizational employment contract template (not separate document)
- Legal review of security clauses (employment law compliance, enforceability)
- DPO review of data protection clauses (GDPR/nDSG compliance)
- Standard clauses for all personnel (customization for role-specific requirements)

### 5.2 Mandatory Contract Clauses

[Organization]'s employment contracts SHALL include the following information security clauses:

#### 5.2.1 Confidentiality and Data Protection Obligations

**Clause Purpose**: Bind employee to protect organizational confidential information and personal data.

**Required Content**:

**Confidentiality Obligation**:
- Employee SHALL maintain confidentiality of organizational information classified as Confidential or Restricted per data classification policy
- Obligation applies during employment and continues after termination (perpetual for trade secrets, defined period for other confidential information)
- Employee SHALL NOT disclose confidential information to unauthorized parties (internal or external)
- Employee SHALL protect confidential information from unauthorized access, loss, or damage

**Scope of Confidential Information**:
- Trade secrets, proprietary information, business strategies
- Customer data, partner information, vendor contracts
- Financial information (non-public), M&A activities
- Technical information (source code, system designs, security architectures)
- Personnel information of other employees

**Authorized Disclosure Exceptions**:
- Disclosure authorized by management in writing
- Disclosure required by law or regulation (with notice to organization if legally permissible)
- Disclosure in course of employment duties (need-to-know basis)
- Disclosure to professional advisors (lawyers, accountants) under confidentiality

**Data Protection Compliance**:
- Employee SHALL process personal data only for authorized purposes per data protection policy
- Employee SHALL comply with GDPR, nDSG, and organizational data protection policies
- Employee SHALL report data breaches immediately per incident response policy
- Employee acknowledges personal data processed in employment context (employee data, monitoring data) per organizational privacy notice

**Penalties for Breach**:
- Breach of confidentiality is serious violation subject to disciplinary action including termination
- Organization reserves right to seek legal remedies for breach (injunction, damages, prosecution)
- Employee may be personally liable for damages caused by confidentiality breach

**Example Clause** (See Annex D for complete template):

> "The Employee acknowledges that during the course of employment, they will have access to and become familiar with confidential information belonging to the Organization. The Employee agrees to maintain the confidentiality of such information during and after employment. The Employee shall not disclose confidential information to any person or entity without prior written authorization from the Organization, except as required in the performance of their duties. This obligation shall survive termination of employment."

#### 5.2.2 Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)

**NDA Purpose**: Formal agreement reinforcing confidentiality obligations (may be separate document or integrated into employment contract).

**Required Content**:

**Parties**: Organization (Disclosing Party) and Employee (Receiving Party)

**Definition of Confidential Information**: Comprehensive definition covering all organizational information not publicly available

**Permitted Use**: Employee may use confidential information solely for performing employment duties

**Prohibited Disclosure**: Employee SHALL NOT disclose to third parties (with exceptions for authorized disclosures)

**Return of Information**: Upon termination, employee SHALL return all confidential information (documents, data, devices)

**Duration**: NDA typically survives termination (perpetual for trade secrets, 2-5 years for other confidential information)

**Injunctive Relief**: Organization entitled to seek injunctive relief (immediate court order to stop breach) without proving damages

**Governing Law and Jurisdiction**: Swiss law governs (or local law if international employee)

**NDA Timing**:
- NDA signed BEFORE granting access to confidential information
- Typically signed with employment contract (or on first day of employment at latest)
- Separate NDA may be required for access to especially sensitive projects (M&A, new products)

**Implementation**: See Annex D for example NDA template.

#### 5.2.3 Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) Acknowledgment

**AUP Purpose**: Define acceptable and unacceptable use of organizational IT systems, data, and networks.

**Required Content**:

**Acceptable Use**:
- Systems provided for business purposes (incidental personal use acceptable within limits)
- Access limited to authorized personnel for authorized purposes
- User accounts personal and non-transferable (no sharing passwords)
- Users responsible for all activity under their accounts

**Prohibited Use**:
- Unauthorized access to systems or data (including "curiosity browsing")
- Installing unauthorized software, bypassing security controls
- Using organizational systems for illegal activities, harassment, discrimination
- Excessive personal use interfering with work duties
- Downloading or distributing copyrighted material, malware, inappropriate content
- Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information

**Monitoring and Privacy**:
- Organization reserves right to monitor system usage (email, web, file access, application use)
- Monitoring conducted in compliance with Swiss employment law (OR Art. 328b) and data protection law
- No expectation of privacy for business use of organizational systems
- Personal use subject to monitoring (users should minimize personal use)

**Security Responsibilities**:
- Users SHALL protect passwords and access credentials
- Users SHALL report security incidents immediately
- Users SHALL comply with security policies
- Users SHALL complete mandatory security training

**Consequences of Violation**:
- AUP violations subject to disciplinary action (warning, suspension, termination depending on severity)
- Serious violations may result in immediate termination and legal action
- Illegal activities reported to law enforcement

**AUP Acknowledgment**:
- Employee acknowledges receiving, reading, and understanding AUP
- Employee agrees to comply with AUP
- Acknowledgment signed with employment contract or separately on first day
- Annual re-acknowledgment required

**Implementation**: Complete AUP maintained as separate policy document (ISMS-POL-A.X.XX or organizational IT policy), acknowledgment form in HR records.

#### 5.2.4 Security Awareness and Training Obligation

**Clause Purpose**: Bind employee to complete mandatory security awareness training.

**Required Content**:

**Training Requirements**:
- Employee SHALL complete mandatory security awareness training within first 30 days of employment
- Employee SHALL complete annual refresher training (or as required by organization)
- Role-specific security training required for roles with specific security responsibilities

**Training Topics** (Typical):
- Information security policy overview
- Data classification and handling
- Password security and authentication
- Phishing and social engineering awareness
- Incident reporting
- Acceptable use policy
- Data protection (GDPR/nDSG basics)
- Physical security

**Training Delivery**:
- Online training modules (e-learning via LMS)
- In-person training sessions (for role-specific training)
- Training materials accessible in organizational language(s)

**Training Completion**:
- Training completion tracked in HR/LMS system
- Training completion required before system access granted (new hires)
- Failure to complete training within deadline results in access suspension until training completed

**Performance Linkage**:
- Training completion considered in performance reviews
- Non-completion may affect performance rating or compensation

**Example Clause**:

> "The Employee agrees to participate in and complete all mandatory information security awareness training programs as required by the Organization. Training must be completed within prescribed timeframes. Failure to complete required training may result in suspension of system access and/or disciplinary action."

#### 5.2.5 Incident Reporting Obligation

**Clause Purpose**: Bind employee to report security incidents promptly.

**Required Content**:

**Reporting Requirement**:
- Employee SHALL report suspected or actual security incidents immediately (within 1 hour for critical incidents, 24 hours for other incidents)
- Security incidents include: malware infections, phishing emails, lost/stolen devices, data breaches, unauthorized access, policy violations

**Reporting Channels**:
- Security incident hotline (phone, email)
- Direct report to manager or Security Team
- Anonymous reporting option (whistleblower mechanism) for serious violations

**Information to Report**:
- Description of incident (what happened)
- When incident occurred or was discovered
- Systems/data affected
- Any actions taken by employee
- Contact information for follow-up

**Investigation Cooperation**:
- Employee SHALL cooperate with incident investigations
- Employee SHALL provide truthful information to investigators
- Employee SHALL NOT tamper with evidence or obstruct investigations

**No Retaliation**:
- Organization prohibits retaliation against employees reporting security incidents in good faith
- Protected reporting includes reporting of policy violations by management or colleagues

**Example Clause**:

> "The Employee shall immediately report any suspected or actual information security incidents, including but not limited to data breaches, malware infections, lost or stolen devices, or policy violations, to the Security Team or their manager. The Employee agrees to cooperate fully with any incident investigations. The Organization prohibits retaliation against employees who report security incidents in good faith."

#### 5.2.6 Access Rights and Termination Obligations

**Clause Purpose**: Clarify that access rights are granted at organization's discretion and must be returned upon termination.

**Required Content**:

**Access Rights**:
- Access to organizational systems and data granted based on business need (least privilege principle)
- Access rights may be modified or revoked at any time per business requirements or policy
- Unauthorized access (accessing data outside scope of role) prohibited

**Intellectual Property**:
- Work product created during employment belongs to organization (work-made-for-hire)
- Employee assigns all intellectual property rights to organization
- Employee SHALL NOT use organizational IP for personal projects or outside employment

**Asset Return**:
- Upon termination, employee SHALL return all organizational assets:
  - Computing devices (laptops, phones, tablets)
  - Physical access badges and keys
  - Documents (physical and electronic)
  - Any other organizational property
- Asset return required before final paycheck released (where legally permissible)

**Access Termination**:
- Upon termination, all system access revoked immediately (immediate for termination for cause, by end of termination date for voluntary resignation)
- Employee SHALL NOT attempt to access organizational systems after termination
- Attempting unauthorized access after termination may result in legal action (computer fraud laws)

**Post-Termination Obligations**:
- Confidentiality obligations continue after termination (see Section 5.2.1)
- NDA obligations continue per NDA terms (perpetual for trade secrets, 2-5 years for other confidential information)
- Employee SHALL NOT solicit organizational customers or employees (if non-compete/non-solicit clause in contract)

**Example Clause**:

> "The Employee acknowledges that all access rights to the Organization's systems and data are granted at the Organization's discretion and may be revoked at any time. Upon termination of employment, the Employee shall immediately return all organizational assets including devices, access badges, documents, and any other property. The Employee's access rights will be terminated effective the date of termination. The Employee shall not attempt to access the Organization's systems after termination. Confidentiality and data protection obligations shall survive termination as specified in this Agreement."

#### 5.2.7 Disciplinary Action and Termination

**Clause Purpose**: Clarify that security policy violations are serious and may result in termination.

**Required Content**:

**Policy Compliance Requirement**:
- Employee SHALL comply with all organizational information security policies
- Policies provided to employee and accessible via organizational intranet/policy portal
- Employee responsible for reading and understanding policies

**Disciplinary Framework**:
- Minor violations: Coaching, written warning, mandatory retraining
- Serious violations: Suspension, termination (with notice where required by law)
- Severe violations: Immediate termination for cause (no notice period)

**Examples of Serious Violations**:
- Intentional unauthorized access to confidential data
- Malicious actions (deleting data, sabotage, introducing malware)
- Sharing access credentials with others
- Theft of organizational information or assets
- Working for competitor while employed (conflict of interest, IP theft risk)

**Fair Process**:
- Employee provided opportunity to explain before disciplinary action (except immediate threats)
- Investigation conducted before termination (where feasible)
- Progressive discipline for non-severe violations (warnings before termination)

**Legal Compliance**:
- Disciplinary actions comply with Swiss employment law (notice periods, severance requirements)
- Termination for cause requires serious breach (Swiss law makes termination difficult)

**Example Clause**:

> "The Employee acknowledges that compliance with the Organization's information security policies is a condition of continued employment. Violations of security policies may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment. The Organization will follow applicable employment law and provide fair process in any disciplinary proceedings. Serious or repeated violations may result in immediate termination for cause."

### 5.3 Additional Clauses for Specific Roles

[Organization] SHALL include additional security clauses for roles with elevated responsibilities:

#### 5.3.1 Privileged Access Roles (Administrators, DBAs, Security Team)

**Additional Clauses**:

**Enhanced Background Checks**: Employee consents to enhanced background screening (Level 3+)

**Ongoing Monitoring**: Employee consents to ongoing monitoring of privileged access usage (where legally permissible)

**Conflict of Interest**: Employee SHALL NOT have outside employment involving IT/security for competitor or client without disclosure and approval

**Separation of Duties**: Employee acknowledges separation of duties requirements (e.g., no developer with production admin access)

**Elevated Responsibility**: Employee acknowledges heightened responsibility to protect organizational assets given privileged access

#### 5.3.2 Roles with Access to Restricted Data

**Additional Clauses**:

**Special Handling Requirements**: Employee SHALL handle Restricted data per enhanced procedures (encryption, access logging, etc.)

**Data Breach Notification**: Employee acknowledges data breach involving Restricted data requires immediate escalation (within 1 hour)

**Legal/Regulatory Awareness**: Employee acknowledges Restricted data may be subject to regulatory requirements (GDPR, financial regulations, etc.)

#### 5.3.3 Executive Management

**Additional Clauses**:

**Leadership Commitment**: Executive acknowledges responsibility to model security-conscious behavior and support security program

**Strategic Oversight**: Executive acknowledges responsibility for ISMS governance and oversight

**Resource Allocation**: Executive acknowledges responsibility to allocate adequate resources for security program

### 5.4 Contract Execution and Acknowledgment

[Organization] SHALL ensure security clauses are properly executed and acknowledged:

**Execution Timing**:

**Before Access Granted**:
- Employment contract (including security clauses) signed BEFORE first day of work
- NDA signed BEFORE access to confidential information
- AUP acknowledged BEFORE system access granted

**Onboarding Process Integration**:
1. Candidate receives offer letter (conditional on background check and contract signing)
2. Background check completed (per Section 4)
3. Employment contract sent to candidate for review (1-2 weeks before start date)
4. Candidate signs contract (physical or electronic signature) before start date
5. First day: NDA signed (if separate from contract), AUP acknowledged, security training assigned
6. System access granted after NDA/AUP acknowledgment and training completion

**Signature Methods**:
- Physical signature (ink on paper)
- Electronic signature (DocuSign, Adobe Sign, etc.) - legally valid in Switzerland and EU per eIDAS regulation
- Checkboxes in HR system (for policy acknowledgments)

**Record Retention**:
- Signed contracts maintained in HR system (employee file)
- Acknowledgment records (NDA, AUP, training) maintained in HR system or LMS
- Records retained per legal requirements (employment period + retention period per local law)
- Access restricted to HR, Legal, authorized personnel

**Annual Acknowledgment**:
- Critical policies (AUP, Confidentiality) re-acknowledged annually
- Annual acknowledgment confirms employee has reviewed updated policies
- Annual training completion includes policy acknowledgment

### 5.5 Enforcement and Compliance

[Organization] SHALL monitor compliance with contractual security obligations:

**Compliance Monitoring**:

**Technical Controls**:
- Acceptable use monitored via security tools (DLP, web filtering, SIEM)
- Policy violations detected automatically (e.g., unauthorized access attempts logged)
- Anomalous behavior flagged for investigation (UEBA - User and Entity Behavior Analytics)

**HR Processes**:
- Training completion tracked in HR/LMS system
- Access reviews verify access aligned with role (quarterly)
- Performance reviews include security compliance discussion
- Exit interviews verify asset return and remind of post-termination obligations

**Audit and Investigation**:
- Internal audits verify contract clause compliance
- Security investigations triggered by incidents or violations
- External audits (ISO 27001, regulatory) review personnel security controls

**Enforcement Actions**:

**Violation Response** (See also Section 2.7):

**Step 1: Investigation**
- Violation identified (technical alert, audit finding, report)
- Facts gathered (what happened, who involved, impact)
- Interview employee (opportunity to explain)

**Step 2: Determination**
- Severity assessed (minor, serious, severe)
- Intentional vs. unintentional
- Pattern of behavior or isolated incident

**Step 3: Action**
- Minor unintentional: Coaching, retraining, written warning
- Serious or repeated: Performance improvement plan, suspension, termination consideration
- Severe intentional: Immediate termination for cause, legal action if appropriate

**Step 4: Documentation**
- Violation and response documented in HR system
- Employee file updated
- Lessons learned (policy updates, training improvements)

**Legal Action** (for serious breaches):
- Civil action: Seek damages for breach of contract, injunction to prevent further breaches
- Criminal action: Report to law enforcement for illegal activities (data theft, sabotage, computer fraud)
- Regulatory reporting: Notify regulatory authorities if required (data breaches, financial sector violations)

**Consistency and Fairness**:
- Enforcement actions consistent across personnel (no favoritism)
- Documented precedents guide decision-making
- Legal review for termination decisions (employment law compliance)

## 6. Governance and Compliance

### 6.1 Integration with HR Processes

[Organization] SHALL integrate information security requirements throughout the employee lifecycle:

**Joiner Process** (Pre-Employment → Onboarding):

```
Recruitment → Screening (Section 4) → Contract Signing (Section 5) → Onboarding
     ↓              ↓                         ↓                          ↓
Job posting    Background check         NDA signed              Security training
includes       - Identity verify         - Confidentiality      Policy acknowledgment
security reqs  - Employment history      AUP acknowledged       System access granted
               - Criminal record (if req)
```

**Integration Points**:
- **Recruitment**: Job posting describes security responsibilities, security requirements disclosed to candidates
- **Screening**: HR conducts screening per Section 4 before employment offer finalized
- **Offer**: Offer letter conditional on background check and contract signing
- **Contract**: Employment contract includes security clauses per Section 5
- **Onboarding**: First day includes NDA signature, AUP acknowledgment, security training assignment, policy portal access
- **Access Provisioning**: System access granted after security onboarding completed

**Mover Process** (Role Changes):

```
Role Change → Security Impact Assessment → Access Adjustment → Training Update
     ↓                 ↓                          ↓                  ↓
Promotion        Higher sensitivity?          Remove old access   Role-specific
Transfer         Enhanced screening?          Grant new access    training if needed
New project      Additional screening?        Privilege elevation
```

**Integration Points**:
- **Role Change Notification**: Manager notifies HR and IT of role change
- **Security Assessment**: CISO/Security Team assesses security implications (need for enhanced screening, new access requirements)
- **Screening**: Enhanced screening if moving to higher sensitivity role (Level 2→Level 3)
- **Access Management**: Old access removed, new access granted per new role
- **Training**: Role-specific security training provided if needed
- **Documentation**: Role change documented in HR system, role assignment updated in ISMS documentation

**Leaver Process** (Employment Termination):

```
Termination Notice → Access Deprovisioning → Asset Return → Exit Interview
       ↓                      ↓                    ↓              ↓
Resignation            Immediate (for cause)   Laptop         Remind of
Termination            Last day (voluntary)    Phone          post-termination
Retirement             -Accounts disabled      Badge, keys    obligations
                       -Data archived          Documents      (confidentiality)
```

**Integration Points**:
- **Termination Notification**: Manager or HR notifies IT/Security of termination (immediate notification for termination for cause)
- **Access Timing**: Access deprovisioned immediately for termination for cause, on last day for voluntary resignation/retirement
- **Asset Inventory**: HR provides checklist of assets to return (device inventory from IT asset management)
- **Exit Interview**: HR reminds employee of post-termination obligations (confidentiality, NDA, non-compete if applicable)
- **Post-Termination Monitoring**: Monitor for unauthorized access attempts, remove from distribution lists/physical access

**Continuous Process** (During Employment):

**Ongoing Activities**:
- **Annual Training**: Security awareness refresher training annually
- **Policy Acknowledgment**: Critical policies re-acknowledged annually
- **Access Reviews**: Quarterly access reviews (manager verifies access still appropriate)
- **Performance Reviews**: Security compliance discussed in performance reviews
- **Policy Updates**: Communicated to all personnel, acknowledgment collected if required

## Annex D: Example Employment Contract Security Clauses

**Purpose**: Provide example security clauses for integration into employment contracts.

**Usage**: These clauses are examples only and must be reviewed by Legal before use. Adapt to organizational needs and local employment law.

**Clause 1: Confidentiality and Data Protection**

> **CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATIONS**
>
> The Employee acknowledges that during the course of employment with the Organization, they will have access to and become familiar with confidential information, trade secrets, and proprietary information belonging to the Organization, its customers, partners, and suppliers ("Confidential Information").
>
> Confidential Information includes, but is not limited to: business strategies, financial information, customer data, technical information, source code, system designs, security architectures, marketing plans, personnel information, and any information marked or identified as confidential.
>
> The Employee agrees to:
> 1. Maintain the confidentiality of all Confidential Information during employment and after termination
> 2. Not disclose Confidential Information to any unauthorized person or entity without prior written authorization from the Organization
> 3. Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing their employment duties
> 4. Protect Confidential Information from unauthorized access, loss, or damage using reasonable care
> 5. Comply with the Organization's data protection policies and applicable data protection laws (including GDPR and Swiss nDSG)
>
> This confidentiality obligation shall survive termination of employment for a period of [2 years / perpetual for trade secrets] and shall be binding upon the Employee, their heirs, and assigns.
>
> The Employee acknowledges that breach of this confidentiality obligation may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment, and that the Organization reserves the right to seek legal remedies including injunctive relief and damages.

**Clause 2: Acceptable Use of IT Systems**

> **ACCEPTABLE USE OF INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY**
>
> The Employee acknowledges that the Organization provides information technology systems, including computers, mobile devices, email, internet access, and applications, primarily for business purposes. Incidental personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with work duties, violate security policies, or consume excessive resources.
>
> The Employee agrees to:
> 1. Use organizational IT systems in accordance with the Organization's Acceptable Use Policy
> 2. Protect access credentials (passwords, tokens, smart cards) and not share accounts with others
> 3. Not install unauthorized software or bypass security controls
> 4. Not use organizational systems for illegal activities, harassment, or inappropriate content
> 5. Report security incidents immediately to the Security Team or manager
>
> The Employee acknowledges that:
> 1. The Organization reserves the right to monitor system usage in accordance with applicable law
> 2. There is no expectation of privacy for business use of organizational systems
> 3. Personal use of organizational systems is subject to monitoring
> 4. Violations of the Acceptable Use Policy may result in disciplinary action including termination
>
> The Employee confirms they have received a copy of the Acceptable Use Policy and agree to comply with it.

**Clause 3: Security Training and Awareness**

> **SECURITY TRAINING AND AWARENESS**
>
> The Employee agrees to participate in and complete all mandatory information security awareness training programs as required by the Organization. Initial training must be completed within 30 days of employment commencement. Annual refresher training and role-specific security training are required as specified by the Organization.
>
> Failure to complete required training within prescribed timeframes may result in suspension of system access and/or disciplinary action. Training completion is considered in performance evaluations.

**Clause 4: Incident Reporting**

> **SECURITY INCIDENT REPORTING**
>
> The Employee shall immediately report any suspected or actual information security incidents to the Security Team or their manager. Security incidents include, but are not limited to: malware infections, phishing emails, lost or stolen devices, unauthorized access attempts, data breaches, and policy violations.
>
> The Employee agrees to cooperate fully with incident investigations and to provide truthful information to investigators. The Employee shall not tamper with evidence or obstruct investigations.
>
> The Organization prohibits retaliation against employees who report security incidents in good faith.

**Clause 5: Intellectual Property and Asset Return**

> **INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY AND ASSET RETURN**
>
> The Employee acknowledges that all work product, inventions, discoveries, and intellectual property created during the course of employment and related to the Organization's business belong to the Organization. The Employee hereby assigns all rights, title, and interest in such intellectual property to the Organization.
>
> Upon termination of employment, the Employee shall immediately return all organizational property including, but not limited to: computing devices (laptops, phones, tablets), physical access badges and keys, documents (physical and electronic), and any other organizational assets. The Employee's final compensation may be withheld pending return of all organizational property, where permitted by applicable law.
>
> The Employee shall not retain any copies of organizational information (including on personal devices or cloud storage) after termination.

**Clause 6: Post-Termination Obligations**

> **POST-TERMINATION OBLIGATIONS**
>
> Upon termination of employment, the Employee's access to all organizational systems and facilities will be immediately revoked. The Employee shall not attempt to access organizational systems after termination. Unauthorized access after termination may result in civil and criminal prosecution.
>
> The Employee's confidentiality and data protection obligations under this Agreement shall survive termination and continue in effect [indefinitely for trade secrets / for 2 years for other confidential information].
>
> The Employee acknowledges that violation of post-termination obligations may result in legal action by the Organization.

**Clause 7: Acknowledgment**

> **ACKNOWLEDGMENT**
>
> The Employee acknowledges that:
> 1. They have received, read, and understood this Employment Agreement and all referenced policies
> 2. They have had the opportunity to ask questions and seek legal advice regarding this Agreement
> 3. They enter into this Agreement voluntarily and with full understanding of its terms
> 4. They agree to be bound by the information security obligations set forth in this Agreement
>
